ASEAN Financial News / ASEAN Briefing on September 15, 2025

Stay updated with the "New Southbound" policy and the economy of ASEAN countries. The Central News Agency's "Southeast Asia Financial Information Network" provides daily top financial news from Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. Get important information about new government policies, industry trends, and business opportunities quickly, so you can understand the market and find chances for success.

**Vietnam: Vietnam Approves Stock Market Upgrade Plan, Focuses on Raising Money and Connecting Internationally**

Vietnam's Prime Minister Pham Minh Chinh recently approved a plan to improve the stock market. The goal is to make the stock market a key way to get money for medium and long-term projects, make the market rules better, and connect more with international markets.

For now, the plan aims to meet all the requirements for "Secondary Emerging Market" status in the FTSE Russell classification by 2025. After achieving this, Vietnam will work to keep this status.

**Laos: Laos Wants to Become a Blockchain Hub, First Blockchain Festival in November**

Laos will hold its first "Laos Blockchain Fest" with the theme of building a strong future chain. This event will create chances for new companies from Laos and other countries, and for businesses in the region. It will help Laos become a center for blockchain technology and finance.

Laos' Minister of Technology and Communications announced at a press conference that the event will be held on November 21-22 in Vientiane, the capital city.

**Singapore: Singapore Post Launches New Retail Postal Service for Changes in US Import Rules**

Bloomberg reported that Singapore Post Ltd. today launched a new service for regular customers who send mail to the United States. This is in response to recent changes in US import rules.

This new service is called "Speedpost Direct International (Retail)." It allows regular customers to send packages to the US using standard envelopes or boxes.

**Philippines: Philippines Gets Investment Promises of 27 Billion NTD from Japanese Companies, Including Mitsui & Co. and Marubeni**

According to Cristina Roque, the Philippine Secretary of Trade and Industry, a Philippine group visited Osaka, Japan, last week. During their visit, Japanese companies promised to invest about 51 billion Philippine Pesos (about 27 billion New Taiwan Dollars) in the Philippines.

The Philippine News Agency (PNA) reported that 34 billion Philippine Pesos of this amount comes from U-Kara Holdings Co., Ltd., the world's largest karaoke chain operator. Marubeni Corporation of Japan promised to invest 15 billion Philippine Pesos in real estate, financial technology, healthcare, and tree planting. Other investors include Sojitz Corporation and Mitsui & Co.

**Myanmar: Myanmar Tries to Attract Chinese Investment, Plans to Allow Investment in RMB**

Myanmar's military leader, Min Aung Hlaing, announced that Myanmar will quickly allow Chinese investments to be made in Chinese Yuan (RMB).

Min Aung Hlaing said that many Chinese investors prefer to use RMB. Myanmar has already used RMB and Burmese Kyat for some trade payments. Allowing investments in RMB and US dollars in Myanmar will help create more opportunities and grow the country's economy. Min Aung Hlaing asked all government departments to focus on attracting investment and to create a Myanmar-China Trade and Investment Association.

**Malaysia: OCBC Malaysia Plans to Invest 72 Million NTD Annually to Train AI Talent**

OCBC Malaysia will invest over 10 million Malaysian Ringgit (about 72 million New Taiwan Dollars) each year. This money will help its 5,000 employees get ready for the future of Artificial Intelligence (AI). The bank will offer hundreds of courses and new scholarships to improve their skills.

Starting next year, OCBC Malaysia will also offer two scholarships to students who study AI-related subjects. This is to help the country develop local AI talent.

**Thailand: Phuket Aims for 550 Billion Thai Baht in Tourism Income During Peak Season, Hopes for Pre-Pandemic Success**

Phuket has set a high goal of 550 billion Thai Baht in tourism income for the upcoming busy travel season. This is because early signs show that tourism is recovering strongly, almost reaching the levels seen before the pandemic in 2019.

The head of the Phuket Tourist Association said that the overall future for tourism looks good. He pointed out that more international direct flights, including possibly more flights from China, are good signs for tourism to improve by the end of the year.

**Indonesia: After Cabinet Reshuffle, 869 Million US Dollars Flow Out of Indonesia**

Indonesian President Prabowo Subianto changed his cabinet, including replacing the Minister of Finance. This led to a large amount of foreign money leaving Indonesia's financial market. In just three days, 14.24 trillion Indonesian Rupiah (about 869 million US dollars) flowed out. Indonesian Rupiah securities and government bonds were the most affected.

Experts believe that changes in leadership might make people think there will be changes in policy. However, if the government can clearly explain its policies, the recent outflow of money might only be a short-term reaction.

Criminal Investigation Bureau's Central Anti-Crime Unit Finds Drug Coffee Bag Factory; Suspect Throws Drugs into Cemetery During Police Check

The Central Anti-Crime Unit of the Criminal Investigation Bureau today announced that they found a factory making drug coffee bags. Police said that one suspect, when stopped by police, quickly threw the drugs into a public cemetery in Changhua and then left his car to escape. However, the police still caught him and found a large amount of drugs. The case has been sent to the Changhua District Prosecutor's Office for investigation.

The Central Anti-Crime Unit of the Criminal Investigation Bureau held a press conference today to explain the situation. They received information that a criminal group was making and selling "Level 3" drug coffee bags in Yuanlin City, Changhua County. A special team was quickly formed with the Yuanlin Precinct of the Changhua County Police Bureau and the Sixth Precinct of the Taichung City Police Bureau. They also reported the case to the Changhua District Prosecutor's Office for guidance.

Police said that after the special team found the location where the drug coffee bags were made, they started a search operation on October 14 last year. This happened at a quiet old farmhouse in Yuanlin City, Changhua County. They first caught the main suspect, a man named Zhang, who was in charge of the drug packaging factory.

Police found that another main suspect, a man named Xu, ran away with finished drug coffee bags. When police tried to stop him, he drove away very fast and threw the finished drug coffee bags into a public cemetery in Yongjing Township, Changhua County. He then left his car and escaped. The police continued to look for Xu and caught him on November 13 last year at his rented home in Qingshui District, Taichung City.

After a long investigation, the police found that the drug manufacturing group, led by Zhang, rented an old farmhouse in the countryside to make drug coffee bags. Then, the drugs were given to Xu to transport and sell for money. The special team found 2,398 bags of drug coffee bags, two large bags of drug pills, and one bag of the drug ingredient cathinone (weighing 269.62 grams), among other evidence.

Police said that after the investigation, the case was sent to the Changhua District Prosecutor's Office. Both Zhang and Xu were charged with drug-related crimes under the Narcotics Hazard Prevention Act in August this year.

Actress Chen Shu-fang Receives Golden Bell Special Contribution Award After More Than 60 Years of Acting; Set Designer Yeh Hui-lung Honored for His Professional Dedication

The nominees for the 60th Golden Bell Awards were announced today. Actress Chen Shu-fang and TV set designer Yeh Hui-lung received the Special Contribution Award. Chen Shu-fang has been acting for over sixty years. She said, "Being able to give my whole life to the performing arts that I love most is the happiest thing for me."

The press conference for the 60th Golden Bell Awards for TV programs and dramas was held today to announce the nominees. This year, 127 companies submitted works, with 1,103 entries for dramas and 889 entries for programs. 138 drama works and 101 program works were nominated. They will compete for 27 awards in the drama category and 21 awards in the program category.

Liang Xiu-shen, the head of the Special Awards Selection Committee, announced today that Chen Shu-fang and Yeh Hui-lung won the Golden Bell Special Contribution Award. Liang Xiu-shen said, "One winner works in front of the camera, and the other works behind the scenes. They are both 80 years old and are still working. They are great examples for us to follow. Also, they are both very open and willing to help new talent. Chen Shu-fang even reduced her fees to work on projects by students and young directors, encouraging and supporting them to stay in Taiwan."

Chen Shu-fang has been acting for over 60 years. Her work includes different types of performances like movies, TV shows, Taiwanese opera, and stage plays. From early Taiwanese-language films and local dramas to recent international award-winning movies, she has always stayed active in acting.

Chen Shu-fang has also worked hard for a long time to promote Taiwanese language culture and share her acting experience. She actively takes part in student films and works by young directors, sometimes even working for half price. She does this to help new talent and keep good people in Taiwan. Her professional and dedicated spirit makes her a role model in Taiwan's drama industry.

About receiving the Golden Bell Special Contribution Award, Chen Shu-fang said through her agent, "This honor is not just for me. It is also for all the audience members who have supported Taiwanese TV and drama over the years. Being able to give my whole life to the performing arts that I love most is the happiest thing for me."

This year is the 60th anniversary of the Golden Bell Awards. Everyone who works in front of and behind the scenes is important. The other winner of the Golden Bell Special Contribution Award is TV set designer Yeh Hui-lung. He is a great example of someone who works behind the scenes. His traditional craftsmanship and dedication are even more valuable in today's digital world. He is still working today and is happy to teach his skills to younger people.
