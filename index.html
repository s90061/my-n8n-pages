Argentina Live Torture and Murder Case Shocks the Nation, Thousands Protest

Today, thousands of protesters walked in the streets of Buenos Aires, Argentina. They asked for fairness for three young women who were tortured and killed. The terrible crime was shown live on social media, making the whole country very upset.

The families of the victims carried signs with the names and pictures of the three women. They marched with different left-wing groups to the country's main government building.

Signs among the protesters said things like: "This is a drug gang's killing of women!" and "Our lives are not something to throw away!"

Twenty-year-old cousins Morena Verdi and Brenda Del Castillo, and 15-year-old Lara Gutierrez, were missing for five days. On September 24, their bodies were found buried in a garden outside a house in a southern part of Buenos Aires.

Police investigators said that this crime is linked to drug gangs. The cruel act was shown live on Instagram to 45 special private accounts.

On September 26, the Minister of National Security, Patricia Bullrich, announced that a fifth person was arrested. This means five people are now in custody for the crime, including three men and two women.

Investigators stated that the three victims thought they were going to a party. But they were tricked and forced into a van. It is believed that this was a "punishment" because they broke the gang's rules. It was also done to scare other people.

Javier Alonso, who is in charge of security for Buenos Aires Province, said that the police learned about the video when they questioned one of the arrested people. This person told them about the video.

In the video, a leader of the gang can be heard saying, "This is what happens when you steal my drugs."

News from Argentina said that the torturers did terrible things to the victims. They cut their fingers, pulled out their nails, beat them, and stopped them from breathing.

Good Weather Expected, Highway Authority Predicts Tourist Traffic on Local Roads

Today is the second day of the Teachers' Day holiday. The Directorate General of Highways expects many cars on the roads for travel and sightseeing. Roads that might be busy include those connected to national highways, main roads between cities, and roads near popular tourist places. People who are driving should check the traffic conditions before they start their trip.

The Ministry of Transportation and Communications' Directorate General of Highways sent out a press release. It said that the weather is good today, so they expect many tourist cars on some roads. Roads that will likely be busy include those connecting to national expressways. In the north, this includes Provincial Highway 64 connecting to National Highway 3 Zhonghe Interchange, and Provincial Highway 65 connecting to National Highway 3 Tucheng Interchange. In the central area, it's Provincial Highway 74 Taichung Beltway connecting to National Highway 3 Wufeng section. In the south, it's Provincial Highway 86 connecting to National Highway 1 Rende System and Guiren Interchange, and Provincial Highway 18 connecting to National Highway 3 Zhongpu Interchange.

Main roads between cities that might be busy include Provincial Highway 61 in the central region, especially the Zhongzhang Bridge section. Also, roads near tourist spots might be busy. In the north, these are Provincial Highway 2 and Provincial Highway 2B from Guandu to Tamsui, Provincial Highway 2 from Wanli to Dawulun, Provincial Highway 2 Fulong section, and Provincial Highway 3 Daxi section. In the central area, it's Provincial Highway 21 around Sun Moon Lake. In the south, it's Provincial Highway 3 from Gukeng to Meishan.

The Directorate General of Highways said that to help with traffic, they will plan other routes for drivers. They will also change the timing of traffic lights, close some road crossings, and control when big trucks can drive. They will also work with local police to keep traffic moving and give tickets to people who break traffic rules.

5 Chinese Military Aircraft Cross Strait Median Line, Taiwan's Military Watches Closely

Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense said today that from 6 AM yesterday until 6 AM today, five Chinese military aircraft flew over the middle line of the Taiwan Strait. They entered the airspace (the sky) north, southwest, and east of Taiwan. Taiwan's military used its planes, ships, and missile systems on land to watch them very carefully and deal with the situation.

This morning, the Ministry of National Defense gave an update on the movements of Chinese military aircraft and ships. They saw 6 Chinese military aircraft. Out of these, 5 crossed the middle line of the Taiwan Strait and flew into the airspace in the northern, southwestern, and eastern parts. They also saw 7 Chinese navy ships and 2 government ships. All these vehicles continued to be active around the Taiwan Strait.

The Ministry of National Defense said that Taiwan's military is using its planes, ships, and missile systems on land to watch the situation very closely and respond as needed.
