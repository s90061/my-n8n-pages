Ministry of Transportation Connects Companies to Help People Repair Their Homes

Typhoon Danas and strong winds from the southwest have caused a lot of damage to homes in southern Taiwan. Today, the Ministry of Transportation said that it is hard to find workers and materials right now. So, the ministry has asked all its departments to help connect companies with people. They want to help people find workers to repair their homes.

The Ministry of Transportation wrote on Facebook today that Vice Minister Chen Yen-po held a meeting yesterday. He brought together different departments to check on the repair work in the damaged areas of Tainan. They also talked about how to continue helping. So far, they have visited more than 100 damaged homes. They are also looking for more workers and resources to help people rebuild their homes quickly.

The Ministry of Transportation explained that it is difficult to find workers and materials. People cannot easily get repair teams, and there are not enough workers or tools. The government has asked all its ministries to help. Each ministry is responsible for a different area. The Ministry of Transportation is in charge of helping the areas of Sinying District, Yanshui District, Baihe District, and Houbi District in Tainan City.

The Ministry of Transportation said that people who want to repair their homes should first register with their district chief. Then, the Ministry of Transportation will help connect different companies with these people. After that, the people and the companies will talk and sign a simple agreement. The Ministry of Transportationâ€™s main job is to connect people with companies; it does not pay for the repairs.

To make the repair work faster, the Ministry of Transportation also set some rules. Besides connecting companies, they will first help repair old one-story houses and homes where the roofs have been torn off, making them unsafe to live in. This is for immediate help. Also, old materials like asbestos tiles that are removed will be collected in one place. The district office will then help to remove them.

Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba Shows Care for Taiwan's Flood Victims; Foreign Minister Lin Chia-lung Thanks Him and Hopes for Future Cooperation

Recently, heavy rains caused floods and damage in Taiwan. Japan's Prime Minister, Shigeru Ishiba, sent a message to Taiwan to show his sympathy. Today, Taiwan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs said that Foreign Minister Lin Chia-lung thanked Japan again for their care. He also hopes that Taiwan and Japan can work together to solve problems. Both the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Taiwan's office in Japan have already thanked the Japanese government.

Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba wrote a message to show his sympathy to Taiwan. He said he was very sad for those who lost their lives and their families. He also sent his sincere wishes to the people who were affected by the floods. He prayed that daily life would return to normal soon. He ended his message with "Taiwan, go for it!" The Japan-Taiwan Exchange Association shared his message on Facebook this afternoon.

In response, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs released a statement this afternoon. It said that both the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Taiwan's office in Japan immediately thanked the Japanese government.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs also mentioned that Prime Minister Ishiba had sent a message of sympathy on July 12th. That message was for the damage caused by Typhoon Danas in Taiwan. This shows the deep and true friendship between Taiwan and Japan. The government and people in Taiwan are still working very hard to help in the disaster areas and to rebuild. They want to help the affected people rebuild their homes and get back to their normal lives quickly.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs added that Foreign Minister Lin Chia-lung once again gave his heartfelt thanks to the Japanese government for their warm care and real support. He also hopes that Taiwan and Japan will continue to work more closely in different areas in the future. They want to build a stronger, friendlier, and more lasting partnership to face all challenges together.
