Berlin Police Arrest 3 Hamas Suspects, Find Weapons, Suspect Terrorist Attack Plan

On October 1st, the German Federal Public Prosecutor's Office ordered the arrest of three people who are believed to be members of Hamas. Prosecutors said the suspects had been collecting guns and bullets for Hamas since this summer. They planned to attack Jewish and Israeli places in Germany. Police found an AK-47 assault rifle, several pistols, and hundreds of bullets during the operation.

The German Federal Public Prosecutor's Office said the three arrested people are Abed Al G. (36 years old), Wael F. M. (43 years old), and Ahmad I. (44 years old). Two of them are German citizens, and one was born in Lebanon. The Berlin newspaper reported that the two German suspects have Syrian and Lebanese family backgrounds.

Prosecutors stated that the three suspects are believed to have joined a foreign terrorist group. They worked as "overseas agents" for Hamas. They had direct contact with the military leaders of the group. They were planning violent actions that would be dangerous for Germany's national security.

Investigators watched the suspects for a long time. On October 1st, they learned that the suspects were exchanging weapons in the Moabit area of Berlin. The Berlin police quickly raided the location. They arrested the three people there and found an AK-47 assault rifle, several Glock pistols, and a lot of ammunition.

The prosecutors said that these weapons were meant for Hamas to carry out deadly attacks on Israeli or Jewish places in Germany. The suspects got the guns and bullets from the black market. They wanted to carry out attacks inside Germany.

This event caused a lot of worry in Germany. Stefanie Hubig, the Federal Minister of Justice, called it a "very serious event." She said that the country has a duty to protect Jewish life and will not allow any anti-Semitism.

Alexander Throm, a politician from the CDU party, asked for strict actions. These actions include taking away German citizenship from people involved in terrorism. After they finish their prison time, they should be sent out of the country, especially if they have two citizenships. He believes that people who plan terrorist attacks should not be allowed to stay in Germany.

Benjamin Jendro, a spokesperson for the Berlin police union, said that the weapons found show a worrying threat. He asked the government to quickly give new technology to security departments. He also asked for clearer laws to fight terrorist attacks more effectively.

The three suspects will be taken to Karlsruhe on October 2nd. A judge from the Federal Supreme Court will decide if they should be kept in prison.

In December 2023, German prosecutors arrested four Lebanese men in Berlin. These men were following orders from Hamas. They were looking for weapons hidden in Europe. Prosecutors said they were planning to attack Jewish places. At that time, this case changed how German security services saw Hamas. They used to think Germany was only a place for Hamas to hide supplies. But now, they believe Germany has become a target for Hamas's terrorist attacks.
