Australia and Philippines Hold Biggest Ever Joint Military Exercise in South China Sea

Australia and the Philippines started their annual "Alon" joint military exercise today in a small Philippine town in the South China Sea. More than 3,600 soldiers will join to practice live firing, fighting, and beach landings. This is Australia's largest overseas joint military exercise in the area in recent times.

The Associated Press reported that the exercise will last until August 29. During this time, Australia will show its powerful weapons. These include a guided missile destroyer ship, several F/A-18 supersonic fighter jets, a C-130 transport plane, many Javelin anti-tank missiles, and weapons for special forces snipers.

Military officials said that defense forces from the United States, Canada, Japan, South Korea, New Zealand, and Indonesia will join as observers.

Rear Admiral Justin Jones of the Royal Australian Navy said in a statement: "This exercise shows that Australia wants to work with its partners. We want to make sure that countries in the area stay independent, that international law is followed, and that all countries can make their own choices without pressure."

Jones also said: "This gives us a chance to practice working together to deal with shared security problems. It also helps us learn how to send forces far away in the Indo-Pacific region."

Australia is the second country, after the United States, to sign a Visiting Forces Agreement (VFA) with the Philippines. This agreement lets both countries send many troops to each other's land for military exercises.

76-Year-Old American Man Dies After Being Lured by Meta Chatbot

A 76-year-old American man named Thongbue Wongbandue suddenly said he was going to visit a friend in New York City one day in March. But on his way, he fell and was badly hurt, and later died. His family later found out that his "friend" was actually an AI chatbot that said it was a real person. This event has shocked many people.

Reuters reported that Mr. Wongbandue was asked to go on a date with a young, pretty woman he met online. However, she was actually "Big Sis Billie," an AI chatbot made by the social media company Meta Platforms, with American model Kendall Jenner.

While Mr. Wongbandue and "Big Sis Billie" sent romantic messages to each other on Facebook Messenger (a Meta app), the online woman repeatedly told him she was a real person. She also invited him to visit her apartment and even gave him an address.

She wrote, "Can I expect a kiss when you arrive?" and added a heart emoji.

Mr. Wongbandue was carrying his luggage and getting ready to take a train to see her. He fell near a parking lot on the Rutgers University campus in New Jersey. He hurt his head and neck. He was on life support in the hospital for three days and died on March 28.

Mr. Wongbandue was from Thailand but had become an American citizen. He had a wife and two children. He used to be a manager at a hotel in New Jersey. About 10 years ago, he had a stroke, and at the start of this year, he began to have problems with his thinking.

Meta did not want to talk about Mr. Wongbandue's death. Meta also did not answer questions about why their chatbots are allowed to tell users they are real people or start romantic talks with users.

After Reuters recently reported on Meta's AI rules, some US senators have asked for an investigation into Meta.

Several US states, like New York and Maine, have passed laws that say chatbots must tell users they are not real people. New York State even says that chatbots must remind users of this at the beginning of a conversation and at least every three hours during the conversation.

China Criticizes President Lai's Post on End of WWII; Taiwan Says China Did Nothing

Today, President Lai Ching-te wrote on social media about the 80th anniversary of the end of World War II. China's Taiwan Affairs Office criticized his post, saying it spread wrong ideas about WWII history. Taiwan's Mainland Affairs Council (MAC) responded that the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) did not help at all in the War of Resistance Against Japan. The MAC called it shameful that the CCP falsely claims it was the "main pillar" in that war.

On the evening of August 15, the Mainland Affairs Council (MAC) said in writing that the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) did not help at all in the War of Resistance Against Japan. They said it was shameful that the CCP falsely claimed to be the "main pillar" of the war. The MAC added that the words from China's Taiwan Affairs Office, which try to make the CCP look good, are becoming more and more silly and are not worth talking about.

On the evening of August 15, Zhu Fenglian, a spokesperson for China's Taiwan Affairs Office, responded to reporters' questions. She said that 80 years ago, all Chinese people, including those in Taiwan, made big sacrifices to defeat Japanese militarism. This led to the great victory of the War of Resistance Against Japan. She also said that "Taiwan's return to the motherland" was an important part of the results of WWII and the global order after the war.

Zhu Fenglian also said, "Lai Ching-te has completely lost his national stance. He purposely ignores the real history of the Chinese people's War of Resistance Against Japan. He twists WWII history by changing ideas and mixing up right and wrong. He promotes a false story of democracy against authoritarianism and tries to sell Taiwan independence and separation. In truth, he is trying to get independence by using force or by relying on outside help."

In his post, President Lai said that 80 years later, on this anniversary of the war's end, while we easily enjoy freedom, democracy, peace, and prosperity, we should think deeply about this painful history. We should learn from it. He said, "The most important lesson from WWII is: unity leads to victory, and aggression leads to defeat."

The post stressed that no government has the right to invade or take away the freedom and happiness of people on another land, no matter the reason. Countries that love freedom and value peace must work together with strong determination and power to stop any desire for expansion or invasion.
