Global Oil Prices Fall Slightly Due to Inventory Data

(New York, July 16, Central News Agency) Global oil prices fell a little today. This was because of signs that the oil market was weak, especially due to information about oil supplies.

In New York, the price for West Texas Intermediate crude oil, which will be delivered in August, went down by 0.2%. It closed at 66.38 US dollars per barrel.

In London, the price for North Sea Brent crude oil, which will be delivered in September, went down by 0.3%. It closed at 68.52 US dollars per barrel. (Translator: Chen Zhengjian / Editor: Ji Jinling) 1140717

US Stocks Go Up After Report About Trump and Powell

(New York, July 16, Central News Agency) News came out that US President Trump might remove Jerome Powell, who is in charge of the Federal Reserve (Fed). Because of this, US stock prices went down for a while today. But by the end of the day, the three main stock indexes went back up. The Nasdaq index reached a new high, while the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index went down a little.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went up by 231.49 points, which is 0.53%. It closed at 44254.78 points.

The S&P 500 index went up by 19.94 points, which is 0.32%. It closed at 6263.70 points.

The Nasdaq index went up by 52.69 points, which is 0.25%. It closed at 20730.49 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index went down by 22.40 points, which is 0.39%. It closed at 5696.21 points. (Translator: Chen Zhengjian / Editor: Ji Jinling) 1140717

US Plans New Rules to Stop Chinese Equipment in Undersea Cables

(Washington, July 16, Central News Agency) The US Federal Communications Commission (FCC) said today that it is planning to make new rules. These rules would stop companies from connecting undersea communication cables that use Chinese technology or equipment to the United States.

Reuters reported that FCC Chairman Brendan Carr said in a statement: "In recent years, we have seen that undersea cable systems are at risk from foreign enemies, like China. So, we are taking action to protect our undersea cables. This will keep them safe from damage and attacks by foreign enemies and from online and physical dangers."

For many years, the US has been worried about China's role in handling internet traffic. They are also concerned about possible spying.

The US has many worries about the safety of data on over 400 undersea cables. These cables carry 99% of all international internet traffic.

The FCC will also ask for ideas on how to make undersea cables safer. They want to know how to protect them more from equipment used by foreign enemies. They will look into other ways to protect the cables.

Before this, two undersea fiber optic communication cables in the Baltic Sea were cut. This made officials start an investigation to see if someone caused the damage. In 2023, Taiwan said that two Chinese ships cut the only two internet cables to the Matsu Islands. (Translator: Ji Jinling / Editor: Chen Zhengjian) 1140717
