Bus Accident in Southern Iran Kills At Least 21 People

State media in Iran reported today that a bus overturned in the southern part of the country. At least 21 people died, and 29 others were hurt.

AFP news agency said that this big accident happened near Kavar town. Kavar is about 1000 kilometers south of Tehran, which is the capital city of Iran. The reason for the accident is not yet clear.

Staff at Kavar Hospital told Iran's state television that 21 people have died and 29 others were injured.

IRNA, Iran's news agency, reported that nearly 20,000 people died in traffic accidents in Iran in the 12 months up to March this year.

US Tech News Says Nvidia Has Limited H20 Chips for China

The Information, a website about technology news in the US, reported today that Nvidia, a big American company that makes AI chips, has told its customers in China that there are not many H20 chips available. Nvidia also does not plan to make more of these chips.

Reuters news agency reported that the H20 chip was the strongest AI chip Nvidia could sell to China. This was allowed under export rules from the US. But in April, the US government stopped the sale of H20 chips. Because of this, The Information, using information from two people who know about the situation, said that Nvidia has had to stop taking new orders. Nvidia also canceled its orders for making these chips with TSMC, the company that produces them.

Nvidia said earlier this week that they are planning to start selling H20 chips to China again.

Tour Boat Sinks in Vietnam's Ha Long Bay, At Least 28 Dead

Vietnamese news reported that a tour boat with more than 50 people on board overturned today in the water of Ha Long Bay. At least 28 people died, and more than 10 people are still missing.

VNExpress news agency reported that the tour boat, which had 53 people, turned over in the afternoon. Vietnam's border guards and navy saved 11 people and found 28 bodies.

Most of the people on the boat were families from Hanoi, the capital city. More than 20 of them were children.
