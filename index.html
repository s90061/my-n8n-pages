US Lawmakers Suggest Stronger Defense Partnership with Taiwan Against China's Threat

(Washington D.C., August 1st, Central News Agency) This week, politicians from both parties in the US Congress introduced a new law. This law tells the US Defense Department to work with Taiwan. The goal is to make both sides' defense industries stronger. This is to help against threats from China's military technology. The law says they should work together on things like drones, computer chips, and missile systems.

The House Select Committee on the Chinese Communist Party gave a press release today. It said that John Moolenaar (Republican leader of the committee), Zach Nunn (Republican member), and Jill Tokuda (Democratic member) all put forward this law this week. It asks the US Defense Department to start a formal partnership with Taiwan.

This law can be called the "U.S.-Taiwan Defense Innovation Partnership Act." The press release explained that the law wants defense companies in the US and Taiwan to work together. They will develop new things and create joint projects. This is to stop China's military technology that can be used for both military and civilian purposes.

The law states that the main areas for working together include developing drones, computer chips, artificial intelligence, and missile systems.

Mr. Moolenaar said that over time, China's leader, Xi Jinping, has been putting more pressure on Taiwan. He said, "This law will make our cooperation with Taiwan in important defense industries stronger. It will also make us more ready to face Beijing, which is becoming more threatening."

Mr. Nunn also added that this defense partnership will be a very important way to stop attacks. He said it will "help Taiwan get new US technology faster and give Taiwan an advantage it needs to protect itself." (Editor: Chen Hui-ping) August 2, 2025.

Hualien County Has a 4.6 Magnitude Earthquake, Strongest Shake Was Level 4

(Taipei, August 2nd, Central News Agency) The Central Weather Bureau has the newest information. Today at 7:48 AM, there was an earthquake. Its strength was 4.6 on the Richter scale. The earthquake was 5 kilometers deep. Its center was 22.5 kilometers southwest of Hualien County Government (in Shoufeng Township, Hualien County). The strongest shaking felt was Level 4 in Hualien County. (Editor: Chang Hsiao-wen) August 2, 2025.

Unstable Weather: Heavy Rain Possible in Southern Mountains, Brief Showers North of Hsinchu

(Taipei, August 2nd, Central News Agency) The Weather Bureau said that today, the southwest wind will get stronger. This means the weather will not be stable. There will likely be strong, short periods of rain. Areas south of Chiayi will have showers or thunderstorms. Areas from Miaoli to Yunlin and mountain areas in the southeast will have heavy rain or even very heavy rain. There is a chance of very heavy rain or more in the mountain areas south of Chiayi.

Also, the Weather Bureau said that areas north of Hsinchu and in the southeast will have some brief showers or thunderstorms. In the afternoon, there might also be some heavy rain. Other areas will be mostly cloudy with some brief afternoon thunderstorms. You should carry an umbrella when you go out.

The Weather Bureau warns that because it has been raining for many days, people in mountain areas should be careful of landslides, falling rocks, mudslides, and rivers that might overflow. In low-lying areas, people should watch out for standing water or floods. Please be safe when you go outside.

Regarding temperatures, high temperatures in central, southern, Hualien, and Taitung areas will be around 29 to 31 degrees Celsius. Other areas will be about 33 to 35 degrees Celsius.

For the weather on the islands: Penghu will be cloudy with brief showers or thunderstorms, with temperatures from 27 to 30 degrees Celsius. Kinmen will be mostly cloudy, with temperatures from 27 to 32 degrees Celsius. Matsu will be mostly cloudy, with temperatures from 28 to 32 degrees Celsius.

The Weather Bureau also said that the southwest wind is quite strong. Today and tomorrow, Taoyuan City, Hsinchu County, Orchid Island, Green Island, Kinmen, and Matsu might have winds of level 6 or stronger, or gusts of level 8 or stronger in some places. Also, the southeastern coast (Green Island, Orchid Island) and the southern coast might have long waves, up to 2 to 3 meters high. Please be careful if you are near the sea.

The Weather Bureau stated that Typhoon Krosa, the 9th typhoon, was about 2240 kilometers northeast of Taipei at 2 AM today. It is moving northeast and then east-northeast. It will not directly affect Taiwan. Tropical Depression No. 13 was about 1510 kilometers east of Taipei at 2 AM today. It is moving northeast towards the sea northeast of Japan. It might become a weak typhoon.

Lastly, according to the Environmental Protection Administration's air quality forecast, the southwest wind today will help clean the air with rain. So, the air quality will mostly be good to fair. In the northern parts, where the wind blows away from, pollutants might build up a bit. The air quality in Zhudong, Central, Yunlin-Chiayi-Nantou, Yilan, Hualien-Taitung areas, and Penghu will be "Good." The air quality in Northern, Kaohsiung-Pingtung areas, Matsu, and Kinmen will be "Fair." You can find more details at https://www.moenv.gov.tw. (Editor: Wu Su-jou) August 2, 2025.
