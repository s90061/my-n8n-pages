Spain's Wildfires Out of Control: Heavy Spring Rains and Empty Farms Make Them Worse

Spain is having many wildfires in August. This year, about 400,000 hectares of land have burned. This is the worst year since records began. News reports say that very hot weather and dry conditions cause fires. Also, a lot of rain in spring made many plants grow. Then, old farms were not used anymore, so there were no fire breaks. The Spanish TV and radio company (RTVE) looked at why this year's fire season was so bad. They found that many things worked together to make the fires spread very quickly and be almost impossible to stop.

First, Spain had unusually strong heat in August. From August 3rd to 18th, there were 16 days of extremely hot weather. This was a new record. The average temperature was 4.6 degrees Celsius higher than usual. This meant that half of the country was at high risk of fire.

Also, Spain had a lot of rain in spring this year. This caused many plants to grow. When these plants dried out because of the sudden drought, they became fuel for fires. Strong winds and land that helps fires spread also made the wildfires very dangerous.

Ukraine Uses Cheap Drones to Destroy Two Important Bridges in Russia

Ukraine says its army used cheap drones to attack many mines and weapons hidden by Russian soldiers under bridges. They successfully blew up two important bridges on Russia's border. These drones cost between 600 and 725 US dollars (about NT$22,178).

CNN reported that the Ukrainian army said Russian troops often used these two bridges near Kharkiv, Ukraine, on the Russian side of the border to move supplies.

These bridges were very important. Russian soldiers had placed mines under them. If Ukrainian soldiers came close, the Russian army could blow up the bridges to stop them.

The 58th Independent Motorized Infantry Brigade of Ukraine, who did this mission, told CNN that they saw unusual activity near the bridges. So, they decided to watch the bridges more closely.

A person from the brigade told CNN: "It was clear something was happening there. We couldn't send a normal drone under the bridge to check because the signal would be lost. So, we sent a first-person view drone with special fiber optic equipment."

The video showed that when the drone got close to the bridge, it found many anti-tank mines and other weapons piled up under it. A piece of fabric, possibly used to hide them, was lying nearby. The representative said, "After we saw the mines, we attacked."

After the drone attacked the mines and weapons, the video stopped. Another camera, filming from a bit further away, showed a big explosion. CNN confirmed that the bridge in the video was in Belgorod, southern Russia, near the Ukrainian border.

The representative said, "Then we decided to check another Russian bridge. We found mines there too, so we attacked it. We saw a chance and we used it."

Russia has not said anything about the bridge attacks.

The 58th Independent Motorized Infantry Brigade told CNN that the drones used in this attack each cost between 25,000 and 30,000 Ukrainian Hryvnia, which is about 600 to 725 US dollars.
