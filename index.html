TSG Hawks Sign New Pitcher From Major League Baseball, Named Namsia

The TSG Hawks baseball team in Taiwan announced today that they have signed a new foreign pitcher, Dinelson Lamet. He has played over 100 games in Major League Baseball (MLB) in the United States. His jersey number will be 45. He has played 121 games in MLB. Last year, he was teammates with Shohei Ohtani on the Dodgers team.

The TSG Hawks team said today that they signed Lamet to make their pitching stronger. Lamet is 33 years old. He has played for the Padres, Rockies, Red Sox, Dodgers, and Royals teams in MLB. Last year, he was teammates with Japanese star Shohei Ohtani when he played for the Dodgers.

Lamet has played in 121 MLB games in total. He started 60 of those games. His record is 17 wins and 24 losses. He pitched 367.2 innings and got 474 strikeouts. His earned run average (ERA) is 4.63.

In 2017, 2019, and 2020, Lamet was a starting pitcher. In 2020, he started 12 games in MLB and pitched 69 innings. His ERA was only 2.09. He only gave up 39 hits and got 93 strikeouts. Lamet also played in MLB last year. This year, he is playing in the Mexican League. He has started 17 games there. His record is 7 wins and 5 losses. His ERA is 5.01, and he has 88 strikeouts in 88 innings.

The second half of the Chinese Professional Baseball League (CPBL) started on July 4th this year. The TSG Hawks currently have 7 wins, 6 losses, and 2 ties in the second half. Their winning rate is 53.8%, and they are in second place.

Ministry of Transportation Connects Companies to Help People Repair Homes

Typhoon Danas and strong winds from the southwest have affected southern Taiwan. Some people's homes are badly damaged. The Ministry of Transportation said today that there are not enough workers or materials right now. So, they have asked different departments to help connect companies with residents. They hope to help people find workers to repair their homes.

The Ministry of Transportation posted on Facebook today that Vice Minister Chen Yen-po held a meeting yesterday. He gathered different departments to check on the repair work in the affected areas of Tainan. They also talked about how to continue helping. So far, they have visited over 100 damaged homes. They are still checking what people and resources they can use. They are working hard to help people rebuild their homes soon.

The Ministry of Transportation said that because there are not enough workers and materials, people cannot find repair teams. Also, there are no workers or tools available. The government has asked all ministries to help. They have divided the affected areas, and the Ministry of Transportation is responsible for Xinying District, Yanshui District, Baihe District, and Houbi District in Tainan City.

The Ministry of Transportation said that people who want to repair their homes can first register with their district chief. The Ministry of Transportation will then connect companies with residents. After that, residents will talk with the companies. Once they agree, they will sign a simple contract. The Ministry of Transportation's main job is to connect them; they do not pay money for the repairs.

To make the repair work faster after the disaster, the Ministry of Transportation also set some rules for action. Besides connecting companies, they will first help old, one-story houses that have lost their roofs and are not safe to live in. This is urgent help. Also, old materials like asbestos tiles will be collected in one place. The district office will then help remove them.

Shigeru Ishiba Sends Condolences for Central and Southern Taiwan Floods, Joseph Wu Thanks Him and Hopes for Cooperation

Recently, heavy rains caused damage in Taiwan. Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba sent a message to Taiwan to express his sympathy. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA) said today that Foreign Minister Joseph Wu thanked Japan again for their care. He also hopes that Taiwan and Japan can work together to face challenges. MOFA and Taiwan's office in Japan have both thanked the Japanese government.

Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba posted a message to show his sympathy to Taiwan. He said he feels sad for those who lost their lives and their families. He also expressed his true sympathy to the people affected by the disaster. He prayed that daily life would return to normal soon and said, "Go, Taiwan!" The Japan-Taiwan Exchange Association shared this message on Facebook in the afternoon.

In response, MOFA released a press statement in the afternoon. It said that MOFA and Taiwan's office in Japan had thanked the Japanese government right away.

MOFA also said that Shigeru Ishiba had sent a message on July 12 to show his sympathy for the damage caused by Typhoon Danas in Taiwan. This shows the deep and true friendship between Taiwan and Japan. The Taiwanese government and many groups in society are still working hard to help with rescue and recovery. They are helping the affected people rebuild their homes and return to normal life soon.

MOFA said that Foreign Minister Joseph Wu once again gave his sincere thanks for the Japanese government's kind care and true support. He also hopes that Taiwan and Japan will continue to work more closely in different areas in the future. They want to build a stronger, friendlier, and more lasting partnership to face all kinds of challenges together.
