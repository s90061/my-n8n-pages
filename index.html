China to Make Social Insurance Mandatory; Official News Says It Will Help People Spend More

Taipei, August 16 ‚Äì China will start a mandatory social insurance system on September 1. This has made many businesses and workers worried. Today, the official newspaper, People's Daily, wrote that social insurance is a "basic need for living" for individuals. For companies, it is a "pass to the future." For China, it is a "way to keep society stable" and also a "way to make people spend more money."

On August 1, China's Supreme People's Court announced a new rule. It says that any agreement between an employer and an employee not to pay social insurance is not valid. If an employer does not pay, employees can end their work contract and ask for money from the company. This new rule will start on September 1.

After this rule was announced, many small businesses and low-paid workers in China became worried. They are afraid they cannot afford the high social insurance costs. Experts say that in the future, many people might choose "flexible work" or part-time jobs to avoid paying social insurance.

People's Daily said today that recent talks about "mandatory social insurance for everyone" were confusing. They said it's important to explain things clearly. The article said the Supreme Court's new rule does not create new laws. Instead, it makes the rules clearer for courts across the country. It also said that social insurance has always been mandatory for legal workers, and it does not cover "everyone," but only people who are working.

The article criticized some online media for using words like "everyone," "mandatory," and "new rule" to get attention. They said this only confuses people and causes problems.

This People's Daily article gave three reasons to support mandatory social insurance.

First, for individuals, social insurance is a "basic need for living," not an "extra option." It said some workers are unsure about joining social insurance because they "only think about money now and what they put in versus what they get out, not about the long-term risks." They also worry that companies might find ways around the rules.

Second, for companies, social insurance is a "pass to the future," not a "burden." It said that making more companies join social insurance will increase costs for businesses, especially small and medium ones. But this is not a reason to avoid paying social insurance.

The article said that not paying social insurance is against the law. If a company uses this to win by having lower prices, it cannot grow well in the long run, especially now when companies want to "invest in people." Companies must be very careful about following rules. They should not try to save money by not paying social insurance. Instead, they should focus on their strengths and special skills to move to better positions in the industry.

The article also said that it is important to understand the money problems that small and medium businesses face. The key to solving this is to have more government policies to help these companies.

The article also said that for the country, social insurance is a "way to keep society stable" and also a "way to make people spend more money." It said that social insurance helps people with medical care, retirement, and unemployment. So, people will not need to save as much money "just in case." Instead, they will spend more money on improving their lives, travel, hobbies, and personal development. The more social insurance helps people, the more it encourages them to spend.

The People's Daily article finished by saying that social insurance is not the only way to help people have good lives. But doing social insurance well is important right now. August 16, 2025.
