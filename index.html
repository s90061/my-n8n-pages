New York State Approves New Underground Power Line, Micron's Huge Chip Factory Project Continues

New York State Governor Kathy Hochul announced that the New York State Public Service Commission has approved a new underground power line. This power line will connect the Clay substation to a very large chip factory that Micron Technology plans to build in Onondaga County.

Reuters reported that this underground power line is more than 3 kilometers long. It is an important part of Micron Technology's 100 billion US dollar investment in central New York State. This investment is for making computer chips.

Governor Hochul stated in a press release on October 16 that this is the biggest private investment in New York State's history.

This investment plan is expected to create over 50,000 jobs in the next 20 years. This includes 9,000 direct jobs within Micron itself.

Hochul said, "This project will greatly change central New York State. We are moving forward very quickly and carefully."

Micron and New York State agreed in 2022 to build an advanced manufacturing base in the state. This large chip factory aims to produce about a quarter of all computer chips in the United States by 2030.

KMT Chairman Election: Pingtung Vote Irregularity, Su Ching-chuan Says 5-Person Team Investigating

Today, during the Kuomintang (KMT) chairman election, something happened in Gaoshu Township, Pingtung County. A polling observer named Mr. Ke, from candidate Hau Lung-pin's team, took other people's ballots without permission. Su Ching-chuan, the chairman of the Pingtung County KMT branch, said a team of 5 people is looking into this. The results will be sent to the KMT headquarters, and they will follow party rules.

The Pingtung County KMT branch said in a press release today that election staff in Gaoshu Township checked the votes. They found that Mr. Ke, the polling observer, seemed to have taken other people's ballots. After checking at the scene, Mr. Ke admitted his wrong actions. After this happened, they stopped using that ballot, and they told the county party branch to record it and check it later.

The Pingtung County KMT branch also mentioned that Mr. Ke wrote a statement by himself. He said that a supporter of a candidate wanted to make things easy and did not follow the rules carefully. He did not think about the fairness and image of the election. He felt very sorry for what he did.

Su Ching-chuan told the Central News Agency that the 5-person team is investigating. The full results will definitely be sent to the party headquarters. They will follow the party rules to make sure the election is fair and proper.

President Lai Holds Meeting with Yunlin Party Officials, Focuses on People's Lives, Economy, and Elections

President Lai Ching-te, who is also the chairman of the Democratic Progressive Party (DPP), visited Yunlin County today. He held a meeting with local party officials. During the meeting, they talked about people's lives, the economy, and next year's local elections. Party Secretary-General Hsu Kuo-yung said the DPP is very careful with every city and county. Their only attitude towards elections is "win!"

President Lai went to Yunlin County this afternoon. He met with Yunlin County party officials. DPP legislator and Yunlin County party branch chairman Liu Chien-kuo was with him.

Before the meeting, Hsu Kuo-yung said that the DPP keeps moving forward. Their love for this land has not changed. Everyone hopes to make Taiwan better and make governing smoother. The goal of this trip was to talk with party officials and exchange ideas. They wanted to hear local opinions and understand the problems party officials face when serving voters and the issues people care about most. They will find common ground from these many opinions, hoping that governing will be smoother.

The topics discussed included people's lives and the economy. They also talked about next year's local elections, especially who the DPP candidate for Yunlin County Magistrate will be.

When asked if Yunlin County is a difficult election area, Hsu Kuo-yung, who is also the head of the 2026 Election Strategy Committee, said that the DPP is very careful with every city and county. Their attitude towards elections is only one word: "win." According to the party's rules, the candidate must be suggested by the Election Strategy Committee to the President. Then the proposal needs to be approved by the Central Executive Committee.
