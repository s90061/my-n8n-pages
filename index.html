MLB Wild Card Series: Dodgers Win with Strong Hitting, Reds Go Home First

The Los Angeles Dodgers, who won last year, played against the Cincinnati Reds in the second game of the Major League Baseball Wild Card Series today. The Dodgers did not start well. Teoscar Hernandez, who played well yesterday, also made a mistake. Because of this, Sal Stewart from the Reds hit a single to right field and got 2 runs.

The Dodgers started to hit well in the third inning. Ben Rortvedt hit a double, and Mookie Betts then hit a single to get 1 run back for the team. Premier Cho Jung-tai: Hualien Disaster Area Will Be Fixed in Stages; Causes Will Be Carefully Checked to Improve Disaster Prevention

Premier Cho Jung-tai said today that the Hualien Mataian landslide disaster happened more than a week ago. They are now working to save people and fix things. Later, they will carefully check why the disaster happened and how they reacted. This will help them make things better and find out who is responsible.

The Hualien Mataian landslide disaster happened over a week ago. As of yesterday, 18 people died, 6 people are still missing, and 139 people were hurt. Lee Hui-chih, a spokesperson for the Executive Yuan, said today that Premier Cho Jung-tai explained the plan in a meeting. He said they are saving lives, helping people rebuild their homes, cleaning up trash and mud, and fixing local roads and nature. The goal is for people to have their basic lives back before the Mid-Autumn Festival.

For the next steps, Premier Cho said the government will rebuild homes, farms, businesses, and public buildings. At the same time, they will carefully look into why the disaster happened and how they managed it. This will help them improve and decide who is responsible. This will also make them better at predicting and preventing disasters caused by bad weather in the future.

Premier Cho Jung-tai also led government officials in a minute of silence today for those who died in the landslide. He sent his sincere sympathy to the families of the victims. Six people are still missing. Premier Cho asked rescue workers to keep searching strongly and hoped for good news. He also prayed for the missing people.

He also said thank you to all levels of government, the army, and volunteers for helping people fix their homes after the event. He said they showed great strength and unity. The government will also fully support the work after the disaster. Minister Ji Lian-cheng from the Executive Yuan has already visited the families of the victims. Vice President Hsiao Bi-khim will also go to the disaster area again to show the government's sympathy and respect.

Taipei City: More Drivers Not Stopping for Pedestrians; Drivers Need to Follow Rules Better

The Taipei City Motor Vehicle Arbitration Office said today that there are more cases of drivers in Taipei not stopping for people walking. This happened in the first six months of this year compared to the same time last year. This means that some drivers need to be better at following the rules. The Ministry of Transportation and Communications has made the fines higher for not stopping for people walking. They ask drivers to be careful.

The Taipei City Department of Transportation held a press conference this morning called "High Cost for Not Stopping for Pedestrians." Data from the Taipei City Motor Vehicle Arbitration Office shows that the Ministry of Transportation and Communications increased fines starting from June 30. This is to warn drivers to stop for people walking. If a driver does not stop and causes a small injury, the fine is now 18,000 New Taiwan Dollars. If it causes a serious injury or death, the highest fine can be 36,000 New Taiwan Dollars.

The Taipei City Motor Vehicle Arbitration Office said that in the first six months of this year, there were 11,818 cases where drivers did not stop for people walking. This is 1,969 more cases than last year, an increase of almost 20%. This shows that some drivers still need to get better at following the rules about stopping for pedestrians.

The Arbitration Office said that if a car hits a person because the driver did not stop, causing injury or death, there will be a large fine. Also, the driver might face legal action in civil and criminal courts. Drivers should follow traffic safety rules. They should also be careful and always look around to see what is happening. This way, they can stop accidents from happening.

Li Tian-xing, the head of the Arbitration Office's penalty section, spoke to reporters. He said that people driving should be very careful when they go through intersections without traffic lights or crosswalks. If not, accidents with injuries might happen. Also, because people walking are weaker, cars should always let them cross, no matter what the traffic lights show.

He pointed out that drivers should be very careful when going through a crosswalk. If a person walks across when the light is not for them, the driver might have less blame. But if an accident happens, there will be a fine and legal problems, including long court cases and large payments. He asks everyone to be careful on the road.

The Arbitration Office said that drivers often think they are not at fault if their car's pillar blocked their view. But this is wrong. If a person walks on a crosswalk with the right light, they are not to blame for an accident. Drivers should be aware of blind spots from the car's pillar and turn their head to check. Cars should stop for people walking when near a crosswalk, with or without traffic lights.
