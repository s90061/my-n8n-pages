Robert Redford Passes Away, Meryl Streep Calls Him a Superstar, Jane Fonda Deeply Saddened

Hollywood legend Robert Redford died this morning. Famous actress Meryl Streep, who worked with him in movies like "Out of Africa," praised him, calling him a "superstar." Oscar-winning actress Jane Fonda, who worked with him on many classic films, also shared her sadness, saying he was "a wonderful person in every way."

Agence France-Presse (AFP) reported that Meryl Streep released a short statement through her public relations. She said, "One of the great stars has passed away. Rest in peace, my dear friend."

Robert Redford's publicist, Cindi Berger, said in a statement that Robert Redford died this morning at his home in Utah. He was 89 years old.

Jane Fonda said, "I was very shocked this morning to hear the news that Bob (Robert Redford) passed away. I cannot stop crying. He meant a lot to me and was a wonderful person in every way. He represented an America that we must keep fighting for."

Robert Redford was famous for films like "Butch Cassidy and the Sundance Kid." When he was young, he was very handsome and a well-known "Hollywood golden boy." Later, he also became a good director and won an Oscar for Best Director.

Markets Watch Fed Meeting, European Stocks Close Lower

The US Federal Reserve (Fed) started a two-day meeting today to discuss money policy. People expect the Fed to lower interest rates. Because of this, many people in the market were waiting to see what would happen. The three main stock indexes in Europe all closed lower today.

The FTSE 100 index in London went down by 81.37 points, or 0.88%. It closed at 9195.66 points.

The DAX index in Frankfurt fell by 419.62 points, or 1.77%. It closed at 23329.24 points.

The CAC 40 index in Paris dropped by 78.71 points, or 1%. It closed at 7818.22 points.

Yageo: Shibaura Electronics Officially Supports Tender Offer, Period Extended to Oct 3

Yageo (2327), a big company that makes electronic parts, has made good progress in buying Japan's Shibaura Electronics. Japanese news reported last night that Shibaura Electronics supports Yageo's offer to buy its shares. Yageo said in a press release last night that it thanks Shibaura Electronics for officially supporting its offer and telling shareholders to take part.

Yageo explained that because of important changes and rules in Japan, the process for buying shares must be 10 days longer. It will now end on October 3rd. This is to make sure shareholders have enough time to think carefully about this positive change and finish the selling process.

Japanese news said that Shibaura Electronics announced yesterday its support. It believes that Yageo's offer to buy shares will bring benefits, like stronger business overseas and more types of products. Today, Shibaura Electronics also officially announced its support for Yageo's offer.

Japanese media also said that Yageo and Shibaura Electronics agreed yesterday that after Yageo finishes buying the shares, Shibaura Electronics will stay a separate company. Yageo will respect Shibaura Electronics' right to run its own business as much as possible. Both companies also agreed that Shibaura Electronics will keep its current employees.

Yageo said last night that they had good talks and worked together well for several months. This was key to Shibaura Electronics changing its mind and supporting the offer.

Yageo said it is happy that Shibaura Electronics fully agrees on the benefits they can achieve together. Yageo also believes that this cooperation will create a better future for Shibaura Electronics and its employees.

Yageo officially started offering to buy Shibaura Electronics shares for 6200 Japanese Yen per share on May 9th. During this time, Yageo changed the deadline for buying shares several times. To compete with Japan's Minebea Mitsumi, Yageo announced on August 23rd that it would increase its offer price for Shibaura Electronics shares to 7130 Japanese Yen per share.

On September 2nd, Yageo said it had received approval for foreign investment in Japan. It expects to finish the process in the third quarter of the year.

Based on information, Shibaura Electronics mainly makes NTC thermistors (parts that sense temperature) and sells sensors for temperature, humidity, and wind speed.
