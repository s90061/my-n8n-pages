Berlin Police Arrest 3 Hamas Suspects, Find Weapons, Suspect Terrorist Attack Planning

On October 1st, the German federal prosecutor ordered the arrest of three people believed to be members of Hamas. The prosecutor said that since this summer, the suspects have been collecting guns and bullets for Hamas. They were planning to attack Jewish and Israeli places in Germany. During the police action, an AK-47 assault rifle, several pistols, and hundreds of bullets were found.

The German federal prosecutor stated that the three arrested people are Abed Al G. (36 years old), Wael F. M. (43 years old), and Ahmad I. (44 years old). Two of them are German citizens, and one was born in Lebanon. According to the Berlin newspaper, the two German suspects have Syrian and Lebanese backgrounds.

The prosecutor said that the three people are suspected of joining a foreign terrorist group. They worked as "overseas agents" for Hamas and were in direct contact with the military leaders of the group. They were planning violent actions that would harm Germany's national security.

Investigators watched the suspects for a long time. On October 1st, they learned that the suspects were exchanging weapons in the Moabit area of Berlin. Berlin police quickly raided the location and arrested the three people on the spot. They found an AK-47 assault rifle, several Glock pistols, and a lot of ammunition.

The prosecutor stressed that these weapons were meant for Hamas to carry out deadly attacks on Israeli or Jewish places in Germany. The suspects got the guns and bullets from the black market and tried to launch attacks inside Germany.

This event has caused great concern in Germany. Stefanie Hubig, the Federal Minister of Justice, called it a "very serious event." She said that the country has a duty to protect Jewish life and will not accept antisemitism.

Alexander Throm, a member of the CDU party, asked for strong actions. These include taking away German citizenship from people involved in terrorism and deporting them after they serve their prison time, especially those with dual citizenship. He believes that people who plan terrorist attacks should not be allowed to stay in Germany.

Benjamin Jendro, a spokesperson for the Berlin Police Union, said that the weapons found show a worrying threat. He asked the government to quickly give new technology to security departments and provide clearer laws to deal with terrorist attacks more effectively.

The three suspects will be taken to Karlsruhe on October 2nd. A pre-trial judge from the Federal Supreme Court will decide if they should be kept in custody.

In December 2023, German prosecutors arrested four Lebanese men in Berlin. These men were following Hamas's orders to find hidden weapons in Europe. They were accused of planning attacks on Jewish places. This case changed how German security agencies saw Hamas. They now believe that Germany, which was once thought of only as a hiding place for Hamas's support, has become a target for Hamas's terrorist attacks.
