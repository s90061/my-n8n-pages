Nanyang Industries: Watching Tariff Changes, Taiwan Car Market Needs to Grow

Nanyang Industries, part of Sanyang Industry, held an event today to show new Hyundai cars. Looking at the car market in Taiwan, Nanyang Industries said that so far in September, some orders have increased. This is because a tax reduction plan for goods helped customers who didn't buy cars in the past two months to decide to buy now. However, the whole market has not grown much. Things will become clearer once the issue of tariffs (taxes on imported goods) is decided.

Zhan Xing-sheng, an executive vice president at Nanyang Industries, said that changes in tariffs are still a concern. These changes can affect the whole economy and also make people less likely to buy cars. The market is still watching to see if tariff changes will cause big problems for the economy. But people still need commercial vehicles (like vans or trucks) no matter what.

Zhan Xing-sheng added that Nanyang Industries continues to bring in new business travel vehicles and large commercial vehicles. They hope to sell more of these.

Statistics show that about 3,500 luxury business vans with 9 seats are sold each year in Taiwan. For large commercial trucks with 3 seats, about 650 are sold each year. For heavy commercial vehicles weighing more than 5 tons, about 11,000 are sold each year.

ASEAN Finance: Richeng-KY's Thai Company Lowers Capital by 25% to Use Money Better

Today, Richeng-KY announced that its grandchild company, Regal Precious Metal Innovation Co., Ltd. RPM in Thailand, will reduce its capital. The amount is 24,975,000 Thai Baht. This is a 25% reduction. The goal is to use money more effectively.

After the capital reduction, Regal Precious Metal Innovation Co., Ltd. RPM, Richeng-KY's grandchild company, will have a capital of 74,925,000 Thai Baht. The value of each share will go down from 100 Thai Baht to 75 Thai Baht.

Richeng-KY is in the business of trade and department stores. Its main work is designing, making, and selling jewelry. In August this year, its sales were 167 million New Taiwan Dollars, which is 10.64% less than last year. For the first eight months of the year, sales were 1.329 billion New Taiwan Dollars, which is 31.09% more than last year.
