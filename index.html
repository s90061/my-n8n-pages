SimplyHelp Foundation Shows Love to Allies, Donates to Guatemala to Help the Vulnerable

The SimplyHelp Foundation, a Taiwanese non-governmental organization in the US, recently visited Guatemala, a country friendly to Taiwan. During their visit, they took part in events to donate supplies with the Guatemalan government and local partner groups. This helps improve the lives of people who need help. Taiwan's Ambassador to Guatemala, Ms. Chang Chun-fei, said again that Taiwan wants to keep working with Guatemala to make life better for everyone and to start health care projects.

The SimplyHelp Foundation shared that its president, Ms. Pao Pan Hsiao-tai, and Ms. Peng Yu-hsun, along with others, attended donation events. These events were held with the Guatemalan First Lady's Office of Social Work (SOSEP), the American non-governmental organization "Food For The Poor" (FFTP), its local partner "CÃ¡ritas Arquidiocesana," and the "Order of Malta." They also visited the "Esperanza La Vida" foundation to hold a donation event there.

At a donation event for charity supplies, held by the Taiwanese Embassy in Guatemala, the SimplyHelp Foundation, and the Guatemalan First Lady's Office of Social Work, Ambassador Chang Chun-fei, Foundation President Pao Pan Hsiao-tai, and the SOSEP director led the event together.

Ambassador Chang Chun-fei said in her speech that the SimplyHelp Foundation has been giving charity supplies with SOSEP since 2005. This has helped many people in Guatemala who are in need. She repeated that Taiwan wants to continue working with Guatemala to improve social well-being.

SOSEP thanked the foundation for donating supplies for many years and for supporting its many social welfare projects. Ms. Pao Pan Hsiao-tai said she was thankful for Guatemala's help in delivering supplies to people who need them through their long-term partnership.

Also, at a donation event held by the Taiwanese Embassy in Guatemala, the SimplyHelp Foundation, and "Food For The Poor," Ambassador Chang Chun-fei said that Taiwan helps improve local people's lives by donating rice, working with non-governmental groups to give supplies, and starting public health projects.

This time, FFTP and the Order of Malta worked together to give out 600 bags of 30-kilogram rice donated by Taiwan. This is expected to help 10,000 people.

The SimplyHelp Foundation stated that for 20 years, they have continued to donate clothes, toys, and daily necessities to social welfare organizations in Guatemala. They show their support for their allied country with real actions. They hope to make the friendship between Taiwan and Guatemala stronger and show their promise to help others for a long time.

Taiwan Stock Market Falls 443.53 Points

Today, the Taipei stock market opened lower and kept falling. It closed down by 443.53 points, reaching 25580.32 points. This is a drop of 1.70%. The total money traded was 479.747 billion New Taiwan dollars.

The main index, called the TAIEX, opened at 25998.28 points. During the day, its highest point was 25998.28 points, and its lowest was 25469.04 points. The index without financial stocks was 22500.97 points, which is a drop of 428.6 points.

Here are the changes for the eight main types of stocks: Cement and ceramics stocks fell by 1.96%. Food stocks fell by 0.37%. Plastic and chemical stocks fell by 1.14%. Textile stocks fell by 0.92%. Electrical and machinery stocks fell by 2.02%. Paper stocks fell by 0.98%. Construction stocks fell by 1.03%. Financial stocks fell by 0.18%.

There were 21,595,444 shares that people wanted to buy and 11,939,670 shares that people wanted to sell. The number of shares actually traded was 8,226,808.

At the end of the day, 121 companies' stocks went up, 882 companies' stocks went down, and 68 companies' stocks stayed the same.

The top 5 stocks with the most trades were Winbond, Walsin Lihwa, Fuh Hwa TAIWAN High Dividend ETF, Yuanta Taiwan 50 ETF, and Taiwan Glass.

Groundbreaking for Chiayi County and City Railway Elevated Extension, Expected to Open in 2031

This morning, a special ceremony was held to start the temporary track part of the Chiayi County and City Railway Elevation Extension Project. Important people like the Minister of Transportation, Mr. Chen Shih-kai, and the Chiayi County Magistrate, Mr. Weng Chang-liang, were there. They wished for the project to go well and finish soon. The Railway Bureau said that the elevated railway is expected to open in the year 2031.

During his speech at the ceremony, Minister Chen Shih-kai said that Chiayi is changing, and its transportation system must change too. He said that starting the temporary track part of the project today means that the whole plan is now in the actual building phase. This also shows that the government is serious about improving transportation in Chiayi County and City, and they are taking real steps to make it happen.

Mr. Chen Shih-kai explained that in the future, many different construction jobs, track work, and electrical system projects will begin one after another. He hopes that the Railway Bureau, the building teams, and the county government teams will work together with everyone in society to make the project go smoothly. This will help Chiayi move into a new time for transportation.

The Director of the Railway Bureau, Mr. Yang Cheng-chun, thanked the Chiayi County Government for their great help in moving this project forward. He believes that once the Minxiong railway is elevated, it will really help the city grow. It will also fix traffic problems caused by the railway blocking paths. This will help the Chiayi County Government achieve its goal of "brave transformation and Chiayi innovation."

Mr. Weng Chang-liang hopes that the Chiayi County and City Railway Elevation Extension Project will not only connect different parts of the city that were separated by the railway. He also hopes that the new Minxiong station will have a small-town style. He also expects that the shopping street under the railway, which will connect to Minxiong's famous goose meat street, will greatly help to refresh and bring life back to the whole city.

The Railway Bureau pointed out that the government approved this project in May 2023. Besides connecting the areas on both sides of the railway and helping the city grow evenly, it will also get rid of 5 railway crossings in Chiayi County. This will improve traffic problems caused by these crossings. The project will cost 17.348 billion New Taiwan dollars. The work will start north of the Dingliao Road crossing and go south to the north end of the Jiabei elevated station. It will be about 8.92 kilometers long. The current Minxiong station will also be changed into an elevated station. The elevated railway is expected to open in the year 2031.
