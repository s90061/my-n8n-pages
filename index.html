Wei Chieh Huang's First Start in CPBL Only Lasted 1 Inning, Dragons Lost 4-7 to Monkeys

Wei Chieh Huang is a pitcher for the Wei Chuan Dragons. He was chosen in the first round of the draft this year. Today, he started his first game in the professional baseball league against the Rakuten Monkeys. He only pitched for one inning and gave up 5 runs. The Dragons lost the game to the Rakuten Monkeys by a score of 4 to 7.

Wei Chieh Huang has played in Major League Baseball (MLB) in the US. In recent years, he has had many injuries. But when he joined the professional baseball draft, the Dragons still chose him first. After 3 games in the minor league, he moved to the main team to help as a relief pitcher. Today, he started the game against the Monkeys. However, Wei Chieh Huang did not play well. Also, his teammates made mistakes. He only pitched for one inning and gave up 5 runs. This caused the Dragons to lose the game.

U.S. August Retail Sales Grew More Than Expected, No Impact From Trump's Tariffs Seen

The U.S. government said today that stores sold 0.6% more in August than in July. This is the same as how much sales grew in July. Compared to last August, sales grew by 5.0%. These numbers were better than what people thought they would be. It seems that new taxes on goods from other countries, put in place by President Trump, did not hurt sales.

News said that when people buy things, it helps the U.S. economy a lot. Many businesses in the U.S. have said that President Trump's new taxes are making prices higher. As these higher costs are put on the prices, everyone is watching to see what American shoppers will do.

In August, places that sell cars and car parts sold 0.5% more than in July. Restaurants and bars sold 0.7% more. Stores that sell fresh food also sold a little more, 0.3%.

But in August, furniture stores in the U.S. sold 0.3% less than in July. Department stores also sold a little less, 0.1%. Big department stores saw a bigger drop in sales. This means people might be buying less of some things.

For a while, companies that bring goods into the U.S. and stores have been paying some of the extra tax costs themselves. But we don't know how long they can do this. Also, many businesses bought a lot of goods before President Trump's new taxes began. When they need to buy more goods, they will have to pay higher prices.

Fun and Budget: First French Sports Festival Causes Discussion

On September 14th, France had its first Sports Festival. Nearly 5000 events happened all over the country. A main road next to the Louvre Museum in Paris changed into a "Sports Street." Even with the rain, many people came to try sports like climbing, running, and sledding, which are Olympic sports, right on the street. But the French government is still cutting money for sports. This has been a big topic in the news.

President Emmanuel Macron announced the "French Sports Festival" after the 2024 Paris Olympics and Paralympics. The main idea is to keep people excited about sports and to show that sports are important for everyone. Every year from now on, September 14th will be the Sports Festival. This year was the first one.

On the 14th, Rue de Rivoli, a very busy street in the middle of Paris, became the "Sports Street." This was a special place for the whole event. From 11 AM to 6 PM, cars had to go on other roads. The activities had popular sports like running, tug-of-war, boxing, judo, taekwondo, and skateboarding. There were also Olympic and Paralympic sports that they wanted people to know more about. These included Flag Football, Boccia (a game played with balls), and Wheelchair Handball.
