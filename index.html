Utah Governor: Suspect in Right-Wing Influencer Kirk's Shooting Lived with Transgender Partner

On September 14, in Washington, a right-wing online personality named Charlie Kirk was shot and killed during a speech at a university in Utah. News said that the suspect, Tyler Robinson, was in a romantic relationship with a transgender person. This made many right-wing people very angry. Today, Utah Governor Spencer Cox confirmed that Robinson did indeed live with a transgender partner.

Kirk, who was 31 years old, died after being shot in the neck while speaking at Utah Valley University. A video from the event showed him talking to a crowd. Suddenly, a gunshot was heard, and he fell into his chair. The camera quickly moved away, and the people listening became very scared.

Kirk was a well-known internet celebrity and a leader in the young conservative movement. He had millions of followers on social media. People liked him because he made clever videos and was good at handling people who tried to cause problems or who had different ideas.

According to AFP, several American news companies reported yesterday that Robinson was in a romantic relationship with a transgender person. This news made many right-wing people very upset.

Governor Spencer Cox was asked about the rumors that Robinson lived with a transgender partner during an interview on CNN's "State of the Union" program.

He said, "Yes, I can confirm this. This roommate is a transgender person, a man who became a woman. This partner has been very helpful and did not know anything about what happened. They are now working with the police officers who are investigating."

Reports say that Kirk was very critical of transgender rights before he died. However, Governor Cox said that it is still not clear if this person's gender change had anything to do with the shooting.

Jamaican Runner Seville Wins Gold in Men's 100-Meter Final at World Athletics Championships

Today, the results for the men's 100-meter final were announced at the 2025 World Athletics Championships in Tokyo, Japan. Jamaican runner Oblique Seville won the gold medal with his best time ever of 9.77 seconds.

AFP reported that Kishane Thompson, also from Jamaica, won the silver medal with a time of 9.82 seconds. Noah Lyles, a famous American runner who won the gold medal in the last World Championships, got the bronze medal with a time of 9.89 seconds.

The last time a Jamaican athlete won gold in the men's 100-meter event at the World Athletics Championships was in 2015. That was the legendary runner Usain "Lightning" Bolt. After that, American athletes won the men's 100-meter gold medal in the next four championships.

Bolt had predicted earlier that Jamaica would win both the gold and silver medals in this year's men's 100-meter race. He publicly supported Seville and Thompson to beat Lyles.

Bolt's prediction turned out to be very accurate. When Seville and Thompson crossed the finish line, Bolt, who was watching at the stadium, cheered for them.

American Runner Melissa Jefferson-Wooden Wins Gold and Breaks Record in Women's 100-Meter Race at World Athletics Championships

Today, Melissa Jefferson-Wooden, a 24-year-old American female runner, won the gold medal in the women's 100-meter final at the World Athletics Championships in Tokyo. She also set a new championship record with a time of 10.61 seconds.

The German Press Agency (dpa) reported that Jefferson-Wooden not only won the gold medal but also achieved the fourth fastest time ever in the women's 100-meter sprint at the World Athletics Championships.

Jamaican runner Tina Clayton won the silver medal with a time of 10.76 seconds. Julien Alfred from Saint Lucia took the bronze medal with a time of 10.84 seconds.
