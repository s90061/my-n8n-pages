Tibetan Youth Singer Detained by China for Praising Dalai Lama

The Tibetan government-in-exile is in India. They said that a young Tibetan singer and activist named Asang, who is in his twenties, was taken by Chinese authorities. This happened after he sang a song called "Prince of Peace," which praised the Dalai Lama, who is the spiritual leader of Tibet. Nobody knows where Asang is now.

News from AFP and Voice of Tibet says that the Central Tibetan Administration (CTA), which is the Tibetan government-in-exile, made a statement yesterday. They said that a Tibetan singer named Tzukte, also known as Asang, was taken by Chinese authorities in early July. This was after he sang a song that praised the Dalai Lama. The song was called "Prince of Peace."

Asang is about 20 years old. He is a student of Gebe, who is a famous Tibetan singer.

The Tibetan government-in-exile said that taking Asang is part of a bigger effort by Beijing to silence Tibetan voices. This especially affects Tibetan artists and writers.

Chinese authorities did not answer AFP's request for comment.

The Tibetan government-in-exile also said that Asang was held but was not accused of any crime. Also, no one knows where he is being held. They said this goes against international rules for human rights. It also shows that China continues to stop Tibetans from having freedom of speech, cultural rights, and religious beliefs.

Teenager Swept Away by Waves While Playing at Zhunan Beach, Miaoli; Body Found on Surface of Sea

A 15-year-old male student named Wu went to a beach in Zhunan Township, Miaoli County yesterday to play in the water. Sadly, a wave swept him away. The police and coast guard were told and started looking for him. An Air National Guard helicopter found his body this afternoon. It was in the water about 400 meters from the shore at Qiding Beach.

Police found out that the young student, Wu, went to the beach to play with several friends. It seems strong waves pulled him away from the shore. His friends saw what happened and quickly called for help.

The Central Branch of the Coast Guard Administration said that they received a report yesterday afternoon. The report said someone was swept away by waves. They immediately started a large search along the coast and in the sea. Private groups, like the Miaoli Water Life Saving Association, also joined. The search is still continuing today.

Today, an Air National Guard helicopter was searching along the coast. Rescue workers there said that around 3 PM, they found the student, Wu, in the water at Qiding Beach in Zhunan, Miaoli. He was about 400 meters from the shore. They immediately lifted him onto the land. The police and other groups will now help the family with the rest of the matter.

At that time, some trains were already at Longxi and Dawu stations. They stopped on the platforms for almost an hour. The trains did not tell people why they stopped, which made people upset. But the train stopped at Jinlun because heavy rain had flooded the tracks. The train had to go back to Taitung station and did not start running again until about 6 PM that evening.

She said she went home to vote yesterday, and today she was going back to work in Jiayi. But the trains were not running again. She also said that TRA did not announce this online.
