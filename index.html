Lake Outflow Causes Disaster; UK, Australia, Canada, Belgium, and Israel Offices in Taiwan Send Condolences

A lake formed by a landslide, called a "barrier lake," overflowed and caused a disaster in Hualien. The command center in Hualien reported that 16 people died and 14 people are still missing. The offices of Canada, the UK, Australia, Belgium, and Israel in Taiwan sent their sympathy on social media. They said they stand with the people of Hualien.

After Liang Kai-wen, the Deputy Director of the American Institute in Taiwan (AIT), the Japan-Taiwan Exchange Association, and the Czech Economic and Cultural Office showed their concern, the Canadian Trade Office in Taipei posted on Facebook today to express its sadness. They sent their deepest sympathy to families who lost loved ones and to everyone affected by the disaster.

The Canadian Trade Office in Taipei also said they hope those who died can rest in peace, and that those who survived can find strength and support. Canada stands with the people of Hualien during this difficult time. They also truly thanked the rescue workers, emergency teams, and volunteers. They are a warm light of hope in this disaster.

The British Office in Taipei said on Facebook that Ruth Bradley-Jones, the British Representative in Taiwan, is still watching the disaster in Hualien. They are sad for all the people affected and their livelihoods, especially families who lost close relatives. They also showed respect to the central and local government groups and social organizations that are working hard to help in the emergency.

The Australian Office on Facebook sent their sincere sympathy to all the people in Taiwan affected by the typhoon. Many families lost loved ones, have family members who are hurt, or are still missing. Their thoughts are with these families.

The Belgian Office in Taipei posted on Facebook that they are very sad about the serious disaster in Guangfu Township, Hualien. They sent their deepest sympathy to the families of those who died. They also thought of those who are missing and those who lost their homes. They wished strength to the rescue teams.

The Israel Economic and Cultural Office in Taipei also posted on Facebook. They sincerely expressed their sympathy to all the people affected by the disaster. They hope their lives can return to normal soon. They also asked everyone to stay safe.

ASEAN Finance: Changes in Chairman, General Manager, and Director at IKKA's Vietnam Subsidiary

Learn about the New Southbound Policy and see what is happening in the ASEAN economy. CNA's "Southeast Asia Finance Information Website" chooses important financial news every day from countries like Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. You can quickly get important information about new government rules, industry changes, or chances to invest. This helps you understand the market and find new business opportunities.

IKKA-KY, a company under the Nengli Group, announced today that there will be changes in the chairman and general manager of its subsidiary, IKKA TECHNOLOGY (VIETNAM) CO.,LTD (IKKA Vietnam). These changes are part of the group's business plans. Hirofumi Aoki will step down as chairman and general manager, and Yoshihiro Takada will take over. These changes will start on October 1st.

IKKA-KY also announced today that there will be changes to the board of directors for IKKA Vietnam. Hirofumi Aoki has also resigned as a director, effective October 1st. A new director has not been chosen yet.

IKKA-KY's main business is making and selling car parts. IKKA-KY said before that as a partner in Toyota's supply chain, the market for car parts looks good for the future. Their subsidiary, Sol-plus, expects to start making drone parts in Japan soon. This means they will officially enter the area of unmanned vehicles.

In the first half of the year, IKKA-KY's total income was NT$1.724 billion. This is a small decrease from NT$1.74 billion in the same period of 2024. In the first half of the year, the profit for the parent company's owners was NT$74.97 million. The profit per share after tax was NT$2.2.

Lu Hsiu-yen Joins Lo Chih-chiang to Eat at an Old Meatball Shop, Offers Good Wishes and Support

Taichung Mayor Lu Hsiu-yen went with Kuomintang (KMT) chairman candidate Lo Chih-chiang tonight to eat at an old restaurant called "Taichung Meatball." They recorded it live. Lu Hsiu-yen said that this restaurant is almost 100 years old and is one of her favorite places to eat. She hopes that by using the candidate's popularity, they can help the economy. She also gave Lo Chih-chiang good wishes and support.

Last week, Lu Hsiu-yen ate sticky rice cakes with another KMT chairman candidate, Hau Lung-pin. Tonight, she ate meatballs with Legislator Lo Chih-chiang, who is also running for KMT chairman. Lu Hsiu-yen said that because the chairman candidates are very well-known, her job as mayor is to help the economy. So, she used the candidates' popularity to help Taichung's economy.

Lu Hsiu-yen said she chose "Taichung Meatball" because it is nearly 100 years old in Taichung and is one of her favorite places. She has eaten there for decades. When she was a news director for a TV station, her apartment was across from "Taichung Meatball," so she often went there to enjoy the meatballs. A shop that only sells meatballs and can operate for almost 100 years, and also received a Michelin Bib Gourmand award, is truly something special for Taichung.

Regarding Lo Chih-chiang running for party chairman, Lu Hsiu-yen said she would also give him good wishes and support. She believes elections are happy events. As a party member, she has a duty to give opportunities to members who are willing to take on responsibilities and serve. She must vote for capable and virtuous people. She will give good wishes and support to every candidate.

Lo Chih-chiang said that many talented people are running in this party chairman election. Everyone is excellent, and whoever wins will be a good thing for the KMT. He personally tries hard but also stays calm during the process. Everyone competes fairly, and whoever gets the blessings of the party members will naturally be elected. Being elected is the start of taking on responsibility. If someone is not elected, they should fully support the party chairman. He believes this is a positive attitude. Lu Hsiu-yen is someone everyone hopes to get blessings from, so he came to visit her today.

Lo Chih-chiang was born in Hualien. He thanked Lu Hsiu-yen for starting the "Return Home Reconstruction Subsidy" and sending firefighters from Taichung City to help in the disaster area of Guangfu Township in Hualien. He said that he thanks Lu Hsiu-yen on behalf of Hualien Legislator Fu Kun-chi and indigenous Legislator Kao Chin Su-mei. He will also suggest that the central government consider doing the same as Taichung City's "Return Home Reconstruction Subsidy" policy.
