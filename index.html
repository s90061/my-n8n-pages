Three Motorcycles Crash in Tainan, Seven People Hurt, Including Two Children

On the evening of October 12th, three motorcycles crashed in Yujing District, Tainan City. This happened at the intersection of Provincial Highway 3, which leads to Guidan. Seven people were hurt in the accident. Among them were a 2-year-old girl and a 9-year-old boy. All seven people were awake when ambulances took them to the hospital.

The Tainan City Fire Department received a report about the accident around 7 PM. They were told there was a traffic accident at the intersection of Provincial Highway 3 in Yujing District, going towards Guidan. The fire department sent 5 vehicles and 10 people to help. When they arrived, they saw that three motorcycles had crashed. There were seven injured people. Two people were lying on the ground, and the other five had scrapes and cuts on their bodies. The injured included a 2-year-old girl and a 9-year-old boy.

After checking the injured, firefighters found that a 30-year-old man had a broken lower right leg. The 9-year-old boy had a more serious injury, an open break in his left lower leg. The other five injured people had cuts and scrapes on their faces and bodies. Ambulances took the seven injured people to Madou Sin Lau Hospital and the Sin-Hua Branch of Tainan Hospital, Ministry of Health and Welfare. All of them were awake when they arrived at the hospitals. The police are still investigating to find out why the accident happened.
