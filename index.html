Songshan Airport Viewing Platform is Ready, Will Open on October 4th

Songshan Airport's viewing platform has been closed for almost two years for repairs. It is now finished. The Civil Aeronautics Administration said today that it will open again on October 4th for visitors.

The repairs took almost two years. Now it is ready. They added a wall with mascots and a very big wall with plants, which are good places for photos. The Minister of Transportation, Chen Shih-kai, said this morning that it will be open to the public during the Mid-Autumn Festival holiday.

The Civil Aeronautics Administration announced that the Songshan Airport viewing platform will open on the 4th. Zheng Jian-zhong, who is the head of Taipei International Airport Station, said they will check all the things at the viewing platform in the next two days. It will open on October 4th, which is the first day of the Mid-Autumn Festival holiday. It will be open every day from 9 AM to 9 PM.

Zheng Jian-zhong said that the viewing platform first opened in 2011. It quickly became a popular place in northern Taiwan to watch airplanes. More than 450,000 people visited it in one year, which was a record.

The newly fixed viewing platform now has two new chipmunk mascots, 'Song Sang' and 'Ai Jiang'. These are in addition to the original Songshan Airport mascot, 'Lu Cheng Cheng'. There is also a 3D painted wall with the mascots, where visitors can take pictures. Next to it, there is a very large plant wall. It is about 16 meters long and 3.5 meters high. It has mountain shapes, smooth stones, and seats that look like clouds. This makes you think of planes flying high in the sky.

Taipei City Checks Indoor Air Quality, Two Stores Found to be Not Good

To keep people healthy, the Taipei City Environmental Protection Department checked the air quality inside 14 large stores. They found that Beitou Costco and Carrefour Chongqing store had too much carbon dioxide. These stores must fix the problem by a certain time. If they don't, they will pay a fine of NT$50,000 to NT$250,000.

The Taipei City Environmental Protection Department said today that many people gather in large stores. This can cause the amount of carbon dioxide to be too high. Too much carbon dioxide can make it hard to breathe. It can also cause headaches, make you sleepy, slow your reactions, and make you feel tired.

The Environmental Protection Department said that from August to September, they checked the air in 14 large stores. This was to make sure people could shop safely. They found that Beitou Costco and Carrefour Chongqing store had too much carbon dioxide. The stores must improve the air by bringing in more fresh air, making ventilation better, or letting fewer people in at one time.

During the time they are fixing the problem, the stores must put a sign at the entrance to show that they failed the check. If the air quality is still not good when they check again, the stores will pay a fine of NT$50,000 to NT$250,000.

Lulu Brings Back Classic TV Shows at Golden Bell 60 with Famous Guests

The 60th Golden Bell Awards for TV shows will happen on October 17th. The artist Lulu will sing and dance to bring back old, famous TV shows. Cao Lan and Yao Dai-wei, known as the 'Betel Nut Sisters,' along with Dong Yue-hua (played by Dong Zhi-cheng) from 'Red White Victory,' and Fuzhou Bo (played by Hsu Hsiao-shun) from 'Happy Yu Ling Long,' will also come back to the Golden Bell stage.

The 60th Golden Bell Awards for TV shows is coming soon. This year is special because it is the 60th Golden Bell Awards. It shows how many different TV shows Taiwan has had in 60 years. The awards show will have three main parts. It will let viewers remember old favorites with both laughter and tears.

The first part of the show is called 'The Great Entertainer.' Lulu (Huang Lu-yin), who is the host for the TV show awards, will start with a song and dance. She will sing about how variety shows have been with everyone over the years. She will also act out famous parts from old TV shows like 'Five Lights Awards,' 'Lian Huan Pao,' 'Super Sunday,' 'Variety Kaleidoscope,' and 'Kangsi Coming.'

The funny people from those old shows, who made many people laugh, will also come together again. This includes Cao Lan and Yao Dai-wei from the 'Betel Nut Sisters,' Dong Zhi-cheng as Dong Yue-hua from 'Red White Victory,' and Hsu Hsiao-shun as Fuzhou Bo from 'Happy Yu Ling Long.' They will all come back to the Golden Bell stage to bring lots of laughter and good memories.

The second part of the show is called 'Fertile Soil Blooms with Rich Flowers.' The stage will become a place where many cultures mix. Four important music groups will perform. They are Yawi Mori, who represents Indigenous music; Hsieh Yu-wei, a talented Hakka musician; Chen Meng-hsien, a new singer of Taiwanese music; and TRASH, the band that just won the Golden Melody Award for Best Band. These four groups will sing together in four languages: Indigenous, Hakka, Taiwanese, and Mandarin. This will show the special and rich music of Taiwan.

The last special performance will be 'Sing, Starting from TV.' It will bring together singers from different generations: Luo Shih-feng, Chien-Tien Aisa, Huang Wei-chin, GENBLUE Fantasy Girls, and ARKis. They will perform together.

These three exciting parts of the show will go one after another. From old TV shows to different kinds of music, and then new groups of singers from different times. Golden Bell 60 will have a very fun stage. It will make viewers laugh and feel emotional. It will show the great history of Taiwanese TV.

The 60th Golden Bell Awards for TV shows and TV dramas will be on October 17th and 18th. The awards will be at the Taipei Pop Music Center. People can watch it live on Sanlih E-Television and the official Golden Bell YouTube channel.
