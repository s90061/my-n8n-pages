Heavy Rains in Central and Southern Taiwan: 540 Areas on Alert for Landslides

Taipei, July 31 (CNA) â€“ Heavy rains continue in central and southern Taiwan. This morning, the Agency of Rural Development and Soil and Water Conservation under the Ministry of Agriculture issued red and yellow alerts for 540 streams where landslides could happen.

The agency announced in a press release that there are 338 streams under a red alert for landslides. These are in Nantou County, Yunlin County, Tainan City, Chiayi County, Kaohsiung City, and Pingtung County. This covers 6 counties and cities, 29 townships, and 115 villages. There are 202 streams under a yellow alert for landslides. These are in Taichung City, Nantou County, Yunlin County, Tainan City, Chiayi County, Kaohsiung City, and Pingtung County. This covers 7 counties and cities, 29 townships, and 81 villages.

The agency also issued red alerts for 8 places where large land collapses could happen. These places are Wanshan Village in Maolin District, Kaohsiung City; Baolai Village in Liugui District, Kaohsiung City; Taihe Village in Meishan Township, Chiayi County; Wenfeng Village in Zhuqi Township, Chiayi County; Nangang Village in Guoxing Township, Nantou County; Ruili Village in Meishan Township, Chiayi County; Xinglong Village in Liugui District, Kaohsiung City; and Baoshan Village in Taoyuan District, Kaohsiung City. Local governments should tell or make people leave these areas and move them to safe places.

Additionally, there are 9 places under a yellow alert for large land collapses. These are in Laiyi Village in Laiyi Township, Pingtung County; Baoshan Village in Taoyuan District, Kaohsiung City; Heya Village in Lugu Township, Nantou County; He'an Village and Datian Village in Jiaxian District, Kaohsiung City; and Baiqi Village in Zhuqi Township, Chiayi County. Local governments should advise people to evacuate. (Edited by Guan Chung-wei) 2025/07/31
