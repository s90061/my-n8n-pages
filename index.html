Investors Wait for Company Reports and Powell's Speech; US Stocks Show Mixed Results

(New York, July 18, Central News Agency) The stock market in Wall Street had mixed results today. Only the Nasdaq index went up, while the other three main indexes went down. Investors are waiting for more company reports to be released and for the head of the US central bank, Jerome Powell, to speak next week.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went down by 142.30 points, or 0.32%. It closed at 44342.19 points.

The S&P 500 index went down a little, by 0.57 points, or 0.01%. It closed at 6296.79 points.

The Nasdaq index for technology stocks went up by 10.01 points, or 0.05%. It closed at 20895.66 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index went down slightly by 5.018 points, or 0.09%. It closed at 5732.625 points.

Financial Times: Trump Tries to Push EU Limits, Asks for 15% to 20% Minimum Tariffs

(Brussels, July 18, Central News Agency) Three people who know about the talks said that US President Trump has asked for more in trade talks with the European Union (EU). He wants any agreement with the EU to have a minimum tax on goods of 15% to 20%.

The Financial Times reported that after weeks of talks about how to make an agreement, Trump became tougher. This is to see how much the EU can take. The plan was to keep the basic tax on most goods at 10%.

The people who know said that Trump did not care about the EU's newest idea to lower taxes on cars. He said he was happy to keep the 25% tax on cars as planned.

A US official told the Financial Times that even if they make a deal, the US government is now thinking about putting a similar tax rate of more than 10% on EU goods.

This situation puts the EU in a difficult spot. This is because Trump said that if they do not make a deal by August 1, he will put a 30% tax on all goods from the EU.

The EU has said it will react strongly to the US move. But people inside the EU have different ideas about whether to fight back. Also, the EU might have to accept a basic tax of more than 10% in any agreement.

EU foreign officials said yesterday that if the EU cannot make a trade deal with the US, the EU leaders will prepare a list of planned limits for US service companies, including big technology companies.
