Turkey's First Lady Asks Melania Trump to Care for Children in Gaza

Today, Turkey's First Lady, Emine Erdogan, wrote a letter to the American First Lady, Melania Trump. She asked Melania to care for the children in Gaza, just as she cares for children in Ukraine.

Reporters say that Emine asked Melania to write a letter to Israel's Prime Minister, Benjamin Netanyahu. She asked him to show kindness to the children in Gaza.

Earlier this month, Melania attended a meeting between the US and Russia in Alaska. There, she wrote a letter to Russian President Vladimir Putin. In her letter, she asked Putin to make peace for the sake of children.

In her letter to Melania, Emine wrote: "I truly believe that your deep care for the 648 Ukrainian children who died in the war will also reach Gaza. In just two years, 62,000 innocent people in Gaza have been cruelly killed. This number includes 18,000 children."

Emine also mentioned in her letter: "As a mother, a woman, and a person, I truly understand the feelings you expressed in your letter. I hope you can bring the same hope to the children in Gaza, who also want peace and quiet."

Turkey's President, Recep Tayyip Erdogan, has tried to help Russia and Ukraine find a solution during their war, which has been going on for four years.

12 Migrants Go Missing After Jumping from Boat Near Spain's Balearic Islands

Today, Spanish officials said that 12 migrants jumped from a boat and disappeared. This happened while they were going to the Balearic Islands, a popular place for tourists in the Mediterranean Sea.

Officials said that another 14 migrants from the same boat were saved. They were found 58 kilometers southeast of Cabrera Island.

In a statement, the authorities said that the saved migrants told officials that 12 passengers jumped into the sea on August 22.

The statement also said that the Spanish Civil Guard and Coast Guard are now looking for these missing people, who are from North Africa.

According to information from Spain's Interior Ministry, many more migrants have arrived in the Balearic Islands from North Africa this year.

By August 15, a total of 4,323 migrants had arrived on these islands since the start of the year. This is a 77% increase compared to the 2,443 people who arrived during the same time in 2024.
