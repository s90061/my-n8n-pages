Cheng Li-Wen, a Former DPP Member, Becomes KMT Chairperson

Former Kuomintang (KMT) lawmaker Cheng Li-Wen started her political career with the Democratic Progressive Party (DPP). She was part of the student movement generation. After leaving the DPP, she joined the KMT and held many important positions in the party and government. When she was young, she supported Taiwan's independence. However, in this election, she said, "I am Chinese." She was a surprising candidate and won the KMT chairperson election because of her fighting spirit. She defeated other candidates, like former Taipei Mayor Hau Lung-Pin. This is a big surprise in politics.

The results of the KMT chairperson election are out. Cheng Li-Wen won against Hau Lung-Pin, KMT lawmaker Lo Chih-Chiang, and other candidates. She is now the new KMT chairperson. She is also the second woman to become KMT chairperson through a direct election by party members, after former chairperson Hung Hsiu-Chu.

Cheng Li-Wen was born in Yunlin and grew up in Tainan. She studied law at National Taiwan University and took part in the Wild Lily student movement. After university, she joined the DPP. She was a National Assembly member and a deputy director of the DPP Youth Department. But in 2002, the DPP stopped her membership because she commented on the "Tu Hsing-Che ear-licking case." So, Cheng Li-Wen decided to leave the party.

After leaving the DPP, Cheng Li-Wen stayed out of politics for a while. On March 27, 2004, the pan-blue parties gathered on Ketagalan Boulevard to protest unfair presidential election results. Cheng Li-Wen was the host and was seen as officially supporting the pan-blue side. Later that year, she ran for a lawmaker position in Kaohsiung but lost.

However, Cheng Li-Wen's good speaking skills, sharp hosting, and clear arguments impressed Lien Chan, who was the KMT chairperson at the time. In 2005, she was invited to join the KMT. She then worked as the KMT's Culture and Communication Committee chairperson and spokesperson. In the 2008 legislative election, the KMT chose her as a non-district lawmaker, and she won. But in 2012, she ran for a local lawmaker position in Taichung and lost to DPP lawmaker Ho Hsin-Chun.

Cheng Li-Wen was brave to run in difficult election areas. She showed a rare fighting spirit for a KMT member. After losing the lawmaker election, Chen Chun, who was the Premier at the time, asked her to be the Executive Yuan spokesperson. She was highly trusted by top officials.

In 2014, Cheng Li-Wen resigned as the Executive Yuan spokesperson and started working in the media, hosting shows. In the 2020 legislative election, Wu Den-Yih, who was the KMT chairperson at the time, chose her as a non-district lawmaker for the 10th Legislative Yuan. She won again and returned to the Legislative Yuan. She then became the KMT party caucus whip.

During the 10th Legislative Yuan session, the COVID-19 pandemic started around the world. As the party caucus whip, Cheng Li-Wen acted as a strong voice for the party. She asked tough questions and often stood at the front to argue for the party.

In this KMT chairperson election, Cheng Li-Wen was a surprise candidate. She visited local communities a lot and showed a fighting image that was different from traditional KMT members. She got a lot of support from military, public servant, and teacher groups. Finally, she won against her opponents and became the party chairperson.

Cheng Li-Wen has successfully become the party chairperson. However, she is not from the traditional KMT background. It will be a challenge for her to manage the KMT, which has a complex organization and strong local groups. She also needs to handle the competition and cooperation with the Taiwan People's Party carefully. Her ability to help the KMT win back power in 2028 is a big test that has just begun.

Chunghwa Telecom Sponsors Taiwan Series; Chairman Chien Chih-Cheng Throws Good First Pitch

Chunghwa Telecom supports baseball in Taiwan. They are again the main sponsor for the 2025 CPBL Chunghwa Telecom Taiwan Series. Today, the company's chairman, Chien Chih-Cheng, was asked to throw the first pitch. He threw a good pitch in front of 40,000 fans at a full stadium.

The Taiwan Series of the Chinese Professional Baseball League (CPBL) started at the Taipei Dome. The CTBC Brothers team played against the Rakuten Monkeys team. They will play seven games, and the first team to win four games will be the champion. The team announced that 40,000 people came, which is the same as the highest number of tickets sold for the first game of a championship series before. Chunghwa Telecom is the main sponsor for the 2025 CPBL Chunghwa Telecom Taiwan Series, and Chien Chih-Cheng was asked to throw the first pitch.

Chunghwa Telecom said that their platforms, MOD and Hami Video CPBLTV, will show all the Taiwan Series games. This means fans who cannot go to the stadium will not miss the games. They can watch to see who wins the championship and feel the exciting atmosphere of Taiwanese baseball.

Chunghwa Telecom's two platforms, MOD and Hami Video, have been showing sports events for a long time. These include the Major League Baseball playoffs, HBL high school basketball, TPVL Taiwan professional volleyball, BWF World Badminton Federation events, and WTT World Table Tennis Series.

Also, TV shows and movies that Chunghwa Telecom invested in were nominated for 37 awards at the 60th Golden Bell Awards. For example, their main Taiwanese drama, "The Outlaw Doctor," was nominated for 15 awards, including Best Drama Series and Best Director for a Drama Series.

Red Alert for Yanziqou Landslide Dam Lake; Parts of Xiulin Township Closed on October 19

Hualien County's Xiulin Township office has announced that some areas will close schools and offices tomorrow. This is because there is a red alert for the landslide dam lake at Yanziqou in the Liwu River. The areas that will be closed are the Greater Tianxiang area (Guanyuan, Luoshao, Xibao, Tianxiang), Minle Community (Neighbor 9) in Fushi Village, and Minyou Community (Neighbors 1 to 3) in Xiulin Village.
