Luka Doncic Renews Contract with Lakers for 3 Years, Donates Money to Help Athletes

(Los Angeles, August 2nd, Central News Agency) The NBA team Los Angeles Lakers announced today that they have signed a new contract with 5-time All-Star guard Luka Doncic. The contract is for 3 years and is worth 165 million US dollars (about 4.9 billion New Taiwan Dollars).

Reuters reported that Doncic posted on social media, "I just signed a new contract with the Lakers."

Doncic said, "I am happy to keep working to win championships for the Lakers and make Lakers fans proud. Thank you to the Lakers, my teammates, and all the fans who have shown me so much love since day one. This is just the beginning."

Doncic, who is 26 years old, joined the Lakers in February this year after a big trade from the Dallas Mavericks.

Since joining the Lakers, Doncic has played 28 regular season games for the team. On average, he scored 28.2 points, got 8.1 rebounds, made 7.5 assists, and had 1.6 steals per game.

Doncic also announced on social media, "Today, I also promised to donate 5 million US dollars (about 148 million New Taiwan Dollars). This money will help 77 young athletes from around the world follow their dreams, just like I did. Basketball gave me everything, and I am lucky to be able to give back and help the next generation."

Weather Agency Issues Heavy Rain Warning: Tainan, Kaohsiung, Pingtung Mountains to Expect Extreme Downpours

(Taipei, August 3rd, Central News Agency) The Central Weather Agency has issued a special warning for heavy rain. Due to a southwest air current, there will likely be strong, short periods of rain.

Today, there will be local very heavy rain or extremely heavy rain in the mountain areas of Tainan City, Kaohsiung City, and Pingtung County. There will be local heavy rain or very heavy rain in the flat areas of Tainan City, Kaohsiung City, and Pingtung County. Also, parts of Yunlin County, Chiayi County, Hengchun Peninsula, and the mountain areas of Taitung County will have local heavy rain or very heavy rain. There is a chance of local heavy rain in Miaoli, central areas, Taitung, and Penghu.

The Central Weather Agency also reminds people to be careful of lightning and strong winds. In mountain areas, please be very careful of landslides, falling rocks, mudslides, and sudden floods. In low-lying areas, please be careful of flooding. People should not go into mountain areas or rivers.

Areas with extremely heavy rain are: Tainan City, Kaohsiung City, and Pingtung County.

Ukraine Finds Defense Corruption Case: Electronic Warfare and Drone Prices Overcharged

(Kyiv, August 2nd, Central News Agency) Ukrainian authorities said today that they have arrested several politicians. These politicians are involved in a large corruption case in the defense department. This happened after public protests began because the independence of Ukraine's two main anti-corruption agencies was taken away. After these protests, the parliament passed a new anti-corruption law.

AFP reported that Ukraine passed a law at the end of July this year. This law took away the independence of the National Anti-Corruption Bureau of Ukraine (NABU) and the Specialized Anti-Corruption Prosecutor's Office (SAPO). It gave control to the Prosecutor General, who is chosen by the country's leader.

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy gave in on July 31st. He gave back the independence of these two agencies. This happened after strong disagreement from allied countries and the first street protests against the government in Ukraine since Russia's full invasion in 2022.

The National Anti-Corruption Bureau stated today that it, along with the Specialized Anti-Corruption Prosecutor's Office, found "a case of regularly taking money that local governments set aside for the army's needs, and also a large amount of illegally taking and giving money."

The National Anti-Corruption Bureau said that this corruption case involves charging too much for electronic warfare and drone equipment. Then, 30% of the contract money was taken for other uses.

The people suspected in this case include a member of parliament, several heads of regional and city offices, members of the National Guard, and managers of defense companies.

The National Anti-Corruption Bureau noted that they have made 4 arrests so far, but they have not shared the names of the people arrested.
