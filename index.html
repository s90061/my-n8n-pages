Nvidia's Earnings and Forecast Beat Expectations, But Stock Price Falls After Hours

Today, after the stock market closed, Nvidia, a top company for AI chips in the US, announced its financial report and future predictions. Both were better than what experts expected. However, the company's stock price went down after hours. This happened because investors are worried about too much spending on AI chips and that Nvidia's business in China might stop growing.

According to reports from AFP and Reuters, Nvidia announced a profit of 26.4 billion US dollars (about 806.8 billion New Taiwan dollars) in the second quarter. Its income reached a new high of 46.7 billion US dollars. This success is because big technology companies need many chips for AI data centers.

Nvidia also expects its income for the current quarter to be around 54 billion US dollars, plus or minus 2%. This is more than the 53.14 billion US dollars that analysts predicted. Nvidia believes that cloud service providers will greatly need its chips because they are making their systems bigger to use generative AI.

Nvidia also said that its predictions released today do not include any planned shipments of its H20 chips to China.

US Stocks Close Higher, S&P Reaches New High as Market Awaits Nvidia's Earnings

Today, the main US stock market indexes closed higher, and the S&P 500 index reached a new record. The market is now focused on the most important event on Wall Street this week: Nvidia's quarterly financial report. This report will show if the recent increase in the value of AI-related companies will continue.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went up by 147.16 points, or 0.32%, ending at 45565.23 points.

The S&P 500 index went up by 15.46 points, or 0.24%, ending at 6481.40 points.

The Nasdaq index went up by 45.87 points, or 0.21%, ending at 21590.14 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index went up by 16.69 points, or 0.29%, ending at 5824.61 points.

US Oil Inventories Fall, International Oil Prices Jump More Than 1%

Today, the US announced that its supply of crude oil and fuel has decreased. This made investors in the oil market less worried about having too much oil. Because of this, international oil prices increased by more than 1% today.

At the New York Mercantile Exchange, the price for West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil for October went up by 1.4%, closing at 64.15 US dollars per barrel.

In London, the price for Brent crude oil for October went up by 1.2%, closing at 68.05 US dollars per barrel.
