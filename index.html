Scottie Scheffler Wins British Open for First Time, Moves Closer to Career Grand Slam

Scottie Scheffler, an American golf player, won the British Open for the first time today in Portrush, UK. He won by 4 shots, getting his fourth major title.

According to AFP, Scheffler is the world's number 1 golfer. In his last round today, he shot 68, which is 3 strokes under par. He did not shoot higher than 70 in any of the four rounds. He won the tournament with a total score of 267, which is 17 strokes under par.

Scheffler has now won 10 times in a row after leading for 54 holes.

For the past two years, Scheffler has been so dominant that if he gets a birdie on the first hole, other players seem to lose hope.

Scheffler is now the second world number one golfer in history, after Tiger Woods, to win the British Open trophy. He has now won 3 major titles, moving closer to a possible career Grand Slam.

Another American player, Harris English, shot 66 in his last round today. His total score was 271, which is 13 strokes under par, placing him second.

Weather Bureau Issues Heavy Rain Warning for Yilan, Hualien, Taitung, Pingtung, Matsu, Lanyu, and Green Island

(Taipei, July 21st, Central News Agency) The Central Weather Bureau has given a special warning for heavy rain. A low-pressure system is causing heavy rain for short periods. Today, Hualien County and Taitung County will have heavy rain or very heavy rain in some areas. Yilan, Pingtung, Matsu, Green Island, and Lanyu might also have heavy rain in some parts.

The Central Weather Bureau reminds people to be careful of lightning, strong winds, and rivers overflowing. In low areas, be careful of water gathering. Because of many days of rain, people in mountain areas should be careful of landslides, falling rocks, and mudslides.

Pope Criticizes Gaza War as Barbaric, Calls for Peaceful Resolution

(Castel Gandolfo, Italy, July 20th, Central News Agency) Pope Leo XIV of the Roman Catholic Church strongly criticized the "barbaric" war in Gaza today. He also strongly spoke against "the misuse of power." A few days ago, the Israeli army attacked a Catholic church in the Gaza Strip, and 3 people died.

According to AFP, Pope Leo XIV said at the end of the Angelus prayer in his summer home in Castel Gandolfo, near Rome: "I ask again for this cruel war to stop at once. We must find a way to solve the conflict peacefully."

On July 18th, the Pope talked with Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu. He said he was "deeply sad" about the Israeli airstrike on the Holy Family Church in Gaza City on the morning of July 17th.

About 600 people who had to leave their homes were staying in this church. Most of them were children, including dozens with special needs.

Leo XIV said today: "Sadly, this action makes the ongoing military attacks on civilians and religious places in Gaza worse."

"I ask the international community again to follow humanitarian law. They must protect civilians. It is strictly forbidden to punish groups of people, use too much power, or force people to move from their homes."

The Pope also showed "understanding and care" for "my dear Christian brothers and sisters in the Middle East" who "often feel helpless" in this difficult situation.
