Northern Taiwan to Expect 38-Degree Heat; Expert Says Tropical Depression Not a Threat to Taiwan

Weather expert Wu Der-rong said that it will be very hot today and tomorrow. Northern Taiwan will have high temperatures of up to 38 degrees Celsius. A tropical depression is slowly getting stronger in the South China Sea. There is a slightly higher chance (60%) it will become a typhoon before August 30th, but it will not affect Taiwan. Next week, other tropical weather systems will be active far east of Taiwan.

The Central Weather Bureau defines "high temperature" in its "High Temperature Information" as when the highest ground temperature reaches 36 degrees Celsius or more. Depending on how high the temperature is and how long it lasts, there are three levels: yellow, orange, and red.

Associate Professor Wu Der-rong from the Department of Atmospheric Sciences at National Central University writes in the "Tian Ji Classroom" column for the Meteorological Applications Promotion Foundation. He stated that the newest European weather model (ECMWF) shows that today and tomorrow, most areas will be sunny with some clouds and very hot. Northern Taiwan will see temperatures of up to 38 degrees Celsius. Around noon, the UV index will be dangerous. People should protect themselves from the sun and heatstroke. The Hengchun Peninsula and Hualien/Taitung might have some short local rains. In the mountains, there could be local showers or thunderstorms in the afternoon.

Regarding temperatures, Wu Der-rong said that today, northern Taiwan is expected to be 24 to 38 degrees, central Taiwan 23 to 37 degrees, southern Taiwan 24 to 35 degrees, and eastern Taiwan 21 to 35 degrees.

Wu Der-rong also mentioned that the latest European model shows that from August 31st to September 2nd, there will be a little more moisture from the south. Most areas will be sunny with clouds. High temperatures will drop slightly, but northern Taiwan will still be warm. The eastern part of Taiwan and Pingtung might have some short local rains. In the mountains, there will be local showers or thunderstorms in the afternoon, and sometimes these might spread to some flat areas. People should be careful about strong local thunderstorms.

Wu Der-rong said that on September 3rd and 4th, most areas will be sunny with clouds. It will be hot, and the UV index will be dangerous around noon. People should protect themselves from the sun and heatstroke. The Hengchun Peninsula and Hualien/Taitung might have some short local rains. In the afternoon, there will be local showers or thunderstorms, mostly in the mountains. People still need to be careful about strong local thunderstorms.

Wu Der-rong pointed out that the newest European ensemble model shows a tropical depression slowly developing in the South China Sea. The chance of it becoming a typhoon before August 30th has increased slightly to 60%. Although it looks like a swordfish, its strength is much weaker, and it will not affect Taiwan. He added that the latest model also shows that next week, other tropical systems will be active far east of Taiwan. The Northwest Pacific is still in the season for typhoons to form often.

Dow Jones Reaches New High, TSMC ADR Falls; Small and Mid-Cap Stocks Expected to Be More Active

The US stock market's Dow Jones and S&P 500 indexes reached new record highs. However, Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company's (TSMC) American Depositary Receipts (ADR) fell by 0.43%. Experts believe that the Taiwan stock market will continue to move up and down at high levels. The GreTai Securities Market (OTC) index reached a new high for this period on Thursday. Smaller and medium-sized stocks are expected to perform better.

On August 28th, the Taiwan stock market started lower, then moved up and down before falling. It closed down by 283.45 points at 24236.45 points, testing the 5-day moving average of 24220 points.

Major indexes on Wall Street ended higher. This was due to the US changing its second-quarter Gross Domestic Product (GDP) data to be higher, and also because of strong earnings reports from Nvidia, a big company that makes AI chips.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose by 71.67 points, or 0.16%, ending at 45636.90 points. The S&P 500 index went up by 20.46 points, or 0.32%, closing at 6501.86 points. The Nasdaq index increased by 115.02 points, or 0.53%, finishing at 21705.16 points. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index rose by 28.44 points, or 0.49%, closing at 5853.05 points.

On August 28th, LED company Ennostar announced, on behalf of its subsidiary Bright Spot Investment, that it sold its shares in Edison Opto. The total amount of this sale was NT$168.278 million. Also, they previously announced that a subsidiary sold shares in PlayNitride Technology-KY for NT$456.709 million. In total, selling these two stocks brought in NT$624.987 million.

The Financial Supervisory Commission (FSC) announced the profit and loss numbers for the insurance industry on August 28th. In the first seven months of this year, life insurance companies made a profit of NT$23.2 billion. This is a big decrease of 92.3% compared to the same time last year.

Their second factory in India will start making products this year. Also, the board of directors recently decided to invest more money in their Vietnamese subsidiary to build a second factory there.

New Taipei Warns of Extreme 38-Degree Heat; Southern Taiwan to Watch for Afternoon Thunderstorms

The Central Weather Administration (CWA) said that today it will be hot everywhere. Temperatures will generally be between 31 and 36 degrees Celsius. Greater Taipei, Taoyuan, and areas near mountains in central and southern Taiwan should prepare for temperatures above 36 degrees. Some parts of New Taipei might even reach an extreme temperature of 38 degrees. On the windward side, Hualien, Taitung, and Hengchun will have occasional showers or thunderstorms. In areas south of Taoyuan, people should watch out for afternoon thunderstorms.

The Central Weather Administration stated that today, the overall wind will be from the east. Hualien, Taitung, and the Hengchun Peninsula will have local short showers or thunderstorms from time to time. The North Coast of Keelung and the northeastern areas will also have some light rain. Other areas will be mostly cloudy to sunny. In the afternoon, areas south of Taoyuan will have local short thunderstorms. When these storms are strong, there might be heavy rain in some places.

Regarding temperatures, high temperatures across Taiwan will generally be between 31 and 36 degrees. Greater Taipei, Taoyuan, and areas near mountains in central and southern Taiwan have a chance of temperatures above 36 degrees. Especially, some parts of New Taipei City might even have extreme temperatures of around 38 degrees. Around noon, the UV radiation will be strong, possibly reaching very high or dangerous levels.

For the outlying islands: Penghu will be sunny with some clouds, 27 to 32 degrees. Kinmen will be sunny with some clouds, 27 to 33 degrees. Matsu will be sunny with some clouds, 27 to 31 degrees.

Also, some parts of the North Coast of Keelung, Taoyuan, and the Hengchun Peninsula might experience average winds of level 6 or higher, or gusts of level 8 or higher. People should be careful about this.

The CWA said that at 2 AM, the center of Tropical Depression TD17 was located 1000 kilometers southwest of Eluanbi. It is moving northwest towards Vietnam and will not directly affect Taiwan.
