Earthquakes Hit Inner Mongolia and Yunnan in China, Many People Felt Them

Taipei, July 26 (CNA) Early on July 26, earthquakes happened one after another in Alashan League, Inner Mongolia, and Pu'er City, Yunnan, China. Many Chinese internet users said they clearly felt the ground shaking.

According to the China Earthquake Networks Center, at 1:02 AM on July 26, a magnitude 4.7 earthquake happened in Alashan Left Banner, Alashan League, Inner Mongolia. The earthquake was 10 kilometers deep. Its center was at 38.24 degrees North latitude and 104.02 degrees East longitude.

The earthquake's center was 91 kilometers from Minqin County, Gansu; 118 kilometers from Jingtai County, Gansu; 126 kilometers from Liangzhou District, Gansu; 129 kilometers from Shapotou District, Ningxia; and 131 kilometers from Gulang County, Gansu. It was also 126 kilometers from Wuwei City, Gansu, and 197 kilometers from Yinchuan City.

Chinese media reported that many internet users from Gansu and Ningxia said, "The earthquake was clear in Gansu," and "I was woken up by the shaking." Some people in Yinchuan also said the shaking was strong.

The Alashan League Emergency Management Bureau said that the area started a level 4 emergency response for earthquake relief. They are now working according to their emergency plan. The area is in the middle of a desert, and so far, there are no reports of injuries or deaths.

The China Earthquake Networks Center also reported that at 5:31 AM on July 26, a magnitude 3.1 earthquake happened in Ning'er County, Pu'er City, Yunnan. This earthquake was also 10 kilometers deep. Its center was at 23.16 degrees North latitude and 101.12 degrees East longitude. Many internet users in Yunnan also said they were woken up by the earthquake. (Editor: Chang Shu-ling) 1140726

Extra Train Delayed by 16 Minutes Because No New Conductor Showed Up for Work

Taipei, July 26 (CNA) The first round of recall votes was held today. Taiwan Railways Administration (TRA) added an extra train, the 5371, from Qidu to Taitung (new Tze-Chiang Limited Express). When the train arrived at Chaozhou Station, the train conductor could not find anyone to take over. After someone was quickly sent to take over, the train was delayed by about 16 minutes. TRA said they will find out who is responsible.

Today, people are voting on whether to remove 24 Kuomintang (KMT) lawmakers and Hsinchu Mayor Kao Hung-an, who is currently suspended. Because more people need to travel, TRA added extra trains for the weekend. This included the 5371 train from Qidu to Taitung today. But it was reported that when the train got to Chaozhou Station, the train conductor could not find anyone to take their place.

TRA said that the deputy head of the Kaohsiung train crew made a mistake when writing down the date for the extra train. This caused them to forget to assign a conductor. As soon as the train crew found out, they quickly assigned a conductor who was on standby to get on the train and start work.

Because of this mistake of not assigning a conductor, the train was delayed by about 16 minutes before it could leave. TRA said that the Southern Region Operations Office will review and fix the problem. They will also find out who was responsible and make sure similar problems do not happen again.
