Doctors Say Don't Forget Muscle Care After Disaster Cleanup Work

Southern Taiwan recently experienced strong winds and floods. Doctors have noticed that many people feel muscle pain or even get hurt while cleaning up after the disasters. They suggest that people rest or see a doctor when needed. Also, people should usually do muscle exercises and take care of their muscles to avoid losing too much muscle.

Chi Mei Hospital held an event today in their clinic hall called "Exercise for Muscle Strength, Active Dads." They set up a place for people to ask questions. Professional trainers and nutritionists from the sports center gave personal advice. They also asked people to learn how to use elastic bands for muscle-strengthening exercises.

Dr. Kuo Hsing-dao, who is a vice president at Chi Mei Hospital, said in his speech that because of the recent storms and floods, many people have been helping with rescue and cleanup work. Many volunteers and company workers have also been busy in the affected areas. During this hard work, some people got muscle pain, and many also got injured.

Dr. Kuo said that fathers, especially, are often very important for their families. Many of them might not exercise or take care of their bodies regularly. So, when they suddenly have a lot more physical work to do, they can get very tired. He hopes that this event will make everyone pay more attention to keeping their muscles strong by exercising and eating well to be ready or to get better.

Dr. Su Jiong-rui, a main doctor in the Rehabilitation Department at Chi Mei Hospital, explained that human muscles start to decrease after age 30. About 3% to 8% of muscle is lost every 10 years. After age 70, this loss can happen even faster. This is called "sarcopenia," or muscle loss. It not only makes people weaker but also affects how they move and their balance. It can also make them more likely to fall, break bones, and become unable to do things on their own. According to a survey by the National Health Agency, more than 34% of people over 65 have sarcopenia.

Dr. Su stated that sarcopenia can be reversed and prevented. With the right exercise and enough food, people can get better. Research shows that older people who do muscle training at least twice a week and eat enough protein can keep their muscles and improve their ability to move. Doing resistance exercises with elastic bands is safe and easy to start, and it helps the main muscles in the body very well. It is a very good way to begin exercising.

He said that some people have been working for many years and lifting heavy things, which caused injuries to their bodies. Because of this, they are afraid to exercise. But this makes their muscles weaker over time. Doctors can use ultrasound to find the injured parts and treat them. After the injured parts are healed, patients can go to the exercise center for muscle training. This helps them get better and slowly return to their normal lives. He asked people who have these problems to see a doctor early.

Taipei City Tests Electric-Assist Dispatch Vehicles to Help YouBike Problem at NTU

Taipei City has started testing "electric-assist small dispatch vehicles" at National Taiwan University (NTU) today. This is to fix a problem at YouBike stations inside the university. Sometimes there are no bikes to rent, or no empty spots to return bikes. This happens at certain times, like ocean tides. The city hopes this new system will help.

The Taipei City Department of Transportation said that National Taiwan University is the only big university in Taipei that has YouBike stations inside its campus. And many people use them there. The university has 59 YouBike stations, and about 15,000 bikes are rented each day. This is about 6% of all YouBike rentals in Taipei.

The Department explained that when students go to and leave classes, the YouBike stations on campus often run out of bikes to rent or places to return bikes. To solve this, the city worked with the university and Smile Cycling company (who runs YouBike). They created electric-assist small dispatch vehicles. These vehicles are more flexible than trucks and also good for the environment. They started testing them today.

He You-ren, the general manager of Smile Cycling company, said that the NTU campus already has 4 trucks and 40 workers who move bikes around. But even with this, sometimes there are not enough bikes in the right places. So, to help the university, they specially made electric-assist dispatch vehicles that can carry 7 YouBikes at one time. They also hired more part-time workers. They hope this will meet the students' needs.

He You-ren said that they plan to test the electric-assist dispatch vehicles for about six months. They are starting with 2 vehicles. During this time, they will watch how the vehicles work and make improvements. They will add more vehicles later if needed, depending on the situation.

The director of the Taipei City Department of Transportation, Hsieh Ming-hung, said that the 2 electric-assist dispatch vehicles are expected to help move an extra 400 to 500 YouBikes each day. This will be much more effective than just using trucks. He hopes this will provide a more convenient service for students when the new school year begins.

The National Taiwan University said that YouBike has become a very important "green" way for people all over Taiwan to travel. They thanked the city government for its help. They believe that the electric-assist dispatch vehicles, which are more flexible, safer, and better for the environment than trucks, will be a good solution for the YouBike "tidal problem" on campus. They also hope to continue working with the city government to make things even better.
