Israel: First Steps to Take Gaza City, Controls Outer Areas

The Israeli army announced today that it has started its first steps to take over Gaza City. They have called up tens of thousands of reserve soldiers to help with the full operation to take Gaza City.

Reuters reported that Israeli army spokesperson Effie Defrin told reporters: "We have started the first part of our attack on Gaza City. The Israel Defense Forces (IDF) now control the areas outside Gaza City."

Earlier today, Israeli Defense Minister Israel Katz approved the plan to take Gaza City. He also allowed about 60,000 reserve soldiers to be called up for this task.

Katz's decision was confirmed by a spokesperson to Agence France-Presse. This move puts more pressure on Hamas, a Palestinian Islamist group. At the same time, countries trying to make peace in the nearly two-year war in Gaza are waiting for Israel's official answer to their newest ideas.

Technology Stocks Sold Off, US Stock Market Mostly Falls

Investors sold off technology stocks and put their money into other parts of the market that were valued lower. They are waiting to hear what officials from the US Federal Reserve (Fed) will say at the world central bankers' meeting this week. Because of this, the Nasdaq and S&P stock indexes in the US closed lower today.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went up a small amount, 16.04 points, which is 0.04%. It closed at 44938.31 points.

The S&P 500 index went down by 15.59 points, which is 0.24%. It closed at 6395.78 points.

The Nasdaq index dropped by 142.09 points, which is 0.67%. It closed at 21172.86 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index also went down by 40.69 points, or 0.72%. It closed at 5630.82 points.

US Crude Oil Supplies Go Down, Global Oil Prices Rise

New information from the US government shows that the amount of crude oil stored in the US went down by 6 million barrels last week. At the same time, traders are still watching the talks that are trying to end the war between Russia and Ukraine. Because of these reasons, international oil prices went up today.

At the New York Mercantile Exchange, the price for West Texas Intermediate crude oil for October went up by 1.5%. It closed at $62.71 per barrel.

In London, the price for North Sea Brent crude oil for October went up by 1.6%. It closed at $66.84 per barrel.
