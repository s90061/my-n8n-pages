Taylor Swift Gets Engaged, Sports World Celebrates; Baseball Team Jokes No One Cares About Their Schedule

American pop star Taylor Swift and NFL player Travis Kelce announced their engagement on social media today. The sports world sent many good wishes. The Cleveland Guardians, a baseball team, joked that now no one cares about next year's game schedule.

According to AFP news, Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce started dating in 2023. The Chiefs team posted on social media with a red heart and an engagement ring. They wrote, "Today is like a fairytale. Congratulations to Travis Kelce and Taylor Swift! We are happy you are now a permanent part of the Chiefs family!"

Travis Kelce has won the Super Bowl three times with his team, most recently last year.

The NFL also posted a message. On the social media site X, they shared a photo of Kelce kneeling in a garden full of flowers, asking Taylor Swift to marry him. They wrote, "Congratulations to Travis Kelce and Taylor Swift."

The NFL also shared a video of the two at a Super Bowl victory celebration. Taylor Swift said on camera, "This is crazy, I can't believe this is really happening, I'm still shocked." The NFL wrote, "Us too."

The official ATP men's tennis tour shared a video of Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce at the US Open. They congratulated the couple on their happy news and said, "At that moment, we knew they were meant to be together."

Also, Major League Baseball (MLB) had just announced its game schedule for the 2026 season. But then, social media was filled with news of Taylor Swift's engagement. The Cleveland Guardians joked about it on X, writing, "Thanks, Taylor Swift. Now nobody cares about the schedule for next year."

The Boston Celtics, an NBA basketball team, posted on X about the engagement, her next album, and their new season. It said: "Days until Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce get engaged: 0. Days until (new album) The Life of a Showgirl is released: 37 days. Days until the Celtics season starts: 56 days."

The US Ryder Cup golf team also jokingly thanked the couple for sharing their news today. This avoided their news clashing with the golf team's big announcement tomorrow. The US team will announce the 6 players chosen by their captain for next month's competition against the European team.

The US team wrote: "Thank you, Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce, for choosing today to share your 'small' news. The 'big' news will come out tomorrow."

Taichung Police Catch Foreign Women in Sex Trade; One Claimed to Be an "Active Adult Film Actress"

The Taichung City Police Department announced today that they caught foreign women who came to Taiwan as tourists to do sex work. Besides women from Thailand, there were also three Japanese women. One of them said she was an "active adult film actress" and charged NT$20,000 for her services.

The Sixth Division of the Taichung City Police Department explained that recently, 22 police officers used a search warrant from the Taichung District Court. They checked short-term apartments and hotels in the Xitun District for illegal sex work. They found women doing sex work in an auto motel. They also found a woman who seemed to be an "active adult film actress."

During their search, police found some Taiwanese and Thai women who were in the middle of a transaction. They also found three Japanese women in the same motel. One of them claimed to be an "active adult film actress." Police saw her room had personal items and an open suitcase. This showed she was using the place to stay for a short time and do sex work.

Police said that they learned the service cost between NT$4,000 and NT$5,000 for 50 minutes. The Japanese women charged a higher price of NT$20,000. They said this was because they were "active actresses" with "films." However, when the police came, no sex acts were happening in the room. The Japanese woman was sitting on the bed, dressed and ready for a customer. It seemed that the high price might have made customers not want to pay.

Police said that in this operation, they caught 15 people in total. This included Taiwanese, Thai, and Japanese women, as well as staff members. The people running the illegal business rented hotel rooms to sell sex. They used messaging apps to talk to customers and avoid being caught. Many foreign women came to Taiwan with tourist visas. They pretended to be tourists but were actually doing sex work. The police will fine these people for breaking the "Social Order Maintenance Act" and send them to the immigration agency.

Zhou Jun-ming, the chief of the Sixth Division, asked illegal businesses not to think they can get away with it. He said they should not use hotels or pretend to be tourists to hide illegal sex work. The police will continue to find evidence and act to keep Taichung safe. This will help maintain Taichung's good image and ensure citizens can live without worry.
