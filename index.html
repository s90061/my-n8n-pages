Global Oil Prices Go Up Due to Geopolitical Risks

Today, international oil prices increased because traders are worried about geopolitical risks. This happened even though there might be more oil than needed in the market in the next few months.

The price for West Texas Intermediate crude oil, which is for delivery in October, went up by 60 cents. It is now 64.61 US dollars per barrel.

The price for Brent crude oil from the North Sea in London, for delivery in November, went up by 67 cents. It is now 68.15 US dollars per barrel.

Naomi Osaka Beats Coco Gauff at US Open, Moves to Quarterfinals

On September 1st, Japanese player Naomi Osaka beat Coco Gauff from the US. Osaka won 6-3, 6-2 and is now in the quarterfinals of the US Open women's singles.

Naomi Osaka won the US Open in 2018 and 2020. Coco Gauff won the US Open in 2023.

Osaka, who is the 23rd seed in the tournament, won against Gauff, the 3rd seed, in just 1 hour and 4 minutes. She also beat Gauff in their first match at the US Open in 2019, when Gauff was only 15 years old.

Gauff changed her coach before this year's US Open to improve her serves. But in this match, she had problems again and made 5 double faults. Gauff made 33 unforced errors, which is much more than Osaka's 12 errors.

After the match, Osaka said, "I feel a bit emotional, I don't want to cry."

She also said, "I really enjoy my time here. This is my favorite court in the whole world, and it means a lot to me to be back here."

Osaka's performance at this US Open is her best in a Grand Slam singles match since she had her baby in 2023 and returned to playing tennis the following year.

Next, Osaka will play against Karolina Muchova from the Czech Republic. Muchova reached the quarterfinals by beating Marta Kostyuk from Ukraine with scores of 6-3, 6-7 (0/7), 6-3.

Iga Swiatek Wins Easily and Moves to US Open Quarterfinals

On September 1st, Polish tennis star Iga Swiatek won her fourth-round match at the US Open women's singles. She beat Ekaterina Alexandrova from Russia, the 13th seed, in two sets: 6-3, 6-1. This win means Swiatek is now in the quarterfinals of the US Open.

Swiatek, who is the 2nd seed in this year's US Open, took about 1 hour to beat Alexandrova. Because of this win, she has reached at least the quarterfinals in all four Grand Slam tournaments this year.

The first set was very close at the start, with the score tied at 3-3. But then Swiatek won the 7th game, and then won two more games in a row to take the first set.

In the second set, Swiatek kept playing well and did not let her opponent score many points. She easily won the second set 6-1, which meant Alexandrova was out of the tournament.

Even though Swiatek has won 4 of her 6 Grand Slam titles on the clay courts of the French Open, this win today shows that she plays very well on all types of courts.

Swiatek, who is 24 years old, was happy with her performance after the match. She said, "Things are different every month. As long as I feel good about my game and have a good mindset, I can handle any court."

In the US Open women's singles quarterfinals, Swiatek will play against the winner of the match between Amanda Anisimova from the US and Beatriz Haddad Maia from Brazil.
