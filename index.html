Songshan Airport Viewing Deck Finished, Reopens October 4th

The viewing deck at Songshan Airport has been fixed for almost two years. It is now finished. Today, the Civil Aeronautics Administration (CAA) announced that it will open again for visitors on October 4th.

The Songshan Airport viewing deck has been fixed for nearly two years. It is now done and has new things like a wall with mascots and a big wall with plants for photos. This morning, Minister of Transportation Chen Shih-Kai said it could open during the Mid-Autumn Festival holiday.

The CAA said in a press release that the Songshan Airport viewing deck will open on October 4th. Zheng Jian-zhong, who is the head of the Taipei International Airport Office, said that they will check all the things at the viewing deck in the next two days. It will open on October 4th, the first day of the Mid-Autumn Festival holiday. It will be open from 9 AM to 9 PM every day.

Zheng Jian-zhong said that the viewing deck first opened in 2011. It quickly became a popular place to watch planes in northern Taiwan. It once had over 450,000 visitors in one year.

The new viewing deck at Songshan Airport now has two more chipmunk mascots, "Song-Sang" and "Ai-Jiang", in addition to the original Songshan Airport mascot, "Lu-Cheng-Cheng". There is also a 3D painted wall with the mascots where visitors can take photos. Next to it, there is a large plant wall about 16 meters long and 3.5 meters high. It also has walls shaped like mountains, and seats shaped like pebbles and clouds. These designs are meant to remind people of planes flying in the sky.

Taipei City Checks Indoor Air Quality, Costco Beitou and Another Store Fail

To keep people healthy when they breathe, the Taipei City Environmental Protection Department checked the air quality in 14 big stores. They found that Costco Beitou store and Carrefour Chongqing store had too much carbon dioxide. They asked these stores to fix the problem by a certain time. If they don't, they will have to pay a fine between NT$50,000 and NT$250,000.

Today, the Taipei City Environmental Protection Department announced in a press release that big stores are places where many people gather indoors. Because of this, the level of carbon dioxide can easily become too high. When carbon dioxide levels are too high, it can make it hard to breathe. It can also cause headaches, make people sleepy, slow down their reflexes, and make them feel tired.

The Environmental Protection Department said that from August to September, they checked the indoor air quality of 14 big stores to make sure people could shop safely. They found that both Costco Beitou store and Carrefour Chongqing store had carbon dioxide levels higher than the allowed limit. They have told the businesses to fix this problem by a certain date. For example, they should let more fresh air into their air conditioning systems, make sure air circulation is better, and control how many people come into the store.

The Environmental Protection Department said that while they are fixing the problem, the businesses must put a sign at the entrance to show that they did not pass the check. If they do not fix the problem when checked again, they will have to pay a fine of NT$50,000 to NT$250,000.

Lulu to Bring Back Classic Variety Shows at Golden Bell 60, Betel Nut Sisters and Dong Yue-Hua Are Coming

The 60th Golden Bell Awards ceremony for TV shows will happen on October 17th. The artist Lulu will open the show with singing and dancing to bring back old, famous variety shows. The "Betel Nut Sisters" Cao Lan and Yao Dai-wei, "Dong Yue-Hua" Dong Zhi-cheng from "Red and White Victory," and "Uncle Fuzhou" Xu Xiao-shun from "Happy Jade Bell" will also return to the Golden Bell stage.

The 60th Golden Bell Awards ceremony for TV shows is coming soon. This year is the 60th Golden Bell, which shows how many different Taiwanese TV shows have been made over 60 years. The ceremony for TV shows will have three main parts, letting the audience remember classic moments with both laughter and tears.

The first part of the show is called "The Great Entertainer." Lulu (Huang Lu-zi-yin), who hosts the TV show awards, will start it with singing and dancing. She will sing about the years that variety shows have been with everyone. She will also act out famous moments from old shows like "Five-Light Awards," "Lian Huan Pao," "Super Sunday," "Variety Kaleidoscope," and "Kangxi Lai Le."

Famous people from variety shows who made many people laugh will also come together again. These include "Betel Nut Sisters" Cao Lan and Yao Dai-wei, "Dong Yue-Hua" Dong Zhi-cheng from "Red and White Victory," and "Uncle Fuzhou" Xu Xiao-shun from "Happy Jade Bell." They will all come back to the Golden Bell stage to bring lots of jokes and good memories.

The second part of the show is called "Rich Flowers Bloom from Diverse Soil." The stage will become a place where different cultures meet. Four important musicians will take turns performing. These include Ya-Wei Mo-Rui, who represents Indigenous music, Hsieh Yu-Wei, a talented Hakka singer, Chen Meng-Hsien, a new Taiwanese singer, and TRASH, a band that just won the Golden Melody Award for Best Band. These four groups will sing together in four languages: Indigenous, Hakka, Taiwanese, and Mandarin. This will show the special and rich charm of Taiwanese music.

The last special performance will be a collaboration between different generations called "Sing, Starting from TV." It will feature Luo Shi-Feng, Chien-Ta Ai-Sha, Huang Wei-Jin, GENBLUE Phantom Blue Bear, and ARKis.

There will be three exciting performances in a row. From classic variety shows to diverse music, and then to new combinations of different generations, the 60th Golden Bell Awards will have the most entertaining stage. It will let the audience laugh and feel touched together, watching a special moment for Taiwanese television.

The 60th Golden Bell Awards for TV shows and drama series will be held on October 17th and 18th, respectively, at the Taipei Pop Music Center. They will also be broadcast live on SET Metro, the Golden Bell official YouTube channel, and other platforms.
