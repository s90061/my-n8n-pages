Investors worry about US economy, oil prices fall sharply

(New York, August 1st, Reuters) Oil prices fell a lot today. This happened because investors are now more worried about the US economy. There were some bad economic reports and new taxes on goods. These things made people think that the demand for oil might not grow.

In New York, the price of West Texas Intermediate crude oil for September went down by 2.8%. It ended the day at 67.33 US dollars per barrel.

In London, the price of Brent crude oil for October also fell by 2.8%. It closed at 69.67 US dollars per barrel.

Weak job report causes economic worries, US stocks close lower

(New York, August 1st, Reuters) US stock markets closed lower today. This happened after the US released its July job report, which was not good. This made people in the market worry about the US economy.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell by 542.40 points, or 1.23%. It closed at 43588.58 points.

The S&P 500 index went down by 101.38 points, or 1.60%. It finished at 6238.01 points.

The Nasdaq index, which has many technology stocks, dropped by 472.32 points, or 2.24%. It closed at 20650.13 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index fell by 80.314 points, or 1.43%. It closed at 5527.608 points.

Federal Reserve Governor Kugler resigns as Trump pushes for lower interest rates

(Washington, August 1st, Reuters) The US central bank, the Federal Reserve (Fed), said today that Governor Adriana Kugler will leave her job next week. This gives President Trump a chance to choose a new person for the Fed's board. This happens while Trump is pushing the Fed to lower interest rates.

According to AFP news, Kugler was chosen by former President Biden to be a Fed Governor in 2023. She did not say why she is leaving now.

Kugler was supposed to stay in her job until January 2026. But she is leaving early, next week. This means Trump can choose someone for the Fed's leadership sooner than expected.

This change in staff comes as the Fed is getting more pressure from Trump. He keeps saying that Jerome Powell, the head of the Fed, is too slow to lower interest rates.

The Fed said that Kugler plans to go back to being a professor at Georgetown University this year.
