Surfer Dies After Shark Attack in Sydney, First Time in Over 3 Years

Sydney, September 6 (Central News Agency) â€“ Today, a surfer was attacked by a shark in Sydney, the main city of New South Wales, Australia. Police have said that the man died because of his serious injuries. This is the first time someone has died from a shark attack in Sydney in more than three and a half years. Because of this, some beaches have been closed for a short time.

Reuters reported that the attack happened at Long Reef Beach, which is in the northern part of Sydney. Police said that just after 10 AM today, the man, whose name is not yet known, was surfing with friends about 100 meters from the shore when a large shark attacked him.

John Duncan, a police chief for the Northern Beaches area of Sydney, said in a live TV meeting with reporters that other surfers pulled the man out of the water. But he had lost too much blood and died right there. His injuries were "very bad."

Police also said that the man was an experienced surfer. Two pieces of his surfboard were found and sent for testing.

Officials said that they do not know yet what kind of shark attacked the man today. The Northern Beaches Council in Sydney said that lifeguards are using jet skis to look for sharks at different beaches.

The last time a shark killed someone in Sydney was in February 2022. That time, a swimmer was attacked near a beach. Before that, it was in 1963. This is the fourth shark attack that caused a death in Australia this year.
