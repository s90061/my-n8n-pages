3,000-Year-Old Gold Bracelet Missing from Egyptian Museum; Airports and Ports Increase Searches

Cairo, September 17 (CNA) â€“ The Egyptian Ministry of Antiquities has announced that a 3,000-year-old gold bracelet is missing. It was in a repair lab at the Egyptian Museum in Cairo.

AFP reported that the bracelet is a gold band with round blue lapis lazuli beads. It is from the time of Pharaoh Amenemope, who ruled during Egypt's 21st dynasty (between 1070 and 945 BC).

The Ministry of Antiquities did not say when the item was last seen in a statement given last night.

The Ministry said that an internal investigation has started. All offices that deal with old things at Egypt's airports, sea ports, and land borders have been told about the missing bracelet.

The Ministry also said that they did not tell the public about this immediately to help the investigation. Now, they are checking all the items in the lab.

The Egyptian Museum in Tahrir Square has more than 170,000 old items. This includes the famous gold burial mask of Pharaoh Amenemope.
