Denmark says its airspace was attacked in a 'hybrid' way; unknown drones seen at its biggest military base

Danish police said today that unknown drones were seen again at night above their biggest military base. In recent days, many people in Denmark have seen drones. The authorities call this a "hybrid attack."

Agence France-Presse (AFP) reported that these uninvited drones have caused many airports across Denmark, a country in the Scandinavia Peninsula, to close. This has been happening since September 22nd, when the mysterious drones first appeared in their airspace.

A police officer on duty told AFP, "I can confirm that an event happened last night around 8:15 PM and lasted for several hours. We saw one or two drones flying over and around the Karup air force base."

The police do not know where the drones came from. He said, "We did not shoot them down." The police are working with the military to find out more about these events.

The Karup air force base shares a runway with a civilian airport. The airport was closed for a short time during the event. However, no commercial flights were taking off or landing at that time, so there was no impact on flights.

High temperatures in Northern Taiwan on the second day of Teacher's Day holiday; Central and Southern Taiwan should watch out for afternoon thunderstorms

Tomorrow is the second day of the Teacher's Day holiday. The Central Weather Administration said today that because of a high-pressure system over the Pacific Ocean, temperatures in Northern Taiwan (Taipei, New Taipei, Taoyuan) and areas near the mountains south of Taichung might be hotter than 36 degrees Celsius. Also, Central and Southern Taiwan and mountain areas might have afternoon thunderstorms.

Zeng Chao-cheng, a forecaster from the Central Weather Administration, told CNA that the weather will be mostly stable for the next week. Because of the Pacific high-pressure system, tomorrow's weather will be similar to today's. Most places will be cloudy with some sunny periods. There might be a little rain on the coast of Keelung, in the eastern part of Taiwan, and on the Hengchun Peninsula. Central and Southern Taiwan and mountain areas might have afternoon thunderstorms.

Regarding temperatures, Zeng Chao-cheng said that tomorrow's high temperatures will be about 32 to 35 degrees Celsius in most places. However, Taipei City, New Taipei City, Taoyuan City, and areas near the mountains south of Taichung might have local high temperatures above 36 degrees Celsius.

Zeng Chao-cheng also said that after September 29th, the Pacific high-pressure system will get a little stronger. It will be sunny to cloudy everywhere, and the western part of Taiwan and mountain areas will have afternoon thunderstorms.

For the weather from September 30th to October 3rd, Zeng Chao-cheng stated that it will be sunny to cloudy in most places. Southern Taiwan and mountain areas will have afternoon thunderstorms. On the morning of September 30th, there might be a few showers in western Taiwan, especially near the coast.

Zeng Chao-cheng mentioned that on October 4th, a low-pressure system will pass south of Taiwan. This will bring a bit more moisture. Hualien, Taitung, and the Hengchun Peninsula will have short, local showers. Other areas will be sunny to cloudy, and Southern Taiwan and mountain areas will have afternoon thunderstorms.

About the two typhoons in the Pacific Ocean, Zeng Chao-cheng said that Typhoon Bualoy is moving towards Vietnam. Tropical Storm Racoon, which is east of Japan, is expected to keep moving northeast over the ocean. Both typhoons will not greatly affect Taiwan.

Hsieh Su-wei and her partner win against Chan Hao-ching in a comeback; they pass the first round of China Open women's doubles

Taiwan's famous tennis player Hsieh Su-wei played against Chan Hao-ching today in the first round of women's doubles at the China Open. Hsieh Su-wei and her Latvian partner, Jelena Ostapenko, won the match after falling behind. The final score was 5-7, 6-4, 11-9.

The China Open is happening in Beijing and is a WTA 1000-level event. After playing together at Wimbledon in July, Hsieh Su-wei and her well-known Latvian partner, Jelena Ostapenko, teamed up again. They are the fourth seeds in the women's doubles. However, in the first round, they played against another Taiwanese player, Chan Hao-ching, and her Chinese partner, Jiang Xinyu.

Hsieh Su-wei and Ostapenko played together again after two months. In the first set, they had a close match with their opponents. After eight games, both teams had broken each other's serves once, making the score 4-4. But then, the "Taiwan-Latvia team" missed a chance to win the set. Their opponents quickly broke their serve again, and Hsieh and Ostapenko lost the set 5-7.

Hsieh Su-wei and Ostapenko had to win the next set. They played better in the second set. Even though both teams were tied at 4-4 after eight games, the "Taiwan-Latvia team" broke their opponents' serve in the important 10th game. They won the set 6-4, which tied the match and gave them a chance to win.

In the deciding tie-break (first to 10 points), both teams fought hard again. They were tied 8 times. But Hsieh Su-wei and Ostapenko stayed strong towards the end of the match. They were the first to get a match point. Even though Chan Hao-ching and Jiang Xinyu saved one match point, Hsieh and Ostapenko still won by a close score of 11-9. They will now play in the top 16 teams in women's doubles.
