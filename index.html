Finland 93 to 79 Beats Georgia, Reaches Basketball EuroBasket Semi-finals for the First Time

After surprising Serbia with a win, Finland today beat Georgia 93 to 79. This is the first time in their history that Finland has reached the semi-finals of the European Basketball Championship.

Earlier, in the EuroBasket Round of 16, Finland surprisingly won against Serbia, who was a favorite to win the championship. The score was 92 to 86. This win shocked the international basketball world. Serbia, a top team, has now lost in the Round of 16 for two tournaments in a row.

In today's game, Finland and Georgia played back and forth for the first 6 minutes. But at the end of the first quarter, Finland made a strong run. They quickly changed the score from 12-12 to 28-15. Finland was ahead by 13 points at the end of the first quarter.

In the second quarter, Georgia got closer, only 9 points behind for a short time. But Finland kept playing strongly. At halftime, Finland was leading 57 to 40, making their lead even bigger.

In the third quarter, Finland was ahead by 20 points at one point. They went into the fourth quarter with a score of 71 to 62. In the final quarter, Georgia tried to catch up quickly. With about 8 minutes left, they were only 8 points behind. But Finland pulled ahead again at the end, winning the game 93 to 79 against Georgia.

Mikael Jantunen, a power forward for Finland, played the best with 19 points and 5 rebounds. He made 3 three-pointers in the first half, which helped his team get an early lead. His teammate Lauri Markkanen also played a big part, scoring 17 points, getting 6 rebounds, and blocking 4 shots.

Finland will play in the semi-finals on the 12th. They will play against the winner of the game between Germany and Slovenia.

Oracle Stock Jumps 40%, Founder Ellison Becomes Richest Person, Ahead of Musk

Oracle, a big software company in the US, saw its stock price go up by more than 40%. Because of this, the company's founder, Larry Ellison, now has more money than Elon Musk, the CEO of Tesla. Ellison is now the richest person in the world.

By 10 AM today in New York, Larry Ellison's total wealth was almost 400 billion US dollars. According to Bloomberg, Elon Musk's wealth is about 385 billion US dollars right now.

After Ellison and Musk, the next richest people are Mark Zuckerberg, who started Meta; Jeff Bezos, who started Amazon; and Larry Page and Sergey Brin, who started Google.

Ellison's money grew fast because he and his family have been working hard in artificial intelligence (AI), media, and politics.

Oracle's stock went up a lot today because the company said it has over 450 billion US dollars in cloud service orders that are not yet delivered. Also, Oracle signed deals worth billions of dollars for computing services with Nvidia, a big AI company, and OpenAI, which supports ChatGPT.

The Ellison family's business reach is also growing. Larry Ellison's son, David, recently bought Paramount Global for 8 billion US dollars.

Larry Ellison is a strong supporter of President Trump. He recently visited the White House. There, he announced a plan to build large data centers with Masayoshi Son, the head of Japan's SoftBank Group, and Sam Altman, the CEO of OpenAI.

Larry Ellison is 81 years old. He started Oracle more than 40 years ago. Today, during trading, the company's total value almost reached 1 trillion US dollars.

European Stocks Had Mixed Results as US Rate Cut Hopes Balanced Geopolitical Risks

Today, the main stock markets in Europe had different results, with some going up and some going down. This happened because people expect the US Federal Reserve (Fed) to lower interest rates. This expectation made people less worried about the growing political problems around the world.

The FTSE 100 index in London went down by 17.14 points, or 0.19%. It closed at 9225.39 points.

The DAX index in Frankfurt went down by 85.50 points, or 0.36%. It closed at 23632.95 points.

The CAC 40 index in Paris went up by 11.93 points, or 0.15%. It closed at 7761.32 points.
