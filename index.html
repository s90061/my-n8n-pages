Germany Puts Former Lawmaker's Helper in Prison for Spying for China for 4 Years and 9 Months

A former helper to a German lawmaker has been put in prison for 4 years and 9 months. He was working as a spy for China. This case makes people worried about China's spying in Europe.

According to French news, a court in Dresden said that a German man, Jian Guo, who is of Chinese origin, collected information for a Chinese spy agency. He did this while he was working for Maximilian Krah. Krah is an important politician from the far-right party called "Alternative for Germany" (AfD). At that time, Krah was a member of the European Parliament. Jian Guo collected documents and files from the European Parliament and information about the AfD between 2019 and 2024, when he worked for Krah in Brussels.

The prosecutor wanted to put Jian Guo in prison for 7 and a half years. The prosecutor said that Jian Guo started working for Chinese spy agencies in 2002. His work included watching Chinese people in Europe who did not agree with the Chinese government.

A Chinese woman, known only as Yaqi X., also helped Jian Guo. She was put in prison for 1 year and 9 months, but she will not have to serve the time now.

Jian Guo was also accused of being the person who gave orders to Yaqi X. At the start of the trial, Yaqi X. said she gave Jian Guo information about flight times and cargo movements. She worked for a company that helped with logistics at Leipzig Airport. The prosecutor said that she gave information about military planes, soldiers, and drones, even equipment sent to Israel.

Krah is now a member of the German Parliament. He said in court this month that he did not know Jian Guo was spying. He only hired him because he was good at languages and had experience with import and export companies.

Krah himself is also being investigated. He is suspected of money laundering and taking bribes from China while he was a member of the European Parliament.

The Chinese Embassy in Germany has not said anything to the media about this. In the past, China has said that claims of spying in Europe are not true.

Trump Suggests Gaza Peace Plan; Russia and China Show Support

Former US President Donald Trump has suggested a 20-point peace plan for Gaza. Both Russia and China have said they support this plan. This happened after Trump talked with Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu at the White House about the situation in Gaza, and Israel agreed to the plan. This plan aims to stop the war in Gaza.

French news reported that Dmitry Peskov, who speaks for the Kremlin, told reporters during a daily phone conference that Russia "always supports and welcomes all of Donald Trump's efforts to end this ongoing sadness."

He also said, "Of course, we hope that this plan can happen and help the situation in the Middle East become peaceful."

Guo Jiakun, a spokesperson for China's Ministry of Foreign Affairs, said at a regular press conference, "China welcomes and supports all efforts that can help reduce the tension between Palestine and Israel."

Also, Friedrich Merz, the German Chancellor, met with the families of people held by the Palestinian group Hamas today. He said that Trump's peace plan is the "best chance" to end the war in Gaza.

A spokesperson for the German Chancellor said in a statement that Merz said, "Israel's support for this plan is a big step forward. Now Hamas must also agree to it to make way for peace."
