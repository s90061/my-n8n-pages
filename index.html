Israel's Airstrike on Qatar Condemned by Many Western Countries; Canada to Review Relationship

Yesterday, Israel attacked the capital city of Qatar, Doha, with airstrikes. These attacks targeted leaders of Hamas. Because of this, many Western countries, including the UK, France, and Germany, as well as the European Union, have criticized Israel. Today, Canada said it will look at its relationship with Israel.

According to Reuters, Anita Anand, who is Canada's Foreign Minister, spoke to reporters. This happened during a meeting of the Liberal Party, which is the party in power.

Anand told reporters, "We are looking at our relationship with Israel." She also said again that Canada believes Israel's attacks are not acceptable. This is especially true because Qatar is trying to help bring peace to the Middle East.

Reporters also asked if Canada would think about putting any kind of punishments on Israel. Anand replied, "We will continue to decide what to do next."

Today, Ursula von der Leyen, the head of the European Commission, announced new plans. These plans are to help Ukraine defend itself against Russia's full invasion. She also asked for limits on trade and punishments for Israel because of the war in Gaza.

Regarding Israel's attack, British Prime Minister Keir Starmer said that this attack went against Qatar's right to govern itself. He also said it could make the situation in the region "even worse."

French President Emmanuel Macron clearly stated that Israel's actions are "not acceptable," no matter what Israel's reasons are.

Johann Wadephul, the German Foreign Minister, said in a statement: "Israel's attack on Doha not only went against Qatar's right to rule its own land, but it also put at risk all our efforts to get the hostages released. This attack cannot be accepted."

NASA Bans Chinese Citizens from Space Programs; US-China Space Rivalry Intensifies

Bloomberg reported that people familiar with the situation said the US space agency, NASA, has stopped Chinese citizens who hold US visas from being part of its programs.

NASA has always had some rules about hiring Chinese citizens. However, in the past, Chinese citizens with US visas could still help with NASA's research. They could work as outside helpers, graduate students, or university scientists.

The sources said that since September 5, Chinese citizens who used to work at NASA have not been allowed to access the agency's data systems. They also cannot join meetings related to their work, whether in person or online.

Bethany Stevens, a spokesperson for NASA, confirmed the steps the agency has taken. Stevens said: "NASA has taken internal actions regarding Chinese citizens. This includes limiting their access to our buildings, data, and computer networks. This is to keep our work safe."

Both China and the United States plan to send people to the moon in the next five years.

Finland Defeats Georgia 93-79, Reaches EuroBasket Semifinals for First Time

Today, Finland won against Georgia with a score of 93 to 79. This happened after they surprisingly beat Serbia. This victory means Finland has made it to the semifinals of the European Basketball Championship for the first time in their history.

Before this, Finland had already surprised many in the EuroBasket Round of 16. They beat Serbia, a team expected to win the championship, with a score of 92 to 86. This win allowed them to move to the quarterfinals and surprised the world of basketball. Serbia, a favorite to win, has now lost in the Round of 16 for the second time in a row.

In today's game, Finland and Georgia played back and forth for the first six minutes. But towards the end of the first quarter, Finland started playing much better. They quickly changed the score from 12-12 to 28-15, taking a 13-point lead by the end of the first quarter.

In the second quarter, Georgia managed to get closer, making the score only a 9-point difference. However, Finland kept playing strongly. By the end of the first half, they had increased their lead to 57-40.

Finland was even leading by 20 points at one point in the third quarter. They went into the fourth quarter with a score of 71-62. Georgia tried hard to catch up in the final quarter and got within 8 points with about 8 minutes left in the game. But Finland pulled ahead again in the end, winning the game 93-79 against Georgia.

For Finland, Mikael Jantunen, a power forward, played the best with 19 points and 5 rebounds. He made three 3-point shots in the first half, which helped his team get an early lead. His teammate Lauri Markkanen also played a key role, scoring 17 points, getting 6 rebounds, and blocking 4 shots.

Finland will play in the semifinals on the 12th. They will play against the winner of the game between Germany and Slovenia.
