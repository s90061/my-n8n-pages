TSG Hawks Sign New Pitcher From Major League Baseball, Named Namasia

The TSG Hawks baseball team in Taiwan announced today that they have signed a new foreign pitcher, Dinelson Lamet. He has played in Major League Baseball (MLB) over 100 times. His jersey number will be 45. Lamet has played in 121 MLB games and was teammates with Shohei Ohtani last year when he played for the Dodgers.

The TSG Hawks team said today that they signed the new MLB-level foreign pitcher, Namasia, to make their pitching stronger. Lamet is 33 years old. He has played for the Padres, Rockies, Red Sox, Dodgers, and Royals in his career. Last year, he played for the Dodgers with Japanese star Shohei Ohtani.

Lamet has played in a total of 121 games in his MLB career, with 60 starts. He has 17 wins and 24 losses. He pitched for 367.2 innings and got 474 strikeouts. His ERA (earned run average) is 4.63.

Lamet was a starting pitcher in 2017, 2019, and 2020. In 2020, he started 12 games in MLB and pitched for 69 innings. His ERA was only 2.09. He only gave up 39 hits and had 93 strikeouts. Lamet played in MLB last year. This year, he has been playing in the Mexican League. He has started all 17 games, with 7 wins and 5 losses. His ERA is 5.01, and he has 88 strikeouts in 88 innings.

The second half of the Chinese Professional Baseball League started on July 4th this year. The TSG Hawks currently have 7 wins, 6 losses, and 2 ties in the second half. Their winning rate is 53.8%, and they are temporarily in second place.

Ministry of Transportation Helps People Fix Their Homes by Connecting Them with Companies

Typhoon Danas and southwest air currents have caused serious damage to homes in southern Taiwan. The Ministry of Transportation said today that it has asked different departments to connect people with companies. This is because there is a shortage of workers and materials right now. The ministry hopes to help residents find workers to repair their homes.

The Ministry of Transportation posted on Facebook today that Vice Minister Chen Yen-po held a meeting yesterday. They talked about the progress of repairs in the Tainan disaster area and what to do next. They have already visited over 100 damaged homes. They are still looking for more people and resources to help residents rebuild their homes quickly.

The Ministry of Transportation said that there is a shortage of workers and materials, so people cannot find workers to fix their homes. They also don't have enough people or tools. The government has asked all ministries to help. Each ministry is responsible for certain areas. The Ministry of Transportation is responsible for Xinying District, Yanshui District, Baihe District, and Houbi District in Tainan City.

The Ministry of Transportation said that people who need to fix their homes can first register with their district chief. The Ministry of Transportation will then help connect residents with companies. After that, residents and companies will talk and sign a simple contract. The Ministry of Transportation only helps to connect them; it does not pay for the repairs.

To speed up the rebuilding process after the disaster, the Ministry of Transportation also set some rules for action. Besides connecting people with companies, they will fix old, one-story houses and homes with roofs blown off first, as these need immediate help. Asbestos tiles and other waste materials will be collected in one place. The district office will then help clear them away.

Shigeru Ishiba Expresses Concern for Central and Southern Taiwan Flooding; Lin Chia-lung Thanks Him and Hopes for Cooperation

Heavy rains recently caused damage in Taiwan. Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba sent a message to express his concern for Taiwan. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs said today that Foreign Minister Lin Chia-lung thanked Japan again for their care. He also hopes that Taiwan and Japan can work together to handle challenges. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Taiwan's office in Japan have both thanked the Japanese government sincerely.

Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba sent a message to show his concern for Taiwan. He said he was sad for those who died and their families. He also expressed his true sympathy to those affected by the disaster. He prayed that daily life would return to normal soon and said, "Taiwan, keep going!" The Japan-Taiwan Exchange Association shared this message on Facebook in the afternoon.

Because of this, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs released a news statement this afternoon. It said that the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Taiwan's office in Japan immediately thanked the Japanese government sincerely.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs said that Shigeru Ishiba also sent a message on July 12th to express concern for Taiwan after Typhoon Danas. This shows the deep and true friendship between Taiwan and Japan. The Taiwanese government and many groups in society continue to work hard to help with disaster relief and recovery. They want to help people affected by the disaster rebuild their homes and return to normal life soon.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs said that Foreign Minister Lin Chia-lung once again truly thanked the Japanese government for their warm care and real support. He also hopes that in the future, Taiwan and Japan will continue to work more closely in important areas. He wishes for a stronger, friendlier, and more lasting partnership to face all challenges together.
