Firefighter Wu En-shuo Dies Saving People, Receives Award and Promotion

New Taipei City, July 25 (CNA) â€“ A rescue operation in a water area near Guangxing Bridge and Cukeng Dam in Xindian District, New Taipei City, led to the deaths of firefighters Wu En-shuo and Zhang Jing-qian. Wu En-shuo's funeral service was held today. New Taipei City Mayor Hou Yu-ih attended and gave an award on behalf of the government. Wu En-shuo was also promoted to squad leader after his death.

On July 8, people went into a forbidden area near Guangxing Bridge and Cukeng Dam in Xindian District, New Taipei City, to paddleboard. One person drowned. The fire department's rescue boat then flipped over. This caused the deaths of 37-year-old firefighter Wu En-shuo and 36-year-old firefighter Zhang Jing-qian.

The New Taipei City Fire Department said that Wu En-shuo was a member of the Zhitan team. He graduated from the police academy 25th class and had 16 years of service. He was accepted into the second degree program at the Central Police University this year. He had special training in advanced life support (EMT-P), water rescue, fire investigation, and long tunnel rescue.

Wu En-shuo's funeral service took place this morning. Zhong Shi-ming, a senior technical specialist from the New Taipei City Fire Department, and several battalion leaders performed the covering ceremony. They placed the fire department flag and the national flag on his coffin. Minister of the Interior Liu Se-fang also came to present a professional firefighting medal.

During the ceremony, Mayor Hou Yu-ih presented an award from the government, an Executive Yuan role model certificate, and a fire service honor certificate on behalf of others. Wu En-shuo was also promoted to squad leader, a position with two lines and one star. His wife accepted these honors.

New Taipei City Fire Chief Chen Chong-yueh gave a firefighter role model certificate. Wu Guan-yuan, a director at the Central Police University, gave an honorary degree certificate from the university. These were to recognize Wu En-shuo's brave sacrifice.

The New Taipei City Fire Department shared that Wu En-shuo's colleagues said he was a member of the Firefighters' Rights Promotion Association. He worked hard to get many rights for everyone in the past. They said he was serious and excellent. Everyone felt very sad about this accident.

Slow Cleanup of Damaged Solar Panels in Yizhu: Government to Help Companies Speed Up

Chiayi County, July 25 (CNA) â€“ In Yizhu Township, Chiayi County, solar power panels at the Xinzhuang retention pond were badly damaged by Typhoon Danas. However, the company is slow in cleaning them up. Premier Cho Jung-tai visited today and was quite unhappy. He asked the company to tell the government if they have any problems, and the government will help.

Today, Premier Cho Jung-tai, along with Minister of Economic Affairs Guo Zhi-hui, Minister of Environment Peng Ke-ming, and Democratic Progressive Party lawmaker Cai Yi-yu, visited Budai's Xinwen and Yizhu's Xinzhuang retention ponds in Chiayi County. They came to see how the damaged solar panels from Typhoon Danas were being cleaned up.

In Xinwen, the work to clean up the damaged solar panels is going as expected by the government and will soon be finished. But in Yizhu, most of the damaged solar panels are still stuck in the retention pond. This made Premier Cho Jung-tai very unhappy. He told the Thai company in charge of this solar power plant to handle it quickly. He also said that if they face any problems, they should tell the government, and the government will help. The government will not just ask when the cleanup will be done.

Guo Zhi-hui also told the Thai company that the land around the Xinzhuang retention pond belongs to Taiwan Sugar Corporation. So, the damaged solar panels can be stored there for a while. If the company needs more machines or workers for the cleanup, the Ministry of Economic Affairs can help. He urged them not to delay any longer.

Cai Yi-yu told CNA reporters that the solar panel company in Yizhu is from Thailand. They might not know much about how to do rescue work after a disaster in Taiwan, which is why there was a delay. The Thai company said they also want to clean it up quickly but cannot find companies to help with the cleanup.

After checking the solar panel cleanup, Premier Cho Jung-tai and Guo Zhi-hui went to the memorial hall for Li Yuan-han, a 36-year-old employee of Taiwan Power Company's Chiayi office, who died while working. They gave their condolences to his family and offered money to help them. Taiwan Power Company Chairman Zeng Wen-sheng, Chiayi County Deputy Magistrate Liu Pei-dong, and Democratic Progressive Party lawmakers Chen Guan-ting, Cai Yi-yu, and Wang Mei-hui also visited the memorial hall today to show their respect.

Philippine Supreme Court: Vice President Sara's Impeachment Process Is Against the Rules

Manila, July 25 (CNA) â€“ Philippine Vice President Sara Duterte was facing a process in Congress to remove her from office. This was because of accusations like corruption and threatening to kill the president. Today, the Philippine Supreme Court said that this process is against the country's rules. This is because it breaks the rule that says you cannot try to remove the same official more than once in one year.

Camille Ting, a spokesperson for the Philippine Supreme Court, announced in a press conference today that the House of Representatives' process to remove Sara Duterte from office should be stopped. This is based on the "one-year rule" in the constitution.

The "one-year rule" means that people or groups cannot try to remove the same high-level official from office more than once in a year.

Sara Duterte welcomed the Supreme Court's decision today. The Senate, which was in charge of the impeachment case, also said it "must" respect the Supreme Court's decision.

In December last year, several groups in the Philippines accused Sara of breaking the constitution, corruption, not doing her public duty well, and threatening to kill the president. They sent three different requests to the House of Representatives to remove her from office.

In February this year, the House of Representatives voted to remove Sara. They combined the accusations into a fourth request and sent it to the Senate. According to the Philippine constitution, the House of Representatives starts the removal process, and the Senate hears the case.

In May this year, the Philippines held its mid-term elections. All more than 300 seats in the House of Representatives were re-elected, and 12 of the 24 seats in the Senate were also re-elected. This meant the removal process went across different terms of Congress.

Sara's lawyers already questioned if the fourth removal request was against the rules in February. They said that the first three requests had already been voted on at the committee level in the House of Representatives, which started the removal process.

In June, the lawyers again argued that the removal process should not continue across different terms of Congress. They asked the Supreme Court to temporarily stop the removal process and to say that the requests were not valid.

Camille Ting explained in the press conference today that the House of Representatives did not act on the first three removal requests. So, when the previous session ended, it was like those requests were rejected. This means the fourth removal request was like trying to remove her again. Since the House of Representatives put these three requests on file on February 5th this year, no one can try to remove Sara again until February 6th next year.

Sara is a popular choice for the Philippine presidential election in 2028. If the removal case had passed in the Senate, she would have lost her position as Vice President and would not be able to hold public office again for the rest of her life.

According to a survey by the Philippine survey group "Social Weather Stations" (SWS) released on July 15th, 61% of people "trust Sara very much."
