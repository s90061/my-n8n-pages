Alishan Highway Motorcycle Accident: Rider Falls 50 Meters into Valley, Minor Injuries

On August 23rd, late at night, a serious motorcycle accident happened on the Alishan Highway in Chiayi County. A rider, Mr. Cheng, fell about 50 meters into a deep valley after his large motorcycle crashed. When he was rescued, he had many scratches and bruises on his body, but he was not in danger. The police are now looking into what caused the accident.

The Chiayi County Fire Department received a call at 10:49 PM on August 23rd about a car accident near the 35.8-kilometer mark of the Alishan Highway (Taiwan Route 18). When the ambulance arrived, they found it was a motorcycle accident. A male rider had fallen about 50 meters into a deep valley. Firefighters quickly set up ropes to help him get out. The injured man was awake and had injuries to his body. He was immediately taken to the hospital for treatment.

The Zhongpu Police Station of Chiayi County Police Department stated that this accident involved a 46-year-old man named Cheng. He was riding a yellow heavy motorcycle alone, going down the Alishan Highway. When he reached the 35.8-kilometer mark, he likely lost control and hit the concrete barrier on the outer side of the road. Mr. Cheng fell about 50 meters into a deep valley and was stuck. His motorcycle slid into a ditch on the other side of the road after the crash.

The police said that Mr. Cheng's alcohol test showed zero. They are still investigating the exact cause of the accident. There are many curves on the mountain roads of the Alishan Highway. Drivers are asked to slow down when driving to avoid accidents.

Students from New Southbound Countries Reach 82,000, A New High, Increasing Over 2 Times in 11 Years

The latest statistics from the Ministry of Education show that in the 2024-2025 school year, there are 123,000 international students in colleges and universities. Among these, about 82,000 students are from New Southbound Policy countries. This is the highest number ever, more than double the number from 11 years ago. For the past five years, Vietnam has had the most students.

The Ministry of Education recently released new statistics. These show that the number of international students in colleges and universities was 93,645 in the 2014-2015 school year. This number grew each year, reaching its highest point of 129,207 in the 2018-2019 school year. In the 2020-2021 school year, when COVID-19 started, the number dropped to 90,895. After that, it slowly went back up. In the 2024-2025 school year, it has reached 123,188 students.

For students from New Southbound Policy countries, the Ministry of Education's statistics show that there were 25,289 students in the 2014-2015 school year. This was 27% of all international students. The number grew each year, reaching 82,622 students in the 2024-2025 school year. This is a new record high and makes up 67% of all international students. The number of students from New Southbound Policy countries has increased by more than two times in 11 years.

Looking closer at students from New Southbound Policy countries, which include Vietnam, Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, the Philippines, and India, the Ministry of Education found something interesting. Before and including the 2019-2020 school year, most students from these countries were from Malaysia. But starting from the 2020-2021 school year, Vietnam became the country with the most students (17,534 people). Vietnam has stayed in the top spot since then, reaching 39,695 students in the 2024-2025 school year. This is more than double the number in five years.

The Ministry of Education explained that to attract students from New Southbound Policy countries to study in Taiwan, they have started several plans. These include the "New Southbound Industry-Academia Cooperation International Program," the "Plan to Increase Enrollment of Overseas Chinese, Hong Kong, Macao, and Foreign Students in Key Industries," and the "Plan to Promote International Students Coming to and Staying in Taiwan." These plans give schools more flexibility in admitting students to bring in more international students.

In addition, the Ministry of Education also provides many scholarships and grants. It also keeps improving support services and advice for international students. This helps make sure there are good support systems in place to make studying in Taiwan more attractive for talented students from New Southbound Policy countries. Every year, the Ministry also visits New Southbound Policy countries to join or hold higher education exhibitions and recruitment events. They share information about studying in Taiwan with local students and their parents.
