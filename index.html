US Stocks Mostly Fall as Investors Wait for Nvidia's Report

The main stock market indexes in the United States mostly closed lower today. This happened as investors watched closely for the financial report from Nvidia, a big company that makes chips for artificial intelligence. Nvidia will share its report later this week.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went down by 349.27 points, which is 0.77%. It closed at 45282.47 points.

The S&P 500 Index dropped a little, by 27.59 points, or 0.43%. It finished at 6439.32 points.

The Nasdaq Index also went down slightly, by 47.25 points, or 0.22%. It closed at 21449.29 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index, which tracks chip companies, went up a small amount, by 1.84 points, or 0.03%. It closed at 5756.30 points.

World Oil Prices Go Up After Federal Reserve Hints at Rate Cut

International oil prices kept going up today. This happened after Jerome Powell, the head of the U.S. Federal Reserve, suggested on August 22nd that they might lower interest rates as early as September. This news made crude oil futures prices rise. People in the market think that lower interest rates will help the U.S. economy grow and make people use more oil.

The price of West Texas Intermediate crude oil for October delivery, traded in New York, went up by 1.14 U.S. dollars. It reached 64.80 U.S. dollars per barrel.

The price of Brent crude oil for October delivery, traded in London, also increased by 1.07 U.S. dollars. It reached 68.8 U.S. dollars per barrel.

Trump Says He Might Visit China Soon, Threatens High Taxes on Chinese Goods

Today, U.S. President Trump met with South Korean President Lee Jae-myung at the White House. Trump said he might visit China this year or soon after. He also threatened to put a 200% tax on Chinese goods if China does not give rare earth magnets to the United States.

Bloomberg News reported that Trump said, "I think I will go to South Korea soon for trade talks." He also said he would be happy to go to the APEC summit in South Korea. The APEC leaders' meeting is planned for October 31st in Gyeongju, South Korea.

Trump also said that he might visit China this year or soon. He said, "We will have a good relationship with China." President Lee Jae-myung also said he wants to visit China with Trump.

Trump also said that Chinese students will be allowed to come to the U.S. He stated, "We will get along well with China." In May, U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio said that the U.S. would strongly cancel visas for Chinese students and make the checking process stronger.

Agence France-Presse reported that the U.S. and China had an agreement before to stop putting very high taxes on each other's goods. But Trump said today that if China does not keep its promises, the U.S. might raise taxes again.

CNN reported that Trump threatened to put a 200% tax on Chinese goods if China does not send more rare earth magnets to the U.S.

Trump said, "They must give us magnets. If they do not give magnets, then we will have to put about a 200% tax on them."

He also added, "But I think we will not have any problems."
