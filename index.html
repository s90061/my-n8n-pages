Southwest Wind Gets Stronger, Rain Spreads; West Taiwan to Watch for Heavy Rain from August 2 to 4

The weather agency said that today, the mountainous areas of Kaohsiung and Pingtung continue to have very heavy local rain. From tomorrow until August 4, as the southwest wind gets stronger, the rain will spread. West Taiwan should be ready for heavy rain or very heavy local rain. Tomorrow and on August 3, the mountainous areas south of Chiayi should be careful of very heavy local rain or even more.

The Central Weather Administration of the Ministry of Transportation continues to issue warnings for large or strong heavy rain. Weather forecaster Ms. Chu Mei-lin said in a press conference that today, central and southern Taiwan still had showers or thunderstorms on and off. The mountainous areas of Kaohsiung and Pingtung continue to have very heavy local rain. As of 2 PM, Pingtung's mountainous area had more than 370 millimeters of rain, and Kaohsiung's mountainous area also had over 200 millimeters. In particular, Sanhemen in Pingtung had over 200 millimeters of rain in 3 hours at noon, which is considered short-term very heavy rain.

Ms. Chu Mei-lin said that the southwest wind brings a lot of moisture, and strong air currents are forming. This causes a lot of rain in the mountainous areas of Kaohsiung and Pingtung. Today, the mountainous areas of Greater Taipei, Taoyuan, Hsinchu, and Yilan also had afternoon thunderstorms. Some short, strong rain showers also reached over 40 or 50 millimeters.

As the low-pressure system moves north, Ms. Chu Mei-lin pointed out that from tomorrow until August 4, the stronger southwest wind will bring more obvious rain to Taiwan.

Ms. Chu said that from tomorrow daytime to evening, the rain in central and southern Taiwan will cover a larger area. Northern, northeastern, and eastern Taiwan will also have rain on and off. Western Taiwan might have heavy local rain or very heavy rain.

Ms. Chu Mei-lin stated that on August 5, as the southwest wind becomes weaker, the total rainfall will slowly decrease. From August 6 to 8, the weather will mostly be cloudy to sunny.

In addition, the 13th tropical low-pressure system of this year formed this morning. Ms. Chu Mei-lin pointed out that it is expected to become a light typhoon in the future, but it will not directly affect Taiwan's weather. Its path will generally move towards the northeast.

Afternoon Thunderstorms: Songshan Airport Stops Ground Operations

Because of afternoon thunderstorms, the Civil Aeronautics Administration of the Ministry of Transportation said today that Songshan Airport stopped ground operations from 4:23 PM. Some flights might be late because of this. They remind travelers and people waiting to pick up passengers to pay attention.

The Central Weather Administration issued an immediate warning for strong thunderstorms this afternoon. The warning areas include Taipei City, New Taipei City, Taoyuan City, Hsinchu County, Kaohsiung City, Pingtung County, and Taitung County.

The Civil Aeronautics Administration said that due to the thunderstorms, Songshan Airport stopped ground operations from 4:23 PM. Some flights might be delayed because of this. They remind travelers and people picking up passengers to check for changes in flight information. If you have questions about your flight, please contact your airline.

The Civil Aeronautics Administration stated that airports with flights to and from Songshan Airport will also be affected. Flight take-off or arrival times will be delayed. They have asked relevant airports to immediately inform travelers inside the airport using notice boards, electronic displays, and announcements. They also provide the latest flight information on their official websites for the public to check. Travelers can use these resources more often.

Taichung Metro Promotes Reusing Lost Items, Donating 571 Useful Goods to Help Those in Need

The Taichung Metro Company recently looked through all the lost items that no one claimed. They chose 571 everyday items that were still in good condition and gave them to social welfare groups. They hope to help people in need and turn lost items into a kind of warmth for society.

The Taichung Metro Company announced today that in the first half of this year, they found almost 7,600 lost items. According to rules, if the person who found an item is a company employee, the company owns the item after the storage period ends. The company regularly checks and chooses items that can be used again. They clean and sort these items. They mainly give warm clothes, small daily items, and other useful things to social welfare groups.

The Taichung Metro Company said that they recently picked out 571 useful everyday items. They gave these items to "Taichung Peace Station" and "Fengyuan Peace Station," which are part of the Ren-an Social Welfare Foundation. The items included warm coats, woolen hats, thermos bottles, helmets, stationery, and personal items. All of these items are very practical for daily life. The Ren-an Social Welfare Foundation said that these items are not just real help, but also show that society cares about homeless people and families in urgent need. This makes the donation very special.

The Taichung Metro Company said they will continue to support the plan to reuse lost items for charity. They plan to hold two donation events regularly each year. They will also ask the Taichung City Government's Social Affairs Bureau to suggest social welfare groups to work with. This will make the best use of resources and directly help homeless people and other people in need. It will turn lost items into a different kind of kindness for society.
