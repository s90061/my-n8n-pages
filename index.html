Hsinchu City Party Leader Defends Ko Chien-ming; Lai Ching-te Says "No Scapegoats"

After a big recall election failed, some members of the Democratic Progressive Party (DPP) criticized Ko Chien-ming, who leads the party's group in the Legislative Yuan. Today, Shih Nai-ju, the head of the Hsinchu City DPP branch, spoke at a meeting for local party leaders. She said that party supporters want everyone to work together and not look for someone to blame. President Lai Ching-te, who is also the DPP chairman, said they are definitely not looking for a scapegoat. He said, "If you want a scapegoat, find me. I will take the responsibility." He added that he respects the party group's own decision on whether to choose new leaders and will not get too involved.

After the big recall election did not succeed, some party members criticized Ko Chien-ming. There were also calls within the party to choose new leaders for the party's group in the Legislative Yuan.

The DPP held a meeting for local party leaders today. Those at the meeting said that during the last part, Shih Nai-ju, the head of the Hsinchu City party branch, spoke to defend Ko Chien-ming. She mentioned that local supporters hope the DPP will unite after August 23rd and not look for someone to blame.

People at the meeting also said that Lai Ching-te stated the recall election results were not good enough. He said they are not looking for anyone to blame, and he will take all the responsibility. He said, "I am the scapegoat. If you want a scapegoat, find me. I will take the responsibility."

The local party leaders at the meeting also shared that Lai Ching-te clearly took responsibility. To deal with future situations, the teams in the Presidential Office, the Executive Yuan, and the party have made changes. Regarding the leaders of the party group, he said they will respect the group's own decisions on how to handle it.

Another person at the meeting said that Lai Ching-te said at the end of the meeting that the party group should make its own decisions. He said he could not say too much and that the party group should change its structure by itself. He added that it's not up to him to decide, "It's not a time of authoritarian rule."

Hotai Motor's August Sales Reach Record High for the Period; Aims for 36.7% New Car Market Share This Year

Car companies are slowly announcing their sales for August. Hotai Motor's total sales for August were NT$23.863 billion. This is 15.09% more than last year and the highest ever for this month. For the first eight months of this year, Hotai Motor's sales were NT$186.956 billion, which is 0.48% higher than the same time in 2024.

Hotai Motor said before that its Toyota and Lexus brands sold a total of 11,914 vehicles in August. This is the highest number for this month in history. They had a 40.4% share of the new car market in Taiwan, which is the highest for this month in almost 15 years.

Looking ahead to September, Hotai Motor expects to sell 10,800 Toyota and Lexus cars. This would be about 36% of the estimated 30,000 new cars expected to be sold in Taiwan that month.

Regarding Taiwan's new car market this year, Hotai Motor previously said that the market changes are not very clear. The main thing they cannot control this year is still the tariffs from the United States. The situation of the car market this year will become clearer after policies like tariffs are decided. Uncertain policies might make some customers less willing to buy cars. However, Hotai Motor's car delivery process has become more stable.

Looking at their sales goals for this year, Hotai Motor said they have not changed. Their goal for total new car sales, including passenger cars, light commercial vehicles, and commercial vehicles, is still 165,000 units. They continue to aim for a market share of 36.7% this year. For Toyota cars, they expect to sell 130,000 units, aiming for a 29% market share. For Lexus, they estimate 28,500 units and a 6.3% market share. For Hino, they estimate 6,350 units and a 1.4% market share.

Wang Yu-jie attended an event for beauty products today.
