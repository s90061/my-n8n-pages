Chinese Man Arrested in Guatemala for International Money Laundering, Will Be Sent to US for Trial

Guatemala City, July 18 (CNA) â€“ Police and prosecutors in Guatemala recently arrested a Chinese man suspected of money laundering. They are now working to send him to the United States as requested by the US. The US justice department says this man received money from drug sales and moved it around the world to send it back to drug groups in Mexico.

On July 17, Guatemala's Ministry of Justice announced that, working with the national police, they successfully arrested a Chinese man named Wenshen Xu. They also found that he used other names like Alex, Alex Xiu, Alex Tsui, and Chino.

Mr. Xu is now held in Guatemala. The United States has officially asked to send him to the US Eastern District Federal Court in Virginia for trial. In the US, Mr. Xu is accused of three crimes: planning money laundering, hiding money from crimes, and helping to hide illegal money flow.

US investigations show that Mr. Xu was part of a group that laundered money across China, the US, Mexico, and Colombia. This group helped drug cartels "wash" money they made from selling cocaine. His job was to collect cash from drug sales in the US. Then, he used different ways to make the money seem "legal." Finally, he sent the money back to bank accounts controlled by Mexican drug cartels.

Reports from the US Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA) and the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) have shown that Mexican drug cartels are now using Chinese underground banking systems, cryptocurrency trading, and fake legal businesses more and more to avoid financial rules in different countries.

Guatemala's Ministry of Justice strongly stated again its promise to fight crimes that cross borders, especially money laundering and drug dealing. They also said they will keep working with other countries through international legal help to fight crime.

Right now, Mr. Xu's case for being sent to the US is being looked at by the court. After the court decides, he will be given to the US to be tried there, according to the law. The police and prosecutors in Guatemala have kept the details secret and have not said exactly where he was arrested or where he is being held now.

In recent years, the United States and countries in Central and South America have been working more closely to fight crimes that cross borders. Since drug smuggling and money laundering often involve many countries' laws, how to improve cooperation across borders and react quickly has become a challenge for police and investigative groups in different countries.

Guatemalan media said that more details about how Mr. Xu laundered money, using methods like Chinese underground banks, will be found out when the US court hears his case. But whether these international drug money laundering groups are getting bigger and reaching into other Latin American countries is also something that law enforcement in different countries should pay attention to.
