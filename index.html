Taiwan Men's Basketball Loses by 2 Points to Poland at World University Games, Misses Best Team Record

Today, Taiwan's men's basketball team played a 9th to 12th place game at the Rhine-Ruhr World University Games in Germany. Hsu Te-chi scored a team-high 17 points and had 5 assists. However, Taiwan still lost to Poland 87 to 89. This means they will not break their best team record.

At the last World University Games in Chengdu, China, Taiwan's men's basketball team achieved their best record by finishing 11th. This time, the team is still led by You Ai-che, a player who plays abroad. Other players include Lin Hsin-kuan and Chen Fan-po-yen, who play professional basketball in Taiwan. But because they only won one game in the group stage, they could not make it to the top 8. Luckily, after beating India by a large margin yesterday, the Taiwan team moved on to the 9th to 12th place games.

Taiwan's men's basketball team played against Poland today. They aimed to move on to the 9th or 10th place game and set a new best record for the team. They started the first quarter well. But in the second quarter, their shooting percentage went down, and Taiwan's team went into halftime trailing by 2 points.

After halftime, the game was still very close. Taiwan's team kept the score tight. At the start of the fourth quarter, they made a 9-3 run and took the lead again. Even though Poland then caught up, Li Yun-chieh and Sung Hsin-hao made shots from outside right before the end of the game. This helped Taiwan's team tie the score and gave them a chance to go into overtime.

However, with two points down, Hsu Te-chi took the last shot but missed. So, Taiwan's men's basketball team lost closely to Poland. This means they missed setting their best team record. Now, they will play Latvia for 11th place. If they lose that game, they will finish 12th in this World University Games.

In this game, five players from Taiwan's men's basketball team scored double digits. Hsu Te-chi scored the most with 17 points, also adding 5 assists and 2 steals. Shooter Li Yun-chieh played for less than 12 minutes but scored 14 points efficiently, including four three-pointers. Team captain You Ai-che showed his all-around skills, getting 10 points, 6 rebounds, 4 assists, and 3 steals. (Edited by Chen Yen-chun) 1140724
