NASA's First Trip Around the Moon with People is on Track, Planned for April 2026

(Central News Agency, Washington, September 23, comprehensive foreign news report) The U.S. National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) said today that "Artemis 2," the first mission with people in the "Artemis program," is making good progress. It is planned to launch in April 2026, and it might even launch earlier, in February 2026.

The "Artemis program" is NASA's main plan to go back to the Moon. This space exploration program costs billions of dollars and is competing with a similar Moon landing plan by China. China wants to land people on the Moon by 2030.

Lakiesha Hawkins, a senior NASA official, said today in a meeting, "We truly plan to keep this promise." She added that if the rocket and spaceship are ready earlier, an earlier launch is possible. But the most important rule is always mission safety.

The "Artemis 2" mission will last 10 days. Four astronauts, including three Americans and one Canadian, will fly in a spaceship around the Moon and then come back to Earth.

This mission will not land on the Moon. That is the goal of "Artemis 3."

U.S. President Trump continues to push NASA to make space programs faster during his second term.

This Republican leader started the Artemis program in his first term. He wants NASA to go back to the Moon quickly and then go to Mars.

Li Chenggang Meets with U.S. Midwest Business and Government Group, Focuses on U.S.-China Trade Talks

(Central News Agency, Taipei, September 24) China's Ministry of Commerce said on September 23 that Li Chenggang, China's international trade negotiator and vice minister, met with a group of business and government leaders from the U.S. Midwest on September 22. They talked about trade between China and the U.S., and how different states and provinces can work together on trade.

The Chinese Ministry of Commerce announced this on its official website on September 23, but it did not give many details.

Reuters reported that experts believe food exports from the Midwest will be very important in any trade agreement between the U.S. and China.

The report also said that China is the world's biggest buyer of soybeans. However, China has not yet bought any soybeans from the U.S. fall harvest. Traders say most of these soybeans come from the U.S. Midwest.

Trade experts expect that U.S. President Donald Trump wants China to promise to buy more American farm products and things like Boeing airplanes. This would be part of an agreement to stop the tax war between the world's two biggest economies.

Leaders from Taiwan's Diplomatic Allies Go to "Taiwan Friendship Night" to Make Friends Stronger During UN General Assembly

(Central News Agency, New York, September 23) The Taipei Economic and Cultural Office in New York held "Taiwan Friendship Night" on the first day of the UN General Assembly's main debates. Many leaders from Taiwan's friendly countries and members of the UN General Assembly groups came. Yu Da-tsai, Taiwan's representative to the U.S., said that Taiwan is like a light of democracy in the Indo-Pacific area and is important for international travel by sea and air. He also said Taiwan will keep making its friendships with friendly countries and countries with similar ideas stronger.

The "Taiwan Friendship Night" party was held in the lobby of the Taipei Economic and Cultural Office in New York. This was on the first day of the 80th United Nations General Assembly's main debates. More than one hundred people attended. These included Yu Da-tsai, Taiwan's representative to the U.S.; Li Zhi-qiang, head of the New York office; Li Yu-jie, an advisor from the National Security Council; Hilda Heine, President of the Marshall Islands; Ralph Gonsalves, Prime Minister of Saint Vincent and the Grenadines; and many foreign ministers from friendly countries, as well as officials from the UN General Assembly groups and UN representative groups.

Yu Da-tsai spoke and thanked friendly countries and countries with similar ideas. He thanked them for continuing to help Taiwan join the United Nations and its special groups and other international teamwork platforms in a meaningful way.

He said that Taiwan is a democratic light in the Indo-Pacific area. It is also a very important place for international shipping and air travel. Taiwan will keep working to make its important friendships with friendly countries and countries with similar ideas stronger. They will work together to face future problems and create a good future with democracy, peace, and long-lasting wealth.

President Hilda Heine of the Marshall Islands said again that her government and people strongly believe in the friendship between their two countries. She praised Taiwan for continuing to help with important things like health, climate change, and reaching goals for a sustainable future faster. She said that letting Taiwan take part in a practical way helps the UN achieve its goal of human rights for everyone and its idea of "leaving no one behind."

Also, many other officials led groups to the event. These included Francis Fonseca, Foreign Minister of Belize; Pholile Shakantu, Foreign Minister of Eswatini; Denzil Douglas, Foreign Minister of Saint Kitts and Nevis; Alva Baptiste, Foreign Minister of Saint Lucia; Maina Talia, Climate Minister of Tuvalu; and Blesoch Aderkeroi, a member of Parliament from Palau. They came to show their strong support for Taiwan to be part of the United Nations system.
