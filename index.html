Russia's Drone Attacks on Ukraine Fell in August During US Peace Talks

Kyiv, September 1st (CNA) â€“ In August, Russia used far fewer long-range drones to attack Ukraine. This is according to a report from Agence France-Presse (AFP), based on information from Ukraine's air force. This happened when many important talks were held to try and stop the war between Russia and Ukraine, but these talks did not succeed.

AFP reported that the information released today shows Russia sent 4132 long-range drones in night attacks on Ukraine in August. This is 34% less than in July, which was the highest number of attacks in one month ever.

In August, US President Donald Trump met with Russian President Vladimir Putin and then with Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy in the United States. However, these meetings did not lead to any big steps towards peace.

AFP's report only used official daily updates from the Ukrainian air force each morning. It does not include every single Russian attack.

Even though Russia attacked Ukraine less with drones in August, dozens of ordinary people still died.

For example, on August 28th, Russian forces launched one of their worst drone and missile attacks on Ukraine's capital, Kyiv. This attack killed 25 people, mostly people living in an apartment building.

Since Moscow started a full war against Ukraine in February 2022, which caused the deadliest conflict in Europe since World War II, drone or missile attacks have happened almost every night in Ukraine.

Since May of this year, Russia has even sent long-range drones to attack Ukraine every night without stopping for even one day.
