Premier Zhuo Rongtai: Military Salaries Raised 4 Times Since 2018, Government Continues to Support Army

Today is September 3rd, Military Day. Premier Zhuo Rongtai said on Facebook that since 2018, the salaries for the military have been increased four times. Each time, salaries went up by 3% to 4%. Last year, the government also increased special payments for soldiers in Dongyin and Juguang. This year, special payments for volunteer soldiers, combat units, and those working in cyber warfare, electronic sensing, and air traffic control were also raised. The government will continue to give strong support to the military.

Premier Zhuo Rongtai stated that he wants to thank all soldiers who protect Taiwan on land, at sea, and in the air. He also thanks those who help in disasters and do their jobs every day. He said that what the soldiers do for the country and society deserves respect and support from everyone in Taiwan.

To show support for the military, the government encouraged many businesses to offer special deals. 37 groups and 62 brands, with more than 20,000 stores (like shops, restaurants, and places for fun), are giving discounts to active soldiers and their families during Military Day. This is the biggest number of businesses ever to join this event.

Premier Zhuo Rongtai said that soldiers protect the country all the time, day and night. The country also takes care of its soldiers every day, not just on Military Day. He repeated that since 2018, military salaries have gone up four times, each time by 3% to 4%. Also, the government continues to build better living spaces and improve equipment for the soldiers.

Besides general salary increases, Premier Zhuo Rongtai mentioned that in July last year, the government raised special payments for soldiers in Dongyin and Juguang. In April this year, special payments for volunteer soldiers and combat units were increased. In June and July this year, extra payments were added for those in cyber warfare, electronic sensing, and air traffic control.

He said that giving more support and care to the military, and being their strong backing, is the government's true way of saying thank you to the soldiers.

Thailand's People's Party Supports Bhumjaithai Party to Form New Government, Hopes to End Political Problems

Bangkok, September 3rd â€” The People's Party, which is the biggest political party in Thailand's parliament, said today that it will support the Bhumjaithai Party to form the next government. This news brings hope that a long political problem, which started after the prime minister was removed, might end.

News agencies Reuters and AFP reported that the People's Party, an opposition party, decided to support the Bhumjaithai Party instead of the ruling Pheu Thai Party. This could change the situation a lot, because the People's Party has almost one-third of all the seats in the House of Representatives.

Natthaphong Ruengpanyawut, the leader of the People's Party, said his party decided to support Anutin Charnvirakul, the leader of the Bhumjaithai Party, to become prime minister. They did this to stop a group of old parties that are not good to rule again from coming back into power.

However, Natthaphong said their support has a condition: the parliament must be closed and new elections must be held within four months. This is "to give power back to the people as soon as possible."

He said, "It is possible that the old group of parties that could not govern well for the past two years might return. It is also possible that the person who led the coup might become prime minister again." He was talking about General Prayuth Chan-ocha. General Prayuth took power in 2014, and even though he is retired, he can still be prime minister.

Last week, the ruling Pheu Thai Party lost its prime minister, Paetongtarn Shinawatra, because a court decided she broke ethical rules. Last night, the Pheu Thai Party sent mixed messages. They said they were ready to go against their rival, the Bhumjaithai Party, in the election for the new prime minister, but they might also ask for new elections.

Paetongtarn was removed from her job on August 29th for breaking ethical rules. This started a fight for power in Thai politics. Her party, Pheu Thai, which used to be very strong, is now trying hard to keep its weak alliance together. Their old friends, the Bhumjaithai Party, have also made a strong move to try and form their own government.

Taiwan Railways Sells Over 129,000 Tickets for Mid-Autumn Festival Holiday; Some Taipei-Hualien Express Train Seats Still Available

Taipei, September 3rd â€” Taiwan Railways Corporation announced today that by 10 AM, they had sold over 129,593 tickets for the Mid-Autumn Festival holiday. The booking opened at midnight. There are still some seats left on long-distance Tze-Chiang Limited Express trains going from Taipei to Hualien and Taitung.

The Mid-Autumn Festival holiday this year is 3 days long. Taiwan Railways will add a total of 107 extra trains across all lines for 5 days, from October 3rd to October 7th. People could start booking tickets from midnight today through the internet, the "Taiwan Railways e-booking" app, phone calls, and convenience stores.

Taiwan Railways released a statement saying that by 10 AM today, 80,603 bookings were made, and 129,593 tickets were sold. The booking process went smoothly. Taiwan Railways reminds travelers that after booking, they must pick up their tickets within 2 days (including the booking day) at any Taiwan Railways station, post office, or convenience store (7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Hi-Life, OK Mart). They can also pay for tickets using credit cards online or through the "Taiwan Railways e-booking" app. If tickets are not picked up by the deadline, they will be sold again starting at 9 AM on September 5th.

Taiwan Railways also gave an update on ticket availability for the Mid-Autumn Festival holiday, as of 9 AM. For the Eastern Line, going from Taipei to Hualien and Taitung, there are still a few seats available on long-distance Tze-Chiang Limited Express trains for trips between 6 PM on October 3rd and 1 PM on October 4th. Other times still have many seats. For trips from Taitung and Hualien to Taipei, there are still seats available on long-distance Tze-Chiang Limited Express trains after 7 PM on October 6th.

For the Western Line, Taiwan Railways said that for trips from Taipei to Kaohsiung, there are still a few seats available on long-distance Tze-Chiang Limited Express trains for trips between 6 PM on October 3rd and 11 AM on October 4th. Other times still have many seats. For trips from Kaohsiung to Taipei, there are still seats available on long-distance Tze-Chiang Limited Express trains after 7 PM on October 6th.

Regarding tickets for the Southern Link Line, Taiwan Railways noted that for trips from Kaohsiung to Taitung, all times between October 3rd and October 4th still have seats on long-distance Tze-Chiang Limited Express trains. For trips from Taitung to Kaohsiung, all times on October 6th also have seats on long-distance Tze-Chiang Limited Express trains.

Additionally, Taiwan Railways will add special trains on October 3rd, 4th, and 6th where passengers must use their real names to book tickets. These tickets will be available for booking starting at midnight on September 4th. Only people who live in Hualien County or Taitung County, people whose ID numbers start with U or V, and the spouses and direct family members of these people can book these tickets.
