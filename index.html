TSG Hawks Sign New American Pitcher Called Namasia

The TSG Hawks baseball team announced today that they have signed a new foreign pitcher, Dinelson Lamet, who has played in Major League Baseball (MLB) more than 100 times. He has played for several MLB teams, like the Padres, Rockies, Red Sox, Dodgers, and Royals. Last year, he was a teammate with Japanese star Shohei Ohtani on the Dodgers team.

Namasia has played a total of 121 games in MLB during his career. This includes 60 games where he started. His record is 17 wins and 24 losses. He pitched a total of 367.2 innings, and he struck out 474 batters. His ERA (earned run average) is 4.63.

Ministry of Transportation Helps People Repair Homes by Connecting Them with Companies

Typhoon Danas and southwest air currents have affected the southern part of Taiwan. Some people's homes were badly damaged. The Ministry of Transportation said today that there is a shortage of workers and materials right now. So, they have asked different departments to help connect people with companies. They hope to help residents find workers to fix their homes.

The Ministry of Transportation posted on Facebook today. They said that Vice Minister Chen Yan-bo held a meeting yesterday with different departments. They checked the progress of repairs in the Tainan disaster area and planned what to do next. So far, they have visited over 100 damaged homes. They are also looking for more workers and resources to help people rebuild their homes quickly.

The Ministry of Transportation said that there are not enough workers or materials at the moment. People cannot find workers to repair their homes, and there are no people or machines available. The government has asked all ministries to help and has given them specific areas. The Ministry of Transportation is in charge of Xinying District, Yanshui District, Baihe District, and Houbi District in Tainan City.

The Ministry of Transportation said that people who want to repair their homes can register with their district chief first. Then, the Ministry of Transportation will help find companies for them. After that, residents will talk with the companies. Once they agree, they will sign a simple contract. The Ministry of Transportation's main job is to connect people with companies; they do not pay for the repairs.

To make the rebuilding after the disaster faster, the Ministry of Transportation also set some rules. Besides connecting people with companies, they will first fix old, one-story houses and homes with roofs blown off that people cannot live in. These will get help right away. Waste materials like asbestos tiles will be collected in one place. The district office will then help to remove them.

Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba Offers Sympathy for Floods in Central and Southern Taiwan; Lin Chia-lung Thanks Him and Hopes for Future Cooperation

Heavy rains recently caused damage in Taiwan. Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba sent a message to Taiwan to show his sympathy. Today, Taiwan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs said that Foreign Minister Lin Chia-lung thanked Japan again for its care. He also hopes that Taiwan and Japan can work together to face future problems. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Taiwan's office in Japan have both thanked the Japanese government.

Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba wrote a message to show his sympathy for Taiwan. He said he was very sorry for those who died and their families. He also expressed his true sympathy to those who were affected by the disaster. He prayed that people would return to normal life soon, saying, "Go, Taiwan!" The Japan-Taiwan Exchange Association shared his message on Facebook in the afternoon.

In response, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs released a press statement this afternoon. It said that the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Taiwan's office in Japan had already thanked the Japanese government sincerely right away.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs also said that Shigeru Ishiba had also sent a message on July 12th to show sympathy for Taiwan after Typhoon Danas. This shows the deep and true friendship between Taiwan and Japan. The Taiwanese government and many parts of society are still working hard to help with disaster relief and rebuilding. They are helping affected people rebuild their homes and get back to their normal lives quickly.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs stated that Foreign Minister Lin Chia-lung again thanked the Japanese government for its warm care and true support. He also hopes that Taiwan and Japan will continue to work more closely in related areas in the future. They want to build a stronger, friendlier, and more lasting partnership to face different challenges together.
