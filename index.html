Some news in Taiwan said that trade talks between Taiwan and Canada's former Prime Minister Trudeau's government were delayed by Canada. The Vice President said that Canada is a very important trade partner for Taiwan. The economies of both countries help each other in many ways. Of course, she hopes they can keep talking about trade. These talks have been very good over the years, and she wants them to continue.

When asked if there were delays, she said that trade talks are always very complex. She hopes and expects that these discussions will continue.

The Vice President said that Canada has always been an important supplier of farm products to Taiwan. Taiwan, in return, makes important parts for technology that help Canadian factories. She hopes they can keep their strong and steady trade relationship. She thinks it would be very helpful if they could make better laws to support their trade partnership.

The Vice President also hopes Canada will support Taiwan in joining the "Comprehensive and Progressive Agreement for Trans-Pacific Partnership" (CPTPP). Taiwan continues to ask for fair treatment in international groups and organizations. It is very important for Taiwan to be a part of the international trade system that follows rules. She truly hopes Canada will give its support.

Regarding safety, she thanked Canada very much. Canada has often sailed ships near the Taiwan Strait to show freedom of navigation, following its Indo-Pacific strategy. Canada has also been a leader in the Group of Seven (G7) countries. With other G7 members, it has often said that peace and stability in the Taiwan Strait are very important for global wealth. Peace and stability in the Taiwan Strait are a necessary part of global wealth. This is very important for Taiwan, whether it's about leading in diplomacy or as a country in the Pacific that cares deeply about peace and stability in the Taiwan Strait.

The Vice President especially thanked Canada and Taiwan for recently signing a special agreement called the "Memorandum of Understanding on Dark Vessel Detection System." This helps Taiwan know more about what is happening in its seas. Canada's technology not only helps with rules for fishing around the world but also helps with other issues related to understanding the sea. The cooperation between the two countries will make sure that everyone involved in the area respects the rules and standards that keep the world stable. This is very meaningful.
