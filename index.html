Boy Rescued in Mountain Accident; Grandfather and Female Hiker Pass Away

Today, a mountain accident happened in Kalakolakan Mountain, Xinyi Township, Nantou County. A 70-year-old man and a 63-year-old woman mountain climber were rescued and brought down the mountain, but they showed no signs of life. An 8-year-old boy, who was with them, had signs of hypothermia, but his condition was stable. He is now in the hospital for treatment and observation.

At noon today, the Nantou County Fire Department received a call. The 8-year-old boy, his grandfather, and another female hiker started their climb on August 8th from Hualien County. They planned to hike the Southern Three Peaks and come down on August 17th from Dongpu, Xinyi Township, Nantou County. During their trip, they faced a typhoon and were exposed to cold for a long time. It seemed they became too weak. The Fire Department believed the hikers were in danger and the place was far away, so they immediately asked for a helicopter to help.

The National Airborne Service Corps sent a helicopter, and it arrived at Kalakolakan Mountain in the afternoon. The helicopter landed to help the old man, the female hiker, and the boy get down the mountain. After that, they were taken to the hospital by ambulance. However, the old man and the female hiker showed no signs of life before reaching the hospital and sadly passed away despite rescue efforts. The boy had signs of hypothermia, but his condition was stable. The details of what happened still need to be checked.

The Fire Department asks people to be very careful because mountain weather can change quickly. It is important to prepare well before going. They suggest that you should think about your own ability and not try to climb routes that are too difficult for you. Also, carry a satellite communication device. This can help you quickly share your location in an emergency, which saves important rescue time.
