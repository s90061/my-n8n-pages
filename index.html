Macau Badminton Open: Lee Fang-jen and Lee Fang-chih enter the Men's Doubles Round of 16

The BWF Super 300 Macau Badminton Open started today. Taiwan's twin brothers, Lee Fang-jen and Lee Fang-chih, won their first match in the men's doubles. They beat the Indonesian team, Raymond Indra and Nikolaus Joaquin, with scores of 21-13 and 21-14. This means they are now in the round of 16.

Lee Fang-jen and Lee Fang-chih won the men's doubles title at the Super 300 Canada Open in early July this year. They are currently ranked 20th in the world. This was their first time playing against Raymond Indra and Nikolaus Joaquin, who are ranked 103rd in the world.

Even though it was their first match against each other, Lee Fang-jen and Lee Fang-chih started to play better after the score was 7-7 in the first game. After the technical break at 11-10, they scored 6 points to 1. This helped them win the first game.

In the second game, Lee Fang-jen and Lee Fang-chih understood their opponents' playing style better. After the score was 3-3, they played very well. They used their strong flat drives to get ahead. When the score was 9-8, they scored 9 points to 1. They won the match in two straight games.

In the men's doubles at the Macau Badminton Open, there are four teams from Taiwan. Besides the Lee brothers, another Taiwanese team, Ho Chih-wei and Huang Ruei-shiuan, also won their match in two straight games and moved to the round of 16. It is interesting that these two Taiwanese teams will play against each other in the round of 16.

Heavy Rain Causes 8 Pingtung Townships to Close Work and School on July 30th

According to the Central Weather Administration, areas in the mountains of Pingtung County have received a lot of rain because of a strong southwestern air current. The amount of rain has reached the level where work and school must stop. The Pingtung County Government announced tonight that 8 townships, including Wutai Township, will close work and school tomorrow. Other townships will have normal work and school hours.

The Pingtung County Government said that the 8 townships closing on July 30th are Wutai, Sandimen, Majia, Laiyi, Taiwu, Chunri, Shizi, and Mudan.

Philippine Officials Say South China Sea Stance is Unchanged, Despite President Marcos' Speech

Yesterday, Philippine President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. gave his speech about the state of the nation. Some people thought he seemed softer towards China. But today, the Philippine President's office and the military said that the Philippines' position on the South China Sea has not changed. They will continue to protect the country's power and rights.

President Marcos Jr. gave his fourth state of the nation speech in the House of Representatives yesterday. He did not talk about the South China Sea dispute. He repeated that the Philippines wants to be "friends with everyone and enemies with no one." He also said that the country will "be careful and patient" when protecting its land and interests.

In his speech, which was about 1 hour and 10 minutes long, President Marcos did not clearly mention the disagreement between the Philippines and China in the South China Sea. Some people thought this meant he was becoming softer towards China. They were worried he might give in on the South China Sea issue.

Today, Lucas Bersamin, the Head of the President's Office, was asked by Philippine media about this. He said that the Philippine government's position on the "West Philippine Sea" has not changed and they are not hesitant.

Bersamin said that the Philippines is still friendly with China, but China must be friendly back. He added that if China keeps violating the Philippines' power, President Marcos will "certainly not welcome it."

President Marcos first talked about being friendly and not making enemies in his state of the nation speech in July 2022. But he has also often said in public speeches that the country will not give up "even an inch" of its land.

Also, military spokesperson Francel Margareth Padilla said today in a press conference that the military will "as always" protect the country's power. She also said that President Marcos's instructions on this are very clear.

One week before his state of the nation speech, President Marcos visited the United States. At a press conference there, Trump said that the U.S. does not care if the Philippines is friends with China. He said that the Philippines has its own foreign policy and can do anything to make itself great again.

The Philippines uses the United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS) to say that it has the right to use the resources in a special economic zone that extends 200 nautical miles into the South China Sea. They call this area the "West Philippine Sea."

China, however, claims most of the South China Sea using a "ten-dash line" and historical reasons. This area overlaps with the area the Philippines claims. For many years, the two countries have had conflicts in this disputed sea. The Philippine Navy, Coast Guard, and Bureau of Fisheries have often said that Chinese ships have hit their boats and used strong water cannons to stop Philippine fishing and scientific work.
