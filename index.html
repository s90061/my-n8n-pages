Israel Airstrikes on Qatar Condemned by Western Countries, Canada to Re-evaluate Relationship

Yesterday, Israel attacked Doha, the capital city of Qatar, with airstrikes. These attacks were aimed at leaders of Hamas. Many Western countries, like the United Kingdom, France, Germany, and the European Union, said this was wrong. Today, Canada announced that it will look again at its relationship with Israel.

According to Reuters, Anita Anand, Canada's Foreign Minister, spoke to reporters. She was at a meeting for the Liberal Party, which is the party in power.

Ms. Anand told reporters, "We are thinking about our relationship with Israel." She also said again that Canada believes Israel's attacks are not acceptable. This is especially true because Qatar is trying to help bring peace to the Middle East.

When asked if Canada would put any kind of punishment on Israel, Ms. Anand answered, "We will keep thinking about what we should do next."

Today, Ursula von der Leyen, the head of the European Commission, said there will be new actions to help Ukraine against Russia's war. She also asked for limits on trade and punishments for Israel because of the war in Gaza.

Regarding Israel's attack, Keir Starmer, the Prime Minister of the UK, said the attack went against Qatar's independence. He also said it could make the situation in the region worse.

Emmanuel Macron, the President of France, clearly stated that no matter why Israel did it, such actions are "not acceptable."

Johann Wadephul, the Foreign Minister of Germany, said in a statement, "Israel's attack on Doha not only went against Qatar's independence but also put at risk all our efforts to free the hostages. This attack is not acceptable."

NASA Bans Chinese Citizens from Space Programs; US-China Space Competition Rises

Bloomberg reported that people who know about the situation said the US space agency, NASA, has stopped Chinese citizens with US visas from taking part in its projects.

NASA has always had some rules about hiring Chinese citizens. However, in the past, Chinese citizens who had US visas could still work with NASA. They could be outside workers, students studying for advanced degrees, or scientists from universities.

But since September 5, Chinese citizens who were working at NASA have not been allowed to get into the agency's computer systems for information. They also cannot join meetings, either in person or online, that are connected to their work, according to people who know about it.

Bethany Stevens, a spokesperson for NASA, confirmed these actions by the agency. Ms. Stevens said, "NASA has taken steps inside the agency concerning Chinese citizens. This includes limiting their access to our buildings, information, and computer networks to keep our work safe."

Both China and the United States have plans to send people to the moon within the next five years.

Finland Beats Georgia 93-79, Reaches EuroBasket Semifinals for First Time

Finland surprised many by beating Serbia before. Today, they beat Georgia 93 to 79. This means Finland has reached the semifinals of the European Basketball Championship for the first time in its history.

Earlier, in the Round of 16 at the EuroBasket tournament, Finland won against Serbia by 92 to 86. This was a big surprise because Serbia was expected to win the championship. This win shocked everyone in international basketball. Serbia, which was a favorite to win, has now lost in the Round of 16 for the second time in a row.

In today's game, Finland and Georgia played very closely for the first six minutes. But then, Finland started scoring many points quickly at the end of the first quarter. The score changed from 12-12 to 28-15. Finland was leading by 13 points at the end of the first quarter.

In the second quarter, Georgia managed to get closer, reducing Finland's lead to only nine points. However, Finland continued to play very strongly and made their lead even bigger. By halftime, the score was 57-40, with Finland far ahead.

Finland was leading by as many as 20 points in the third quarter. They went into the fourth quarter with a score of 71-62. In the last quarter, Georgia tried hard to catch up. They got to within eight points of Finland's score with about eight minutes left. But Finland scored more points at the end, and they finally beat Georgia 93 to 79.

Mikael Jantunen, who plays power forward for Finland, was the best player for his team. He scored 19 points and got 5 rebounds. He made three three-point shots in the first half, which helped his team get an early lead. His teammate, Lauri Markkanen, also played very well. He scored 17 points, got 6 rebounds, and blocked 4 shots.

Finland will play in the semifinals on September 12. They will play against the team that wins the game between Germany and Slovenia.
