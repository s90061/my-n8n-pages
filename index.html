Taiwanese-American Player Fairchild Moves to Rays, Continues Major League Opportunity

Jaylin Fairchild, a Taiwanese-American baseball player who plays in the outfield, had lost his place on the Braves team. After being removed from the Major League 40-man roster, the Tampa Bay Rays signed him today. This gives him another chance to play in Major League Baseball.

Fairchild is 29 years old. He has played in Major League Baseball for five seasons and is one of the players who might play for the Taiwan team in the 2026 World Baseball Classic (WBC). He is very good at defense and very fast. When he was interviewed this spring, he said he would like to play for the Taiwan team.

This year, Fairchild played for the Braves as a backup outfielder. He was trying to keep his place in Major League Baseball. Yesterday, the Braves announced they had assigned him for assignment (DFA). This removed him from their Major League 40-man roster. His career now means he could be sent to the minor leagues or move to another team.

Today, the Tampa Bay Rays announced they had signed Fairchild in a cash trade. He will wear jersey number 20. Today, the Rays played against the Chicago White Sox at home. Fairchild was on the active player list but did not start the game.

Fairchild did not get many chances to play for the Braves this year. At the end of May, he hurt his right little finger while sliding, and he missed more than two weeks of games. This season, he played in 28 games. He had 51 at-bats and got 11 hits. His batting average was .216, and he did not hit any home runs.

US Government Considers Punishments for Mexican Airlines Due to Flight Limits

The US government is not happy because the Mexican government has put unfair limits on US cargo airlines since 2022. These limits include where planes can land and take off. The US government recently said that if Mexico does not fix this quickly, they will add more punishments for Mexican airlines that fly to the US.

The US Department of Transportation said that the Mexican government, under former President Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador, ordered US cargo airlines to move their main operations from Mexico City International Airport (AICM) to Felipe Angeles International Airport. This new airport is outside the city center. The Mexican government also canceled some landing and takeoff times. The US believes this makes it unfair for airlines to compete in the market.

The Mexican newspaper "EL FINANCIERO" reported today, July 22, that US Secretary of Transportation Sean Duffy said Mexico "broke its promise, changed the market unfairly, and hurt American companies." He added that the US will not accept this kind of behavior from Mexico anymore. He also said that future punishments are part of US President Trump's "America First" policy. Duffy also gave a similar warning to European airlines.

The US said that American airlines have complained to the Mexican government many times. Moving far from the city center costs more money. Also, the Mexican government stopped US air cargo companies from landing in many places in Mexico to carry more international goods. The US government and airlines have complained and tried to talk many times, but they have not received a reply.

The report said that the US government might take actions like asking Mexican airlines to share all their flight plans in the US beforehand. Also, all large charter flights carrying passengers or cargo would need approval from the US Department of Transportation before they fly. The US might also take away the special permission that allows Delta Air Lines (an American airline) and Aeromexico (a Mexican airline) to work together on prices and routes. This permission usually stops them from breaking competition laws.

The report also said that flights between the US and Mexico are among the busiest in the world. If Delta Air Lines and Aeromexico lose their special permission to work together, Aeromexico will lose its ability to work with other airlines on routes and prices, which gives them an advantage. This would cause them to lose money. The US Department of Transportation also warned that if the Mexican government does not change its rules quickly, the US might not approve new flights from Mexican airlines in the future.

Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum and transport officials defended the former president's policy against the US government's accusations. Sheinbaum said that moving the US cargo airlines' operations from Mexico City International Airport to Felipe Angeles International Airport was a technical decision. The main reason was to make Mexico City International Airport less crowded and to make flying safer and more efficient.

Responding to the US threat of punishments, Sheinbaum said that these changes are good for everyone, and companies are already getting used to them. So, she said there is no reason for them to be punished.

The report's analysis said that the tension between the US and Mexico, which started with issues like changing the name of the Gulf of Mexico and increasing taxes on goods that come by land, has now moved to air travel. The future of this situation is worrying.

US Lowers Import Taxes for Philippines, Japan, Indonesia; Experts Say Taiwan Stock Market Watches US-Taiwan Talks

US technology stocks went down, and TSMC's shares traded in the US (ADR) fell by 1.78%. US President Trump announced he would lower import taxes for the Philippines and Indonesia to 19%, and for Japan to 15%. In evening trading, Taiwan stock index futures fell a little by 9 points, closing at 22987 points. TSMC futures went up a little by 5 points, closing at 1135 points.

Experts in futures markets said that the agreement about import taxes between the US and Taiwan is not final yet. People in the market became more careful. Some people started selling shares in the short term, and awareness of risks increased. Also, there are still things happening in the world that make the future unclear. This week, many US technology companies will announce their financial results. People in the market are waiting to see these results and are being careful with their money. The market is waiting for the results of the import tax talks and how US technology companies' financial reports look.

People in the market are waiting for financial reports from companies like Alphabet (Google's parent company) and Tesla. The four main US stock indexes had mixed results. The Dow Jones Industrial Average went up by 179.37 points (0.40%), closing at 44502.44 points. The S&P 500 index went up slightly by 4.02 points (0.06%), closing at 6309.62 points, which is a new high price when the market closed.

The Nasdaq index, which includes many technology companies, fell by 81.48 points (0.39%), closing at 20892.69 points. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index fell by 100.71 points (1.75%), closing at 5638.72 points.

NVIDIA, a big company that makes chips for Artificial Intelligence (AI), fell by 2.54% after its stock had risen a lot. TSMC's American Depositary Receipts (ADR) fell by 1.78%. Texas Instruments (TI) released its financial report, and its stock fell by more than 11% after trading hours.

On July 22, Taiwan's main stock index moved up and down by more than 500 points. It ended the day down by 352.64 points, closing below 23000 points at 22987.92 points. The total value of trades grew to NT$435.315 billion. The three main groups of large investors together bought NT$11.654 billion more than they sold. Among them, dealers bought NT$4.296 billion more, investment trusts sold NT$2.666 billion more, and foreign and Chinese investors bought NT$10.024 billion more.

For Taiwan index futures, the three main groups of large investors held 875 fewer contracts that bet on prices going up, for a total of 531 contracts on July 22. Foreign investors held 1962 fewer contracts that bet on prices going down, for a total of 44594 contracts.

The Ministry of Economic Affairs said on July 22 that in June, the amount of orders from the US and Southeast Asian countries (ASEAN region) was US$19.94 billion and US$9.67 billion, respectively. Both amounts were the highest ever for that month. Business opportunities in AI helped US orders grow, especially for industries like chipmaking, servers, and semiconductor equipment.
