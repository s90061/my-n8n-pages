Apple's Stock Helps US Stock Market Rise, Nasdaq Leads

New company reports in the United States showed good results. Also, Apple Inc. said it would promise to make 100 billion dollars worth of products in the US. After this news, Apple's stock price went up by more than 5%. This helped the three main US stock market indexes finish higher today. The Nasdaq index went up the most, by more than 1%.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went up by 81.38 points, which is 0.18%. It closed at 44193.12 points.

The S&P 500 index went up by 45.87 points, which is 0.73%. It closed at 6345.06 points.

The Nasdaq index went up by 252.88 points, which is 1.21%. It closed at 21169.43 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index went down by 11.39 points, which is 0.20%. It closed at 5550.30 points.

Oil Prices Fall for 5th Day as Traders Watch Trump's Next Move on Russia

Oil prices went down for the fifth day in a row today. Traders are waiting to see if US President Trump will take stronger actions to stop Russia from selling its energy.

In New York, the price of West Texas Intermediate crude oil for September went down by 1.2%. It closed at $64.35 per barrel.

In London, the price of North Sea Brent crude oil for October went down by 1.1%. It closed at $66.89 per barrel.

CNBC: Jensen Huang Meets Trump Again at the White House, Topics Unclear

CNBC, a US business news channel, reported that Jensen Huang, the CEO of Nvidia, a big US company that makes AI chips, visited the White House today. He met with US President Trump. It is not clear what they talked about.

At the end of January this year, Trump and Huang met at the White House. People who know about the meeting said they talked about a new Chinese company called "DeepSeek."

Last month, Huang visited China. People who know about it said he met with Trump at the White House before his trip.
