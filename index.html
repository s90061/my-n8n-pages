Typhoon Wipha Gets Stronger: Hualien and Taitung See Over 200mm Rain in One Day

The Central Weather Administration (CWA) says that light typhoon Wipha is closest to Taiwan today. In the last three hours, it has become a bit stronger, and its stormy area has grown. Because of the typhoon's outer bands, Hualien and Taitung counties have received over 200 millimeters of rain from midnight until 8 AM.

The CWA continues to give a sea typhoon warning. According to the CWA website, Wipha's center was about 240 kilometers southwest of Eluanbi at 8 AM. Its central pressure is 980 hectopascals. The fastest wind near the center is 28 meters per second, which is like a level 10 wind. The fastest gust is 35 meters per second, like a level 12 wind. The average stormy area with level 7 winds is 150 kilometers. It is moving at 24 kilometers per hour, first to the northwest and then to the west.

Ms. Hsieh Pei-yun, a technical specialist at the CWA's Weather Forecast Center, said that the typhoon is expected to get stronger and its stormy area will get bigger. It should move away from Taiwan's nearby waters later today. Because of its outer bands, there will be a lot of rain. Besides Hualien and Taitung, other areas will also have short rain showers.

For the next 24 hours of rain, Ms. Hsieh Pei-yun said that today's rain will still be very heavy. People in eastern Taiwan need to be very careful. Mountainous areas in Hualien and Taitung might get very heavy rain. Flat areas in Hualien and Taitung, mountainous areas in Yilan, Nantou, Kaohsiung, Pingtung, and Hengchun Peninsula will also get heavy rain. New Taipei City, Taipei City, Yilan County, Kaohsiung and Pingtung areas, Taoyuan City, and mountainous areas in Taichung will have local heavy rain.

Due to the sinking air from the typhoon's outer bands, the CWA has issued a high-temperature warning. Miaoli County has an orange light today, meaning there is a high chance of temperatures staying at 36 degrees Celsius. Hsinchu City, Hsinchu County, Taichung City, Changhua County, and Kinmen County have a yellow light, meaning they should watch out for temperatures above 36 degrees Celsius.

The CWA also continues to give a strong wind warning for land. From today until tomorrow, parts of Keelung City, New Taipei City and Taipei City, Taoyuan City, Hsinchu County and City, Miaoli, Taichung, Nantou County, Tainan City, Pingtung County, Yilan County, Hualien and Taitung (including Orchid Island and Green Island), Penghu County, Kinmen, and Lienchiang County may have average winds of level 6 or higher, or gusts of level 8 or higher. Today, the greater Taipei area should watch out for stronger gusts.

The CWA said that today, the northern coast of Keelung, eastern Taiwan (including Orchid Island and Green Island), the south, the coast of Hengchun Peninsula, and Penghu will likely have long waves. In the Bashi Channel, the sea southeast of Taiwan, and the southern Taiwan Strait, waves will get higher than 5 meters. In the northern and northeastern sea of Taiwan and the northern Taiwan Strait, waves will also slowly get higher, from 3 to 4 meters. People are advised to avoid going near the sea.

Taiwan's Military Watches Closely as 27 Chinese Aircraft and Ships Enter Taiwan Strait on Last Day of Han Kuang Drill

The Ministry of National Defense stated that during the last day of Taiwan's "Han Kuang 41" military drill, from 6 AM yesterday to 6 AM today, the military found 18 Chinese aircraft. Among these, 15 crossed the middle line of the Taiwan Strait and entered the central and southwestern airspaces. They also found 8 Chinese warships and 1 Chinese government ship. In total, 27 Chinese aircraft and ships were active around the Taiwan Strait. Taiwan's military is watching them very carefully and dealing with the situation.

According to a map released today by the Ministry of National Defense, which shows where China's military was active near the Taiwan Strait, 8 Chinese fighter jets and drones were seen in the Taiwan Strait airspace from 7:30 AM to 5:20 PM yesterday. Five of these crossed the middle line of the Taiwan Strait.

Also, Taiwan's military detected 10 Chinese fighter jets in the southwestern airspace of Taiwan from 7:30 AM to 10:20 AM yesterday.

The Ministry of National Defense said that Taiwan's military is using its planes, ships, and missile systems on land to watch and respond to these activities very closely.

Taiwanese-American Swimmer An-Chi Han Breaks National Record Again in 100m Freestyle, Misses Finals

At the Rhein-Ruhr World University Games in Germany, Taiwanese-American swimmer An-Chi Han swam the women's 100-meter freestyle semi-finals today in 55.26 seconds. She broke her own national record again, but sadly, she did not make it to the finals.

An-Chi Han, who will represent Taiwan in the Paris Olympics, is once again competing at the Rhein-Ruhr World University Games. On the first day, she not only set a new national record in the women's 200-meter backstroke with a time of 2 minutes and 11.96 seconds, but she also swam with her teammates Liao Yu-fei, Chiu Yi-chen, and Liu Pei-yin in the women's 4x100-meter freestyle relay final. They set a new national record with a time of 3 minutes and 46.77 seconds.

An-Chi Han, who holds the national records for the women's 100-meter freestyle and 100-meter butterfly, swam the women's 100-meter freestyle preliminaries yesterday in 55.34 seconds, setting a new national record. She continued to improve her time in the women's 100-meter freestyle semi-finals, which started early this morning. She finished in 55.26 seconds, breaking the national record again. However, An-Chi Han was ranked 10th overall and could not make it to the finals.

In other news, Taiwanese swimmer Fu Kun-ming swam the men's 200-meter individual medley preliminaries in 2 minutes and 2.25 seconds. He not only reached the semi-finals but also met the standard for the Nagoya Asian Games. However, he could not improve his time in the semi-finals and did not make it to the finals. Another Taiwanese-American swimmer, Chou Ching-lei, also met the Nagoya Asian Games standard in the women's 400-meter individual medley with a time of 4 minutes and 49.12 seconds, but she also did not make it to the finals.
