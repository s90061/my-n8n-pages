National Filial Piety Award Winners Are Over 60 Years Old, Showing the Importance of Care for Older People and Help Between Generations.

The 2025 National Filial Piety Awards ceremony will happen on August 13th in Penghu County Performing Arts Center. 30 people will receive awards. The Ministry of Interior said that 20 of this year's winners are over 60 years old. This shows that helping older people and support between different generations are becoming more important. It also highlights how "smart filial piety" is valuable and useful today.

The Ministry of Interior shared in a news release today that the National Filial Piety Award has been running for 43 years. Every year, through public selection and awards, it finds and praises ordinary heroes who quietly take good care of their families. This helps remind society about the value of respecting parents and older people. The 2025 National Filial Piety Awards ceremony will be held on August 13th at the Penghu County Performing Arts Center.

The Ministry of Interior said that this year's National Filial Piety Award continued the idea of "different ways of showing filial piety." They chose 30 excellent examples of filial piety from 5 groups: long-term care, good use of resources, helping to achieve goals, working together, and showing respect. For example, Lin Yu-cai from Penghu County, in the long-term care group, has patiently helped her mother go to the doctor and do physical therapy for more than 20 years. She took on the care work without complaint and supported her mother kindly through hard times. Yu Su-ching from Taichung City, in the good use of resources group, applied for long-term care services for her father-in-law. She brought in an oxygen machine and equipment to check blood oxygen. She also learned to be a nursing assistant in an integrated hospital ward and got a care license, so she could personally help care for her father-in-law while he was in the hospital.

Taiwan has become a super-aging society, meaning many older people live here. The Ministry of Interior said that the average age of this year's award winners and the people they care for has also gone up a lot. This year, 20 winners are over 60 years old. Also, 3 people they care for are over 100 years old. This shows that helping older people and support across generations are more important now. It also highlights the value and use of smart ways to show filial piety today.

To show respect to the filial piety role models, the Ministry of Interior gives each winner NT$50,000 and an award. Those from low or middle-income families also get an extra NT$30,000. This is to encourage more people to value family love and show filial piety now. The Ministry of Interior also said that you can see more stories of this year's filial piety role models in the "2025 Filial Piety Award Album" (https://www.moi.gov.tw/cl.aspx?n=11177).

Teacher Dong Yi-chun from Tainan First Senior High School Wins Award for Creating Museum Research Course.

Dong Yi-chun, a teacher at Tainan First Senior High School, has worked in art education for many years. She created the first museum research course. She encourages students to use art to connect with their local area and the world, helping many students learn and grow. She won this year's Teacher's Day Award.

The Ministry of Education released a news report today, introducing 8 Teacher's Day Award winners from schools under the National and Pre-school Education Administration. Dong Yi-chun, a teacher at Tainan First Senior High School, created the country's only course called "Museum Inquiry, Visits, and Practice." This course combines humanities, technology, and art. She leads students to plan and create real exhibitions, helping them understand culture and take part in public life. She also worked with the National Museum of Taiwan History for an exhibition and won the 2018 Ministry of Education Teaching Excellence Gold Award.
