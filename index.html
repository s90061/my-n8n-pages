Fed Cuts Interest Rates by 0.25%, US Stock Market Closes Mixed

The US central bank, called the Federal Reserve (Fed), had a meeting about interest rates. As many people thought, they lowered interest rates by a small amount (0.25%). After the meeting, the leader, Jerome Powell, talked about how the job market is not very strong. Because of this, the main stock market indexes in the US showed different results when they closed today.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went up by 260.42 points, which is 0.57%. It closed at 46018.32 points.

The S&P 500 Index went down by 6.41 points, which is 0.10%. It closed at 6600.35 points.

The Nasdaq Index went down by 72.63 points, which is 0.33%. It closed at 22261.33 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index also went down by 19.01 points, which is 0.31%. It closed at 6060.22 points.

Markets React to Fed's Decision, International Oil Prices Fall

Today, international oil prices went down. This happened after they had gone up for three days in a row. Traders were thinking about the newest information on oil supplies in the US. They were also thinking about the US central bank's (Fed's) decision to lower interest rates.

The price for West Texas Intermediate crude oil, which is for delivery in October at the New York Mercantile Exchange, dropped by 0.7%. It closed at $64.05 for one barrel.

The price for Brent crude oil from the North Sea in London, for delivery in November, dropped by 0.8%. It closed at $67.95 for one barrel.

Israeli Army Speeds Up Clearing Gaza City, Over 65,000 Palestinians Dead in Two Years of Conflict

The Israeli army said today that they will open a new road for 48 hours. This road will help Palestinians leave Gaza City. Israel is also working faster to get civilians out of the city and to fight thousands of Hamas soldiers.

According to Reuters, hundreds of thousands of Palestinians are now hiding in Gaza City. Many people do not want to go south as Israel told them. This is because the journey is dangerous, conditions in the south are bad, there is not enough food, and they are worried they will never return home.

Ahmed, who teaches at a local school, spoke on the phone. He said, "Even if we want to leave Gaza City, how can we be sure we can come back? Will the war end? That is why I would rather die in Sabra. This is my own neighborhood."

Health officials in Gaza said that Israel's air attacks and shootings today killed at least 50 people across the Gaza Strip. 39 of these people were in Gaza City.

Officials said that with these new deaths, more than 65,000 Palestinians have now died in the two years of war between Israel and Hamas.

Palestinian officials and rescue workers said the actual number of dead people might be even higher. Many bodies are still buried under the broken buildings.
