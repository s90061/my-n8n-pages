Taiwan Beats Venezuela: Little League Team Heads to International Championship

The Williamsport Little League World Series is almost over. The Dongyuan Elementary School baseball team from Taipei, representing the Asia-Pacific region, won against the team from Venezuela today. They won 7 to 3 and will now play in the International Group championship game.

The World Little League Championship, held in Williamsport, Pennsylvania, USA, has been going on for eight days. The International Group has 10 teams. These teams are from the Asia-Pacific region, Australia, Canada, the Caribbean, Europe-Africa, Japan, Latin America, Mexico, Panama, and Puerto Rico. There are also 10 teams from the United States. All these teams are competing for the main championship over 12 exciting days.

The young Taiwanese players won against Mexico and Aruba, which represented the Caribbean. On August 20th, they played against Venezuela, which was the winner from the Latin America group. Venezuela had previously won against Puerto Rico, Canada, and Japan.

The Dongyuan Elementary School baseball players from Taipei used careful batting and strong defense. They scored 2 runs in the first inning and 5 runs in the top of the fifth inning. In the bottom of the fifth, they gave up 3 runs. In the end, they defeated the strong Venezuela team with a score of 7 to 3.

The Taiwanese team will play again at 0:30 AM Taiwan time on August 24th to compete for the International Group championship.

Reuters Poll: Most Americans Think Countries Should Recognize Palestine

According to the newest poll from Reuters/Ipsos, more than half of Americans (58%) think that countries in the United Nations should recognize the State of Palestine. 33% of people asked did not agree, and 9% did not answer.

The Reuters/Ipsos poll was done online for six days. It gathered answers from 4446 adults across the United States. The results can be off by about 2 percentage points. The poll found that Americans are clearly divided on the issue of recognizing Palestine, depending on their political party.

78% of Democrats support recognizing Palestine. This is much higher than Republicans, where only 41% support it. More than half of Republicans (53%) do not agree that all UN countries should recognize the State of Palestine.

About 65% of the people asked said that the United States should do something in Gaza to help people who are facing starvation. 28% of people did not agree, and 41% of them were Republicans.

This poll also showed that 59% of Americans believe Israel's military actions in Gaza are too much. 33% of people asked did not agree with this.

CNBC: Intel Is Talking to Large Investors About More Shares at a Lower Price

US business news channel CNBC reported that people who know the situation said Intel is talking to other big investors. They are discussing the possibility of these investors buying more shares in Intel at a lower price.

Earlier this week, Japan's SoftBank Group Corp. announced it would invest 2 billion US dollars in Intel. Also, there were rumors that the US government was thinking about investing in Intel in different ways. After these announcements, Intel's stock price went up for a short time, but today it fell by more than 7%.

Howard Lutnick, the US Secretary of Commerce, said yesterday in an interview with CNBC that the US government must get shares in Intel in exchange for providing money from the "CHIPS Act."

People who know the situation said that Intel is now looking for other large investors besides SoftBank to get more money.

Intel is trying to change and become strong again after many years of lower sales and losing market share.
