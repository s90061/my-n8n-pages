China to Implement Mandatory Social Security; State Media Calls It a "Consumption Catalyst"

Taipei, August 16 (CNA) ‚Äì China will start a mandatory social insurance system on September 1. This has made many businesses and workers worried. Today, the Chinese state media, People's Daily, wrote that social insurance is a "basic safety net" for individuals. For companies, it is a "ticket to the future." For China, it is a "social stabilizer" and also a "driver for spending."

On August 1, China's Supreme People's Court gave an explanation. It said that agreements between employers and employees not to pay social insurance fees are not valid. If workers end their work contracts for this reason, they can ask their employer to pay them money. This explanation will start on September 1.

After this explanation was made public, many small businesses and low-wage workers in China became worried. They fear they cannot pay the high social insurance costs. Experts say that in the future, many people might choose "flexible employment" or part-time jobs to avoid paying social insurance.

Today, the People's Daily published a comment. It said that the recent talks about "mandatory social insurance for everyone" have caused much discussion. It is important to make clear any misunderstandings of the policy and any wrong information given to the public.

The article said that the Supreme Court's explanation does not create "new rules." Instead, it makes legal judgments the same across the country. This removes differences in how laws are used in different areas. It is not "mandatory" only now. According to China's labor law and social insurance law, paying social insurance has always been required. Also, it does not cover "everyone," but only workers as defined by law.

The article criticized that a few online media, to get attention, use words like "everyone," "mandatory," and "new rules." This only misleads the public and causes confusion.

This People's Daily comment gave three reasons to support mandatory social insurance.

The article said that for individuals, social insurance is a "basic safety net," not an "optional extra." It also suggested that some workers doubt joining social insurance because they "only consider short-term money and not long-term risks." They worry that companies will find ways to avoid the benefits of the system.

The article also said that for businesses, social insurance is a "ticket to the future," not a "burden of rules." It explained that making social insurance cover more people and making payments stricter will indeed bring some cost pressure to businesses, especially small and medium-sized ones. But this is not a reason to "avoid social insurance."

This comment stated that not paying social insurance is against the law. Also, if a company gets an advantage in "price competition" by doing this, how can it last long today when we want good quality development and "investing in people"? With this big trend, companies must pay great attention to legal risks. They must stop trying to lower costs by not paying social insurance. Instead, they should improve in other areas and develop special skills to move to higher levels of the industry and value chain.

The article also mentioned that we must face the real business pressures on small and medium-sized companies. The key to solving this problem is to offer more ways to help businesses.

The article also claimed that for the country, social insurance is a "social stabilizer" and a "driver for spending." It said that social insurance gives basic protection for health, old age, and unemployment. People will then need to save less money "just in case." Instead, they will use more of their income for improving their lives, travel, hobbies, and personal growth. The better the social insurance protection, the more it will encourage spending.

Finally, this People's Daily comment said that social insurance is not the only way to help everyone have a good life. But doing social insurance work well and steadily is the right thing to do now.
