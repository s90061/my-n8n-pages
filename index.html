Ye Baodi's First Home Run Helps Hawks Win; Lin Shixiang Gets 30 Saves

The TSG Hawks played their last regular season game against the Wei Chuan Dragons. Ye Baodi hit his first home run in his professional baseball career in the bottom of the eighth inning. This home run won the game for the Hawks, making the score 4-3. The Dragons have now lost five games in a row. Lin Shixiang, a player for the Hawks, got his 30th save of the season. He is the eighth player in league history to do this.

The Hawks and Dragons did not score any runs in the first three innings. In the bottom of the fourth inning, the Hawks scored first with a home run by Yan Yu-hsuan.

In the top of the sixth inning, the Dragons scored two runs with a home run by Giljegiljaw Kungkuan. This was Kungkuan's 24th home run this season. In the top of the seventh inning, the Dragons scored another run because of a hit, a dropped ball by the catcher, and a sacrifice fly by Wang Shun-ho.

The Hawks started to score again in the bottom of the eighth inning. After two outs, Wang Po-hsuan and Tseng Yu-ching both walked to first base. Then, Ye Baodi hit a three-run home run that put the Hawks ahead. This was his first home run in the professional league, and it won the game. Ye Baodi was chosen as the Most Valuable Player (MVP) for the game.

The Hawks' starting pitcher, Sakurai Shuto, pitched for six innings and gave up two runs. He pitched well, but he did not get the win or the loss. The win went to the relief pitcher, Huang Chun. The closing pitcher, Lin Shixiang, pitched for one inning, struck out two players, and did not give up any runs. He got his 30th save of the season. This makes him the eighth player in professional baseball history to get 30 saves in one season.

Sanae Takaichi Plans New Roles: Taro Aso Expected to Be LDP Vice President

Sanae Takaichi, the new leader of Japan's ruling Liberal Democratic Party (LDP), started to arrange new job roles today. In the evening, she talked with Taro Aso, a top advisor for the LDP, for about one hour. Aso supported Takaichi when she was chosen as the new leader. It is expected that Taro Aso will become the Vice President of the party. His brother-in-law, Shunichi Suzuki, is likely to become the Secretary-General. These decisions are expected to be confirmed as early as October 7.

According to the Nikkei newspaper, Takaichi is planning to appoint Taro Aso as Vice President and Shunichi Suzuki, who is currently the chairman of the General Council and part of Aso's group, as Secretary-General. Aso was very important in helping Takaichi win the leadership election.

Takaichi and Aso talked for about one hour in the evening. It is thought that they discussed who would get important jobs in the party. After the meeting, when reporters asked about the person chosen for Secretary-General, Takaichi only said, "The person has not been told yet."

Shunichi Suzuki is 72 years old. He worked as the Minister of the Environment under Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi. Later, he was the Deputy Foreign Minister and Minister for the Tokyo Olympics under Prime Minister Shinzo Abe. His father, Zenko Suzuki, was a former Prime Minister of Japan. Taro Aso is married to Suzuki's third older sister.

Yesterday, Takaichi spoke at a press conference about the Secretary-General role. She said, "I hope this job will be given to someone who can communicate well with all political parties."

Besides the top jobs in the party, Takaichi is also working to create a new government cabinet. Reports say that former Defense Minister Minoru Kihara might get an important job. Kihara was the campaign manager for Takaichi in the 2021 LDP leadership election, and they have a close relationship.

Minoru Kihara is currently the Secretary-General of the Japan-Taiwan Parliamentary Group, so he has good relations with Taiwan.

Takaichi also mentioned the four people who ran against her in the election yesterday: Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries Minister Shinjiro Koizumi, Chief Cabinet Secretary Yoshimasa Hayashi, former Minister in charge of Economic Security Takayuki Kobayashi, and former Secretary-General Toshimitsu Motegi. She said, "Everyone has great qualities, and I hope they can use their strengths." This means she plans to give them important roles in the government or the party.
