FBI: DNA at Scene of US Conservative Influencer Kirk's Murder Matches Suspect

Kash Patel, who is the head of the FBI, said today that DNA taken from where Charlie Kirk was killed matches Tyler Robinson, who is suspected of the crime.

Tyler Robinson, who is 22 years old, was caught on September 12th after police looked for him for 33 hours. He is expected to be officially charged with murder later this week.

Charlie Kirk was shot and killed on September 10th during a speech at a university in Utah. He started a very important conservative group for young people called "Turning Point USA."

Authorities said the suspect used a sniper rifle from a roof. He fired one shot that hit Kirk in the neck.

Patel said on Fox News this morning, "Today, I can tell you that the DNA on a towel wrapped around the gun and on a screwdriver found at the crime scene both match the DNA of the suspect we have arrested."

Patel also mentioned that Robinson is believed to have written a note before the crime.

Patel said the note "basically said... 'I have a chance to get rid of Kirk, and I will take this chance.' This note was written before the shooting."

He added that the note was left at the suspect's home.

Patel said, "Even if the note was destroyed, we still found forensic evidence related to it."

Trump's Visit to the UK; US and UK to Announce Over $10 Billion Agreement

Today, high-level US officials said that during President Donald Trump's visit to the UK this week, the US and the UK plan to announce economic agreements worth over 10 billion US dollars in total.

According to Reuters, US officials said in a phone briefing that the two governments plan to sign a trade agreement with three main parts. These parts include creating new technology partnerships to make both countries' technology industries stronger, working together on peaceful nuclear energy, and helping each other with defense technology.

Officials said that many important people from the US technology industry are expected to be at this visit.

Trump and the First Lady, Melania Trump, will arrive tomorrow evening. On the 17th, they will meet with King Charles III and Queen Camilla at Windsor Castle. They will also attend a state dinner that evening.

On the 18th, Trump will meet with the Prime Minister of the UK, Keir Starmer. They plan to hold a joint press conference. After that, Trump will fly back to Washington D.C. on the evening of the 18th.

Trump is the first US President to be invited to a state dinner in the UK twice. The first time was in 2019, during his first term as president.

US officials said that the upcoming agreements will focus on working together economically. The total amount is expected to be at least 10 billion US dollars.

One US official said, "This visit gives the President a chance to make our relationship stronger with this very close partner and friend. At the same time, it helps both countries with their shared economic and foreign policy goals."
