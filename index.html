After Mid-Autumn Festival, Egg Supply Goes Up, Price Drops by NT$2 per Catty

After the Mid-Autumn Festival, there are more eggs available. The Taipei Egg Dealers' Association decided today to lower both the farm price and the wholesale price by NT$2 per catty.

The Ministry of Agriculture shared new information about egg production and sales. There are now 37.06 million laying hens. Every day, they can provide 126,000 boxes of eggs (200 eggs per box). This is the first time the supply has increased after staying at 124,000 boxes for seven weeks. The current supply is 6,000 boxes (1.2 million eggs) more than the daily need of 120,000 boxes.

Lin Tian-lai, the chairman of the Taipei Egg Dealers' Association, told the media that after the Mid-Autumn Festival, people usually buy fewer eggs. So, starting today, the farm price will drop from NT$33.5 to NT$31.5 per catty. Tomorrow, the wholesale price will drop from NT$43 to NT$41.

Taipei is the city where most eggs are sold in Taiwan. The prices set by the Taipei Egg Dealers' Association are often used as a guide by other cities and counties.

Taichung Sold 173 Plots of Land, 18% Were Bought

The Taichung City Government's Land Administration Bureau sold 173 pieces of land today. These included leftover land from the Shui-nan district rezoning and compensation land from the 13th and 14th urban land consolidation projects. 31 pieces of land were successfully sold, which is about 18% of the total. The total money from these sales was over NT$4.14 billion.

The Taichung City Land Administration Bureau explained that today, with supervision from the Anti-Corruption Office and the Accounting Office, they finished the public bidding for the leftover land in the "Taichung City Shui-nan Airport Original Site District Rezoning Area" and the compensation land in the "Taichung City 13th Da-ching Urban Land Consolidation Area" and "Taichung City 14th Mei-he Village Urban Land Consolidation Area."

The Land Administration Bureau said that they announced the sale of 17 leftover plots and 156 compensation plots, making a total of 173 plots. These plots covered an area of 10.83 hectares. They received 41 bids, and 31 plots were successfully sold. The success rate was about 18%, and the total amount of money from the sales was over NT$4.14 billion. The extra money paid above the asking price was 1.26%.

The Land Administration Bureau also said that for the Shui-nan district rezoning leftover land, the highest total price paid was for plot number 21 in Fengda Section, Xitun District, which was over NT$1.82 billion. The highest price per square meter was also for plot number 21 in Fengda Section, Xitun District, at about NT$340,040 per square meter (about NT$1.12 million per ping).

For the 13th urban land consolidation area compensation land, the highest total price paid was for plot number 182 in Da-ching Section, South District, which was over NT$270 million. The highest price per square meter was for plot number 470 in Chang-ming Section, Nantun District, at about NT$206,601 per square meter (about NT$682,978 per ping).

For the 14th urban land consolidation area compensation land, the highest total price paid was for plot number 19 in Zhou-ji Section, Beitun District, which was over NT$199 million. The highest price per square meter was for plot number 351 in Mei-he Section, Beitun District, at about NT$211,171 per square meter (about NT$698,087 per ping).

The Land Administration Bureau pointed out that district rezoning and urban land consolidation are ways to develop land that pay for themselves. The leftover land and compensation land are sold publicly to get back the money spent on development. If there is extra money, it will be used for managing and maintaining public facilities in each developed area. It will also be used for public construction projects, helping the whole area grow, so all citizens can benefit from the development.

Taiwan Stock Market Reaches New High, Over 3.92 Million Trading Accounts in August, Up for 3rd Month

The Taiwan stock market continues to go up, reaching a new high at the end of August. Investors are feeling more confident. According to new information from the Taiwan Stock Exchange today, there were 3,927,948 active trading accounts in the stock market in August. This is 519,104 more than in July, and it's the third month in a row that this number has grown.

In August, individual investors were responsible for 55.9% of the trading value, which is 4.63 percentage points higher than July's 51.27%. At the same time, foreign investors' share of the trading value went down from 34.37% to 32.84%.

The trading turnover rate in August was 10.99%, which is higher than July's 9.75%. The percentage of day trading value was 36.16%, and the average number of people day trading each day was 133,439. Both of these numbers are clearly higher than July's 35.66% and 99,828 people.

Other numbers for the stock market in August include ETF trading value, which was 5.91%, a drop from July's 6.8%. The amount of money invested through regular fixed deposits was about NT$16.746 billion, which is more than July's NT$15.642 billion.
