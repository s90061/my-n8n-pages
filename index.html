Motorcycle Rider Falls into Valley After Crash on Alishan Highway

Yesterday night, there was a big motorcycle accident on Alishan Highway (Taiwan Provincial Highway 18) at the 35.8-kilometer mark in Chiayi County. A 46-year-old man named Zheng was riding a yellow-plated heavy motorcycle down the road. It seems he lost control and hit a concrete barrier on the same side of the road.

After hitting the barrier, the rider fell about 50 meters down the side of the road into a valley. Firefighters were called at 10:49 PM. They quickly set up ropes and rescued him. He had many scrapes and bruises, but he was awake and not in danger of losing his life. He was taken to the hospital for treatment. His motorcycle slid across the road and into a ditch on the other side.

The Zhongpu Police Station in Chiayi County said that the man had no alcohol in his blood. They are still checking to find out exactly why the accident happened. The police ask drivers to drive slowly on the many winding roads in the Alishan mountains to avoid accidents.

Number of Students from New Southbound Countries Reaches Record High of 82,000, More Than Doubles in 11 Years

The newest numbers from the Ministry of Education show that in the 2024 to 2025 school year, 123,188 students from other countries studied in colleges and universities in Taiwan. About 82,000 of these students were from countries in the New Southbound Policy. This is the highest number ever, more than double what it was 11 years ago. For the last five years, most of these students have come from Vietnam.

The Ministry of Education recently shared its newest numbers. In the 2014 to 2015 school year, there were 93,645 international students in Taiwan's colleges and universities. After that, the number grew each year, reaching its highest point of 129,207 students in the 2018 to 2019 school year. In the 2020 to 2021 school year (when COVID-19 started), the number went down to 90,895. Since then, it has gone up each year, and in 2024 to 2025, it is back to 123,188 students.

Looking at students from New Southbound countries, the Ministry of Education's numbers show that there were 25,289 such students in the 2014 to 2015 school year. This was 27% of all international students. After that, the number grew each year, and in the 2024 to 2025 school year, it reached 82,622. This is the highest number ever and makes up 67% of all international students. The number of students from New Southbound countries has more than doubled in 11 years.

When we look closer at students from New Southbound countries, which include Vietnam, Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, the Philippines, and India, the Ministry of Education found something interesting. Before the 2019 school year, more students from New Southbound countries came from Malaysia. But since the 2020 school year, Vietnam has been the top country, with 17,534 students. After that, it stayed number one. By the 2024 school year, this number reached 39,695 students, which is more than double in five years.

The Ministry of Education explained that to get more students from New Southbound countries to study in Taiwan, they have started several programs. These include the "New Southbound Industry-Academia Cooperation International Program," the "Program to Get More Overseas Chinese, Hong Kong, Macau, and Foreign Students in Important Industries," and the "Program to Help International Students Come and Stay in Taiwan." These programs give schools more choices for finding international students.

Also, the Ministry of Education offers many scholarships and help. They continue to make student support and advice services better for international students. They are making all support services better to make it more attractive for good students from New Southbound countries to study in Taiwan. Every year, they also go to New Southbound countries to join or hold higher education fairs and events. There, they tell students and parents about studying in Taiwan.
