Man Arrested After Stabbing Ex-Girlfriend and Her Mother at Fengyuan Train Station

(Central News Agency reporter Su Muchun, Taichung, July 24) A man named Huang argued and struggled with his ex-girlfriend, Ms. Huang, in front of the ticket gate at Fengyuan Train Station tonight. He then used a knife to hurt Ms. Huang and her mother. The police arrived after getting a report and caught the man. Ms. Huang and her mother were taken to the hospital and are not in danger. The police are still investigating the case.

The Taichung Branch of the Railway Police Bureau said that the Fengyuan Police Station received a report at 5:45 PM today. The report said there was an argument and someone was hurt in front of the ticket gate at Fengyuan Train Station. The police went to the scene right away.

Police found that the 28-year-old Mr. Huang and 27-year-old Ms. Huang were ex-partners. They started arguing and pulling each other for a small reason. During the struggle, Mr. Huang pulled out a fruit knife and cut Ms. Huang and her mother. The police arrived quickly and arrested Mr. Huang.

Police and firefighters took Ms. Huang and her mother to Fengyuan Hospital. After treatment, their lives were not in danger. It is understood that Ms. Huang had a protection order against Mr. Huang. The police are still looking into the case. (Editor: Chen Qingfang) 1140724

Lin Fei-fan: Stand Up and Vote on July 26 to Protect Taiwan's Sovereignty

(Central News Agency reporter Wen Guixiang, Taipei, July 24) The first wave of recall votes will happen on July 26. Lin Fei-fan, who is the deputy secretary-general of the National Security Council, said tonight that 11 years ago, he and many people worked together to stop Taiwan from being taken over by China. Now, 11 years later, he is in a different role but still works to keep Taiwan's sovereignty. He asked everyone to come out and vote on July 26 and August 23.

The recall cases for 24 Kuomintang (KMT) legislators and the recall case for Hsinchu Mayor Kao Hung-an, who is suspended, will have their votes and counting on July 26. The Central Election Commission recently approved the recall cases for KMT legislators Luo Ming-tsai, Chiang Chi-chen, Yang Chiung-ying, Yen Kuan-heng, and Lin Sze-ming. Their votes will be on August 23.

Lin Fei-fan responded tonight to the "All Industries Support Recalls" event by posting a photo from the 2014 Sunflower Student Movement on Facebook. He said he now works in national security and is also a father. He was lucky to be part of a historical event 11 years ago that changed Taiwan. They worked together to stop China from taking over Taiwan's economy. 11 years later, in a different role, he still believes Taiwan's sovereignty, democracy, and freedom must not be taken away or given up.

Lin Fei-fan said thank you to everyone who came out, including partners and citizen groups. He also thanked those who have walked the same path for 11 years, continuing to protect this small, mountainous country they love so much. He asked everyone to stand up and vote on July 26 and August 23. (Editor: Lin Keren) 1140724

Von der Leyen: EU and China Agree on Improved Rare Earth Export System

(Central News Agency, Beijing, July 24, from foreign reports) Ursula von der Leyen, the President of the European Commission, is visiting Beijing. She said that the European Union and China have agreed on a "better version" of the system for China to export rare earth minerals to the EU. She also said that how China works with Russia will be a "very important factor" for the future of relations between the EU and China.

Von der Leyen and Antonio Costa, the President of the European Council, are visiting Beijing. This morning, they met with Chinese President Xi Jinping at the Great Hall of the People in Beijing.

Agence France-Presse reported that Von der Leyen held a press conference in Beijing. She said: "We agreed... on an improved export supply system (for rare earths). This means that if there are problems, this improved system can quickly check and solve the issues or disagreements that happen."

Von der Leyen also talked about how China keeps working more with Russian President Vladimir Putin. She said this "will be a deciding factor for our future relationship."

Costa said today that during their meeting in Beijing, they told Chinese officials about the EU's concerns regarding human rights in China.

Costa said at the press conference: "Protecting human rights is a main part of how the EU works with other countries, including China... We repeated our concerns today and will keep working on this important issue." (Translator: Hung Pei-ying / Editor: Ho Hung-ju) 1140724
