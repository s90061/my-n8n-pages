Finland Beats Georgia 93-79, Reaches EuroBasket Semifinals for the First Time

After surprisingly beating Serbia, Finland today defeated Georgia 93 to 79. This means Finland has reached the semifinals of the European Basketball Championship for the first time in its history.

Before this, Finland had surprised everyone by beating Serbia, a team expected to win the championship, 92 to 86 in the EuroBasket Round of 16. This win helped them move to the quarterfinals and surprised the world of basketball. Serbia, a favorite to win, has now lost in the Round of 16 for two tournaments in a row.

In today's game, Finland and Georgia played very closely for the first 6 minutes. But towards the end of the first quarter, Finland made a lot of points quickly. They changed the score from 12-12 to 28-15, taking a 13-point lead at the end of the first quarter.

In the second quarter, Georgia managed to get closer, only being 9 points behind for a short time. However, Finland kept playing strongly. By halftime, Finland had increased their lead to 57-40.

Finland was ahead by 20 points at one point in the third quarter. They went into the fourth quarter leading 71-62. In the final quarter, Georgia tried hard to catch up and got within 8 points with about 8 minutes left. But Finland pulled ahead again in the end and won against Georgia 93 to 79.

Mikael Jantunen, a power forward for Finland, played the best with 19 points and 5 rebounds. He made 3 three-point shots in the first half, which helped his team get an early lead. His teammate Lauri Markkanen also played well, scoring 17 points, getting 6 rebounds, and blocking 4 shots.

Finland will play in the semifinals on the 12th against the winner of the game between Germany and Slovenia.

Oracle Stock Rises 40%, Founder Larry Ellison Becomes World's Richest Person, Passing Elon Musk

The stock price of the American software company Oracle went up by more than 40%. Because of this, Oracle's founder, Larry Ellison, became much richer. He is now the richest person in the world, passing Elon Musk, the CEO of Tesla.

By 10 AM Eastern Time today, Larry Ellison's total wealth was almost 400 billion US dollars. According to Bloomberg, Elon Musk's wealth is currently about 385 billion US dollars.

After Ellison and Musk, the richest people are Mark Zuckerberg, the founder of Meta, Jeff Bezos, the founder of Amazon, and Larry Page and Sergey Brin, the co-founders of Google.

Ellison's money grew quickly because he and his family have been actively working in artificial intelligence (AI), media, and politics.

Oracle's stock went up a lot today. This happened because the company announced that it has over 450 billion US dollars in cloud service orders that have not been delivered yet. Also, Oracle signed deals worth billions of dollars for computing services with important AI companies like Nvidia and OpenAI, which supports ChatGPT.

The Ellison family has also become more powerful. Larry's son, David, recently finished buying Paramount Global for 8 billion US dollars.

Larry Ellison is a strong supporter of President Trump. He recently visited the White House. There, he announced a plan for a large data center project with Masayoshi Son, the head of Japan's SoftBank Group, and Sam Altman, the CEO of OpenAI.

Larry Ellison, who is 81 years old, started Oracle more than 40 years ago. Today, the company's market value was almost 1 trillion US dollars during trading.

European Stocks End Mixed as Hopes for US Rate Cut Balance Geopolitical Risks

Today, major stock markets in Europe had mixed results at the end of trading. This happened because people expect the US Federal Reserve (Fed) to lower interest rates soon. This expectation helped to balance the worries about increasing political problems in different parts of the world.

The FTSE 100 index in London went down by 17.14 points, or 0.19%, closing at 9225.39 points.

The DAX index in Frankfurt went down by 85.50 points, or 0.36%, closing at 23632.95 points.

The CAC 40 index in Paris went up by 11.93 points, or 0.15%, closing at 7761.32 points.
