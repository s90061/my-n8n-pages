US and China May Agree on TikTok; US Lawmaker Worries About China's Influence

Washington, September 17 (CNA) The United States and China are close to an agreement about the video app TikTok. This agreement would change who owns TikTok, giving ownership to the US. John Moolenaar, who leads a special US House committee on competition with China, said today that he is concerned about this.

According to Reuters, Moolenaar said in a statement: "From early reports, I worry that this agreement might still make the new TikTok use ByteDance's software and apps. This could allow the Chinese Communist Party to keep control or influence."

Scott Bessent, the US Treasury Secretary, recently said that the US and China have a basic agreement about TikTok. This agreement is designed to move the ownership of TikTok to the US.

US President Trump also mentioned that he has an agreement with China about TikTok. He said that several large companies want to buy it. He will talk with Chinese President Xi Jinping to confirm all the details.

Federal Reserve Lowers Interest Rate as Expected; US Stock Market Ends Mixed

New York, September 17 (CNA) The US Federal Reserve (Fed) held a meeting about interest rates and lowered them by 0.25%, as many people expected. After the meeting, Chairman Jerome Powell talked about problems in the job market. Because of this, the main US stock market indexes ended the day with mixed results.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went up by 260.42 points, or 0.57%, to close at 46018.32 points.

The S&P 500 index went down by 6.41 points, or 0.10%, to close at 6600.35 points.

The Nasdaq index went down by 72.63 points, or 0.33%, to close at 22261.33 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index went down by 19.01 points, or 0.31%, to close at 6060.22 points.

Oil Prices Fall as Market Considers Federal Reserve Decision

New York, September 17 (CNA) Today, international oil prices went down, stopping a three-day increase. This happened as traders thought about the newest US oil storage numbers and the US Federal Reserve's (Fed) decision to lower interest rates.

On the New York Mercantile Exchange, the price for West Texas Intermediate crude oil for October delivery went down by 0.7%, closing at $64.05 per barrel.

In London, the price for North Sea Brent crude oil for November delivery went down by 0.8%, closing at $67.95 per barrel.
