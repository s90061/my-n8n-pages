Aaron Kwok to Perform Extra Concert in Kaohsiung in November; All Tickets Sold Out, Making Him Very Happy

Hong Kong star Aaron Kwok will perform for the first time at Kaohsiung Arena on November 8th. The concert organizers announced today that there will be an extra show on November 9th. Kwok thanked his Taiwanese fans for their support. He said that returning to Kaohsiung to perform after 30 years, plus all tickets being sold out, makes him twice as happy.

Since his concert in Taipei Arena last year, Aaron Kwok has wanted to perform in Taiwan again. He said today through the organizers that after 30 years, the place he most wanted to visit in Kaohsiung was the Kaohsiung Arena to perform for everyone. He also misses the applause from his fans. He is looking forward to trying local Kaohsiung snacks and hopes fans will enjoy the show.

Regarding the November 8th concert tickets selling out and an extra show being added the next day, Aaron Kwok said, "Thank you, Taiwanese fans, for your continuous support and love. It makes me very emotional to perform in Kaohsiung again. And with tickets selling out so quickly, I feel double the happiness." He added, "My team and I are working hard on all the show details. We have also added an extended stage so I can interact closely and happily with everyone. See you in Kaohsiung!"

For Aaron Kwok's extra concert in Kaohsiung, DBS cardholders can buy tickets early on September 5th. General ticket sales will begin on September 6th at 11 AM.

It is like the military governments 80 years ago that used nationalism to start wars. So, it is very ironic that China now talks about remembering the "fight against fascism."
