Chiayi, Kaohsiung, Pingtung Mountain Areas Should Prepare for Extremely Heavy Rain; Nantou, Yunlin, Tainan Need to Watch Out for Very Heavy Rain

Taipei, July 30 (CNA) The Central Weather Administration has issued a special report about heavy rain. A southwest air current is causing short, heavy rain. From tonight until tomorrow, mountain areas in Chiayi County, Kaohsiung City, and Pingtung County will have very heavy or extremely heavy local rain. Nantou County, mountain areas in Yunlin County, and mountain areas in Tainan City will have heavy or very heavy local rain.

Also, areas in Taichung City, Changhua County, Chiayi City, Chiayi County, Tainan City, Kaohsiung City, Pingtung County, and Taitung County will have heavy local rain. Yunlin County and mountain areas in Miaoli County might have much local rain.

The Weather Administration reminds people to watch out for lightning and strong winds. In mountain areas, be careful of landslides, falling rocks, mudslides, and sudden floods. Low-lying areas should watch out for floods.

Areas with extremely heavy rain: Chiayi County, Kaohsiung City, Pingtung County. Areas with very heavy rain: Nantou County, Yunlin County, Tainan City. (Edited by Yang Chao-yen) 1140730

Heavy Rain in Kaohsiung Mountains; Car Suspected to Have Fallen into Valley in Liugui, Police and Firefighters Searching

Kaohsiung, July 30 (CNA) At about 9:40 PM today, police officers from the Liugui District Police Station in Kaohsiung City received a report. It said that a car was suspected to have fallen into a valley at the 81.1-kilometer mark on Provincial Highway 20 in Liugui District. Because of heavy rain, bad visibility, and rocks falling on the road, police and firefighters are currently searching.

The Kaohsiung City Fire Department sent 16 vehicles and 31 people to help. They have not found the car yet and are still searching. The police said the person who reported the accident stated that a car was suspected to have fallen into the valley while passing another car. The number of injured or dead, and the cause of the accident are still unclear. (Edited by Yang Chao-yen) 1140730

Republican Party Chairman Chou Ke-chi Sentenced to 3 Years for Bribery in Petition Drive; Prosecutors Arrest Him and Send Him to Prison

Taipei, July 30 (CNA) Chou Ke-chi, the chairman of the Republican Party, registered to run for vice president in 2023. During the petition drive, he was suspected of giving cash bribes to more than 20 people. Both the first and second courts sentenced him to 3 years in prison and a fine of 2 million NT dollars. Today, the Supreme Court rejected his appeal, making the judgment final, and told prosecutors to stop him from escaping.

The Taipei District Prosecutors Office said that they told the police to arrest Chou Ke-chi tonight. He will be sent to prison according to the law.

According to the judgment from the Taipei District Court (first trial), Chou Ke-chi and a man named Lan wanted to run together for the 16th president and vice president. In 2023, they applied to register with the Central Election Commission by collecting signatures. After paying a deposit, the Central Election Commission announced them as candidates for the 16th president and vice president who needed signatures.

On October 21 and 22, 2023, Chou Ke-chi held campaign events and set up tables for collecting signatures. He told staff to ask people to sign the petition. Besides giving out items like rice wine and toothbrushes, he also allegedly gave 200 NT dollars to people who signed. More than 20 people in total received money.

In the first trial, Chou Ke-chi was sentenced to 3 years in prison and a fine of 2 million NT dollars for breaking the law against giving bribes in presidential and vice presidential elections. The case went to appeal, and the Taiwan High Court heard it in the second trial. During the second trial, Chou Ke-chi asked to stop the trial and asked for a constitutional interpretation.

The second court believed that the rules about giving bribes in presidential and vice presidential elections were not against the constitution. They also thought that the first court's judgment was correct in facts, law, and sentencing. So, they rejected the appeal and kept the original judgment. After another appeal, the Supreme Court today rejected it, making the entire case final. Prosecutors were also told to stop him from escaping.

Also, Chou Ke-chi is suspected of taking money from the Chinese Communist Party to hold events for the Republican Party, protest, and promote political influence in Taiwan. He is accused of breaking the Anti-Infiltration Act. In June this year, the Taipei District Court sentenced Chou Ke-chi to 2 years in prison for this. This case is now being heard by the High Court in the second trial. (Edited by Yang Chao-yen) 1140730
