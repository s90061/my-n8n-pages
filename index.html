Apple Stock Rises, Helping US Stock Indexes Close Higher, Nasdaq Leads

New company reports in the US were mostly very good. Also, Apple Inc. said it would invest 100 billion dollars to make things in the US. After this news, Apple's stock price went up by more than 5%. This helped the three main US stock indexes end the day higher. The Nasdaq index went up the most, by more than 1%.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went up by 81.38 points, which is 0.18%. It closed at 44193.12 points.

The S&P 500 index went up by 45.87 points, which is 0.73%. It closed at 6345.06 points.

The Nasdaq index went up by 252.88 points, which is 1.21%. It closed at 21169.43 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index went down by 11.39 points, which is 0.20%. It closed at 5550.30 points.

Traders Watch Trump's Next Move on Russia, Oil Prices Fall for 5th Straight Day

Oil prices went down for the fifth day in a row today. This happened because traders are waiting to see if US President Trump will take stronger actions to limit Russia's energy exports.

In New York, the price of West Texas Intermediate crude oil for September delivery fell by 1.2%. It closed at 64.35 US dollars per barrel.

In London, the price of North Sea Brent crude oil for October delivery fell by 1.1%. It closed at 66.89 US dollars per barrel.

CNBC: Jensen Huang Meets Trump at White House Again, Discussion Content Unclear

CNBC, an American financial news company, reported that Jensen Huang, the CEO of NVIDIA, a big company that makes AI chips, visited the White House today. He met with US President Trump. It is not clear what they talked about.

In late January this year, Trump and Huang also met at the White House. People who know about it said they talked about a new Chinese company called "DeepSeek."
