Taiwan Advances to Little League World Series Finals

The Dongyuan Elementary School baseball team from Taipei City is representing the Asia-Pacific region in the Williamsport Little League World Series. Early this morning, they won against the Aruba team, who represents the Caribbean region, with a score of 1-0. They will play against the American group champions early tomorrow morning to compete for the overall championship.

The Taiwanese team played against Aruba, the Caribbean representative, for the second time. After a difficult game, they won the international group title and earned a spot in the final championship game.

12 Migrants Missing After Jumping From Boat Near Balearic Islands

Spanish officials said today that 12 migrants disappeared after jumping from a boat. This happened while they were traveling to the Balearic Islands, a popular tourist spot in the Mediterranean Sea.

According to AFP, Spanish officers reported that 14 other migrants from the same boat were saved. They were found 58 kilometers southeast of Cabrera Island.

In a statement, officials said that the rescued migrants told them 12 passengers jumped into the sea on August 22nd.

The statement also added that the Spanish Civil Guard and Coast Guard are now looking for these missing people, who are from North Africa.

Data from the Spanish Interior Ministry shows that the number of migrants going from North Africa to the Balearic Islands has increased a lot this year.

By August 15th, 4,323 migrants had arrived at these islands since the beginning of the year. This is 77% more than the 2,443 migrants who arrived in the same period in 2024.

India Stops Sending Packages to US Due to Trump's Tariff Rules

The Indian government announced today that it will stop sending packages to the United States. This decision comes because of problems caused by President Donald Trump's tariff rules.

AFP reported that India made this announcement less than a week before new rules from the Trump administration take effect. These new rules will remove special tax exemptions for some small packages.

India's Ministry of Communications said that the US authorities have not given enough information about important processes. These processes include how to collect tariffs (taxes) and how to handle money transfers. Because of this, India will stop related services.

The Indian Postal Department stated that starting August 25th, they will stop accepting "all types of postal items" sent to the US. However, letters, documents, and gifts worth less than 100 US dollars (about 3,036 New Taiwan dollars) are still allowed.

Many European postal and delivery companies have also done similar things. For example, La Poste, the French postal service, recently said it would stop sending packages to the US starting August 25th.

Based on an order signed by Trump, starting August 29th, packages sent to the US through channels other than the international postal network will have all normal tariffs applied if they are worth 800 US dollars or less.
