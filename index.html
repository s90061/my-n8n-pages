Apple Updates iOS 26 and Other Software, Adds AI Live Translation and Visual Intelligence

Today, Apple updated its software platforms like iOS 26, iPadOS 26, macOS Tahoe, watchOS 26, tvOS 26, and visionOS 26. They now use a new design called Liquid Glass. New features from the personal smart system, Apple Intelligence, are also added. These include live translation, smart vision for screen content, and smart actions in "Shortcuts."

Apple explained on its official website that the new Liquid Glass design is now on all its software platforms for the first time. It keeps each platform's special parts but makes them feel more similar. Liquid Glass is a new see-through material. It reflects and bends light from around it. It also changes to show content more clearly.

Apple Intelligence helps users talk easily across different languages. It does this with live translation in Messages, FaceTime, Phone, and AirPods. The "Visual Intelligence" update lets users take a screenshot of what they see on their screen. Then they can ask questions about it to the ChatGPT chatbot. They can also use Google and other apps to find similar pictures.

Apple Intelligence has more new features. Users can now combine emojis to make new "Genmoji" emojis. They can also use ChatGPT in "Image Playground" to find new image styles. Apple Intelligence also powers the "Workout Buddy" feature. This gives personal voice encouragement during workouts.

iOS 26 brings more ways to customize the "Lock Screen." This includes adaptive time display and 3D space scenes. It also improves apps like Camera, Photos, Safari, and Phone. "Call Screening" can filter calls from unknown numbers. "Hold Assist" can hold your place in line when you wait for a real person to answer a customer service call.

iOS 26 also adds "Lyric Translation and Pronunciation" to Apple Music. It adds "Visited Places" to Apple Maps. It also offers order tracking in Apple Wallet. Updates to AirPods let creators record with great sound quality. They can also control the Camera app remotely.

The new menu bar in iPadOS 26 lets users open in-app commands. They can do this by swiping down from the top of the screen or moving the cursor to the top. The "Files" app offers new ways to manage files and make your own folders. Also, folders can now be placed on the Dock. This lets users get to downloaded files or documents easily, anywhere, anytime.

macOS Tahoe makes Mac computers much better for work. It offers more ways to personalize your Mac. This includes an updated "Control Center" and new colors for folders, app icons, and widgets. The menu bar is fully clear, making the screen look wider. Spotlight has a big update. It now has new ways to look at files and apps and better search. "Shortcuts" can use Apple Intelligence to do many complex tasks automatically.

watchOS 26 offers more ways to personalize. A new "Sleep Score" feature is on Apple Watch. It helps users understand their sleep quality better. The new system has "Smart Stack" alerts. These give useful tips right away. "Live Translation" in Messages can automatically translate text into the user's chosen language. A new one-hand wrist flip gesture can skip notifications, silence calls, stop alarms, and go back to the watch face.

tvOS 26 has an "Apple Music Sing" karaoke feature. It can turn an iPhone into a wireless microphone for Apple TV. Friends can also use their iPhones to choose songs. FaceTime's "Contact Posters" make it easier for users to start calls in their living room.

visionOS 26 offers small tools that fit perfectly into the user's surroundings. They appear automatically every time you wear the Apple Vision Pro spatial computer. The spatial browsing feature changes how articles look in the Safari browser. Developers can now put 3D objects directly into web pages. visionOS 26 also adds support for playing 180-degree, 360-degree, and wide-view sports camera content. This includes certain Insta360, GoPro, and Canon models.

German "Rust Belt" Voters More Worried, Expert Says: Could Become Stronghold for AfD Party

The election results in Germany's North Rhine-Westphalia state are mostly final. The ruling Christian Democratic Union (CDU) got the most support. They stopped the Alternative for Germany (AfD) party, which has grown in recent years. But experts say the AfD got three times more votes in the Ruhr industrial area, Germany's "Rust Belt." This shows that voters are more and more worried about immigration and a bad economy. The "Rust Belt" might become a strong support area for the AfD in the future.

North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany's most populated state, held local elections on September 14. In 53 cities and districts, the ruling party, CDU, led in more than 30 areas. The Green Party lost many votes. But they still did well in young university cities and big cities like MÃ¼nster, Cologne, and Bonn.

Norbert Kersting, a political science professor at MÃ¼nster University, said today in an online meeting with reporters that this election is very important for Berlin. It is the biggest local election after the German federal government has been in power for 100 days.

Before the election, people wondered if the AfD would get more power locally. The results show that older parties like the CDU and the Social Democratic Party (SPD) lost some votes. But they still stopped the AfD from gaining more control.

Kersting said that the AfD could not put forward candidates in many cities. They did not have enough people to work at the local level. Also, their policies only talked about immigration for a long time. They did not talk about local issues like transport and housing. This is why the AfD did not do as well in North Rhine-Westphalia as they did in national elections.

However, in Ruhr area cities like Duisburg, Gelsenkirchen, and Hagen, the AfD's votes went up a lot. Experts think this is an important sign.

The Ruhr industrial area used to be the heart of Germany. It grew because of coal and steel. Working-class people traditionally supported the Social Democratic Party (SPD), which is center-left. As German industries changed, many factories closed. This caused some small towns in the Ruhr area to lose jobs and people.

In the local elections on September 15, the AfD got almost 30% of the votes in Gelsenkirchen. This is more than three times what they got before. Their support is now close to the SPD, which has traditionally been the biggest party there.

Ansgar Hudde, a sociologist from Cologne University, said that the SPD keeps losing support from traditional working-class voters. Many loyal voters would rather not vote than vote for the SPD again.

Kersting believes that the AfD attracts more than just working-class people in the Ruhr area. They also attract "fearful citizens" who become extreme because they are afraid. These people are very worried about changes in society, immigration, and a bad economy. This worry turns into strong political anger.

The AfD does not have much experience running local governments. But they can grow slowly in the "Rust Belt" by making people's worries bigger. This challenges the older parties. Kersting expects this support to grow even more. This is because local governments have less money and the economy is slow. This will happen especially in West Germany, which was not traditionally their strong area.

Next March, local elections will start in Bavaria, South Germany. In September next year, the state parliament election will be held in Saxony-Anhalt, Central Germany. The latest surveys show that the AfD now has 40% support in Saxony-Anhalt. This is more than the ruling CDU party.

Kersting said that the election results in North Rhine-Westphalia gave the ruling CDU and SPD parties "a relatively safe pass." This gives the federal government six months without big elections to worry about. They can now focus on "autumn reform" plans. These include changes to citizen benefits, pensions, and healthcare.

Kersting warned that major parties must offer better solutions for immigration and social inequality. Only then can they stop extreme right-wing groups from getting stronger in "Rust Belt" areas, where factories have closed.
