Disaster Recovery Work Can Cause Aches; Doctors Advise Not to Ignore Muscle Care

Recently, southern Taiwan has had strong winds and floods. Doctors have seen that many people who are helping with disaster recovery work feel sore or even get hurt. Doctors say that people should rest and see a doctor if needed. Also, they should exercise their muscles regularly to avoid getting "sarcopenia," which is when you lose muscle.

Today, Chi Mei Hospital held an event called "Exercise to Build Muscle, Dads Stay Strong!" in their main hall. They had a special area where expert trainers and dietitians gave advice to people. They also taught people how to use elastic bands to make their muscles stronger.

Mr. Kuo Hsing-dao, the vice president of Chi Mei Hospital, spoke at the event. He said that after the recent winds and floods, many people were busy helping with rescue efforts and cleaning up. Many volunteers and workers went into the affected areas. During this hard work, some people got muscle aches, and many also got injured.

Mr. Kuo Hsing-dao said that fathers, especially, are very important to their families. Many fathers might not exercise or take care of their bodies often. So, when they suddenly have to do a lot more physical work, they can get tired easily. He hopes this event will help everyone pay more attention to keeping their muscles strong by exercising and eating well to prepare or get better.

Dr. Su Chiung-jui, a doctor in the Rehabilitation Department at Chi Mei Hospital, said that people start losing muscle after age 30. They lose about 3% to 8% every 10 years. After age 70, this loss can happen even faster. This is called "sarcopenia." It not only makes you feel less strong but also affects how you walk and balance. It can also make it more likely for you to fall, break bones, and become unable to do things. A survey by the Health Promotion Administration shows that more than 34% of people over 65 years old have sarcopenia.

Dr. Su Chiung-jui said that sarcopenia can be stopped or even improved. With the right exercise and food, people can get better. Studies show that if older people do muscle-strengthening exercises at least twice a week and eat enough protein, they can keep their muscles strong and move better. Using elastic bands for exercise is safe, easy to start, and can really help your core muscles. It's a very good way to begin exercising.

He added that some patients he sees have worked for many years lifting heavy things. This has caused them to get injuries, so they are afraid to exercise. As a result, their muscles get weaker and weaker. Doctors can use ultrasound to find the injured areas. After treating and fixing the injured parts, patients can go to the exercise center to build muscle. This helps them get better and slowly return to their normal lives. He advises people with similar problems to see a doctor early.

Taipei City Tries Electric-Assist Dispatch Vehicles to Improve YouBike "Tidal" Problem at NTU

To fix the problem at National Taiwan University (NTU) where YouBike stations often have no bikes to rent or no empty spots to return bikes, like the changing tides, Taipei City has started a new project. Starting today, they are trying out "electric-assist micro-dispatch vehicles." These vehicles are expected to move more than 400 YouBike bicycles each day.

The Taipei City Department of Transportation said that NTU is the only university in Taipei City with YouBike stations on its campus. And many people use them there. The campus has 59 stations, and over 15,000 bikes are rented each day. This is about 6% of all YouBike rentals in the entire city.

The Department of Transportation explained that because students have regular class times, YouBike stations on campus often run out of bikes or empty spots during certain hours. To solve this, they worked with the university and the YouBike company to create the electric-assist micro-dispatch vehicles. These vehicles are more flexible than trucks and are good for the environment. They started testing them today.

Mr. Ho Yu-jen, the general manager of the YouBike company, said at a press conference that NTU already had 4 trucks and 40 staff members to move bikes around the campus. But it still wasn't enough. So, to meet the university's needs, they specially developed the electric-assist dispatch vehicle that can carry 7 YouBike bicycles at once. They also hired more part-time workers. They hope this will help meet students' needs.

Mr. Ho Yu-jen said that they plan to test the electric-assist dispatch vehicles for about six months. They are starting with 2 vehicles. During this time, they will watch how they work and make improvements. Then, they will decide if they need to add more vehicles.

Mr. Hsieh Ming-hung, the Commissioner of the Department of Transportation, said that the 2 electric-assist dispatch vehicles are expected to move an extra 400 to 500 YouBike bicycles daily. This will definitely make moving bikes more efficient compared to only using trucks. He hopes that when school starts, students will have an even more convenient service.

NTU said that YouBike has become an important green way to get around for people all over Taiwan. They thanked the city government for its help. They believe that the electric-assist dispatch vehicles, which are more flexible, safer, and better for the environment than trucks, will be a good solution for the YouBike problem on campus. They also hope to keep working with the city government to make things even better in the future.
