TSG Hawks sign a new foreign pitcher from the MLB named Namasia

The TSG Hawks baseball team in Taiwan announced today that they have signed a new foreign pitcher, Dinelson Lamet, who has played over 100 games in Major League Baseball (MLB). His jersey number will be 45. Lamet played 121 games in the MLB. Last year, he was teammates with the Japanese star Shohei Ohtani when they were both with the Dodgers team.

The TSG Hawks team said today that they signed the MLB pitcher Namasia to make their pitching stronger. Namasia is 33 years old. He has played for the Padres, Rockies, Red Sox, Dodgers, and Royals in the MLB. Last year, he played with Japanese star Shohei Ohtani on the Dodgers.

During his time in MLB, Namasia played in a total of 121 games. He started 60 of those games. His record was 17 wins and 24 losses. He pitched for 367.2 innings and got 474 strikeouts. His ERA was 4.63.

Namasia was a full-time starting pitcher in 2017, 2019, and 2020. In 2020, he started 12 games in the MLB and pitched a total of 69 innings. His ERA was very low at 2.09. He only allowed 39 hits and got a lot of strikeouts, 93 in total. Last year, Namasia still played in the MLB. This year, he has been playing in the Mexican League. He has started 17 games there. His record is 7 wins and 5 losses, with an ERA of 5.01. He pitched for 88 innings and got 88 strikeouts.

The second half of the Chinese Professional Baseball League (CPBL) started on July 4 this year. The TSG Hawks currently have 7 wins, 6 losses, and 2 ties in the second half. Their winning rate is 53.8%, and they are in second place.

The Ministry of Transportation helps people fix their homes by connecting them with companies.

Typhoon Danas and strong winds from the southwest recently affected southern Taiwan. Many people's homes were badly damaged. The Ministry of Transportation said today that there are not enough workers or materials right now. So, they have asked different parts of the ministry to connect people with companies. They hope to help residents find workers to fix their homes.

The Ministry of Transportation wrote on Facebook today that Vice Minister Chen Yen-po held a meeting yesterday. He brought together different parts of the ministry to check the progress of repairs in the Tainan disaster areas and decide what to do next. They have already visited over 100 damaged homes. They are also looking at all available workers and resources. They want to do their best to help people rebuild their homes quickly.

The Ministry of Transportation said that people cannot find workers to do repairs because there are not enough workers, materials, or machines. The government has asked all ministries to help. They have also given each ministry an area to take care of. The Ministry of Transportation is responsible for the Xinying, Yanshui, Baihe, and Houbi areas in Tainan City.

The Ministry of Transportation said that people who want to repair their homes can first register with their district chief. Then, the Ministry of Transportation will connect different parts of the ministry to find companies. After that, residents will talk with the companies. Once they agree, they will sign a simple contract. The Ministry of Transportation's main job is to connect people with companies; it does not pay money for the repairs.

To make the rebuilding after the disaster faster, the Ministry of Transportation has also set rules for action. Besides connecting people with companies, they will first help old, single-story houses and houses with roofs blown off that people cannot live in anymore. This is immediate help. Also, waste materials like asbestos tiles should be gathered in one place. The district office will then help remove them.

Shigeru Ishiba sends sympathy for floods in central and southern Taiwan; Lin Chia-lung thanks him and hopes for cooperation to face challenges.

Recently, heavy rain caused disasters in Taiwan. Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba wrote a message to show his sympathy to Taiwan. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs said today that Foreign Minister Lin Chia-lung thanked Japan again for its care. He also hopes that Taiwan and Japan can work together to face challenges. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Taiwan's office in Japan have both sincerely thanked the Japanese government.

Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba wrote a message to express sympathy to Taiwan. He said, "I want to express my sadness for those who died and their families. I also express my true sympathy to those affected by the disaster. I pray that daily life will return to normal soon. Taiwan, keep strong!" The Japan-Taiwan Exchange Association shared this message on Facebook in the afternoon.

Because of this, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs released a press statement in the afternoon. It said that the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Taiwan's office in Japan immediately thanked the Japanese government.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs said that Shigeru Ishiba also sent a message on July 12. He showed sympathy for the damage caused by Typhoon Danas in Taiwan. This shows the true and deep friendship between Taiwan and Japan. The Taiwanese government and all parts of society continue to fully help with disaster relief and recovery work. They are helping people affected by the disaster rebuild their homes and return to normal life soon.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs said that Foreign Minister Lin Chia-lung again expressed heartfelt thanks for the Japanese government's warm care and true support. He also hopes that Taiwan and Japan will continue to work more closely together in the future. He wants to build a closer, friendlier, and stronger partnership to face all challenges together.
