Taiwan Stock Market Goes Up by 52.80 Points

The Taipei stock market closed today with a rise of 52.80 points. The market closed at 24547.38 points, which is a 0.22% increase. The total money traded was 433.558 billion New Taiwan Dollars.

The main index started the day at 24610.85 points. During the day, it went as high as 24729.96 points and as low as 24547.38 points. The index without financial stocks went up by 65.47 points, reaching 21529.04 points.

Here are the changes for the eight main types of stocks: Ceramics and bricks stocks fell by 0.91%. Food stocks went up by 0.79%. Plastics and chemicals stocks rose by 0.60%. Textile stocks increased by 0.46%. Electrical and mechanical stocks went up by 0.32%. Paper stocks rose by 0.12%. Construction stocks fell by 2.19%. Financial stocks fell by 0.57%.

The number of buy orders was 20,493,081 units. The number of sell orders was 11,413,186 units. The number of traded units was 7,701,863.

At the end of trading, 505 companies saw their stock prices go up, 441 companies saw their prices go down, and 123 companies stayed the same.

The top 5 most traded stocks were Winbond, Nanya Technology, CSBC Corporation, KYEC, and Yuanta Taiwan High Dividend ETF.

The top 5 stocks that gained the most were Chunghwa Telecom Security, Apex, Aces Electronic, Weltrend, and T-Cat.

The top 5 stocks that lost the most were Zyxel, Eden, Actron, Hi-Life, and Shin Ba Ba.

Green Party Says Mayor Lu Left the Biggest Pile of Trash in Taiwan; Taichung City Says Landfill is Not Full

News reports recently said that the Dali landfill is full and causing problems for citizens. Today, Mr. Cho Kuan-ting, a spokesperson for the Democratic Progressive Party (DPP), said that Taichung Mayor Lu Shiow-yen has been missing and has left the biggest pile of trash in Taiwan during her 8 years in office. The Taichung Environmental Protection Bureau (EPB) replied that the landfill follows environmental rules and is not full. They also said that after the waste incinerator is repaired, the trash can be moved back to be burned.

Mr. Wu Tseng and Mr. Cho Kuan-ting, spokespersons for the DPP, held a press conference this morning. Mr. Cho Kuan-ting said that the media showed on September 1st that the Dali landfill was full. After that, the DPP group in the Taichung City Council held more press conferences. But Mayor Lu has been quiet for 7 days and has not responded.

Mr. Cho Kuan-ting explained that before the previous Taichung Mayor Lin Chia-lung left office, the Dali landfill had only 7,000 tons of trash. In 2019, when Mayor Lu started her first year, it reached 37,000 tons. Now, it has grown to 340,000 tons, which is 40% of all the open-air trash piles in Taiwan's 22 cities and counties.

Mr. Cho Kuan-ting pointed out that as early as 2018, when Mr. Lin Chia-lung was mayor, the city had already set aside a budget of 1 billion New Taiwan Dollars to improve the Wenshan Incinerator. At that time, it was expected to be finished by 2021. If Mayor Lu had continued this plan when she started, the trash problem could have been solved easily. But sadly, after Mayor Lu took office, she changed the previous plan. She said it would be changed to a BOT (Build-Operate-Transfer) project. The cost for the project grew a lot, to 8.5 billion New Taiwan Dollars. So, during her 8 years in office, she left the biggest pile of trash in Taiwan.

Mr. Wu Tseng said that audit reports had warned every year that Taichung City's incinerators could not handle enough trash, and the landfill was also running out of space. Taichung's three incinerators are all old and need to be updated. But because Mayor Lu kept changing her mind between ROT (Rehabilitate-Operate-Transfer) and BOT plans, the Wenshan Incinerator project only started looking for companies this year. It is expected to be finished in 2032.

In response, the Taichung Environmental Protection Bureau said that the Dali landfill follows strict environmental rules and is not full. They also use it as a place to temporarily store trash when the incinerators are closed for yearly repairs. When the incinerator repairs are done, the trash from the landfill can be moved back to be burned. They call this "in and out."

The Environmental Protection Bureau stated that they started moving trash from the Dali landfill back to the incinerator storage in 2023. The Minister of Environment, Mr. Peng Chi-ming, even visited and said that the handling was good. It was not listed in the national meeting about uncovered trash.

The Environmental Protection Bureau said that to get rid of household trash faster, they are improving the incinerators to handle more trash. They also temporarily move trash to the landfill. While doing this, they follow strict environmental rules. They cover the trash with special recycled ash from burning or use nets to stop it from spreading. This makes sure that the environment around is kept clean.

3-Story House in Luzhou, New Taipei, Collapses While Being Taken Down, Scaffolding is Crushed; No One Was Hurt

A 3-story house on Guanghua Road, Lane 150, in Luzhou District, New Taipei City, was being taken down. But suddenly, the floor on the third story fell down. It also crushed the scaffolding. Luckily, no one was hurt or trapped when it happened. The Public Works Department has been told to send people to check the site and find out exactly why it collapsed.

The Luzhou Police Station in New Taipei sent a message to explain. They said that today at about 10 AM, a 3-story house that was being demolished suddenly had its floor collapse. Quickly, dirt and dust filled the air, and the scaffolding and power lines were pushed down.

The police said that after they got the report, they sent officers to block off the area to keep people safe. They checked with the construction team and confirmed that no one was trapped or hurt at the site. The head of Luzhou District Office, Mr. Chung Yao-lei, also came to see what happened. He told the Public Works Department to send staff to the site to check and find out the reason for the collapse and decide what to do next.
