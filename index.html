Baseball Asian Championship: Lin Sheng-en Strikes Out 10 in 4 Innings, Taiwan Wins First Game Against Palestine

Taiwan played against Palestine in their first game of the Asian Baseball Championship today. The Taiwanese team won 14-0 in 7 innings. The pitcher, Lin Sheng-en, played very well, striking out 10 batters in 4 innings. Lin Yu-li also hit 3 times and helped score 3 runs. Tomorrow evening, Taiwan will play against South Korea.

The Asian Baseball Championship is happening in Pingtan, China. Taiwan is in Group B with Palestine, Hong Kong, and South Korea. Today, they played their first game against Palestine.

Taiwan started scoring early in the game. In the first inning, after one player was out, Pan Chun-yu hit a triple. Chen Min-tzu then got a free walk to first base. With a double by Kao Yu-wei, Taiwan scored its first run. Yang Chen-yu then hit another single, adding one more run.

In the second inning, Taiwan started scoring again. Lin Yu-li hit a single and then stole second base. Chen Hsiao-yun hit another single, and Taiwan scored its third run with a ground ball.

In the fourth inning, Taiwan filled all the bases with hits from Lin Yu-li, Chen Hsiao-yun, and Pan Chun-yu. Chen Min-tzu hit a single, bringing in one run. After two players were out, two more runs were scored because of free walks. Tsai Chih-yu hit a single to add a run. Tsai Wei-tai also got a free walk, bringing in another run. Lin Yu-li then hit a double, bringing in three more runs and making Taiwan's lead much bigger.

Taiwan scored more runs in the fifth inning. They filled all the bases with a hit-by-pitch and free walks. Lee Yi-wei then hit a fly ball that let one more run score. In the sixth inning, they scored three more runs with several hits in a row.

Taiwan's starting pitcher today was Lin Sheng-en, who plays for the Cincinnati Reds in America. At the start of the game, his pitching was a little difficult. He gave two free walks in a row. But he quickly got better, striking out three batters in a row and stopping the other team from scoring. In the second inning, he hit the first batter, but the runner was caught trying to steal a base. Lin Sheng-en then struck out two more batters and did not let any runs score.

Lin Sheng-en got better as he pitched. In the third and fourth innings, he got three batters out quickly. In total, he pitched for 4 innings, using 65 pitches. He did not let the other team hit any balls. He struck out 10 batters and gave 3 free walks, but he did not let any runs score. He won the game.

In the fifth inning, Taiwan used other pitchers. Lo Meng-yang from CTBC Brothers, Lee Chih-lin from Wei Chuan Dragons, and Wang Yu-chieh from Taipower each pitched one inning. They did not let any runs score, helping the team win. All four Taiwanese pitchers together struck out 13 batters. The other team only hit one ball and did not score any runs.

Hong Kong, September 22nd â€“ A very strong typhoon named Koinu is coming close to Hong Kong. Hong Kong International Airport said today that the airport will stay open and work during the typhoon. However, many flights will be cancelled or changed tomorrow evening.

The weather office said earlier that typhoon Koinu is very large. It will pass south of Hong Kong. The wind will be very strong, like a storm, at levels 8 to 9. Near the coast and on high ground, it could be even stronger, like a typhoon, at level 11. There will also be very big waves in the sea.

Germany's New Representative: Taiwan Shows That Democracy Is Important to Everyone, After Seeing Hong Kong's Freedom Shrink

Taipei, September 22nd â€“ The new German representative in Taiwan, Dr. JÃ¶rg Polster, spoke to the Central News Agency. He said that Taiwan is becoming more important in many areas. Because of this, the German Institute Taipei is getting bigger. Among all German offices in the Asia-Pacific region, only the one in Taiwan is adding more staff. Dr. Polster also talked about his time working in Hong Kong. He said he saw Hong Kong's freedoms become smaller. After that, he found democratic Taiwan very inspiring, showing that democracy is important for everyone.

Dr. JÃ¶rg Polster became the new German Representative in Taiwan and Director General of the German Institute Taipei in mid-August. He recently gave his first interview since coming to Taiwan to the Central News Agency. He talked about his main goals, which are to make Germany's relationship with Taiwan wider, deeper, and stronger.

Dr. Polster said that even though the German Foreign Office has less money, the German Institute Taipei is still growing. Taiwan is the only place in the Asia-Pacific region where Germany is adding more staff to its office. By January next year, four new people will join, two of whom will be senior political staff. This shows how important the relationship between Germany and Taiwan is. Dr. Polster hopes this will help more people in Taiwan know about Germany.

Dr. Polster explained why they are adding staff now. He said that for almost 10 years, the German Institute Taipei did not have enough people. Also, Taiwan is becoming more important in many fields like semiconductors. Both countries are working together in microelectronics, artificial intelligence (AI), and hydrogen energy, involving businesses, government, and universities. Germany has seen this and is now fixing the situation.

In September 2024, two German warships sailed through the Taiwan Strait. This was the first time German navy ships had done this in 22 years. About this, Dr. Polster said that the Taiwan Strait is international water, and all countries have the right to sail there. He also said that Germany wants to keep international rules that are based on law.

Dr. Polster said clearly that sailing through the Taiwan Strait should not be a problem. All countries should follow international rules. He also mentioned that Germany released its "China Strategy" in 2023. This strategy is a response to changes in the political world and is not a secret anymore. The "China Strategy" explains the dangers to peace and stability in this area, including depending too much on China. Germany's work with Taiwan fits this strategy.

Dr. Polster also said that Germany wants to lower risks and have many different places to get goods from (diverse supply chains). Taiwan is a very good partner because it shares the same values as Germany: freedom, democracy, and the rule of law. Everyone knows that countries should not depend on only one country, "especially not on countries that have different ideas about international rules based on law."

Before coming to Taiwan, Dr. Polster worked in Hong Kong from 2015 to 2018. During that time, he saw the end of the Umbrella Movement in Hong Kong. He also saw how Hong Kong's freedom and democracy became smaller.

"Working in Hong Kong was very hard," Dr. Polster said sadly. When he arrived, Hong Kong still had freedom of the press, freedom to protest, freedom of speech, and some voting rights. He even met with student leaders from the Umbrella Movement. These students believed Hong Kong could be an example of democracy for China. Some students even became members of the Legislative Council.

Dr. Polster said that later, these students were seen as enemies of the country. Some lawmakers lost their positions, and some were even arrested. Because of this, he could not say goodbye to these students before he left. This affected him a lot. He said he "basically saw a free society change into a society where people were afraid to use their legal freedoms."

Dr. Polster said that his experience in Hong Kong helped him understand his work in Taiwan. He deeply understands that people in Taiwan see what happened in Hong Kong as a warning about "One Country, Two Systems." He saw clearly what happened in Hong Kong, and this made him want to come to Taiwan. It also helps him understand how Taiwanese people feel about Hong Kong.

When asked if "One Country, Two Systems" is possible, Dr. Polster carefully said, "From what I saw, it did not work in Hong Kong."

Dr. Polster emphasized that democratic Taiwan is very encouraging. He is happy to see that people in Taiwan value democracy and the rule of law. This shows that these are not just Western values but values that all people should have.
