High Temperatures of 33 to 35 Degrees Expected, Watch Out for Afternoon Thunderstorms

The Weather Agency said that today, the weather will be mostly sunny to cloudy across Taiwan. In the afternoon, there will be short thunderstorms in the western part of Taiwan and in the mountains of eastern Taiwan. There is still a chance of heavy rain in some mountain areas. For temperatures, most places will be between 33 and 35 degrees Celsius. Remember to drink a lot of water if you are outside.

The temperatures across Taiwan will mostly be between 33 and 35 degrees Celsius. However, in the greater Taipei area, central and southern Taiwan, and Hualien, temperatures might go above 36 degrees Celsius in some spots. Please drink plenty of water if you are doing activities outside.

For the islands, Penghu will be cloudy with short rain, with temperatures between 27 and 32 degrees Celsius. Kinmen will be cloudy with short rain, with temperatures between 27 and 32 degrees Celsius. Matsu will be sunny to cloudy, with temperatures between 27 and 33 degrees Celsius. In the morning, Matsu might have strong winds, with average speeds of level 6 or higher, or gusts of level 8 or higher.

The Weather Agency also wants to remind people that this is the time of the annual big tides. In coastal areas from Changhua to Chiayi and in Hualien, the water level will be high during high tide. This might cause seawater to flow into land. In low-lying coastal areas, please be careful of drainage problems and local flooding. It is best to avoid staying near the coast, in ports, or in intertidal zones (areas covered by water at high tide and exposed at low tide).

Also, Typhoon Tapah, which was the 16th tropical storm and located near Guangdong, became a weaker tropical depression last night at 8 PM. It will not affect Taiwan's weather.

According to the Environmental Protection Agency's air quality forecast, the wind today will blow from the south to southwest. Northern areas are downwind, which means air pollution might build up there more easily. The air quality in Hsinchu-Miaoli, Central, Yunlin-Chiayi-Tainan, Kaohsiung-Pingtung, Yilan, Hualien-Taitung areas, and on Matsu, Kinmen, and Penghu islands is expected to be "Good." The air quality in the northern area is expected to be "Normal."

Heathrow Airport Terminal 4 Reopens After Safety Concern

Tonight, Terminal 4 at Heathrow Airport in the UK was temporarily closed. This was because of a "possible dangerous item incident" in the check-in area. A few hours later, airport officials announced that the terminal was "safe and open again."

Heathrow Airport wrote on social media: "Emergency services have confirmed that Terminal 4 is safe and can reopen. We are working hard to make sure all flights leave as planned today."

The London Fire Brigade said that the situation was cleared after about three hours. They had received a call about a "possible dangerous item incident" at the terminal.

The Fire Brigade also said that medical staff from the London Ambulance Service looked after about 20 people at the airport.

Heathrow Airport is the main international airport in the UK and a very busy place for flights around the world. Terminal 4 is mostly used for flights to Europe and for long-distance journeys.

Later, the Metropolitan Police Service said in a statement that "no signs of any dangerous items were found."

Even though Terminal 4 was closed for a short time, the other terminals at Heathrow Airport continued to work as normal during the incident.

The airport added: "We are very sorry for any trouble this caused. The safety of our passengers and our colleagues is our most important goal."
