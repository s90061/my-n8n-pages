Taiwan Stock Market Rises to New High of 26761 Points; TSMC Reaches 1400 NT Dollars

Today, the Taiwan stock market opened strong and kept going up, passing 26700 points. This was mainly because of TSMC, a very important company in the market. Companies in artificial intelligence (AI), military industry, and memory chips also went up together. By the end of the day, the stock market index reached its highest point today at 26761.06 points. It went up by 382.67 points, setting a new record. The market increased by 1.45%, and the total money traded was 463.09 billion New Taiwan Dollars. This week, the Taiwan stock market grew a lot, by 1180.74 points. This is the sixth week in a row that it has gone up.

The index for electronic stocks closed at 1583.06 points today, rising by 2.08%. The semiconductor index closed at 815.53 points, rising by 2.38%. Both of these also set new historical records.

TSMC, a leading company, saw its stock price go up early because of positive news from its investor meeting. Many people bought its shares. Today, its stock closed at a new high price of 1400 NT dollars. It went up by 35 NT dollars, a 2.56% increase.

True Yoga Fitness Closes Down and Goes Bankrupt; Lawmakers Ask Government to Investigate Possible Illegal Actions

True Yoga announced on September 30 that it would stop operating from October 1st. The company will also start the process to close down, dissolve, and go bankrupt. Several lawmakers and city council members held a press conference today. They said that the legal representative of True Yoga left Taiwan in January 2020 and has not returned. They suspect that this person might have run away without caring about customers' rights. They are asking the government to investigate immediately to see if any illegal actions were committed.

This morning, lawmakers Fan Yun and Wang Mei-hui from the Democratic Progressive Party, along with Taipei City Council members Ho Meng-hua and Miao Po-ya, and New Taipei City Council member Chang Wei-chien, and Taoyuan City Council member Huang Chiung-hui, held a press conference in the Legislative Yuan. The conference was titled "True Yoga Suddenly Closes! Who Will Protect the Rights of Tens of Thousands of Victims?"

"True Yoga Fitness," a gym chain with many branches across Taiwan, suddenly announced it was closing down. This has harmed the rights of tens of thousands of members and instructors nationwide. A group for victims has quickly grown to over 3,500 people.

Fan Yun explained that True Yoga was the largest yoga chain in Taiwan. Now that it has suddenly announced its closure, members will find it hard to get back their pre-paid annual fees and class payments. Also, many instructors and employees have been fired and not paid. Many suppliers are also owed money by True Yoga, causing many problems. This situation reminds people of the bad closure of Alexander Fitness. It shows that this is not just one customer problem, but a bigger problem with how the fitness industry is supervised and how consumer protection works. The government department in charge should offer help right away, check on the refund guarantees, and plan legal help for victims and ways to solve work disagreements. This is to protect the rights of tens of thousands of victims.

Ho Meng-hua said that by law, there is only 210 million NT dollars available in the trust account. However, the total amount of money that customers are trying to get back could be over 420 million NT dollars. Suppliers can only get 50% of their money back. The True Yoga management team has not shown up, and even their refund email cannot be reached. The government should fully review how customers are protected in "pre-paid transactions" to stop them from becoming victims again and again.

Huang Chiung-hui also stated that the Taoyuan City Government's consumer protection officer should get involved actively. She also asked the Labor Bureau to properly handle the labor disputes involved and protect the basic rights of workers. A harmed supplier complained that True Yoga previously owed them over 2 million NT dollars for construction work. The company even replied, "Go ahead and sue us, many companies are suing us anyway."

Miao Po-ya said there are over 40,000 registered members, but only over 200 million NT dollars are left in the performance guarantee account. This means each member might only get about 5,000 NT dollars back, which worries many customers. The Taipei City Government should be more active. If they wait for consumer protection groups to file a class action lawsuit, True Yoga could already have gotten rid of its assets. So, it is most important to protect all changeable assets first for the members.

Liang Ming-chen, a consumer protection officer from the Executive Yuan's Consumer Protection Department, said that his department will quickly contact the Financial Supervisory Commission and banks about disputed credit card payments and trust account funds. Currently, they can help with credit card disputes. According to current rules, if a purchase was made within 540 days and the business stopped services within 120 days, and both conditions are met, customers can usually ask their bank for a refund. The Executive Yuan's Consumer Protection Department will meet with relevant government bodies next week to discuss this.

Liu Tzu-chun, Deputy Director of the Sports Department's Industry Division, said that if a class action lawsuit is filed, the Sports Department will offer relevant help. They will check for any weaknesses in the current system for fitness businesses and improve it.

Jin Shih-ping, Section Chief of the Labor Relations Department at the Ministry of Labor, said that hundreds of workers have already applied for mediation. He urged employees who have not yet applied to the Labor Bureau to do so quickly. He also hopes True Yoga will quickly solve the problem of unpaid wages. If the problem is not solved, the local government will report it to the Ministry of Labor, and they will handle it according to the law.

Changhua Starts "Cultural Blue Belt 2.0" with 5 Walking Tours to Experience Culture

The Cultural Affairs Bureau of Changhua County has launched "Cultural Blue Belt Living Circle 2.0" walking tours. They have planned 5 different routes. These tours will take people to coastal areas, old towns, and flower fields. This will allow visitors to deeply experience Changhua's culture and the special charm of its cultural venues.

The Cultural Affairs Bureau announced today in a press release that these events are designed to connect different cultural centers in Changhua County. They aim to promote the cultural resources around museums and local cultural centers. The "Cultural Blue Belt Living Circle 2.0" walking tours will take place over 4 days with 5 different sessions on October 12, October 26, November 1, and November 2.

The Cultural Affairs Bureau explained that the "Coastal Culture Line" will take everyone to the tidal flats. There, they can experience opening oysters and making crafts from sea waste. They will also learn about the culture and environment of Wanggong fishing village. Another route is the "Twin Cities Ancient Art Line," which focuses on photography. Through cameras, people can capture the beauty of Lukang, an old town.

The "Rural Fun Slow Tour Line" will visit the Erlin Sugarcane Farmers' Incident and a Japanese-style hall. People can also tour a sugar factory and make dough crafts, enjoying the feeling of a old village. The "Bagua Mountain Scenery Line" starts from the Indigenous Peoples' Culture Hall and goes to the Cut-Skin Love Memorial Hall. Then, it visits the former residence of Lai Ho, to feel the history of culture and medicine together.

In addition, the "Colorful Flower Fields Line" will guide people through flower fields and the Shetou community. This tour includes cultural guidance, DIY activities, and a visit to Luocuo Catholic Church. It connects local industries with memories of life. The Cultural Affairs Bureau said that online registration is open now. Each session has 25 to 30 spots. For more details, you can check the Changhua County Cultural Affairs Bureau's Facebook page.
