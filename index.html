Typhoon Willow Stops 129 Shipping Trips on 14 Routes on August 13

Typhoon Willow is coming. The Maritime and Port Bureau said that today, 14 shipping routes and a total of 129 trips are stopped. The affected routes include Donggang-Xiaoliuqiu and Budai-Magong. The bureau asks people not to travel to islands during the typhoon. This is to avoid getting stuck on the islands if boats stop running.

The Central Weather Administration continues to warn about Typhoon Willow on both sea and land. They expect Willow to land near Taitung around noon today.

Because the typhoon is affecting sea travel, the Ministry of Transportation and Communications' Maritime and Port Bureau said that 14 routes and 129 trips are stopped today.

The routes that are stopped include: Keelung-Matsu, Nangan Fuao-Fuzhou Langqi, Beigan Baisha-Fuzhou Huangqi, Kinmen-Shijing, Kinmen-Wutong, Kaohsiung-Magong, Budai-Magong, Magong-Wangan-Qimei, Jiangjun-Dongji, Donggang-Xiaoliuqiu, Yanpu-Xiaoliuqiu, Fugang-Green Island, Fugang-Lanyu, and Houbihu-Lanyu. All these trips are stopped.

Police Officer Found Not Guilty After Shooting Car and Killing Passenger; Victim's Aunt Charged with Threatening Judge

A police officer shot at a car that did not stop when asked. He accidentally killed a passenger, but a judge said he was not guilty. The dead person's aunt, Ms. Zhong, was very unhappy with this decision. She called a High Court judge, Mr. Lin, and said she would "kill" him. She also tried to make him open the case again. The Taipei District Prosecutors Office has now charged Ms. Zhong with forcing a public official to act and threatening safety.

On the night of March 10, 2022, Officer Zhang Boyong was on patrol in New Taipei City. He saw a Mercedes-Benz car with a fake license plate and told the driver to stop. The driver, Mr. Lin, drove away fast. Officer Zhang chased him. When Mr. Lin's car got stuck in traffic, Officer Zhang shot three times at the back of the car. One shot accidentally hit Mr. Zhong, who was sitting in the back, in the head. Mr. Zhong died later in the hospital. The New Taipei District Prosecutors Office later charged Officer Zhang Boyong with causing death by accident.

The first trial was at the New Taipei District Court. The court decided that Officer Zhang Boyong used his police gun because it was an emergency. They also said that shooting the car was not too much. So, they found him not guilty. The second trial at the Taiwan High Court agreed with the first decision. The highest court also said no to more appeals, so Officer Zhang Boyong is officially not guilty.

The Taipei District Prosecutors Office said in its charges that Ms. Zhong, the dead Mr. Zhong's aunt, was upset that Officer Zhang Boyong was found not guilty. On the afternoon of November 15, 2024, she called the High Court. She told the clerk who answered the phone, "I have found bad people and bought guns. The person I want to kill most is Judge Lin. I don't even want to kill the police officer who shot the gun the most." She also said, "I have seen a doctor, I have a medical report, so if I kill someone, will I not be put to death?" And she added, "If Judge Lin says he made a mistake and starts the case again, I can forgive him."

The prosecutors think that Ms. Zhong's words were a threat to Judge Lin's life and body. These words could make him afraid. She used these threats to try and make him start the case again. Because of this, she is charged with forcing a public official to act and threatening safety under the law. The more serious charge of forcing a public official to act will be used.

Typhoon Willow is Coming, 14 Main Road Sections Closed for Safety

Typhoon Willow is coming. Because of this, 14 sections of main roads are now closed for safety. This includes a part of Highway 20 in Taoyuan District, Kaohsiung City, from Baolai Second Bridge to the Taoyuan District Office. The Directorate General of Highways tells people not to go into mountain areas during the typhoon. This is because rocks can fall and land can slide easily there.

According to the Central Weather Administration's website, at 8:00 AM today, Typhoon Willow was a medium-strength typhoon. Its center was about 130 kilometers southeast of Taitung. It is moving towards the northwest at a speed of 29 to 32 kilometers per hour. This morning, it will rain more in eastern Taiwan. From this afternoon until evening, it will rain more in areas south of Chiayi.

The Directorate General of Highways said in a press release that as of 9:30 AM, 14 road sections are closed for safety. These include: Highway 9 in Taimali Township, Taitung County (from 397K+250 to 398K+600, Jinlun Bridge); Highway 20 in Jiaxian District, Kaohsiung City (from 58K+300 to 72K+500, Jiaxian to Laonong section); Highway 20 in Taoyuan District, Kaohsiung City (from 79K to 89K+500, Baolai Second Bridge to Taoyuan District Office section); and Highway 20 Temporary Route 93 in Taoyuan District, Kaohsiung City (from 0K to 5K, Qinhe to Fuxing section).

Other road sections closed for safety include: Highway 20 Temporary Route 105 in Haiduan Township, Taitung County (from 0K to 44K, Meishan Pass to Xiangyang); Highway 20 in Haiduan Township, Taitung County (from 149K+110 to 199K+000, Xiangyang to Chulai section); Highway 23 in Donghe Township, Taitung County (from 16K+840 to 25K+100, Beiyuan section); and Highway 24 in Sandimen Township, Pingtung County (from 27K+400 to 37K+300, Dewen Intersection to Shenshan section).

Also, these roads are closed: Highway 27 in Liugui District, Kaohsiung City (from 11K+500 to 23K+100, Zhongxing to Dajin section); Highway 29 in Namasia District, Kaohsiung City (from 2K+700 to 3K, Minsheng Bridge); Highway 29 in Namasia District, Kaohsiung City (from 4K+300 to 4K+800, Sanminghu section); Highway 29 Temporary Route 11 in Namasia District, Kaohsiung City (from 0K to 12K, Namasia to Wulipu section); and Highway 29 in Shanlin District, Kaohsiung City (from 47K+100 to 47K+500, Xincuo section).
