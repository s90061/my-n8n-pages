Oil Prices Go Up More Than 1% Because Russia-Ukraine Peace Talks Stop and US Demand Is Strong

Today, the price of oil went up by almost one dollar per barrel. This happened because Russia and Ukraine are blaming each other for delays in peace talks. Also, the United States, which uses the most oil in the world, showed that it needs a lot of oil.

Reuters reported that Brent crude oil futures went up by 83 cents, which is a 1.2% increase. It closed at $67.67 per barrel, the highest it has been in two weeks.

West Texas Intermediate crude oil futures in the US went up by 81 cents, a 1.3% increase. It closed at $63.52 per barrel.

US Stock Market Closes Lower as People Wait for Federal Reserve Chairman's Speech

Today, the main stock market indexes in Wall Street closed lower. Investors are getting ready for an important speech tomorrow morning by Jerome Powell, the head of the Federal Reserve. They are waiting for news about what will happen with interest rates in the US.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went down by 152.81 points, which is 0.34%. It closed at 44785.50 points.

The S&P 500 Index went down by 25.61 points, or 0.40%. It closed at 6370.17 points.

The Nasdaq Index, which has many technology companies, went down by 72.55 points, or 0.34%. It closed at 21100.31 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index went down by 27.56 points, or 0.49%. It closed at 5603.26 points.

National Guard in Washington D.C., Trump Says He Will Patrol Streets with Military and Police Tonight

After sending the National Guard to Washington D.C. last week, US President Trump said today that he will patrol the streets of the city tonight with military and police.

Reuters reported that Trump spoke on a radio show with Todd Starnes from Newsmax, a conservative news outlet. He said, "I think I will go out with the police tonight, and of course, the military."

Trump used his power as president to send a thousand National Guard members and federal agents to Washington D.C. He said this was because of a lot of violent crime in the city.

However, officials in Washington D.C. do not agree with Trump's reason. They said that official numbers from both the federal government and the city show that violent crime has gone down a lot after it increased in 2023.

Yesterday, when US Vice President JD Vance and Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth checked on the National Guard at Union Station in Washington D.C., people protesting there made a lot of noise and laughed at them.
