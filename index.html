Taiwan Drama "Sea of Intimacy" Ends, Yu Zi-Yu Painfully Ends Love

The final episode of the Taiwan drama "Sea of Intimacy" had many exciting emotional scenes. Wu Kang-Ren, who could not get the love he wanted, went to a gay sauna by himself to express his feelings. He had strong feelings for Li Ming-Shun for many years. This scene was very open and made many viewers talk about it.

In the drama, Yu Zi-Yu loved Li Ming-Shun very much. She gave him 25 years of silent effort. People made fun of her, calling her an old "third person" who was left behind. Finally, she understood that "true closeness means entering someone's heart, not how well you know them, and not just physical contact." In the end, she bravely faced him and ended this love. She told Li Ming-Shun, "I don't love you anymore."

This part of the drama was real and hard. It made many women on social media feel the same way about their love problems. Many online users said they felt "so sad they cried, as if they saw their past selves."

About the high number of discussions caused by the final episode, Li Ming-Shun said in a press release: "This drama was a journey for me to understand love and choices again."

All 7 episodes of "Sea of Intimacy" are now available on CATCHPLAY+ (Taiwan, Indonesia, Singapore), LINE TV, Hong Kong Now TV, and Amazon Prime Video. Viewers can watch this wonderful story, which mixes emotions and social issues, as many times as they want.

High-Speed Rail Offers 50% Off for University Students Returning to School, Tickets from August 6

Taiwan High-Speed Rail (THSR) announced today that from September 3 to 9, they will add special trains for university students. Besides the usual student discounts, there will be two extra trains each day going south and two going north. These trains will stop at all stations and offer a 50% discount for students returning to school. Tickets will be available starting August 6.

To help university and college students go back to school in September, THSR will add these special 50% off trains. These trains will run from September 3 to 9, with two trains going south and two trains going north each day, stopping at every station. This is in addition to the existing student discounts of 50%, 75%, or 88% off.

THSR said that they will offer a total of 28 special trains during this time. Travelers can buy tickets starting from 12:00 AM on August 6, based on when booking opens.

THSR noted that tickets for student discount trains can be booked online through the THSR ticketing system. Students can also use the T-EX mobile app, partnered convenience stores, or station ticket counters to book, pay, and get their tickets all at once.

Also, THSR stated that students using the discount must bring their valid student ID card when traveling. If the student ID card does not have the current semester's registration stamp or the stamp is not clear, students must also show an original certificate of enrollment for checking.

Constant Rain in Changhua: Fenyuan Courtyard Backyard Collapses, Yuanlin House Foundation Washes Away

Changhua has been getting a lot of rain. In Fenyuan Township, a large part of the backyard of a traditional courtyard house has collapsed. Even though they covered it with a tarp, soil is still washing away. In Yuanlin City, the ground under a house washed away, and the falling soil turned into a mudslide. The local government quickly used large bags of soil to make a temporary wall to stop the disaster from getting worse.

Due to the southwest air current, Changhua has had continuous heavy rain for several days. This has caused small disasters like floods and mudslides in areas such as Fenyuan Township, Changhua City, Yuanlin City, and Ershui Township in Changhua County. For example, in Tong'an Village, Fenyuan Township, Changhua, a traditional courtyard house on the Bagua Mountain Range had its slope soil wash away. The residents found that the backyard of their house had become a hole about 10 meters deep.

Lin Zi-Long, the head of Tong'an Village, received the report and quickly helped the family living in the courtyard house to leave. At the same time, he covered the collapsed area with a tarp. However, because of the heavy rain, the collapsed area kept getting bigger. Lin Zi-Long told reporters today that a new 3-meter-long crack appeared next to the collapsed area yesterday. If the rain does not stop, the collapse will become more serious, and the residents might lose their homes.

Besides the collapsed backyard in Fenyuan Township, a house foundation in Yuanlin City also washed away yesterday. The washed-away soil, mixed with rainwater, flowed down and became a mudslide. Workers from the Yuanlin City government helped the residents leave and also quickly brought large bags filled with soil to use as a temporary wall. This was to protect the houses below the hill from being affected.
