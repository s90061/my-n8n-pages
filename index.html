Elon Musk Says Netflix Promotes Transgender Ideas, Asks People to Stop Subscribing

Elon Musk, a very rich businessman, said today that Netflix, a company that streams movies and TV shows, is promoting what he called "transgender ideas." He asked his 227 million followers on social media to stop their Netflix subscriptions.

Musk joined a group that is stopping using Netflix. This group was started by a conservative social media account called Libs of TikTok. This is part of Musk's latest fight about social and cultural issues.

Libs of TikTok asked people to stop using Netflix. They said it was because of an animated TV show called "Dead End: Paranormal Park" and Netflix's rules about having many different kinds of people in their company.

The person who created "Dead End: Paranormal Park," Hamish Steele, caused a problem. This was because of things he wrote on social media about a conservative internet personality named Charlie Kirk, who was attacked in September. Some people online thought Steele's words were not respectful.

Yesterday, Musk shared a post from Libs of TikTok on X, which is his social media platform. He wrote, "Cancel Netflix."

The post included a picture from a Netflix company report. The report showed that Netflix had increased the number of directors and main actors who were not white.

Musk then sent more messages. He told his followers, "For your children's well-being, cancel Netflix."

Hamish Steele himself wrote a post on another social media platform, Bluesky, about this argument. But his post has now been removed. He wrote, "These are all lies and bad stories!"

Musk has a personal link to transgender topics. His oldest daughter, Vivian Jenna Wilson, publicly became a woman in 2022. She also legally changed her name and her gender.

Musk has said that his child was "destroyed" by something he called the "woke mind virus."

Technology and Car Company Stocks Lead to Higher European Markets

Today, most European stock markets closed higher. This was because technology companies and car companies' stocks performed well. The stock markets in Paris and Frankfurt both increased by more than one percent.

The FTSE 100 index in London fell by 18.70 points, which is 0.20%. It closed at 9427.73 points.

The DAX index in Frankfurt went up a lot, by 308.94 points, or 1.28%. It closed at 24422.56 points.

The CAC 40 index in Paris also went up a lot, by 89.68 points, or 1.13%. It closed at 8056.63 points.

Tesla Delivers 7% More Cars in Third Quarter, Better Than Expected

Tesla, a big electric car company, announced today that its car deliveries in the third quarter went up by 7% compared to last year. This was better than what experts thought would happen. This changed the recent trend where sales had been going down for a few months. This drop happened because some customers were not happy with CEO Elon Musk's actions in politics.

AFP news reported that Tesla delivered 497,099 vehicles in the three months ending on September 30. This number was higher than the 447,600 vehicles that FactSet, a company that provides financial information, had predicted.

Tesla did not share specific sales numbers for different areas. But, car companies saw a clear increase in electric car sales in the United States last quarter. This happened before a federal tax discount for buying electric cars, which President Donald Trump supported, came to an end.

Elon Musk warned in July that Tesla's financial reports might be difficult once the tax discount for electric cars stopped.

In July, Trump approved a financial plan. It said that a federal tax discount of up to $7,500 for each new electric car would slowly be removed, starting from September 30.

However, before this rule ended, car companies like General Motors and Ford Motor saw a large increase in their electric car sales in the third quarter.

Recently, Tesla has been facing more competition from electric car brands in China. Also, there has not been much demand for its electric pickup truck called Cybertruck.

Musk has said that Tesla can grow a lot in self-driving cars and artificial intelligence (AI) technology. But some people who study the market are disappointed that Tesla has not yet made a cheaper car, which is a goal Tesla has had for a long time.

Even though the numbers for car deliveries were good, Tesla's stock price started high today but then dropped. It went down by more than 3% during the day.
