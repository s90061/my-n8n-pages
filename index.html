Man Arrested for Hurting Ex-Girlfriend and Her Mother with a Knife at Fengyuan Train Station

On the evening of July 24th, a man named Huang had an argument with his ex-girlfriend, Ms. Huang, at the ticket gate area of Fengyuan Train Station. During the fight, he used a knife to hurt Ms. Huang and her mother. The police arrived quickly and arrested Mr. Huang. Ms. Huang and her mother were taken to the hospital. Their lives are not in danger. The police are now looking into the whole situation.

The Taichung branch of the Railway Police Bureau said that the Fengyuan Police Station received a report at 5:45 PM today. The report said there was a fight and someone was hurt at the ticket gate of Fengyuan Train Station. Police went to the scene right away.

Police found out that Mr. Huang, 28, and Ms. Huang, 27, used to be a couple. They had a disagreement which led to an argument and a physical fight. During the fight, Mr. Huang pulled out a fruit knife and cut both Ms. Huang and her mother. Police quickly stopped and arrested Mr. Huang when they arrived.

Police and emergency workers took Ms. Huang and her mother to Fengyuan Hospital. After they received treatment, they were stable and not in danger. It is understood that Ms. Huang had a protection order against Mr. Huang. The police are still investigating the case.

Lin Fei-fan: Stand Up and Vote on July 26th to Protect Taiwan's Independence

The first group of recall elections will happen on July 26th. Lin Fei-fan, who is the Deputy Secretary-General of the National Security Council, said tonight that eleven years ago, he worked with everyone to protect Taiwan from being taken over by China. Now, eleven years later, he is still working to keep Taiwan's independence, but in a different role. He asked everyone to come out and vote on July 26th and August 23rd.

The recall elections for 24 Kuomintang (KMT) legislators and Hsinchu Mayor Kao Hung-an, who is currently suspended, will be held on July 26th. The Central Election Commission recently approved the recall cases against KMT legislators Luo Ming-tsai, Chiang Chi-chen, Yang Chiung-ying, Yen Kuan-heng, and Lin Sze-ming. Their elections will be on August 23rd.

Lin Fei-fan posted a picture from the 2014 Sunflower Student Movement on Facebook tonight, supporting the "All Industries Support Recall" movement. He wrote that he now works in national security and is also a father. Eleven years ago, he was lucky to be part of a historical event that changed Taiwan. They worked together to stop China from taking over Taiwan's economy. Eleven years later, he continues to protect Taiwan's independence and democratic freedom in a different way, making sure they are not attacked or given up.

Lin Fei-fan said thank you to all the people and groups who have stood up. He thanked everyone who has walked the same path for the past eleven years, continuing to protect this small, mountainous country they love deeply. He asked everyone to stand up and vote on July 26th and August 23rd.

Von der Leyen: EU and China Agree to Better System for Rare Earth Exports

The head of the European Commission, Ursula von der Leyen, is visiting Beijing. She said that the European Union (EU) and China have agreed on a "better system" for China to send rare earth materials to the EU. She also stated that how China continues to work with Russia will be a "key factor" for how the relationship between the EU and China develops.

Ms. von der Leyen and the President of the European Council, Antonio Costa, are currently in Beijing. This morning, they met with Chinese President Xi Jinping at the Great Hall of the People in Beijing.

According to AFP news agency, Ms. von der Leyen said at a press conference in Beijing, "We have agreed... on an improved system for the supply of rare earths. This means that if there are problems with supply, this improved system will quickly check and solve any issues or disagreements that come up."

Ms. von der Leyen also talked about how China continues to strengthen its relationship with Russian President Vladimir Putin. She said this "will be a key factor in our future relationship."

Mr. Costa said today that during their meeting in Beijing, they told Chinese officials that the EU is worried about human rights in China.

Mr. Costa explained at the press conference, "Protecting human rights is a very important part of how the EU interacts with other countries, including China. Today, we repeated our concerns and will continue to focus on this important topic."
