Typhoon Willow Causes 14 Shipping Routes and 129 Sailings to Stop on August 13th

(Taipei, August 13th) Typhoon Willow is coming. The Maritime and Port Bureau said that today, 14 shipping routes with a total of 129 trips have stopped. Affected routes include Donggang-Xiaoliuqiu and Budai-Magong. The bureau asks people not to travel to outlying islands during the typhoon. This is to avoid getting stuck on the islands because boats stop running.

The Central Weather Administration continues to issue sea and land warnings for Medium Typhoon Willow. It is expected that Willow will land near Taitung around noon.

Because the typhoon affects sea travel, the Ministry of Transportation and Communications Maritime and Port Bureau stated that today, 14 routes with a total of 129 trips have stopped.

The affected routes are Keelung-Matsu, Nangan Fuao-Fuzhou Langqi, Beigan Baisha-Fuzhou Huangqi, Kinmen-Shijing, Kinmen-Wutong, Kaohsiung-Magong, Budai-Magong, Magong-Wangan-Qimei, Jiangjun-Dongji, Donggang-Xiaoliuqiu, Yanpu-Xiaoliuqiu, Fugang-Green Island, Fugang-Lanyu, and Houbihu-Lanyu. All these services are stopped.

Police Officer Found Not Guilty After Shooting Fleeing Car and Accidentally Killing Passenger; Deceased's Aunt Sued for Threatening Judge

(Taipei, August 13th) A police officer shot at a car that refused to stop, and a passenger died. The passenger's aunt, a woman named Zhong, was unhappy that the case was finalized with a loss for them. She called and threatened to "kill" Judge Lin from the High Court. She also tried to force him to open the case again. Today, the Taipei District Prosecutors Office charged Ms. Zhong with forcing a public servant to act and threatening people's safety.

Late at night on March 10, 2022, Officer Zhang Bo-yong from the Zhongyuan Police Station in New Taipei City was on patrol. He saw a Mercedes-Benz car with fake license plates and told it to stop. The driver, Mr. Lin, sped away. Officer Zhang chased him in his car. When Mr. Lin's car got stuck in traffic, Officer Zhang shot three times at the back of the car. He accidentally hit Mr. Zhong, who was in the back seat, in the head. Mr. Zhong died after being taken to the hospital. The New Taipei District Prosecutors Office charged Officer Zhang with causing death by accident.

The New Taipei District Court, in the first trial, decided that Officer Zhang was in an urgent situation when he used his weapon. His shots at the car were not more than needed. So, he was found not guilty. The Taiwan High Court kept this decision in the second trial. The Supreme Court rejected the appeal, so Officer Zhang's not guilty verdict is final.

Based on the charges from the Taipei District Prosecutors Office, Ms. Zhong, the aunt of the deceased Mr. Zhong, was unhappy that Officer Zhang was found not guilty. On the afternoon of November 15, 2024, she called the High Court. She told the clerk who answered the phone, "I have found gangsters and bought guns. The person I want to kill most is Judge Lin. I don't even want to kill the police officer who shot the gun the most." She also said, "I have seen a doctor, and I have a medical report. If I kill someone this way, will I not get the death penalty?" And, "If Judge Lin admits he made a mistake and starts a new trial, I can forgive him."

Prosecutors believe that Ms. Zhong's words threatened Judge Lin's life and safety. These words could make the judge feel scared. She also used this to force him to open a new trial. Therefore, they believe she broke the law by forcing a public servant to act and threatening safety. She has been charged with these crimes, and the more serious charge of forcing a public servant to act will be used.

Typhoon Willow Approaches: 14 Sections of Provincial Roads Are Closed for Safety

(Taipei, August 13th) Typhoon Willow is coming. As of this morning, 14 road sections have been closed for safety reasons. These include parts of Provincial Highway 20 in Taoyuan District, Kaohsiung City, from Baolai Second Bridge to Taoyuan District Office. The Ministry of Transportation and Communications Highway Bureau asks people not to go into mountain roads during the typhoon, as landslides and falling rocks can easily happen.

According to the Central Weather Administration website, Medium Typhoon Willow was about 130 kilometers southeast of Taitung at 8 AM today. It is moving northwest at 29 to 32 kilometers per hour. This morning, rain will increase in eastern Taiwan. From this afternoon until evening, rain will slowly increase in areas south of Chiayi.

The Highway Bureau's press release states that as of 9:30 AM, 14 road sections are closed as a precaution. These include parts of Provincial Highway 9 in Taimali Township, Taitung County (from 397K+250 to 398K+600, Jinlun Bridge). Also, sections of Provincial Highway 20 in Jiaxian District, Kaohsiung City (from 58K+300 to 72K+500, Jiaxian to Laonong sections). Dongzhuang Temporary Bridge on Provincial Highway 20 at 91K+500 in Taoyuan District, Kaohsiung City, is also closed. Additionally, the temporary road on Provincial Highway 20 (Temporary Line 93) from 0K to 5K (Qinhe to Fuxing sections) in Taoyuan District, Kaohsiung City, is closed.

Provincial Highway 23 from 16K+840 to 25K+100 (Beiyuan sections) in Donghe Township, Taitung County. And Provincial Highway 24 from 27K+400 to 37K+300 (Dewen Intersection to Shenshan sections) in Sandimen Township, Pingtung County.
