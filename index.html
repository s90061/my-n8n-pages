Taiwan's Defense Ministry: We will store more supplies to fight if China blocks us

The Executive Yuan recently approved a special budget for resilience. This budget will increase Taiwan's war supplies from 30 days to 120 days. Huang Wen-chi, who is in charge of war plans at the Ministry of National Defense, said today that China's ability to block the sea is getting stronger. So, Taiwan must have more war supplies of all kinds to keep fighting if the enemy attacks.

On September 11, the Executive Yuan approved the special budget for resilience. This budget includes NT$113.2 billion for the Ministry of National Defense. This money will be used to make command and communication stronger (NT$67.3 billion), update facilities for war readiness (NT$42.9 billion), and protect Taiwan's waters and people (NT$3 billion).

Minister of National Defense, Gu Lixiong, said at that time that Taiwan must be aware of China's threats. This special budget will help Taiwan increase its war supplies from 30 days to 120 days. It will also make Taiwan's reserve forces, including one-year conscripts, stronger. The money will also be used to fix training areas and store different types of ammunition. This is to make sure Taiwan can keep fighting.

The Ministry of National Defense held a press conference this morning about the "New Version of the National Defense Handbook." Lieutenant General Huang Wen-chi, head of the War Planning Department, said that because China's ability to block the sea is getting better, Taiwan must increase its war supplies of all goods. Among these, getting supplies quickly is the most important.

Huang Wen-chi explained that the budget will first cover oil and drinking water. For other items like spare parts, ammunition, and food, special storage is needed. So, the budget includes money to repair ammunition depots, food storage areas, and parts warehouses. Later, these needs will be met as the repairs are done and supplies are bought.

Huang Wen-chi also said that the Ministry will keep looking at plans for possible blockades by the enemy. This is to make sure Taiwan can keep fighting if the enemy attacks.

War supplies are military goods stored according to battle plans. They are needed to support the entire operation. Safety stock, also called the safety supply level, is the amount of military goods needed to keep supplies coming if normal supply is stopped or if more is needed in a war.

First International Children's and Youth Book Fair Starts in Taichung; Mayor Lu Invites Everyone to Come

The first Taiwan International Children's and Youth Book Fair will begin in Taichung on September 18. Taichung Mayor Lu Shiow-yen said today that Taichung has been ranked first in reading for four years in a row. Because of this, the Ministry of Culture chose Taichung to host the first children's and youth book fair. She welcomes everyone to join.

The first Taiwan International Children's and Youth Book Fair is planned by the Ministry of Culture and the Taichung City Government. It will be held from September 18 to 21 at the Taichung International Exhibition Center. Mayor Lu Shiow-yen talked about it at a city meeting today and invited everyone to visit.

Mayor Lu said that the city government has increased its budget for buying books every year. It has doubled from NT$50 million to NT$100 million. Also, five big libraries, including the Green Art Museum, will open soon. The Ministry of Education has also ranked Taichung first in reading scores for four years in a row. So, the Ministry of Culture specially chose Taichung to hold the first international children's and youth book fair. There will also be talks by famous writers and new book launches at the fair. She welcomes everyone to come.

The Ministry of Culture explained that the first children's and youth book fair will have the Taichung City Government as the main city. It will also have a special Taichung City Pavilion. New areas like the Taiwan Comics Pavilion and the Picture Book Story Pavilion will be added. The Italian Guest of Honor Pavilion and the Children's Book Pavilion from the Taipei International Book Exhibition will also move there. There will be combined book areas from local publishers and children's book areas. Famous writers from Taiwan and other countries will give talks. The fair will combine exhibitions, sales, and events to encourage reading. It will be a reading celebration for people in central Taiwan.

The Taichung City Cultural Affairs Bureau said that the Taichung City Pavilion will show the many sides of Taichung. This includes its mountains, rivers, people, history, nature, and water. They hope the book fair will show how Taichung helps people read. They also hope it will help more children enjoy books and help build a city that loves reading.

One of the groups joining the exhibition is the Hsin-Yi Foundation. They said they were inspired by the popular book "Sushi on a Trip" to create a "Reading Forest" that is 3.5 meters tall. They also made three sushi-person standees that are as tall as children and prepared sushi costumes. They invite families to the broccoli forest to enjoy a unique, funny, and creative experience.

The Hsin-Yi Foundation also invited the "Talented Children's Story Writer" Lin Che-chang to give a talk on September 21. His talk is called "Let's Go to the Pet Kung Fu School to Learn Martial Arts." He asks children to come and play and learn martial arts on the holiday. On September 20, the Hsin-Yi story sisters will share classic stories like "Guji Guji the Duckling" and the newest popular story "The Rule-Followers are the Winners." Readers are welcome to sign up online.

Tickets for "2025 Kaohsiung Love & Moon Hot Air Balloon" Event Go on Sale September 17

The Kaohsiung City Tourism Bureau announced today that the "2025 Kaohsiung Love & Moon Hot Air Balloon" event will take place. It will be held from September 27 to 29 and October 4 to 6 in Tianliao Moon World. It will also be held from October 10 to 12 by the Love River. Hot air balloons will fly at two main locations. Tickets will be available for purchase starting September 17.

Gao Min-lin, the Director of the Tourism Bureau, said that the "Kaohsiung Love & Moon Hot Air Balloon" event is now in its fifth year. Every year, it offers new and interesting things. This year, besides the chance to ride in a hot air balloon, there will also be many places to take photos with hot air balloons. There will also be new and better art displays.

Tianliao Moon World is famous for its unique badlands. When hot air balloons are there, it feels like "landing on the moon." The Love River location, with its city views and water, lets visitors see Kaohsiung from a different angle. Both places have their own special features.

Gao Min-lin said that this year, the "2025 Kaohsiung Love & Moon Hot Air Balloon" tethered flight event will happen during three long holidays: Teachers' Day, Mid-Autumn Festival, and Double Ten Day. The Kaohsiung Bear hot air balloon will also be there for everyone to enjoy.

The Tourism Bureau said that tickets for the "2025 Kaohsiung Love & Moon Hot Air Balloon" tethered flight experience will go on sale on September 17 at 12:00 PM. You can buy them at 7-ELEVEN ibon machines across Taiwan and online. Each ticket costs NT$500. There are only a limited number of tickets.

Also, the professional flight team will decide the flight times based on the weather, sunrise, and sunset conditions for that day. Morning flights will be from about 6:00 AM to 8:00 AM. Afternoon flights will be from about 4:00 PM to 5:30 PM. Safety is the most important thing for all experiences.
