Trump: New Trade Deals Coming, Tariffs for Over 150 Countries

(Washington D.C., July 16, CNA correspondent Hou Tzu-Ying) US President Donald Trump has been announcing new tariff rates for more than 20 countries. Taiwan is not on this list. Today, he said that "some good agreements will be announced," and India might be one of them. He also said he will send tariff letters directly to over 150 other countries that are not talking with the US.

In early April, Trump started his tariff policy. He put tariffs of 11% to 50% on many countries. Then, he gave them a 90-day break to talk with the US. Last week, he signed an order to make this break longer until August 1. He also started sending letters to leaders of many countries. These letters tell them the new tariff rates starting on August 1.

So far, Trump has reached agreements with the UK, Vietnam, and Indonesia. He also has a trade plan with China. Politico, a political news group, reported yesterday that Taiwan, Switzerland, and India have not yet received Trump's tariff letters. This means they might be close to reaching an agreement. These agreements could be announced in the next few weeks.

Today, Trump met with Salman bin Hamad al-Khalifa, the Crown Prince and Prime Minister of Bahrain, at the White House. He told the media that they would talk about trade and other topics.

He also said that the US has made deals with some countries, like the one with Indonesia announced yesterday. Another deal is coming soon, possibly with India.

Trump said, "We will announce some good agreements."

But he also said again, "Every tariff letter sent means an agreement." The best agreement is to send a letter directly. This letter tells the other country the tariffs they will pay: "30%, 35%, 25%, 20%." Brazil even faces a 50% tariff.

Trump said that for the other over 150 countries that are not talking with the US, America will send tariff letters directly. These countries are smaller and "do not trade much with us." They will all have the same tariff rate.

He said these countries are not big ones like China or Japan. The US is talking with China and Japan. But he thinks that for Japan, the US might still use tariff letters.

Trump has sent four rounds of tariff letters so far. These letters went to 24 countries, including Japan, South Korea, the Philippines, Canada, Brazil, and the European Union. Japan and South Korea both face 25% tariffs. Last week, Trump sent a letter to Indonesia saying they would get a 32% tariff. But yesterday, he announced a deal with Indonesia. Now, Indonesian goods sent to the US will have a 19% tariff.

Regarding talks about tariffs between Taiwan and the US, Yen Hui-Hsin, a deputy trade negotiator from Taiwan's economic and trade negotiation office, said on July 16 that Taiwan wants to get a tariff rate that is not higher than its competitors, like Japan and South Korea.
