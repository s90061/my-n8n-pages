Spain's Valencia Issues Red Alert for Heavy Rain, Worries About Another Flood Disaster

Madrid (CNA) â€“ Spain's weather agency today put the eastern Valencia region on a red alert for heavy rain. They are worried that a bad flood, like the one that killed 235 people last October, might happen again.

AFP news says that the Spanish National Meteorological Agency (Aemet) warned of "extremely dangerous" weather in Tarragona, Castellon, and the Valencia region. This bad weather is expected this afternoon, evening, and tomorrow. Prime Minister Pedro Sanchez also gave the same warning.

Local officials in Valencia said that schools and universities in Valencia city will be closed all day tomorrow. Also, all public places like libraries, parks, gardens, markets, and cemeteries will be closed temporarily.

People in Valencia also got a red alert message on their phones this afternoon. The message told them to be careful because of the bad weather.

Climate change makes the effects of heavy rain worse. Last October, the Valencia region had very bad floods. After that, many people protested. They said that the government did not handle the problem well. They pointed out that even though the weather agency gave warnings, officials did not tell people quickly enough.
