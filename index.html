Bus Accident in Southern Iran Kills at Least 21 People

State media in Iran reported that a bus crashed in the southern part of the country today. At least 21 people died, and 29 others were hurt.

According to Agence France-Presse (AFP), this serious accident happened near Kavar town, about 1000 kilometers south of Tehran, Iran's capital. The reason for the accident is not known yet.

The Kavar Hospital told Iran's state TV that 21 people have died and 29 are injured.

The IRNA news agency reported that almost 20,000 people died in traffic accidents in Iran in the 12 months up to March this year.

Nvidia Tells Chinese Customers That H20 Chip Supply is Limited

The Information, a US tech news website, reported today that Nvidia, a big US company that makes AI chips, has told its customers in China that there is not enough supply of H20 chips. Nvidia also does not plan to make more of these chips.

Reuters reported that the H20 chip was the strongest AI chip Nvidia could sell to China under US rules about selling things to other countries. But after the US government stopped the sale of H20 chips in April, Nvidia had to stop taking new orders. It also canceled its orders with TSMC, the company that makes the chips for Nvidia, according to two people who know about the situation, as told to The Information.

Earlier this week, Nvidia said they are planning to sell H20 chips to China again.

Tourist Boat Overturns in Vietnam's Ha Long Bay, At Least 28 Dead

Vietnamese media reported that a tourist boat with more than 50 people on board turned over today in Ha Long Bay. At least 28 people died, and more than 10 people are still missing.

VNExpress reported that the boat, which had 53 people on it, turned over in the afternoon. Vietnamese border guards and the navy saved 11 people and found 28 dead bodies.

Most people on the boat were families from Hanoi, the capital city. More than 20 of them were children.
