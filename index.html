Oil Prices Fall as Market Focuses on OPEC+ Increasing Production

(New York, September 29, Central News Agency) International oil prices fell sharply today. This happened after they went up last week. It seems that OPEC+, a group of oil-producing countries, will make more oil in November.

The price for West Texas Intermediate crude oil, to be delivered in November at the New York Mercantile Exchange, went down by $2.27. It reached $63.45 per barrel.

The price for Brent crude oil from the North Sea in London, also for November delivery, dropped by $2.16. It reached $67.97 per barrel.

US Stock Market Closes Slightly Higher, But New Taxes and Government Shutdown Worries Limit Gains

(New York, September 29, Central News Agency) Today, the main US stock market indexes went up a little when trading ended. However, people are worried about new taxes and the chance of the government stopping its work. Also, important economic news will come out soon. These things stopped the market from going up more.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went up by 68.78 points, which is 0.15%. It closed at 46316.07 points.

The S&P 500 index went up by 17.51 points, which is 0.26%. It closed at 6661.21 points.

The Nasdaq index increased by 107.09 points, or 0.48%. It closed at 22591.16 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index went up by 10.06 points, or 0.16%. It closed at 6315.11 points.

White House Says Netanyahu Promised Qatar No More Attacks After Israel Hit Doha

(Washington, September 29, Central News Agency) On September 9, Israel launched air attacks on Doha, the capital of Qatar. These attacks were aimed at the leaders of Hamas, a Palestinian armed group. The US government said that Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu called Qatar's Prime Minister from the White House today. He said he was sorry about the attack and promised that Israel would not do it again.

News agencies AFP and BBC reported that Netanyahu's attitude had been firm since the Israeli attack on Qatar. Today's call happened while he was meeting with US President Donald Trump to talk about a ceasefire in the Gaza Strip.

A statement from the White House said that Israel's missile attack on Hamas targets in Qatar accidentally killed Qatari soldiers. The statement read, "Prime Minister Netanyahu expressed his deep regret."

The statement also mentioned that Netanyahu "said again that Israel will not launch such attacks in the future."

The White House also said that the leaders of Israel and Qatar agreed to President Trump's idea. They hope to create a three-way system. This system would help them work together better, talk more openly, and increase their efforts to stop threats.
