Labor Group Says Zhengzhou Factory Overworks to Make Phones; Foxconn Says Employee Safety is Important

Zhengzhou â€“ A labor rights group has released a report. It says that workers at Foxconn's Zhengzhou factory in China, where the newest iPhone 17 phones are made, still work in very bad conditions. They work long hours and their pay is often late. Foxconn has replied, saying that the company puts the health and safety of its employees first.

The Financial Times and Bloomberg News reported on this. "China Labor Watch," a human rights group based in the US, did a six-month investigation of Foxconn's Zhengzhou factory this year. They sent undercover workers to talk to more than 100 employees at the factory.

China Labor Watch found that during busy production times, Foxconn's Zhengzhou factory employs about 200,000 workers. More than half of these workers are "temporary workers." But Chinese law says that the number of temporary workers should not be more than 10% of all company employees.

These temporary workers do not get the same benefits as regular employees. The China Labor Watch report also said that some of the temporary workers' pay is held back and paid late. This is to stop them from leaving during the busiest time of making Apple iPhone phones.

Also, most temporary workers earn between 25 and 28 Chinese Yuan per hour. But some earn as little as 12 Chinese Yuan per hour, depending on their experience. Many employees said they often work extra hours to earn more money per hour.

China Labor Watch pointed out that most workers work 60 to 75 hours per week. This is much more than the maximum legal working hours in China. It is also more than Apple's own limit of 60 hours per week.

China Labor Watch also said that the Zhengzhou factory has a system that treats some ethnic minority groups and pregnant women unfairly when hiring.

Foxconn replied to this. They said the company does not accept unfair treatment and puts employee health and safety first. They welcome helpful talks with all groups involved and are willing to share their improvements and progress.

Apple also said that after learning about China Labor Watch's findings, their team went to the factory and started an investigation right away. Apple "follows the highest standards for workers, human rights, the environment, and ethics."
