US and Israel Recall Gaza Ceasefire Negotiation Teams; US Accuses Hamas of Not Being Sincere

Israel and the United States today called back their teams who were talking about a ceasefire in Gaza. They did this to discuss the situation. An American special envoy, Steve Witkoff, said that Hamas, a Palestinian group, was not honest during the talks.

Reuters reported that Witkoff wrote on X, a social media platform: "Even though mediators worked very hard, Hamas seemed to be unorganized and not sincere. Now we will look at other ways to bring the hostages home and try to make a more peaceful place for the people in Gaza."

After almost two years of war between Israel and Hamas, the situation for people in Gaza has become much worse. Israelis are also worried about the other hostages. Both sides are under pressure to make an agreement.

Mediators are still trying to find an agreement to stop the fighting and free the hostages held by Hamas in the Gaza Strip.

According to health officials in Gaza, many people have died from hunger there in the past few weeks.

Trump Denies Wanting to Destroy Musk's Companies, Hopes All US Businesses Thrive

Today, former U.S. President Trump said he does not want to destroy the companies owned by Elon Musk, a very rich person in technology. People are wondering if Trump supports the U.S. government working with companies owned by Musk, who is also the head of the electric car company Tesla.

The Hill newspaper reported that Trump wrote on his social media platform, Truth Social: "Everyone says I will destroy Elon Musk's companies by stopping some or all of the large financial help the U.S. government gives him. This is not true!"

He also wrote: "I want Elon and all companies in our country to do very well, even better than before! The better they do, the better America will be, which is good for all of us. We are setting new records every day, and I want that to continue!"

Trump said these things the day after Karoline Leavitt, who works for the White House press, said she does not think Trump supports the government signing deals with xAI, Musk's artificial intelligence company.

When asked if Trump wants the government to stop the recent contract with xAI, Leavitt said she would talk to the President about it.
