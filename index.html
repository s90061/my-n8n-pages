Russian Drone Enters Poland, NATO to Start 'Eastern Sentry' to Boost Defense

Brussels, September 12 (Central News Agency) â€“ NATO Secretary General Mark Rutte announced today that NATO will strengthen its defenses on its eastern side. This is in response to a Russian drone flying into Polish airspace this week.

This announcement by Secretary General Rutte comes after NATO, for the first time in three and a half years since the Russia-Ukraine war began, used fighter jets to shoot down a Russian drone that entered a member country's airspace.

Rutte held a joint press conference with General Alexus Grynkewich, the top commander of NATO forces in Europe. Rutte said at the press conference, "NATO is starting 'Eastern Sentry' to make our presence on the eastern side even stronger."

Rutte stated that these military actions will begin in the next few days. They will involve forces from member countries like Denmark, France, the UK, and Germany. He stressed that these extra forces will not only include "more traditional military setups" but will also add "special elements to deal with drone problems."

He also said that NATO is still checking if Russia purposely flew into Polish airspace. But no matter what, he said, "this is careless and unacceptable."

Before this, member countries like France and Germany had already said they would send more fighter jets to Poland. Grynkewich said, "Even though we are focusing on Poland now, this situation is bigger than just one country. If one member country is affected, the whole NATO is affected."

He also added, "'Eastern Sentry' will be flexible and quick. It will provide more focused deterrence and defense when and where it is most needed."

Poland's Defense Minister, Wladyslaw Kosiniak-Kamysz, thanked NATO for "showing strong action and decisions when facing Russia's aggressive policies." He said that this new deployment "is not just a smart decision, but it shows NATO's responsibility for the safety of the entire eastern side."
