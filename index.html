EU Commissioner: US and EU to Form Metal Alliance Against China's Overproduction

Brussels, July 28 (CNA) â€“ Maros Sefcovic, the European Union (EU) Commissioner for Trade, said today that the EU and the United States will create a "Metal Alliance." This alliance will be based on a trade agreement. Its goal is to lessen the impact of China's subsidized production on global markets.

Reuters reported that Sefcovic said US officials understood that steel companies in the US and EU face similar problems. This was after hours of talks to reach an agreement. Because of this, steel and aluminum makers in the EU will get a special system. This system would replace President Trump's 50% import tax with a very low or no tax. But this system is not yet final.

Sefcovic said at a press conference: "This agreement clearly shows that we plan to work together on steel, aluminum, copper, and related products. I call this the Metal Alliance. It will effectively build a shared protection around our economies by using special tax amounts and good treatment for trade, which are at historic levels."

This idea for an alliance and a special tax system shows that Europe and America are increasingly trying to stop China's overproduction.

Sefcovic also said: "It is clear that the problem is overproduction. We believe this problem is connected to illegal subsidies." (Translator: Lu Ying-tzu / Editor: Chen Yen-chun) 1140729
