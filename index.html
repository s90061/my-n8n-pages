US and China Talks Finish; China Says Both Sides Will Continue Tariff Break

Officials from the United States and China finished two days of talks today. They were trying to solve problems with their economies and stop a trade war from getting worse. China said that both sides agreed to make their 90-day break from tariffs, which started in mid-May, longer. However, China did not say when this extension would start or how long it would last.

Reuters news agency reported that no big breakthroughs were announced from these talks. But Li Chenggang, who is China's trade representative and Deputy Minister of Commerce, said that both sides agreed to try and make the 90-day tariff break, agreed in mid-May, longer. He did not say exactly when it would start or how long it would be.

These talks might prepare the way for US President Donald Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping to meet at the end of the year. However, Trump said he did not ask for the meeting, and Chinese officials did not mention it.

After many months of saying he would put high taxes on goods from other countries, Trump has made trade deals with countries like the European Union (EU) and Japan. But China is a very strong economy and controls the world's supply of rare earth materials. This makes talks between the US government and China very difficult.

In May, the US and China stopped their plans to put very high tariffs on each other's goods. This helped avoid a situation where they would stop trading with each other. But if they do not make a deal, the world's supply chains and money markets might become unstable again.

To show how important this is, the International Monetary Fund (IMF) today increased its prediction for the world's economic growth. But it also said that tariffs could go up again, which would be a big risk.

European Stock Markets Close Higher; Investors Look at Company Reports

Major stock market indexes in Europe ended higher today. Investors are now looking at company financial reports that will be shared this week, instead of focusing on trade agreements.

The FTSE 100 index in London went up slightly by 54.88 points, or 0.60%. It closed at 9136.32 points.

The DAX index in Frankfurt rose by 247.01 points, or 1.03%. It closed at 24217.37 points.

The CAC 40 index in Paris went up by 56.48 points, or 0.72%. It closed at 7857.36 points.

Weather Agency Warns of Heavy Rain in Southern Taiwan

The Central Weather Agency has announced a special heavy rain warning. Because of a southwest air current, there will likely be strong, short periods of rain. From tonight until tomorrow, some mountain areas in Pingtung County will have very heavy rain or extremely heavy rain. Parts of Chiayi City, Chiayi County, Tainan City, Kaohsiung City, and Pingtung County will have heavy rain or very heavy rain. Also, central Taiwan, Taitung, and mountain areas in northern Taiwan and Yilan and Hualien might have heavy rain.

The Central Weather Agency reminds people to be careful of lightning and strong winds. In mountain areas, please be careful of landslides, falling rocks, mudslides, and sudden floods. In low areas, please be careful of floods. The area expected to have extremely heavy rain is Pingtung County.
