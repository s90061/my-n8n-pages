US Stocks Go Up Even After Trump Tries to Fire Fed Official

US President Trump ordered to remove Lisa Cook from her job at the Federal Reserve (Fed). This could lead to a legal fight between them. But today, investors did not let these problems stop them. All four major stock indexes in the US closed higher.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went up by 135.60 points, or 0.30%, to 45418.07 points. The S&P 500 index rose a little by 26.62 points, or 0.41%, to 6456.94 points.

The Nasdaq index went up by 94.98 points, or 0.44%, to 21544.27 points. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index increased by 51.62 points, or 0.90%, to 5807.92 points.

Oil Prices Go Down as Market Waits for Clear Supply News

US President Trump tried to fire Lisa Cook from the Federal Reserve. This caused some worries in the market. At the same time, investors are waiting for clearer information about how much oil will be available. Because of this, international oil prices went down today, ending four days of going up.

In New York, the price for West Texas Intermediate crude oil for October went down by 1.55 US dollars, or 2.39%. It closed at 63.25 US dollars per barrel.

In London, the price for Brent crude oil for October went down by 1.58 US dollars, or 2.30%. It closed at 67.22 US dollars per barrel.

Apple Will Show New iPhones and Other Items on September 9th

Apple company officially sent invitations to the media today. They announced that their autumn event will happen on September 9th (1 AM on September 10th, Taipei time). At this event, they are expected to show new iPhone models, watches, and other devices.

Reuters reported that Apple will hold this autumn event at the Steve Jobs Theater in Apple Park, California. They plan to show how they are putting artificial intelligence (AI) into their devices.

Investors in Apple are watching this event closely. They are worried that Apple might be slower than other companies in using new AI technology.

News reports say that Apple will also show a new, thinner iPhone. It might be called iPhone Air, like the iPad Air and MacBook Air products.

Bloomberg News also said that Apple plans to show new basic and advanced Apple Watch smartwatches. They will also show improved iPad Pro products and a faster Vision Pro headset.
