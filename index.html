Yeh Pao-ti's First Home Run Helps Hawks Win; Lin Shih-hsiang Gets 30th Save

Taipei (Central News Agency reporter Hsieh Ching-wen) â€“ In their last regular season game, the TSG Hawks played against the Wei Chuan Dragons. Yeh Pao-ti hit his first career home run in the Chinese Professional Baseball League (CPBL) in the bottom of the 8th inning. This home run won the game for his team, making the score 4-3 and giving the Dragons their fifth loss in a row. Lin Shih-hsiang from the Hawks also got his 30th save of the season, becoming the 8th player in CPBL history to do so.

The Hawks played the Dragons at their home stadium, Chengcing Lake Baseball Stadium. Neither team scored in the first three innings. In the bottom of the 4th inning, Yen Yu-hsuan hit a solo home run, giving the Hawks the first point.

In the top of the 6th inning, the Dragons took the lead with a two-run home run by Giljegiljaw Kungkuan. This was Giljegiljaw Kungkuan's 24th home run of the season. In the top of the 7th inning, the Dragons scored another point with a hit, a passed ball, and a sacrifice fly by Wang Shun-ho.

In the bottom of the 8th inning, the Hawks started to attack again. With two outs, Wang Po-hsuan and Tseng Yu-ching both got walks. Then, Yeh Pao-ti hit a three-run home run, which put his team ahead. This was his first career home run in the CPBL, and it won the game for his team. Yeh Pao-ti was chosen as the Most Valuable Player (MVP) of the game.

The Hawks' starting pitcher, Sakurai Shuto, pitched for 6 innings and allowed 2 runs. He pitched well but did not get the win or loss. The win went to relief pitcher Huang Chun. Relief pitcher Lin Shih-hsiang pitched for 1 inning, striking out 2 batters and not allowing any runs. He got his 30th save of the season. Getting 30 saves in one season makes him the 8th person in CPBL history to achieve this.
