Trump Asks Thailand and Cambodia to Stop Fighting, or No Trade Deals

The fight between Thailand and Cambodia has now been going on for three days. Today, former US President Donald Trump said he talked to Hun Manet, the Prime Minister of Cambodia, and the acting Prime Minister of Thailand. He asked them to stop fighting. He also said that the US is talking about trade with both countries. If they keep fighting, the US will not make a trade deal with either of them.

News reports from other countries say that the long-term border problem between Thailand and Cambodia became a serious fight on July 24th. Both sides used warplanes, big guns, tanks, and soldiers on the ground. This fight is the most violent between them in many years. At least 33 people have died, and many more are hurt.

Donald Trump is visiting Scotland now. Today, he wrote on his social media site, Truth Social, that he talked with Hun Manet, Cambodia's Prime Minister. They talked about ending the "war" with Thailand. After that, he also talked with Phumtham Wechayachai, Thailand's acting Prime Minister. He asked him to stop fighting to end "this war" too.

Trump said that the US is already talking about trade with both Thailand and Cambodia. But if the two countries keep fighting, the US will not want to make a deal with either of them.

After talking to Thailand's acting Prime Minister, he wrote online again. He said they had a "good talk." Both Thailand and Cambodia want to stop fighting and have peace right away. Then he told this message to Cambodia.

Trump said he is "trying to make this difficult situation simpler." This fight has caused many people to die or get hurt. It made him remember the past fight between Pakistan and India.

Trump had said before that Pakistan and India agreed to a full ceasefire after the US helped them.

Gaza Civilian Defense: Israeli Attacks Kill 11 People

According to the Gaza Strip Civil Defense, Israeli military actions there today caused 11 deaths. This area in Palestine has been damaged by war for more than 21 months.

Mahmud Bassal, a spokesperson for the Gaza Strip Civil Defense, told AFP news agency that four Palestinians died in an air attack in the Rimal area of Gaza City in the north.

The Gaza Strip Civil Defense also said that one person was shot and killed in the northwest of Gaza City. At that time, "Israeli soldiers shot at people waiting for help." Witnesses told AFP that thousands of people were there, waiting for supplies.

The Gaza Strip Civil Defense also stated that another man died from a drone attack near Khan Yunis, a city in the southern Gaza Strip. Also, one person died from shelling in the Al-Bureij refugee camp in the central Gaza Strip.

The Israeli military told AFP that they would look into these events.

The Israeli military also put out a statement saying they are continuing their military actions in the Gaza Strip. They said they killed several members of a "terrorist group" who put a bomb to attack Israeli soldiers.
