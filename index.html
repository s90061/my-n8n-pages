American Conservative Internet Star Kirk Shot Dead; Suspect Arrested, Family Reveals Motive

Washington, September 12 (Central News Agency) - American conservative internet star Charlie Kirk was recently shot and killed. This shocking event has created a lot of discussion across the United States. Today, officials confirmed that the suspect has been caught. They also showed a photo of the suspect's criminal record. The suspect is reportedly a 22-year-old man from Utah named Robinson. His family says he has become "more political" in recent years.

Kirk, 31, died after being shot in the neck while giving a speech at Utah Valley University in Utah. A video from the event shows him speaking to a crowd. Suddenly, a gunshot was heard, and he fell into his chair. The camera quickly moved away, and the people listening became very scared.

Kirk was an important supporter of Donald Trump. He was also a well-known internet personality and a leader in the youth conservative movement. He had millions of followers on social media. People liked him because his videos were smart and well-edited. He was also good at dealing with people who tried to cause problems or disagree with his ideas.

Utah Governor Spencer Cox announced today in a press conference that authorities have arrested Tyler Robinson, the suspect in Kirk's shooting.

News channels like CNN and NBC News reported that law enforcement officers said Robinson is 22 years old and lives in Utah.

Cox explained, "On the evening of September 11, one of Robinson's family members contacted a family friend. This friend then told the Washington County Sheriff's Office that Robinson had told them, or suggested, that he was involved in the crime."

Cox also said that Robinson's family told investigators that Robinson had become "more political" in recent years. This family member remembered that during a dinner, Robinson mentioned that Kirk was going to Utah Valley University. Robinson also talked about why he did not like Kirk and his ideas.

Robinson reportedly told his family that he believed "Kirk was full of hate and spread hate."

CNN reported that law enforcement officials said Robinson's father recognized his son from the photo shared by the authorities. He then called a young pastor for help. The pastor later contacted the police, and the suspect was arrested.

Law enforcement has released Robinson's booking photo.
