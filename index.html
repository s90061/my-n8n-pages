TSMC's Human Resources Now Led by Chen Pei-hung; Ho Li-mei Moves to Corporate Strategy Development

(Central News Agency reporter Chang Chien-chung, Hsinchu, July 16) TSMC has changed the head of its Human Resources group. TSMC recently announced internally that Senior Vice President Ho Li-mei will move to the Corporate Strategy Development group. She will also continue to lead the Corporate Sustainability group. Vice President Chen Pei-hung will now be in charge of the Human Resources group.

Chen Pei-hung joined TSMC in 1990. He has more than 34 years of experience in management. He has a lot of knowledge in operations, research and development (R&D), corporate planning, and the customer project office.

Chen Pei-hung also has skills in technology development and manufacturing leadership. He has led teams to reach important goals. From 2010 to 2018, he was the manager of Fab 12B. There, he led his team to work with the R&D department to successfully make TSMC's 40-nanometer, 28-nanometer, 20-nanometer, and 16-nanometer technologies in large amounts.

From 2018 to 2020, Chen Pei-hung was the manager of Fab 14A. He used processes from 0.13 micrometers to 40 nanometers to make many special products at the same time. This helped TSMC achieve a record of making 300,000 wafers per month in one factory.

In December 2020, Chen Pei-hung became the Senior Director of the Customer Project Office. Customers trusted him very much. After that, he joined the Human Resources group as a Senior Director. In February this year, he was promoted to Vice President.

Chen Pei-hung graduated from National Sun Yat-sen University with a degree in chemistry. He also received a master's degree in chemistry from the same university. In 2010, he won the National Outstanding Manager Award. So far, Chen Pei-hung has 25 patents around the world, and 14 of them are US patents. (Edited by: Yang Kai-hsiang) July 16, 2025.

Migrant Boat Capsizes Off Dominican Republic, 8 Dead; Search Ends

(Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic, July 15, Comprehensive Foreign Report) A boat full of migrants recently overturned off the coast of the Dominican Republic. Today, officials finished looking for people. In total, 17 people were saved, and 8 people were confirmed dead. The number of missing people is not known.

According to reports from Agence France-Presse and Al Jazeera, the small boat overturned on July 11 while on its way to Puerto Rico, a territory of the United States. Survivors said there were about 40 to 50 passengers on the boat, but officials could not confirm the exact number.

Fernando Castillo, a provincial civil defense official in charge of the rescue, told Agence France-Presse, "We have finished the search and rescue operation. In total, 17 people were saved, and 8 bodies were found."

Castillo said the people who died were from the Dominican Republic and Haiti, and they were both men and women. He did not know how many people are still missing from this sad accident.

Both the Dominican Republic and Haiti are on the island of Hispaniola. In recent years, the sea route between the Dominican Republic and Puerto Rico has become a way that more and more migrants choose to travel. (Intern translator: Li Yu-ching / Editor: Yang Chao-yen) July 16, 2025.

Internet Celebrity "Pineapple" Shot At 12 Times While Driving; No Injuries, Police Identify Suspect

(Central News Agency reporter Huang Kuo-fang, Chiayi City, July 16) Last night, internet celebrity "Pineapple" (real name Wu Hung-yi) was driving with his wife on Bo'ai Road in Chiayi City. A person on a motorcycle followed them and shot at the back right side of their car 12 times. This was likely a warning. Wu Hung-yi and his wife were not hurt. Police have identified a suspect and are looking for them.

The First Precinct of the Chiayi City Police Bureau said today that they received a report at 8:05 PM yesterday about a shooting on Section 2, Bo'ai Road. Police immediately went to the scene, but both sides had already left. No one was hurt. Twelve bullet casings were found at the scene. The police are still trying to understand why the shooting happened. They are now looking at security camera videos from around the crime scene and are searching for the man who was riding the motorcycle.

Police stated that the shooter fired all shots at the back right side of Wu's car. Luckily, neither Wu nor his wife inside the car were hurt. After the crime, the shooter left the motorcycle used for the crime on the side of the road and escaped in another car that was waiting for them. The motorcycle was not stolen. Police are now looking for the owner of the motorcycle to question them. The shooter escaped to a city in the southern part of the country. Police have now identified a specific suspect and are trying to find them. (Edited by: Chang Ya-ching) July 16, 2025.
