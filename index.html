Brothers and Lions Baseball Game on September 7 Postponed Due to Rain, Rescheduled for October 5 in Taichung

The professional baseball game between CTBC Brothers and Uni-President 7-Eleven Lions, planned for today, was postponed. The game was stopped because of rain. It will now be played on October 5 at Taichung Intercontinental Baseball Stadium.

The Brothers team started a three-game series against the Lions at their home stadium in Taichung on September 5. In the first game on September 5, the Lions lost 1-2 after extra innings. Yesterday, the Lions won 7-1. This was thanks to their new foreign pitcher, Cameron Alldred, who played well for 7 innings and did not let the other team score.

The game that was supposed to start today at 5:05 PM could not happen because of the rain. The game will now be played on October 5 at 5:05 PM, at the same stadium.

Right now, the Brothers team is in first place for the whole season. They have won 59 games and lost 41. The Lions team is in second place. They have won 57 games and lost 44. The Lions are 2.5 games behind the Brothers. For the second half of the season, the Brothers are also in first place with 25 wins and 15 losses. They are 3.5 games ahead of the Rakuten Monkeys, who are in second place.

President Lai Visits Cimu Temple in Taoyuan, Says Policies Will Be Closer to People's Needs

Today, President Lai Ching-te, who is also the leader of the Democratic Progressive Party, visited Cimu Temple in Luzhu District, Taoyuan. President Lai said that his visit was to listen to what everyone thinks and to understand problems. This is to make government actions better for the public. He said the central government will definitely help with anything it can do. He did not specifically talk about election plans.

President Lai has started a "Unity Tour" where he visits all 22 cities and counties in Taiwan. Today, he was with others like the DPP Secretary-General Hsu Kuo-yung and presidential office officials. In the morning, he went to Hsinchu City. In the afternoon, he visited Cimu Temple in Luzhu District, Taoyuan. After his visit to the temple, President Lai talked to reporters.

President Lai explained that this tour is to listen to local party members, government workers, and people from all parts of society. This will help make future government work more aligned with what people want and run more smoothly. He wants party and local officials to tell him about problems with development. He said the central government will definitely help if it can. The central and local governments will work together to help cities and the country improve in the future. He did not specifically talk about election plans.

After this, President Lai traveled by car to the South Garden Resort Hotel to meet with party and government officials.

Israeli Foreign Minister: Gaza War Could End if Hamas Releases Hostages and Gives Up Weapons

Today, Israel's Foreign Minister, Gideon Saar, spoke at a press conference in Jerusalem with Denmark's Foreign Minister. He said that the war in Gaza could end if Hamas, a Palestinian armed group, lets go of the people they are holding and gives up their weapons.

Reuters news agency reported that yesterday, Hamas repeated its usual stance. They said they would release all the people they are holding if Israel agrees to end the war and removes its soldiers from Gaza City.

Yesterday, Israel bombed a tall building in Gaza City, destroying it. Israel said that Hamas was using the building to "watch" Israeli soldiers. Israel also said it took "steps to reduce harm to civilians."

Hamas said this attack was wrong. They also said that they do not use homes or other buildings for military reasons.
