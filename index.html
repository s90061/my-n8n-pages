The "Power Industry Union" only has about a hundred members.

Taipower said tonight in a press release that all repair photos released to the public were real pictures taken by workers randomly during their actual work. There were no orders or requests for fake poses. Only sometimes, when news reporters came to film, they might have asked workers to help a little for their reports.

Taipower said the company greatly respects and thanks all its employees for their hard work. It will also continue to make working conditions better for repair workers. This includes giving them bonuses, extra time off, and danger pay. Taipower hopes that everyone in society will keep giving the repair workers on the front lines a lot of support.
