Former NBA Star Guard John Wall Announces Retirement After 11-Year Career

Today, American basketball star John Wall announced his retirement on social media. Wall used to be one of the fastest point guards in the entire league and was chosen for the All-Star game five times.

Wall posted a video on his Instagram account to announce his retirement. In the video, he said, "Every jersey I wore meant much more than just wins and numbers. It represented something deeper."

He also added, "Today, I will step off the court, but I will not leave basketball. Basketball will always be a part of my life. As new chances come, I feel now is the right time to confidently move into the next part of my life."

Wall will turn 35 next month. He played for the Washington Wizards, Houston Rockets, and Los Angeles Clippers. Later in his career, he had many knee problems and has not played since the 2022-2023 season.

Wall, known as "Wall-Star," was chosen for the All-Star game five times. In his 11 seasons in the NBA, he played a total of 647 games. On average, he scored 18.7 points, made 8.9 assists, got 4.2 rebounds, and had 1.6 steals per game.
