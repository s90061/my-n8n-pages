Luka Doncic Signs New Contract with Lakers for 3 Years, Donates Money to Help Athletes

The NBA team Los Angeles Lakers announced today that they have signed a new contract with Luka Doncic. He is a five-time All-Star player. The contract is for three years and is worth 165 million US dollars. This is about 4.9 billion New Taiwan dollars.

Reuters reported that Doncic wrote on social media, "I just signed my new contract with the Lakers."

Doncic said, "I am happy to keep working to win a championship for the Lakers. I also want to make Lakers fans proud. Thank you to the Lakers, my teammates, and all the fans who have loved me since day one. This is just the start."

Doncic is 26 years old. He joined the Lakers in February this year. He came from the Dallas Mavericks in a big trade.

Since joining the Lakers, Doncic has played 28 regular season games for them. On average, he scored 28.2 points, got 8.1 rebounds, gave 7.5 assists, and made 1.6 steals per game.

Doncic also announced on social media, "Today, I also promised to donate 5 million US dollars. This is about 148 million New Taiwan dollars. It will help 77 young athletes from all over the world follow their dreams, just like I did. Basketball has given me everything. I am lucky to give back and help the next generation."

Weather Agency Warns of Heavy Rain: Tainan, Kaohsiung, Pingtung Mountains to Get Very Heavy Rain

The Central Weather Agency has issued a special report for heavy rain. A southwest air current is causing strong rain for short periods.

Today, parts of Tainan City mountains, Kaohsiung City mountains, and Pingtung County mountains will have very heavy rain. Areas in Tainan City, Kaohsiung City, and Pingtung County will have heavy rain. Parts of Yunlin County, Chiayi County, Hengchun Peninsula, and Taitung County mountains will have heavy rain. There is also a chance of heavy rain in Miaoli, central Taiwan, Taitung, and Penghu.

The Central Weather Agency also reminds people to watch out for lightning and strong winds. In mountain areas, be careful of landslides, falling rocks, mudslides, and sudden floods. In low areas, be careful of flooding. People should not go into mountain areas or rivers.

The areas with very heavy rain are Tainan City, Kaohsiung City, and Pingtung County.

Ukraine Finds Defense Corruption Case: Electronic Warfare and Drone Prices Were Too High

Ukrainian authorities said today they have arrested several politicians. These people are involved in a big corruption case in the defense department. Before this, people protested because the independence of Ukraine's two main anti-corruption agencies was taken away. After the protests, the parliament passed a new anti-corruption law.

Agence France-Presse (AFP) reported that Ukraine passed a law at the end of July this year. This law took away the independence of the National Anti-Corruption Bureau of Ukraine (NABU) and the Specialised Anti-Corruption Prosecutor's Office (SAPO). It put them under the control of the Prosecutor General. The President appoints the Prosecutor General.

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy gave in on July 31st. He made the two agencies independent again. This happened after strong disagreement from allies and the first street protests against the government in Ukraine since Russia's full invasion in 2022.

The National Anti-Corruption Bureau said today that it and the Specialised Anti-Corruption Prosecutor's Office found a case. It was about "systematic stealing of money given by local governments for the needs of the defense forces. It also involved taking and giving large amounts of illegal money."

The National Anti-Corruption Bureau said that this corruption case involved making the prices of electronic warfare and drone equipment too high. Then, 30% of the contract money was stolen.

The suspects in the case include a member of parliament, several heads of local and city government offices, members of the National Guard, and leaders of defense companies.

The National Anti-Corruption Bureau said they have made four arrests so far. But they have not shared the names of the people arrested.
