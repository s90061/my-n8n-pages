Heavy Rain Affects Alishan Forest Railway, Fully Closed on July 28

(Central News Agency reporter Huang Guofang, Chiayi County, 28th) Because of heavy rain from a low-pressure system, the Chiayi County Government quickly announced today that work and school would stop. The Alishan Forest Railway and Cultural Heritage Office said that the entire railway line has stopped running as a safety measure. They will announce when it will open again later, depending on the situation.

The Alishan Forest Railway and Cultural Heritage Office also said that people who have already bought tickets can get a full refund. They can do this at any Alishan Forest Railway station or at any 7-Eleven IBON machine in Taiwan. They have one year from the date they planned to travel to get their money back. For more information, please check the Alishan Forest Railway website. (Edited by Guan Zhongwei) 1140728

Chiayi County Experiences Heavy Rain, Taibao and Other Areas Reach Level 1 Flood Warning Zone

(Central News Agency reporter Huang Guofang, Chiayi County, 28th) Because of a low-pressure system and southwest winds, Chiayi County had heavy or very heavy rain in some areas today. The Chiayi County Government said that Taibao City, Puzi City, and Xingang Township are now in a Level 1 flood warning area. As a safety step, they have closed the east and west lanes of the Zhonghe interchange on Provincial Highway 82.

The Central Weather Administration of the Ministry of Transportation issued a heavy rain warning. Due to the low-pressure system and southwest winds, there will likely be strong, short periods of rain. Today, Chiayi City and Chiayi County have had heavy or very heavy rain in some parts. In Taibao, Chiayi County, the hourly rainfall reached 118 millimeters early in the morning. Xingang Township, Minxiong Township, and Shuishang Township also had more than 90 millimeters of rain per hour.

A resident named Cai from Puzi, Chiayi County, said that it rained and thundered almost all night in their neighborhood, which is near Chang Gung Memorial Hospital in Chiayi. When they woke up in the morning, they were shocked to see their garage flooded. They have lived in this neighborhood for many years, and this is the first time they have experienced this.

The Chiayi County Government also announced that the intersections of Provincial Highway 82 and County Road 175 in Shuishang Township are flooded. Police went to the area and found the water was knee-deep. They have closed the east and west lanes of the Zhonghe interchange on Provincial Highway 82 and the north and south lanes of County Road 175 as a safety measure. The areas with a Level 1 flood warning include Taibao City, Puzi City, Dalin Township, Minxiong Township, Xingang Township, Liujiao Township, and Dongshi Township.

The county government said that the service road next to Provincial Highway 82 at the 13.1-kilometer mark in Puzi City was also flooded. The Daxing Joint Police Station in Puzi found that the water was too deep for safe travel and closed the road as a safety measure. (Edited by Guan Zhongwei) 1140728
