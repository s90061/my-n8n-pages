Cheng Li-wen, a Former Member of the Green Party, Becomes the Leader of the Kuomintang.

Cheng Li-wen, who used to be a lawmaker for the Kuomintang party, has become the new leader of the party. She started her political career with the Democratic Progressive Party, which is often called the Green Party. Later, she left that party and joined the Kuomintang. She has held many important roles in politics. This time, she was a surprise candidate, known for her strong and fighting spirit. She won against other candidates, like Hau Lung-bin, who used to be the mayor of Taipei. Her win is a big surprise in Taiwan's politics.

The results of the Kuomintang party leader election are now known. Cheng Li-wen, a former Kuomintang lawmaker, won. She beat other candidates, including Hau Lung-bin, who was the mayor of Taipei, and Lo Chih-chiang, who is a Kuomintang lawmaker. She is now the new leader of the Kuomintang. She is also the second woman to be chosen as party leader by direct votes from party members, after former leader Hung Hsiu-chu.

Cheng Li-wen was born in Yunlin and grew up in Tainan. When she was a university student at National Taiwan University, she studied law. During that time, she was part of the Wild Lily Student Movement. After finishing university, she joined the Democratic Progressive Party (DPP). She worked for the DPP as a National Assembly member and the Deputy Director of the DPP Youth Department. However, in 2002, she commented on a case called the "Tu Hsing-che ear-licking incident." Because of her comments, the DPP stopped her from doing party activities. So, Cheng Li-wen decided to leave the party.

After Cheng Li-wen left the DPP, she stayed away from politics for a while. On March 27, 2004, a group of parties called the "pan-blue camp" gathered to protest the unfair presidential election. Cheng Li-wen was the host of this event, which showed she was now supporting the pan-blue side. Later that same year, she ran for a lawmaker position in Kaohsiung City, but she did not win.

However, Cheng Li-wen is a good speaker, a sharp host, and she can explain her ideas clearly. Because of these skills, Lien Chan, who was the leader of the Kuomintang at that time, noticed her. In 2005, she was asked to join the Kuomintang party. After that, she worked as the head of the Kuomintang's Culture and Communications Committee and as a spokesperson. In the 2008 lawmaker election, the Kuomintang chose her as a party-list lawmaker, and she won. But in 2012, she ran for a local lawmaker position in Taichung City and lost to Ho Hsin-chun from the DPP.

Cheng Li-wen was brave and joined elections in areas that were hard to win. This showed her as a strong fighter, which is not common for the Kuomintang. After she lost the lawmaker election, Chen Chun, who was the Premier at that time, asked her to be the spokesperson for the Executive Yuan. She earned a lot of trust from important government leaders.

In 2014, Cheng Li-wen stopped being the spokesperson for the Executive Yuan. She then started working in media and hosted TV shows. In the 2020 lawmaker election, Wu Den-yih, who was the leader of the Kuomintang then, chose her to be a party-list lawmaker for the 10th Legislative Yuan. She won again and went back to the Legislative Yuan. After that, she became the party whip for the Kuomintang in the Legislative Yuan.

During the 10th session of the Legislative Yuan, the COVID-19 pandemic started all over the world. After Cheng Li-wen became the party whip, she became a very strong speaker for the party. She asked many tough questions and often stood at the forefront to argue and speak out for her party.

For this Kuomintang party leader election, Cheng Li-wen was a surprise candidate. She visited many local communities and showed a strong, fighting style that was different from most traditional Kuomintang members. Many people from the military, government, and education fields supported her. In the end, she beat her opponents and became the leader of the party.

Cheng Li-wen has successfully become the party leader. But she is not from the usual Kuomintang background. The Kuomintang is a very complex party with strong local groups. It will be a challenge for her to manage the party and work carefully with the Taiwan People's Party. Her biggest test will be to help the Kuomintang win power again in 2028. This challenge has just begun.

Chunghwa Telecom Supports Taiwan Series; Chairman Chien Chih-cheng Throws the First Pitch.

Chunghwa Telecom is a big supporter of baseball in Taiwan. They are sponsoring the 2025 CPBL Chunghwa Telecom Taiwan Series again this year. Today, their chairman, Chien Chih-cheng, was asked to throw the first pitch. He threw a good ball in front of 40,000 fans at the stadium.

The Taiwan Series, which is the big championship for professional baseball in Taiwan, started at the Taipei Dome. The CTBC Brothers team played against the Rakuten Monkeys team. They will play up to seven games, and the first team to win four games will be the champion. The teams announced that 40,000 people came to watch, which is the most people ever for the first game of a championship series. Chunghwa Telecom is the main sponsor of this event, and Chairman Chien Chih-cheng was asked to throw the first pitch.

Chunghwa Telecom said that their services, MOD and Hami Video CPBLTV, are showing all the games of the Taiwan Series. This means that fans who cannot go to the stadium can still watch the games. They can see who wins the championship and feel the exciting atmosphere of Taiwan's top baseball event.

Chunghwa Telecom has two platforms, MOD and Hami Video. For a long time, they have shown many live sports events. These include baseball playoffs from the United States, high school basketball games (HBL), professional volleyball games in Taiwan (TPVL), and international badminton and table tennis matches.

Also, Chunghwa Telecom invests in TV shows and movies. Their projects were chosen for many awards at the 60th Golden Bell Awards, with a total of 37 nominations. For example, their main Taiwanese drama, "The Outlaw Doctor," was nominated for 15 awards. These awards include Best Drama and Best Director for a Drama.

Red Warning for Swallow Grotto Dammed Lake; Some Areas in Hsiulin Township Close Schools and Offices on October 19.

Today, October 18, there is a red warning for a dammed lake at Swallow Grotto in the Liwu River. Because of this, the Hsiulin Township Office in Hualien County has announced that some areas will close schools and offices tomorrow, October 19. These areas include the larger Tianxiang region (Guanyuan, Luoshao, Xibao, and Tianxiang). Also, the Minle Community in the 9th neighborhood of Fushi Village, and the Minyou Community in the 1st to 3rd neighborhoods of Hsiulin Village will be closed.
