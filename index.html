President Lai's National Day Speech: Three Strategies for Competition and Building a "Shield of Taiwan" for Defense

The National Day event took place on October 10th. This year, the Ministry of National Defense's band played a song from last year's World Baseball Premier12 tournament. Teams like the Little League World Series champions were welcomed in special "hero" vehicles. Five "Brave Eagle" advanced jet trainers from the Air Force flew over the Presidential Office, releasing colored smoke.

President Lai Ching-te spoke, saying that "Democratic Taiwan," which was created through much hard work, is the clear identity for the 23 million people of Taiwan, Penghu, Kinmen, and Matsu in the world. Taiwan is a shining example of democracy in Asia, and it always gives hope to everyone living under authoritarian rule.

President Lai stated that to keep Taiwan competitive, the government will use three main strategies. These are: increasing investment, making international trade cooperation stronger, and developing important industries. The goal is for Taiwan to become one of the top five computing power centers globally. Also, Taiwan's defense budget for next year will be more than 3% of its GDP, and it will reach 5% of GDP by 2030. The increased defense spending has three main goals. One of these is to quickly build the "Shield of Taiwan" (T-Dome). This system will create a strong air defense that can stop attacks at different levels, sense threats very well, and block them effectively.
