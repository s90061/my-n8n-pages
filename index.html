UN: Over 1,000 people killed in Gaza trying to get aid since late May

The United Nations (UN) said today that more than 1,000 Palestinians trying to get food in the Gaza Strip have been killed by Israeli soldiers. This has happened since the "Gaza Humanitarian Fund" (GHF), supported by the US and Israel, started working at the end of May.

A spokesperson for the UN Human Rights Office, Thameen Al-Kheetan, told Agence France-Presse (AFP): "By July 21, we have recorded a total of 1,054 people killed in Gaza while trying to get food."

Al-Kheetan said: "Out of these, 766 people were killed near the Gaza Humanitarian Fund sites, and 288 people died near aid convoys from the UN and other humanitarian groups." He also clearly stated that the victims were "killed by the Israeli army."

Mohammad Abu Salmiya, the director of Al-Shifa Hospital, which is the biggest hospital in Gaza, told reporters today that 21 children have died from a lack of food and hunger in different parts of Gaza in the last three days.

US leaves UNESCO again; Secretary-General expresses deep regret

Today, the United States announced that it has left UNESCO, which is the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization. The US said that being part of UNESCO anymore is not good for America's national interests. Audrey Azoulay, the head of UNESCO, said she was very sad about this.

Tammy Bruce, a spokesperson for the US Department of State, said that the United States has left UNESCO. She explained that UNESCO shows unfairness towards Israel and promotes "social and cultural issues that cause division." Because of this, staying in UNESCO is not good for America's national interests.

Audrey Azoulay, the head of UNESCO, felt very sad about this. She said, "I am deeply sad that President Donald Trump has decided again to make the United States leave UNESCO." But she also said that she was not surprised.

The US leaving is a bad thing for UNESCO. UNESCO was created after World War II to help countries work together in education, science, and culture to make peace. Its main office is in Paris.

During his first time as president, Trump also did similar things. He left the World Health Organization (WHO), the U.N. Human Rights Council, the Paris Agreement on climate change, and the Iran nuclear deal.

When Joe Biden became president in 2021, he changed these decisions. He made the US join UNESCO, WHO, and the Paris Agreement again.

Now that Trump is back in the White House, the US has left these international organizations again. He has decided for the US to leave WHO. He has also stopped giving money to UNRWA, which is a UN agency that helps Palestinian refugees. This is part of a review of how the US is involved in UN groups, which should finish in August.

UNESCO is most known for naming World Heritage Sites. These include the Grand Canyon in the US and the ancient city of Palmyra in Syria.

Taipei Dangdai Art Fair to pause in 2026, re-evaluating future model

The official website for the Taipei Dangdai Art Fair announced that it will not happen in 2026. This break will allow them to carefully look at the fair's plans for the future. They will check the way it is organized, the timing, its size, and how it is presented.

The website said that the Taipei Dangdai Art Fair will take a break in 2026. The fair's team will use this time to think about their plans. They will completely review how the next fair will be set up, its schedule, its size, and its format. They hope to plan its growth step by step, which will continue to positively affect the art world in Taiwan and the surrounding areas.

On its website, the Taipei Dangdai Art Fair stated that it aims to be a key place for modern art in Taiwan and the whole region. "We are proud of the community we have built together. This community is made of galleries, partners, collectors, curators, and all visitors. They have all helped to create what the Taipei Dangdai Art Fair is known for."

The website also said that they are thankful for the support and trust from everyone for the Taipei Dangdai Art Fair. The fair feels honored to have worked with many galleries, partners, collectors, curators, and many art lovers. Together, they have built this diverse and lively community. "Because of everyone's involvement and support, the Taipei Dangdai Art Fair has become what it is today."

UBS Group organizes the "Taipei Dangdai Art Fair." It started in Taipei in 2019. Its goal is to show visitors the newest trends in modern art in Asia "right now." Art and new ideas are the two main parts of the fair. They want art to connect different people in society, so that everyone can talk and share ideas.
