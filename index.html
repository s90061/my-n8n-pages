US Stocks Rise Despite Trump's Action Against Fed Official

On August 26th, in New York, US President Trump ordered to remove Lisa Cook from her position as a Federal Reserve (Fed) Governor. This action might lead to a legal dispute between them. However, investors ignored these possible problems for now, and all four major US stock indexes closed higher today.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went up by 135.60 points, which is 0.30%, closing at 45418.07 points.

The S&P 500 index increased by 26.62 points, or 0.41%, ending at 6456.94 points.

The Nasdaq index rose by 94.98 points, or 0.44%, to 21544.27 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index increased by 51.62 points, or 0.90%, closing at 5807.92 points.

Global Oil Prices Fall as Market Awaits Clear Supply Signs

On August 26th, in New York, US President Trump tried to remove Federal Reserve (Fed) Governor Lisa Cook from her job, which caused some market instability. At the same time, investors were waiting for clearer information about oil supply. Because of this, international oil prices closed lower today, ending a four-day period of rising prices.

The price for West Texas Intermediate crude oil for October delivery, traded in New York, dropped by 1.55 US dollars, or 2.39%. It closed at 63.25 US dollars per barrel.

The price for Brent crude oil for October delivery, traded in London, fell by 1.58 US dollars, or 2.30%. It closed at 67.22 US dollars per barrel.

Apple's Fall Event on September 9th, New iPhones and More Expected

On August 26th, in San Francisco, Apple company officially sent out invitations to the media today. They announced that their fall event will be held on September 9th (1:00 AM on September 10th in Taipei time). During this event, Apple is expected to show new iPhone models, watches, and other devices.

Reuters reported that Apple will hold its fall event at the Steve Jobs Theater in Apple Park, which is the company's main office in California. Apple plans to show how it is using Artificial Intelligence (AI) in its devices.

Investors who own Apple shares are watching this event closely. They are worried that Apple might be slower than other companies in using new AI technology.

News reports also say that Apple will show a new iPhone that is thinner. It might be called iPhone Air, similar to the names of the iPad Air and MacBook Air.

Bloomberg News also said that Apple is expected to introduce new basic and advanced Apple Watch smartwatches, updated iPad Pro devices, and a faster Vision Pro headset.
