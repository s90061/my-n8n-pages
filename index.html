Brothers Baseball Team Signs All 8 Drafted Players; First Pick Cai Sheng-jie Gets NT$4.9 Million

The CTBC Brothers baseball team announced today that all 8 new players chosen in this year's draft have signed contracts. The first player picked, infielder Cai Sheng-jie, received a signing bonus of NT$4.9 million and a monthly salary of NT$60,000. Pitcher Hsu Lei, chosen in the second round, had an old injury found during his health check. He signed a conditional contract with a monthly salary of NT$60,000 for the minor league.

The Brothers team shared that they have signed all 8 new players from the 2025 Chinese Professional Baseball League draft. These include 5 pitchers and 3 infielders. All of them have recently had their health checks and started training with the CTBC Brothers' minor league and farm team. The team hopes these new players will bring good competition and become important future players for the "Mighty Elephants" team.

The Brothers officially announced that Cai Sheng-jie, from Guobao Home Economics and Commercial Vocational High School, who was the first pick, signed for NT$4.9 million and a monthly salary of NT$60,000. Cai Sheng-jie is a good player who can play both inner and outer parts of the field. He is also strong in both attacking and defending, and the team expects him to be a future star. Currently, Cai Sheng-jie is preparing with the Taiwan team for the U18 World Cup Baseball Tournament. He is expected to join the team in late September. The team hopes to see Cai Sheng-jie show his skills in the main league soon and help the team win games.

The second player picked was pitcher Hsu Lei, from Mailiao High School in Yunlin County. During his three years in high school, with the help of his coaches and his own hard work, he won many pitching awards. However, Hsu Lei and Wang Xing-kai, an infielder picked in the fifth round, had old injuries found during their health checks. After talking with the team, they signed conditional contracts to join the CTBC Brothers' training system with monthly salaries of NT$60,000 and NT$50,000 respectively. The team hopes Hsu Lei and Wang Xing-kai will get better and become very important players for the "Yellow Shirts" team in the future.

Jiao Zi-jie, a catcher chosen in the third round by CTBC Brothers, officially joined the team with a signing bonus of NT$2.9 million and a monthly salary of NT$60,000. Pitcher Lu Yu-ting, chosen in the fourth round, officially joined the "Mighty Elephants" team with a signing bonus of NT$2.4 million and a monthly salary of NT$60,000.

Qian Ke-lun, a pitcher picked in the sixth round, joined with a signing bonus of NT$1.1 million and a monthly salary of NT$60,000. Pitchers Chen Ying-rui and Gao Bing-yang, picked in the seventh and eighth rounds, joined the CTBC Brothers with minor league monthly salaries of NT$75,000 and NT$70,000 respectively.

Also, the team recently registered 3 self-trained players with the league. This means a total of 11 new players have joined the team. Team leader Liu Zhi-wei said, "We hope these new players will continue to improve their skills and mental strength for professional baseball. We want them to play their best and become important new players for the team's future."

School Year Starting: Pingtung Police Warn University Students, "Your Diploma Should Not Be Paid For With a Car Accident"

The new school year is about to begin. The Pingtung police have seen over 3,600 traffic accidents involving university students from January to August this year. To help, the police have released a video about safe riding called "Your Diploma Should Not Be Paid For With a Car Accident." It uses real accident videos to teach students how to drive safely.

The Pingtung County Police Bureau reported that from January to August this year, there were 3,650 road accidents involving university students aged 18 to 22. These accidents caused 2,147 injuries or deaths. Compared to the same time last year, the number of accidents went down by 549, and the number of injuries and deaths went down by 392. Overall, the numbers are getting lower, but there are still many accidents.

The Pingtung County Police Bureau said in a press release today that many new university students are riding motorcycles for the first time. They may not have enough driving experience, might not know the roads well, or don't know about defensive driving. These things often lead to accidents. To make university students aware of the dangers of riding, the police made the video "Your Diploma Should Not Be Paid For With a Car Accident." It uses real accident scenes and strong words to help students learn safe driving ideas.

The video says, "If you don't yield to a car going straight when turning, it's not just against the rules, it can also kill you." It also says, "Wait three more seconds, and you can be safe for life." These lively phrases remind young riders that "speed does not guarantee safety. Going fast doesn't mean you will arrive safely; going slowly gives you a chance to arrive safely."

The police looked at common reasons for accidents. These include not keeping a safe distance from other vehicles, turning vehicles not yielding to straight-going vehicles, driving while distracted, and running red lights. This shows that many students have not fully learned safe driving habits after getting their driver's licenses.

The Pingtung police said they will continue to catch people who drive without a license, drive dangerously, speed, and run red lights. This is to keep students safe and maintain good traffic order around schools.

Department of Defense May Be Renamed Department of War; White House Looks for Ways to Do What Trump Wants

After US President Trump asked for the Department of Defense to be renamed the Department of War, his team is actively working on plans to make this happen. The Wall Street Journal reported that White House officials said Congress might need to approve this. However, the White House is looking for other ways to make this plan real.

The Department of Defense is the biggest department in the US government. Reuters reported that Greg Steube, a Republican member of the House of Representatives from Florida, has added a change to the yearly defense policy bill. This shows that some Republican members of Congress support Trump's idea.

Anna Kelly, a spokesperson for the White House, said, "As President Trump said, our military should focus on attacking, not just defending. This is why the President prioritizes soldiers at the Pentagon, not policies about diversity, equality, and inclusion (DEI), or 'woke' ideas. Please stay tuned!"

Trump has often suggested this idea since he became president. On August 25th, he said, "When it was called the Department of War, we won all wars. We won everything."

The Wall Street Journal reported that Trump said the old name "sounds stronger" when he met with South Korean President Lee Jae-myung in the Oval Office that day. He also said the change would be done in "about a week." Trump was talking about wars the US fought before World War II ended and before the Department of Defense was created.

A former official said that the Pentagon started preparing legal proposals for this change in the first few weeks of Trump's second term as president. One idea was to ask Congress for permission to use the old name during a national emergency. Also, the highest civilian official of the Department of Defense would once again be called "Secretary of War."

Reuters reported that the White House did not share the details of this plan. However, they stressed what Trump said this week about the US military's ability to attack.

The US Department of War slowly became the Department of Defense after a series of changes. This change started with the National Security Act passed in 1947. This law combined the Army, Navy, and Air Force into one group called the "National Military Establishment."

An amendment passed in 1949 officially used the name "Department of Defense" and created the current organizational structure.

While Trump and Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth work to make the US military look stronger, they are also pushing for other changes. This includes removing many high-ranking military officers who did not agree with Trump.

Also, the Trump government tried to stop transgender people from joining the military and wanted transgender soldiers currently serving to leave. The Pentagon said that transgender people are not fit to serve. However, civil rights activists argued that this is not true and is illegal discrimination.
