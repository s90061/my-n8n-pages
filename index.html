Reuters Poll: Most Americans Believe Countries Should Recognize Palestine

More than half (58%) of Americans think that all countries in the United Nations should recognize Palestine as a country. This is according to a new poll by Reuters/Ipsos. 33% of people asked did not agree, and 9% did not answer.

Reuters/Ipsos did an online poll for six days. They asked 4446 adults across the United States. The results could be wrong by about 2 percent more or less. This poll showed that Americans have different opinions about recognizing Palestine, based on their political party.

78% of Democrats support recognizing Palestine. This is much higher than Republicans, who support former US President Trump (41% support). A little over half of Republicans (53%) do not agree that all UN countries should recognize Palestine.

About 65% of people asked said that the United States should do something in Gaza to help people who are very hungry. 28% of people did not agree with this, and 41% of those were Republicans.

The poll also showed that 59% of Americans believe Israel's military actions in Gaza are too much. 33% of people asked did not agree with this view.

CNBC: Intel Talks with Big Investors About Getting More Money at a Lower Price

US business news channel CNBC says that Intel is talking with other big investors. Intel wants to get more money from them by selling shares at a lower price. This information comes from people who know about the talks.

Earlier this week, Japan's SoftBank Group Corp. said it would invest 2 billion US dollars in Intel. There were also rumors that the US government might invest in Intel in different ways. After this, Intel's stock price went up for a short time. But today, its stock price fell by more than 7%.

Yesterday, Howard Lutnick, the US Secretary of Commerce, told CNBC that the US government must get a share of Intel's ownership if it gives money from the "CHIPS Act" to Intel.

People who know about the situation say that Intel is now looking for other big investors, not just SoftBank, to get more money.

Intel has had fewer sales and a smaller share of the market for many years. Now, the company is trying to change and become strong again.

Markets Wait for Fed Meeting Notes; European Stocks End Mixed

The US Federal Reserve (Fed) will soon release notes from its latest meeting. Also, the yearly meeting of central banks from around the world will happen this week. Many investors are waiting to see what happens. They hope to find clues about what will happen with US interest rates. Because of this, major stock markets in Europe ended the day with mixed results. Some went up, some went down.

The FTSE 100 index in London went up by 98.92 points, which is 1.08%. It closed at 9288.14 points.

The DAX index in Frankfurt went down by 146.10 points, which is 0.60%. It closed at 24276.97 points.

The CAC 40 index in Paris went down by 6.05 points, which is 0.08%. It closed at 7973.03 points.
