Hong Kong's Managed Assets Reach US$4.53 Trillion, Up 13%

The Hong Kong financial authority announced today that the total value of assets managed in Hong Kong reached US$4.53 trillion (about NT$133.3 trillion) in 2024. This is a 13% increase from the year before. Also, new money coming into the city grew by 81% compared to the previous year.

The Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) of Hong Kong shared its "2024 Asset and Wealth Management Activities Survey" today. The survey said that the growth in managed assets mainly came from US$91 billion of new money. The survey also described Hong Kong as an important place for managing money and wealth, saying it is "getting better."

The survey pointed out that in private banking and wealth management, the assets managed went up by 15% to US$1.3 trillion. For funds started in Hong Kong and approved by the SFC, their total value increased by 22% to US$211 billion. By the end of May this year, this value grew even more to US$256 billion.

In 2024, new money coming into these funds was US$20.9 billion. For the first five months of this year, it was US$30.5 billion.

The survey also showed that 59% of the assets managed in Hong Kong are invested outside mainland China and Hong Kong.

Overall, Hong Kong's asset and wealth management business includes managing assets, advising funds, private banking and wealth management, and real estate investment trusts and trust assets approved by the Hong Kong SFC.

Afternoon Thunderstorms Cause Songshan Airport to Stop Ground Work Twice, Affecting 19 Flights

Due to afternoon thunderstorms, Songshan Airport stopped its ground operations two times today. This affected a total of 19 flights.

The Central Weather Administration of the Ministry of Transportation and Communications issued a heavy rain warning. This is because strong rain clouds are forming, which can cause heavy rain for short periods. Today, Taipei City and New Taipei City might have heavy rain or downpours. Keelung, Taoyuan, Hsinchu, Nantou, and mountainous areas in Miaoli, Yilan, and Hualien might also have heavy rain. People should be careful of lightning and strong winds. In mountainous areas, there is a risk of landslides and falling rocks. In low areas, be careful of flooding.

Because of the afternoon thunderstorms, Songshan Airport stopped ground work at 1:21 PM. The storm passed at 2:17 PM. This affected 10 flights.

Since the rain kept falling, Songshan Airport stopped ground work again at 2:53 PM. Some flights might be delayed because of this.

The Civil Aeronautics Administration of the Ministry of Transportation and Communications said that Songshan Airport stopped having thunderstorms at 3:17 PM. This second stop affected 9 flights.
