Moscow Airport Accident: Rossiya Airlines Plane's Tail Damaged

News from Russia says that a plane from Rossiya Airlines had an accident today at Sheremetyevo Airport in Moscow. The plane touched another plane on a taxiway. This caused damage to the back part, or tail, of the Rossiya Airlines plane.

Reuters news agency reported that the plane was a Sukhoi Superjet. The accident happened in the evening. The plane was getting ready to fly to St. Petersburg, which is the second-largest city in Russia.

All the people on the plane had to leave. They were put on another plane to continue their journey.

Some unofficial news from Russian Telegram channels said that the other plane involved in the accident was an Airbus A330 from China's Hainan Airlines. This plane was supposed to fly to Beijing.

Youtuber Sun Sheng Accused of Sexual Crimes, Prosecutors Ask for Severe Punishment

Sun Sheng, a member of the YouTube group "Wackyboys," is accused of serious sexual crimes. He is said to have sexually assaulted a teenage girl by inviting her to watch him practice dancing. Strong Shallow Earthquake Hits Venezuela, Felt in Colombia and Nearby Islands

The U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) reported that a 6.2 magnitude earthquake happened today in western and northern Venezuela, including the capital city, Caracas. The Venezuelan government stated that there are no reports of serious damage yet.

According to Agence France-Presse (AFP), the USGS said the earthquake's center was about 24 kilometers from Mene Grande. This is a small oil town with few people in Zulia state. The earthquake was shallow, only 7.8 kilometers deep.

The Colombian Geological Survey measured the earthquake as 6.1 magnitude and called it a "shallow" earthquake. People also felt the earthquake in nearby Colombia and on the Caribbean islands of Aruba, Curacao, and Bonaire.

In Venezuela, strong earthquakes are not common. Because of the shaking buildings, many people in cities like Caracas, the capital, and Maracaibo became scared.

Many people went out into the streets to be safe. But the Interior Minister, Diosdado Cabello, said on state television that the earthquake "did not cause major damage to buildings."

Venezuela's national earthquake research center, Funvisis, measured the earthquake at a magnitude of 5.4.

About 80% of people in Venezuela live in areas where earthquakes can happen. However, Venezuela has not had a big earthquake since 1997. That year, an earthquake hit Cariaco in Sucre state in the east, and 73 people died. In 1976, an earthquake in Caracas killed almost 300 people and injured 2,000.
