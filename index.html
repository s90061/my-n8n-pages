US Runner Melissa Jefferson-Wooden Wins Gold in Women's 100m, Sets New Record at World Athletics Championships

Melissa Jefferson-Wooden, a 24-year-old American runner, won the gold medal in the women's 100-meter final today at the World Athletics Championships. She also set a new championship record with a time of 10.61 seconds.

According to dpa news agency, Jefferson-Wooden's gold medal win in the women's 100-meter final at the World Athletics Championships also marked the fourth fastest time ever recorded in women's 100-meter sprint history.

Tina Clayton from Jamaica won the silver medal with a time of 10.76 seconds. Julien Alfred from Saint Lucia took the bronze medal with a time of 10.84 seconds.

Huang Hsiao-wen Wins Gold at WB Boxing World Championships; Mayor Chiang Wan-an Calls Her "Taipei's Pride"

Taiwanese female boxer Huang Hsiao-wen won a gold medal today in the 54 kg category at the WB World Boxing Championships. After her win, Taipei Mayor Chiang Wan-an quickly posted on social media, calling Huang Hsiao-wen "Taiwan's glory and Taipei's pride."

In the 54 kg gold medal match at the WB World Boxing Championships today, Huang Hsiao-wen beat American boxer Yoseline Perez 4-1. This was her third gold medal at the World Championships. She cried tears of joy in the ring after her victory.

After hearing the news tonight, Mayor Chiang Wan-an specially shared a photo of Huang Hsiao-wen and her coach Liu Tsung-tai on Instagram. He wrote, "Taiwan's glory, Taipei's pride."

Last December, Huang Hsiao-wen won the World Boxing Federation's year-end championship. Mayor Chiang Wan-an gave her a NT$100,000 reward. He praised her excellent performance and her spirit of never giving up. He hoped she would continue to do well and compete in the 2026 Asian Games in Nagoya, Japan.

The Taipei City Department of Sports said that Huang Hsiao-wen is a key athlete developed by their grassroots training center. This World Championships is her third time winning a gold medal, making her the first Taiwanese boxer to win three gold medals at the World Boxing Championships. The Department of Sports will continue to support athletes and be there for them.

Huang Hsiao-wen, who is 28 years old, won gold medals twice before in 2019 and 2023 at the World Championships organized by the International Boxing Association (IBA), which is now no longer recognized. This year, the World Boxing (WB) federation hosted the World Championships for the first time, and Huang Hsiao-wen won her third career gold medal.

Landowner in Meinong "Grand Canyon" Case, Former Assistant to Kaohsiung City Councilor, Shi Lijun, Detained for Questioning Overnight

Shi Lijun, a former assistant to independent Kaohsiung City Councilor Chu Hsin-chiang, is suspected of breaking environmental protection laws. Today, police searched and arrested Shi, her husband, Mr. Wu, and a tenant, Mr. Wang. The Qiaotou District Prosecutors Office stated that the three individuals are currently being questioned by prosecutors overnight.

Recently, KMT Legislator Ko Chih-en visited the site of the Meinong "Grand Canyon" case. Shi Lijun, former assistant to independent Kaohsiung City Councilor Chu Hsin-chiang, was one of the people there. Later, the Kaohsiung City Government confirmed that Shi was the landowner of the illegal mining pit, which was already on their watch list. She is suspected of breaking the Waste Disposal Act and was fined NT$3 million.

The city government explained at that time that Shi was involved in a new type of illegal sand and gravel mining. After she bought the land, illegal mining and dumping started happening unusually quickly. The city government had already collected information about this and sent all the evidence of illegal activity to the Qiaotou District Prosecutors Office for investigation.

Today, the police searched and arrested Shi, her husband Mr. Wu, and the tenant Mr. Wang. The decision on whether to take further legal action will be made after the prosecutors finish questioning them.

The whole case involves an illegal mining pit in the Xijiyang section of Meinong District. Last month, the city government and investigators worked together to stop these illegal activities. They sent the case for investigation under the Waste Disposal Act, and the person responsible has already been held. On September 11, the city government added more fines totaling NT$11.3 million, based on the Waste Disposal Act, the Soil and Rock Exploitation Act, and the Regional Plan Act. This means the total fines for this case are now NT$14.3 million.
