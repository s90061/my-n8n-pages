Next year's general budget proposal: Cho Jung-tai says a big money problem will lead to borrowing

Taiwan's Premier, Cho Jung-tai, said today that the government is busy preparing next year's budget. But because of a new law about money for local governments, there will be a big money problem. So, the government will need to borrow money to deal with it.

The plan for next year's government budget will be ready by the end of August. Premier Cho already held a meeting to check it. A new law about how money is shared means the central government must give 375.3 billion New Taiwan dollars to local governments. Also, money for defense must be more than 3% of the country's total income. People are watching to see how the budget will be made.

Today, the Legislative Yuan invited Premier Cho and other government leaders to talk about helping areas after Typhoon Danas and the heavy rains on July 28. They also answered questions.

Before the meeting, Premier Cho talked to reporters. He spoke about whether the government would give out cash to everyone when it changes a special law for recovery. He also talked about if the central government faced problems making next year's budget after the new money-sharing law started.

Premier Cho said that the special law for recovery has already been announced and started by President Lai Ching-te. Based on this law, they will carefully study how to change or increase help for certain types of businesses. They hope to change the special law and have the Legislative Yuan approve it. For other parts, they will mostly follow what President Lai announced.

Premier Cho stated that they are now fully working on next year's central government budget. Because the law about sharing money has changed, there will be a very big financial gap. They will have to borrow money to deal with it. So, next year's central government budget will involve a lot of borrowed money.

To end the Russia-Ukraine war, Trump says both countries must give up some land

U.S. President Trump said today that both Kyiv and Moscow must give up some land to stop the war between Russia and Ukraine. This week, Trump will talk with Russian President Putin. This meeting will quickly show if Putin wants to make a deal.

Reuters reported that Trump will meet Putin in Alaska, USA, on August 15. European leaders and Ukraine's President Zelenskyy plan to talk with Trump before this meeting. They are worried that Washington might try to force Ukraine to accept bad peace terms.

Trump has acted tougher towards Moscow. He agreed to send more U.S. weapons to Ukraine and threatened to put taxes on those who buy Russian oil. But Europe is still worried that Trump might agree to a deal that makes Kyiv give up a lot.

Kaja Kallas, who handles foreign policy and security for the European Union, said today that to end the war, there needs to be "unity across the Atlantic, support for Ukraine, and pressure on Russia." She added that they must also "stop Russia from attacking Europe in the future."

At a White House press conference, Trump spoke about his meeting with Putin. He said, "This is really a first talk." He added that they would know if they could make progress "in about the first two minutes."

He said, "I will tell him (Putin) that you must end this war."

Trump pointed out that future talks might include Zelenskyy. America's goal is to quickly stop this terrible fight that has lasted three and a half years. He plans to talk with European leaders soon after his meeting with Putin.

Trump has spoken before about exchanging land, but neither Russia nor Ukraine wants to give up land as part of a peace agreement.

Trump told reporters, "There will be some land exchange."

The Hill newspaper reported that Trump also said, "So there is good and bad, but the situation is very complicated because the border lines are very uneven. There will be some exchanges and some land changes."

Trump said, "I have learned through talking with Russia and everyone that this is good for Ukraine." He stated that Russia has taken some "very important land," but "we will try to get some important land back."

BBC reported that Trump mentioned if Putin offers a "fair deal" in the talks, he will tell European leaders about it. He added that, "out of respect," he would talk to Zelenskyy first.

He said, "I will call him first... I will also call him after the meeting. I might say, 'Good luck, keep fighting,' or I might say, 'We can make a deal.'"

Andriy Yermak, Zelenskyy's chief of staff, said he spoke with U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio today. He posted on X that lasting peace needs "a ceasefire with no conditions, which is the first step for real talks."

Typhoon Yangliu still getting stronger and bigger; Hualien, Taitung, and southern Taiwan on August 13 should prepare for strong winds and very heavy rain

The Weather Bureau announced a sea warning for Typhoon Yangliu early this morning. As it gets closer to Taiwan, it is expected to get a bit stronger and bigger. Yangliu will pass over Taiwan tomorrow. People in Hualien, Taitung, and southern Taiwan should prepare for very heavy rain and strong winds.

The Central Weather Bureau put out a sea warning for Moderate Typhoon Yangliu at 5:30 AM today. They expect to put out a land warning between noon and afternoon. At 8:00 AM, Yangliu's center was at 20.7 degrees North and 127.8 degrees East. This is about 730 kilometers southeast of Taitung. It is moving towards the northwest at 25 kilometers per hour.

Yangliu's central air pressure is 970 hectopascals. Its strongest winds near the center are 33 meters per second. The average area of strong winds (level 7) is 120 kilometers. The average area of very strong winds (level 10) is 40 kilometers.

Weather Bureau forecaster Liu Yu-chi said at a press conference that Yangliu is a threat to the seas east of Taiwan and the Bashi Channel. It will affect Taiwan tomorrow, bringing strong winds and heavy rain. As Yangliu gets closer to Taiwan, it is expected to get a bit stronger and bigger. Tomorrow during the day, Yangliu will pass over Taiwan. In the evening, it will enter the Taiwan Strait.

About the winds, Liu Yu-chi said that from tonight until early tomorrow morning, there will be strong gusts of 8 to 9 levels off the eastern coast. Winds along the coast will get stronger. Tomorrow during the day, most coastal areas will have gusts of 8 levels or more. Areas south of Changhua and Hualien and Taitung might have gusts of 11 levels. If the typhoon lands in Taitung, the gusts could be even stronger.

Liu Yu-chi pointed out that starting late tonight, moisture from the outer parts of the typhoon will move in. Keelung's north coast and Yilan will start to have scattered showers. Tomorrow, because of the typhoon, Hualien, Taitung, and southern Taiwan will have very heavy rain or more. Areas south of Chiayi and Penghu will also have heavy rain. On August 14, the typhoon will move away. Kinmen will still have strong winds and rain in the morning, but the wind and rain on Taiwan island will get lighter.

Liu Yu-chi warned that starting tonight, waves in the seas east of Taiwan and the Bashi Channel will slowly get bigger, reaching 2 to 3 meters high. The center of the typhoon might even cause large waves of 5 meters.
