Brothers Team Signs All 8 Draft Picks; First-Round Pick Cai Sheng-chieh Gets NT$4.9 Million

The CTBC Brothers, a professional baseball team in Taiwan, announced today that all 8 new players chosen in this year's draft have signed contracts. Cai Sheng-chieh, a position player picked in the first round, signed for NT$4.9 million and will earn NT$60,000 per month. The pitcher Xu Lei, chosen in the second round, had an old injury found during his medical check. He signed a conditional contract with a monthly salary of NT$60,000 for the minor league team.

The Brothers team said that all 8 new players from the 2025 Chinese Professional Baseball League draft have signed their contracts. These include 5 pitchers and 3 position players. They have all had their medical checks recently and have started training with the CTBC Brothers' minor league and farm teams. The team hopes that these new players will bring good competition and become important future players for the "Mighty Elephants" team.

The Brothers announced that Cai Sheng-chieh, a first-round pick from Guobao Home Economics and Commercial High School, signed for NT$4.9 million and a monthly salary of NT$60,000. Cai Sheng-chieh can play both infield and outfield, and he is good at both offense and defense. The team sees him as a future star. Currently, Cai Sheng-chieh is training with the Taiwan team for the U18 Baseball World Cup. He is expected to join the team in late September. Everyone hopes to see Cai Sheng-chieh play well in the main league soon and help the team achieve good results.

The pitcher Xu Lei, chosen in the second round, is from Mailiao High School in Yunlin County. During his three years in high school, with his coach's help and his own hard work, he won many pitching awards. However, Xu Lei and Wang Xing-kai, a position player picked by the CTBC Brothers in the fifth round, both had old injuries found during their medical checks. After talking with the team, they both signed conditional contracts to join the CTBC Brothers' training system, with monthly salaries of NT$60,000 and NT$50,000 respectively for the minor league team. The team hopes Xu Lei and Wang Xing-kai will improve and become key players for the "Yellow Shirts" in the future.

The catcher Jiao Zi-jie, picked in the third round by the CTBC Brothers, officially joined the team with a signing bonus of NT$2.9 million and a monthly salary of NT$60,000. The pitcher Lu Yu-ting, picked in the fourth round, officially joined the "Mighty Elephants" with a signing bonus of NT$2.4 million and a monthly salary of NT$60,000.

The pitcher Qian Ke-lun, picked in the sixth round, joined with a signing bonus of NT$1.1 million and a monthly salary of NT$60,000. Pitchers Chen Ying-rui and Gao Bing-yang, picked in the seventh and eighth rounds, joined the CTBC Brothers with minor league monthly salaries of NT$75,000 and NT$70,000 respectively.

Also, the team recently registered 3 self-trained players with the league, bringing a total of 11 new talented players to the team. Team leader Liu Zhi-wei said, "We hope these new players will continue to improve their skills and mental strength, work hard for their professional baseball careers, perform their best, and become important future players for the team."

School Year to Start: Pingtung Police Tell University Students "Don't Trade Your Diploma for an Accident"

The new school semester is coming soon. The police in Pingtung have looked at traffic accidents involving university students between January and August this year. There were more than 3,600 such accidents. The police have made a video to promote safe riding called "Don't trade your diploma for an accident." It uses real accident scenes to teach students how to drive safely.

The Pingtung County Police Department reported that from January to August this year, there were 3,650 road accidents involving university students aged 18 to 22. These accidents caused 2,147 injuries or deaths. Compared to the same period last year, the number of accidents went down by 549, and the number of injuries and deaths went down by 392. Overall, the numbers are decreasing, but there are still many accidents.

The Pingtung County Police Department said in a press release today that many new university students use motorcycles for the first time. They might not have enough driving experience, might not know the roads well, or might not know about defensive driving. This often causes accidents. To make university students aware of the dangers of riding, the police made the safety video "Don't trade your diploma for an accident." It uses real accident scenes and strong messages to help students learn correct driving habits.

The video explains, "If you don't yield when turning, it's not just breaking a rule, it can also kill." It also says, "Wait three more seconds for a lifetime of peace." The video uses lively language to remind young riders, "Speed does not guarantee safety. Being fast doesn't mean you'll arrive safely; going slow gives you a chance to get there safely."

Police looked at common reasons for accidents. These include not keeping a safe distance while driving, turning vehicles not yielding to straight-ahead vehicles, distracted driving, and running red lights. This shows that many students, after getting their driver's licenses, still don't fully understand driving safety.

Pingtung police ask everyone to be careful. They will continue to stop people for serious traffic violations like driving without a license, dangerous driving, speeding, and running red lights. This is to keep students safe and maintain good traffic order around schools.

Defense Department May Change Name to War Department; White House Seeks Ways to Follow Trump's Idea

After US President Trump asked to change the name of the Department of Defense to the Department of War, his team is working hard on plans for this. The Wall Street Journal reported that White House officials said this change might need approval from Congress. However, the White House is looking for other ways to make this idea happen.

The Department of Defense is the biggest part of the US federal government. Reuters reported that Greg Steube, a Republican member of the House of Representatives from Florida, has suggested this change in a yearly bill about defense policies. This shows that some Republican members in Congress support Trump's idea.

White House spokesperson Anna Kelly said, "As President Trump said, our military should focus on attacking, not just defending. This is why the President wants the Pentagon to focus on fighting forces, not on policies about diversity, equality, and inclusion (DEI), or 'woke' ideas. Please stay tuned!"

Trump has often suggested this idea since he became president. On August 25, he said, "When it was called the War Department, we won all our wars. We won everything."

The Wall Street Journal reported that on that day, when Trump met with South Korean President Lee Jae-myung in the Oval Office, he said the old name "sounds stronger." He also said the change would happen "in about a week." Trump was talking about wars the US fought before World War II ended and the Department of Defense was created.

A former official said that the Pentagon started preparing legal proposals for this change in the first few weeks of Trump's second term. One idea was to ask Congress for permission to use the old name during a national emergency. Also, they wanted the top civilian official of the Department of Defense to use the title "Secretary of War" again.

Reuters reported that the White House did not give many details about this plan. However, they strongly supported what Trump said this week about the US military's ability to attack.

The US War Department slowly changed into the Department of Defense after many changes. This change began with the National Security Act, passed in 1947. This law brought the Army, Navy, and Air Force together into one organization called the "National Military Establishment."

An update passed in 1949 officially named it the "Department of Defense." This created the structure it has today.

Trump and Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth are working to make the US military look tougher. They are also making other changes, such as removing several high-ranking military officers who did not agree with Trump.

Also, the Trump government tried to stop transgender people from joining the military and asked transgender people already serving to leave. The Pentagon said that transgender people are not suitable for military service, but civil rights groups said this was not true and was illegal discrimination.
