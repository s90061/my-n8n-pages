Trump Plans to Lower Taxes on EU Cars, Bloomberg Reports

Bloomberg says that people who know about the situation shared some information. The European Union (EU) believes that US President Trump will announce official actions this week. These actions will confirm lower taxes on cars from the EU. They will also remove taxes on some industrial goods, like airplane parts.

According to Bloomberg News, these sources also said that the US and EU will likely release a joint statement. This statement will explain the promises that Trump and EU Commission President Ursula von der Leyen made last month. The US will decide the legal details of these actions.

Based on the agreement between the EU and the US, America will put a 15% tax on most goods coming from the EU. Officials from both sides have said this tax rate will also apply to cars. This new 15% tax will replace the current 25% tax. It will also apply to any future actions for products like medicines and computer chips.

Foshan, China: Buy Fever and Joint Pain Medicine with Your ID to Track Chikungunya

The Chikungunya disease is spreading more in China. The number of confirmed cases is almost 7,000. To help the government find cases and stop the disease from spreading, officials in Foshan City, Guangdong Province, where the disease started, have made a new rule. From now on, all drugstores in the city must record your name if you buy any of the 47 types of medicine for Chikungunya symptoms, like fever, rash, or joint pain.

The Southern Metropolis Daily newspaper reported that these 47 medicines include well-known ones in China, like Lianhua Qingwen Capsules and Banlangen. They also have versions for children.

According to the report, today the Foshan City Market Supervision Administration shared a document on its WeChat official account. The document is called "Notice on Strengthening Registration Management for Key Controlled Medicines Sold by Retail Drugstores During the Epidemic (Chikungunya, called 'Jikongkenya fever' in China) Prevention and Control Period." It talks about the new rules and says they started on August 1st.

This notice says that all drugstores in Foshan City must follow a strict "registration and reporting system for key controlled medicines" starting from August 1st. This is part of Foshan City's plan to control the disease. The 47 medicines that need ID registration are for treating symptoms of Chikungunya, such as fever, rash, and joint pain.

The notice also says that when people buy these medicines at drugstores in Foshan City, they need to help the staff with "ID verification by scanning a code." They also need to fill in their personal information. If they have symptoms like fever, rash, or joint pain, or if they have been bitten by mosquitoes recently, they should tell the drugstore staff.

The notice states that for older people or others who cannot use smartphones or register by themselves, family members should help. Or, drugstore staff should give the necessary help to complete the registration.

The notice further mentions that all drugstores and related government departments will "strictly follow national laws on protecting personal information." They will keep the information collected from medicine buyers "strictly secret" and only use it "for epidemic prevention and control needs."

After the Chikungunya disease continued to spread, reaching Macau and Hong Kong, the head of China's National Disease Control and Prevention Administration, Shen Hongbing, went to Foshan on August 2nd. He went to guide the efforts to control Chikungunya. He asked them to try to stop the disease as quickly as possible. This is to make sure people's health is safe and that "social order is stable."
