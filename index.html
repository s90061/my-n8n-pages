TSG Hawks Sign New MLB-Level Foreign Pitcher, Named Namasia

The TSG Hawks baseball team announced today that they have signed a new foreign pitcher, Dinelson Lamet, also known as Namasia. He used to play in Major League Baseball (MLB) in the United States and has played in over a hundred games. He will wear jersey number 45. In his career, he played 121 games in the MLB. Last year, he was a teammate of Japanese star Shohei Ohtani when he played for the Dodgers.

The TSG Hawks team said today that they signed Namasia to make their pitching stronger. Namasia is 33 years old. He has played for several MLB teams, including the Padres, Rockies, Red Sox, Dodgers, and Royals. Last year, he played with Shohei Ohtani on the Dodgers team.

In his MLB career, Namasia played in a total of 121 games. He started 60 of those games. His record was 17 wins and 24 losses. He pitched a total of 367.2 innings, and he struck out batters 474 times. His earned run average (ERA) was 4.63.

Namasia was a starting pitcher in 2017, 2019, and 2020. In 2020, he started 12 games in MLB and pitched 69 innings. His ERA was very low, only 2.09. Batters only got 39 hits against him, and he struck out 93 batters. Last year, Namasia still played in MLB games. This year, he is playing in a league in Mexico. He has started 17 games there. His record is 7 wins and 5 losses, and his ERA is 5.01. He pitched 88 innings and struck out 88 batters.

The second half of the Chinese Professional Baseball League started on July 4th this year. The TSG Hawks currently have 7 wins, 6 losses, and 2 ties in the second half of the season. Their winning rate is 53.8%, and they are in second place.

Ministry of Transportation Connects Companies to Help People Repair Homes

Typhoon Danas and strong winds from the southwest have caused a lot of damage to homes in southern Taiwan. Today, the Ministry of Transportation said that there are not enough workers or materials right now. So, the ministry has asked its different departments to help connect people with companies. They hope to help residents find workers to fix their homes.

The Ministry of Transportation posted on Facebook today that Vice Minister Chen Yen-po held a meeting yesterday. He brought together different departments to check on the repair work in the damaged areas of Tainan and plan for future work. So far, they have visited over 100 damaged homes. They are also looking at available workers and resources to fully help people rebuild their homes quickly.

The Ministry of Transportation explained that because there are not enough workers and materials, people cannot find repair teams. They also lack workers and tools. The government has brought together different ministries and given each one an area to help. The Ministry of Transportation is responsible for Xinying District, Yanshui District, Baihe District, and Houbi District in Tainan City.

The Ministry of Transportation said that people who want to fix their homes should first register with their district chief. Then, the Ministry of Transportation will help connect different companies. Residents can then talk to these companies. After they agree, they will sign a simple contract. The Ministry of Transportation's main job is to connect people with companies; they do not pay for the repairs.

To make the repair work faster after the disaster, the Ministry of Transportation also set some rules for action. Besides connecting companies, they will first help fix old one-story houses and homes where the roof has come off, making them impossible to live in. This is for immediate help. Also, old materials like asbestos tiles will be collected in one place. Then, the district office will help remove them.

Shigeru Ishiba Sends Condolences for Floods in Central and Southern Taiwan; Lin Chia-lung Thanks and Hopes for Cooperation to Face Challenges

Recently, heavy rains caused damage in Taiwan. Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba wrote a message to show his concern for Taiwan. Today, Taiwan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA) said that Foreign Minister Lin Chia-lung thanked Japan again for its care. He also hopes that Taiwan and Japan can work together to solve problems. Both MOFA and Taiwan's office in Japan have already thanked the Japanese government.

Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba wrote a message to express his sympathy to Taiwan. He said that he feels sad for those who died and their families. He also sends his true sympathy to those affected by the disaster. He hopes that daily life will return to normal soon and said, "Go, Taiwan!" The Japan-Taiwan Exchange Association shared his message on Facebook in the afternoon.

Because of this, MOFA released a press statement in the afternoon. It said that MOFA and Taiwan's office in Japan immediately thanked the Japanese government.

MOFA said that Prime Minister Ishiba also sent a message on July 12th. He showed his concern for Taiwan after Typhoon Danas. This shows the deep and true friendship between Taiwan and Japan. The Taiwanese government and people are still working hard to help with rescue and recovery. They are helping people rebuild their homes and return to normal life soon.

MOFA added that Foreign Minister Lin Chia-lung once again truly thanked the Japanese government for its warm care and real help. He also hopes that Taiwan and Japan will continue to work more closely in different areas in the future. They want to build a stronger, friendlier, and more resilient partnership to face all kinds of challenges together.
