Taiwan Stocks Reach New Highs; TSMC Closes at Record NT$1400

Taiwanese stocks went up today. The main stock index, called the Taiex, rose by 382.67 points. It closed at 26761.06 points, which is a new record high. This happened because TSMC, a very important company, performed strongly. Also, companies in artificial intelligence (AI), defense, and memory chip sectors all gained. The total value of shares traded was NT$463.09 billion. This is the sixth week in a row that Taiwan's stock market has gone up a lot, with a total increase of 1180.74 points this week.

The index for electronic stocks closed at 1583.06 points today, up 2.08%. The semiconductor index closed at 815.53 points, up 2.38%. Both of these are also new record highs.

TSMC, a leading company, saw a lot of people buying its shares before its official meeting with investors. Today, its stock price reached a new record of NT$1400. This was an increase of NT$35, or 2.56%.

True Yoga Fitness Closes Due to Bankruptcy; Lawmakers Call for Investigation into Possible Illegal Acts

True Yoga, a fitness company, said on September 30 that it would close on October 1. It will now go through the legal process of closing down and bankruptcy. Several lawmakers and local council members held a press conference today. They said that the legal person in charge of True Yoga left Taiwan in January 2020 and has not returned. They asked if the person ran away without caring about customers. They also asked the government to quickly check if illegal things happened.

Members of Parliament Fan Yun and Wang Hui, along with city council members Ho Meng-hua, Miao Po-ya, Chang Wei-chien, and Huang Chiung-hui, held a press conference today in the Parliament building. The press conference was titled, "True Yoga suddenly closed! Who will protect the rights of tens of thousands of victims?"

True Yoga Fitness, which has many branches across Taiwan, suddenly announced it was closing. Tens of thousands of members and trainers are affected. A group for victims to help each other quickly grew to over 3,500 people.

Fan Yun said that True Yoga was the biggest chain of yoga centers in Taiwan. Now it has suddenly decided to close. It is hard for members to get back their prepaid yearly fees and class money. Also, many trainers and employees were fired, and they are owed wages. Many companies that worked with True Yoga are also owed money. This situation reminds people of the bad closing of Alexander fitness centers. This shows that it is not just one problem with customers. It is a bigger problem with how the fitness industry is watched and how consumers are protected. The government should help immediately. It should make sure that money is returned as promised. It should also plan how to give legal help to victims and deal with problems between workers and the company. This will protect the rights of tens of thousands of victims.

Ho Meng-hua said that by law, there is only NT$210 million in a special trust account. However, customers may be owed more than NT$420 million. Other companies that are owed money can only get 50% of what they are owed. The True Yoga management team is not meeting with anyone. Even the email address for refunds does not work. The government should check all rules for "prepaid transactions" to protect customers. This will stop customers from being victims again and again.

Huang Chiung-hui also said that the consumer protection officer in Taoyuan City should take action. She also asked the Labor Bureau to handle problems between True Yoga and its workers. This will protect the basic rights of the workers. A company that was owed money said that True Yoga refused to pay more than NT$2 million for construction work. True Yoga reportedly said, "Go ahead and sue us, many companies are already suing us anyway."

Miao Po-ya said that there are over 40,000 members. The guarantee account only has over NT$200 million left. This means each member might only get back about NT$5,000. Many customers are worried about this. The Taipei City Government should act more quickly. If they wait for consumer groups to file a group lawsuit, True Yoga could sell off its assets. So, it is important to save any assets that can be turned into money first. This is most important for the members.

Liang Ming-chen, a consumer protection officer from the Executive Yuan's Consumer Protection Department, said that his department will quickly contact the Financial Supervisory Commission and banks about credit card dispute claims and money in the trust account. Right now, they can focus on credit card refunds. By current rules, if a purchase was made within 540 days, and the company stopped services within 120 days, people can ask their banks for a refund. The Consumer Protection Department will hold a meeting next week with relevant government offices to discuss this.

Liu Tzu-chun, Deputy Director of the Department of Sports Industry, said that if a group lawsuit is filed, the Sports Department will help. They will check for any problems in the current rules for fitness companies and make them better.

Chin Shih-ping, section chief of the Labor Relations Department at the Ministry of Labor, said that about 100 workers have already asked for help. He urged other employees who have not yet asked the Labor Bureau for help to do so quickly. He also hopes True Yoga will quickly pay the wages it owes. If it does not, local governments will report it to the Ministry of Labor, and the law will be followed.

Changhua Launches Cultural Blue Belt 2.0: Five Walking Tours to Explore Local Culture

The Cultural Affairs Bureau of Changhua County has started "Cultural Blue Belt 2.0" walking tours. There are 5 different tours. They will take people to coastal areas, old towns, and flower fields. These tours will help people experience Changhua's culture and the special charm of its cultural centers.

The Cultural Affairs Bureau said in a press release today that these tours will connect different cultural centers in Changhua County. They want to promote museums and local cultural places. The "Cultural Blue Belt 2.0" walking tours will take place on October 12, October 26, November 1, and November 2. There will be 5 tours over these 4 days.

The Cultural Affairs Bureau explained that the "Coastal Culture Route" will take people to the intertidal zone. There, they can try opening oysters and making crafts from sea waste. They will learn about the culture and nature of Wanggong fishing village. Another route is the "Twin Towns Old Art Route," which focuses on photography. People can use cameras to capture the look of the old town of Lugang.

The "Farm Fun Slow Tour Route" will visit the Erlin sugarcane farmer incident site and a Japanese-style hall. People will tour a sugar factory and try making dough crafts, experiencing old rural life. The "Bagua Mountain Scenery Route" goes from the Indigenous People's Lifestyle Museum to the Cut-Skin Love Memorial Hall, and then to the old home of Lai He. This route lets people feel the history of culture and medicine together.

Additionally, the "Colorful Flower Fields Route" will guide people through flower fields and the Shetou area. Through cultural tours and DIY activities, and a visit to Luocuo Catholic Church, this route connects local industries with memories of life. The Cultural Affairs Bureau said that online registration is open now. Each tour has space for 25 to 30 people. More details can be found on the Changhua County Cultural Affairs Bureau's Facebook page.
