Taipei Court Checks Recordings of Former Official Peng Zhen-sheng in Jinghua City Case

The Taipei District Court is hearing the Jinghua City case. Today, the court looked at the recordings and videos of former Taipei Deputy Mayor Peng Zhen-sheng's questioning during the investigation. The court called former Taipei Mayor Ko Wen-je, who is in custody, and Peng Zhen-sheng to be present. Shen Ching-ching and Huang Jing-mao, who are also involved in the case, also asked to be there.

The Taipei District Prosecutor's Office finished investigating the Jinghua City case and Ko Wen-je's political donation case at the end of last December. They accused former Taipei Mayor Ko Wen-je, Wei-Ching Group Chairman Shen Ching-ching, and 9 other people of crimes like taking bribes, making illegal profits, taking public money, and breaking trust. The prosecutors asked for a total prison sentence of 28 years and 6 months for Ko Wen-je.

In April this year, Ko Wen-je's lawyer said that Peng Zhen-sheng and others might have been questioned unfairly by the prosecutors. The lawyer claimed that the records of the questioning were not consistent and could not be used as evidence. Especially, the lawyer pointed out that during Peng Zhen-sheng's questioning, the prosecutor said, "Are you willing to be Yu Wen now?" This clearly showed that the prosecutor wanted Peng Zhen-sheng to accuse Ko Wen-je, which is a type of unfair questioning.

The Taipei District Court had planned to check Peng Zhen-sheng's interrogation recordings and videos on July 1st and call him to testify. However, Peng Zhen-sheng asked for time off because his wife passed away, so his testimony was moved to July 26th. But on July 26th, Peng Zhen-sheng was still sad, so his questioning was moved again to September 2nd. If it cannot be finished in one day, the court will continue on September 4th. Today, the court is checking Peng Zhen-sheng's interrogation recordings and videos.

Nvidia's Stock Drops After Hours Due to Worries About China Business; Experts Say It Could Affect Taiwan's Stock Market

On August 27th, US stocks closed higher. The S&P 500 index reached a new high of 6481.4 points. TSMC's American Depositary Receipts (ADR) went up by 0.24%. However, experts said that Nvidia's stock dropped by more than 3% after the market closed. This happened because investors are worried that Nvidia's business in China might slow down. TSMC's ADR also fell by over 1.5% after hours. This could be bad for Taiwan's stock market.

All the main US stock indexes went up on August 27th. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose by 147.16 points, which is a 0.32% increase. The S&P 500 index went up by 15.46 points, or 0.24%, and closed at a new high of 6481.4 points. The Nasdaq index increased by 45.87 points, or 0.21%, and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index rose by 16.69 points, or 0.29%.

In other business news from Taiwan and abroad, Nvidia, a top company for AI chips, shared its financial report after the market closed. Its earnings and future predictions were better than what experts expected. In the second quarter, its income reached a new high of 46.7 billion US dollars. For the third quarter, the company expects its income to be around 54 billion US dollars, plus or minus 2%. This is more than the 53.14 billion US dollars that experts had predicted.

Nvidia did not ship its H20 chips to China in the second quarter. The company also said that its predictions for the third quarter do not include any planned shipments of H20 chips to China. Investors are worried that Nvidia's business in China might stop growing, which caused its stock price to fall by more than 3% after the market closed.

Experts said that TSMC's American Depositary Receipts (ADR), which is the main maker of Nvidia's chips, dropped by more than 1.5% after the market closed. Other AI-related stocks like AMD and Super Micro also fell after hours. This could have a negative effect on Taiwan's stock market.
