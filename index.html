European Stocks Show Mixed Results Before Nvidia's Financial Report

Investors are waiting for an important financial report from Nvidia, a very big company that makes chips for artificial intelligence (AI). Because of this, the main stock markets in Europe had mixed results today, with some going up and some going down. For many months, technology stocks have been growing strongly, mostly because Nvidia has shown strong growth.

The FTSE 100 index in London went down by 10.30 points, or 0.11%, closing at 9255.50 points.

The DAX index in Frankfurt went down by 106.66 points, or 0.44%, closing at 24046.21 points.

The CAC 40 index in Paris, however, went up by 34.12 points, or 0.44%, closing at 7743.93 points.

Shooting at Catholic Primary School in Minnesota, USA: Officials Report 3 Dead, 20 Hurt

Officials from the US Department of Justice said that a shooting happened today at a Catholic school in the south of Minneapolis, Minnesota. Three people died, and 20 people were hurt.

Reuters news agency reported that the official, who did not want to be named, also said that the shooter was among those who died.

The school where the shooting happened is called Annunciation Catholic School. It had only opened two days before. This private school has about 395 students.

Police in Richfield, a nearby town, said that a man in all black clothes and carrying a rifle was reportedly seen at the school. Local news said that the children were having morning church service when the shooting happened.

Pictures from local TV showed parents walking with their heads down, taking students away from the school. The students wore green polo shirts, blue shorts, and skirts.

US President Donald Trump wrote on social media that he had received information about the shooting. He also said that the FBI was at the school. He asked people to "Please pray with me for everyone involved in this!"

Yang Kun-pi and Liu Wan-yu Win Bronze in Mixed Team Trap Shooting at Asian Championships

At the Asian Shooting Championships in Kazakhstan, Taiwanese athletes Yang Kun-pi and Liu Wan-yu won a bronze medal today. They played in the mixed team trap shooting bronze medal match and beat Qatar 43 to 37. This is the first medal for Taiwan's shooting team at these championships.

Yang Kun-pi, a skilled shooter who has been to the Olympics three times for Taiwan, competed today with his partner Liu Wan-yu in the mixed team trap shooting event at the Asian Championships. They both did well in the three rounds of qualification. Yang Kun-pi shot 24, 25, and 24 points, missing only two clay targets. He scored the highest in the whole event with 73 points. Liu Wan-yu also got better as the competition went on, scoring 20, 21, and 24 points. Together, they scored 138 points, which put them in 4th place and allowed them to move to the next stage.

They missed the chance to compete for the gold medal by just 2 points. However, in the bronze medal match against Qatar, Yang Kun-pi and Liu Wan-yu showed great skill. In the third round, they both hit all 10 targets perfectly. This gave them a 6-point lead and set them up for the win. Their opponents could not catch up, and Yang Kun-pi and Liu Wan-yu won the match 43 to 37. This win also brought the first medal for Taiwan's shooting team at this Asian Championship.

It is important to note that Yang Kun-pi and Liu Wan-yu have been competing together in international events for many years. After winning a silver medal together at the 2019 Universiade in Naples, they later won other mixed team events, like the Shooting World Cup and the Asian Clay Target Cup. Now, they have won a bronze medal together at the Asian Shooting Championships. As they continue to gain more international experience, they have a good chance to win medals at the 2026 Asian Games in Nagoya.
