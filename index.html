Taiwan Stock Market Rises by 52.80 Points

The Taipei stock market closed today with a gain of 52.80 points. The market closed at 24547.38 points, which is a 0.22% increase. The total amount of money traded was 433.558 billion New Taiwan dollars.

The main index started at 24610.85 points. During the day, it reached a high of 24729.96 points and a low of 24547.38 points. The index without financial stocks went up by 65.47 points, reaching 21529.04 points.

Here's how different types of stocks performed: Clay and kiln stocks went down by 0.91%. Food stocks went up by 0.79%. Plastic and chemical stocks went up by 0.60%. Textile stocks went up by 0.46%. Electrical and mechanical stocks went up by 0.32%. Paper stocks went up by 0.12%. Construction stocks went down by 2.19%. Financial stocks went down by 0.57%.

There were 20,493,081 shares that people wanted to buy, and 11,413,186 shares that people wanted to sell. The number of shares actually traded was 7,701,863.

At closing, 505 companies saw their stock prices go up, 441 companies saw them go down, and 123 companies stayed the same.

The top 5 most traded stocks were Winbond, Nanya Technology, CSBC Corporation, KYEC, and Yuanta/P-shares Taiwan Dividend Plus ETF.

The top 5 stocks that increased the most were Chunghwa Telecom Cybersecurity, Apex, Accton Technology, Weltrend Semiconductor, and T-Cat Express.

The top 5 stocks that decreased the most were Exar Technology, Etron Technology, Altum RF, Hi-Life, and Sinpaba.

Green Party Criticizes Taichung Mayor for "Biggest Landfill in Taiwan," City Says Landfill Not Full

News reports recently said that the Dali landfill is full, causing problems for local people. Today, Democratic Progressive Party (DPP) spokesperson Cho Kuan-ting stated that Taichung Mayor Lu Shiow-yen has not responded, and her 8 years in office have led to the biggest "trash mountain" in Taiwan. The Taichung Environmental Protection Bureau (EPB) replied that the landfill follows environmental rules and is not full. They said that once the incinerators are fixed after their yearly check, the trash can be moved back and burned.

DPP spokespersons Wu Cheng and Cho Kuan-ting held a press conference this morning. Cho Kuan-ting said that on September 1st, the media reported that the Dali landfill was full. Since then, the DPP Taichung City Council has held more press conferences, but Mayor Lu has not said anything for 7 days.

Cho Kuan-ting explained that before former Taichung Mayor Lin Chia-lung left office, the Dali landfill had only 7,000 tons of trash. In 2019, when Mayor Lu started her first year, this went up to 37,000 tons. Now, it has reached 340,000 tons, which is 40% of all the open-air trash in Taiwan's 22 cities and counties.

Cho Kuan-ting pointed out that back in 2018, during Mayor Lin Chia-lung's time, 1 billion New Taiwan dollars were set aside to improve the Wenshan incinerator. It was planned to be finished by 2021. If Mayor Lu had continued this plan, the trash problem could have been solved easily. However, Mayor Lu stopped the previous plan and said she wanted to use a different method (BOT). This made the project cost go up to 8.5 billion New Taiwan dollars, leaving the biggest "trash mountain" in Taiwan after 8 years.

Wu Cheng added that audit reports have warned for years that Taichung's incinerators are not able to handle enough trash, and the landfills are also not big enough. All three incinerators in Taichung are old and need to be updated. But because Mayor Lu kept changing her mind between two different plans (ROT and BOT), the Wenshan incinerator project was delayed until this year to find companies to work on it. It is now expected to be finished in 2032.

In response, the Taichung EPB said that the Dali landfill follows environmental rules and is not full. They use it as a place to temporarily store trash when the incinerators have their yearly check. After the incinerators are fixed, the trash from the landfill can be moved back and burned, meaning "trash goes in and then goes out."

The EPB also stated that they started moving trash from the Dali landfill back to the incinerators in 2023. Even the Minister of Environment, Peng Chi-ming, visited and said that the trash was being handled well. It was not listed in the national meeting about exposed trash.

The EPB said that to quickly get rid of household trash, they are replacing old incinerators with new ones to burn more trash. They also temporarily take trash to the landfill. To keep the environment clean around the landfill, they cover the trash with recycled materials from burning or with nets to stop smells and prevent trash from blowing away.

Three-Story House Being Taken Down in Luzhou, New Taipei, Collapses, No One Hurt

In Luzhou District, New Taipei City, a three-story house on Lane 150, Guanghua Road, was being taken down. But suddenly, the floor on the third story fell down. It also broke the scaffolding. Luckily, no one was hurt or trapped when this happened. The Public Works Department has been told to send people to check what caused the collapse.

The Luzhou Police Station in New Taipei said that today around 10 AM, a three-story house being taken down suddenly had its third-floor collapse. Dirt and dust quickly filled the air, and the scaffolding and electric wires fell down.

The police said that after they heard about it, they sent officers to block off the area to keep everyone safe. After checking with the construction workers, they confirmed that no one was trapped or hurt. Zhong Yao-lei, the head of the Luzhou District Office, also came to see what happened. He told the Public Works Department to send people to the site to find out why the collapse happened and what to do next.
