Trump May Visit Japan in Late October; US Says He Really Wants To

Today, the Nikkei newspaper reported that officials from Japan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs said that the US and Japanese governments are planning for US President Trump to visit Japan at the end of October. This visit might happen before the APEC summit in South Korea.

South Korea will hold the APEC summit in Gyeongju from October 31st to November 1st.

If Trump visits Japan, he might meet the person who will become Japan's new Prime Minister. The current leader of Japan's ruling party, Shigeru Ishiba, has said he will step down. After the party's election on October 4th, Japan's parliament will choose the new Prime Minister.

In February this year, when Shigeru Ishiba visited the US, he invited Donald Trump to visit Japan. If this trip happens, it will be Trump's first visit to Japan since his second term started.

On October 24th, Mignon Houston, a deputy spokesperson for the US Department of State, told Nikkei that "Trump really wants to visit Japan, and this shows the long-standing friendship between the US and Japan."

She also said, "This also shows that our relationship and plans with Japan in the Indo-Pacific region are very important."

When asked if Trump would visit Japan before the APEC summit, Houston only said, "We don't guess... whether he visits or not, Japan is important for safety, stability, and growth in the Indo-Pacific region."

Trump might have a meeting with Chinese President Xi Jinping in South Korea during the APEC event. This would be his first meeting with Xi since his second term began. The Nikkei newspaper said that if Trump visits Japan before the APEC summit, Japan could talk about its China policy with the US first.

Also, Malaysian Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim said in late July that Trump will go to the ASEAN leaders' meeting in Kuala Lumpur in late October. Malaysia is currently the leader of ASEAN.

Taiwan Stock Market Drops 172.88 Points

The Taipei stock market closed today with a fall of 172.88 points. The final score was 26023.85 points, a drop of 0.66%. The total money traded was 488.827 billion New Taiwan dollars.

The main index started at 26067.97 points. During the day, it went as high as 26247.85 points and as low as 26023.85 points. The index without financial stocks fell by 151.89 points, closing at 22929.57 points.

Here's how different types of stocks changed: Cement and Ceramics stocks rose by 2.35%, Food stocks fell by 0.30%, Plastics and Chemicals stocks rose by 1.11%, Textile stocks rose by 0.84%, Electrical and Machinery stocks fell by 0.87%, Paper stocks rose by 1.43%, Construction stocks fell by 0.52%, and Financial stocks fell by 0.68%.

There were 21,995,454 shares people wanted to buy, and 12,961,850 shares people wanted to sell. The total number of shares traded was 8,765,912.

At the end of trading, 465 companies saw their stock prices go up, 458 companies saw their prices go down, and 145 companies stayed the same.

The top 5 most traded stocks were Winbond, Innolux, Compal, AUO, and Taiwan Glass.

The top 5 stocks that rose the most were Chia Ta, Medigen, First Copper, Hwa Rong, and Sinogiant.

The top 5 stocks that fell the most were Yubang, HannStar Board, BizLink-KY, YMTC, and Walsin Technology.

TSMC Shares Fall by NT$20; Taiwan Stock Market Drops 172 Points to Lowest Close at 26023

US stock markets fell for the second day in a row after Jerome Powell, the head of the US central bank (Fed), warned that US stock prices might be too high. Today, TSMC, a major company, saw its shares drop by NT$20. The Taiwan stock market also went down, losing 172.88 points. It closed at its lowest point of the day, 26023.85 points, just above the 26000-point level. The total money traded was 488.827 billion New Taiwan dollars.

The prices of important stocks went up and down. TSMC's shares dropped by NT$20, which is 1.49%, and closed at NT$1320. Hon Hai's shares went up by NT$7, or 3.14%, closing at NT$230, because foreign investors have been buying its shares. Delta Electronics' shares moved up and down at a high price, then fell by NT$7, or 0.79%, closing at NT$884.
