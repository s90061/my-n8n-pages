Moscow, August 24 (Central News Agency) â€“ Russia and Ukraine exchanged 146 prisoners of war today. This happened with the help of the United Arab Emirates (UAE). Both the Russian Ministry of Defense and Ukrainian President Zelenskyy confirmed this.

Canada's Prime Minister: Sending Peacekeepers to Ukraine is Possible

Kyiv, August 24 (Central News Agency) â€“ Today, Canadian Prime Minister Carney told Ukrainian President Zelenskyy that he supports Ukraine's request for strong security promises in any peace deal. Carney also said that Canada might send peacekeeping troops to Ukraine under such a plan.

Reuters reported that the war where Russia fully invaded Ukraine has been going on for three and a half years. US President Trump is leading efforts for peace. Ukraine is working with its European friends to create a plan that could give Kyiv security after the war. Trump has shown he is open to this idea.

Today, President Volodymyr Zelenskyy led a ceremony in central Kyiv to celebrate Independence Day. Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney, who is visiting Ukraine for the first time since becoming Prime Minister in March, and Trump's special envoy Keith Kellogg were both there.

At Sophia Square in Kyiv, Zelenskyy told his guests, "We are all working to make sure that when this war ends, Ukraine will have peace. This way, our children and grandchildren will not have to suffer from war or the threat of war."

Zelenskyy said he wants future security promises in any peace deal to be as close as possible to Article 5 of the North Atlantic Treaty. This article says that if one member country is attacked, it is like an attack on all member countries.

Prime Minister Carney agreed with Ukraine's request for possible international help.

At a joint press conference, Carney said, "Canada believes that it's not enough to only rely on the strength of Ukraine's army for security... It must be supported and made stronger."

The leaders of both countries also signed an agreement to work together to make drones. Carney said that next month, Ukraine will get more than 1 billion Canadian dollars (about 22 billion Taiwan dollars) in military help from a plan that was announced before.
