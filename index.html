Putin Attends China's Parade, Russia Launches Big Air Attack on Ukraine

Ukraine and Poland said today that Russia launched a large air attack on Ukraine late yesterday. This attack hurt at least four railway workers. It also made Poland quickly send up its fighter jets to protect itself.

Reuters reported that this air attack happened while Russian President Vladimir Putin was in Beijing. He was there for a military parade to remember the end of World War II. Chinese President Xi Jinping warned that the world has to choose between "peace and war."

Ukrainian officials and news reports said that alarms rang for many hours in many parts of the country. People heard explosions in nine regions, from Kyiv to Lviv and Volyn in the west.

Poland, which is Ukraine's neighbor to the west and a member of NATO, sent its own and allied fighter jets into the air to be safe.

The Polish Armed Forces Command said on social media platform X that "the Russian Federation has again attacked targets inside Ukraine."

Ukraine's state railway company said on Telegram that four railway workers in the Kirovohrad region were hurt in the Russian attack and went to the hospital. The company also warned that many train routes would be delayed because railway buildings were damaged.

Ukraine's emergency services said that the Russian attack on the Znamianka district in the Kirovohrad region hurt five people and damaged 28 homes.

Public transport in the western city of Khmelnytskyi was also "very disturbed" by the air attack. Local government said that there were fires and damage to homes and other buildings in the area.

Russia did not say anything about this attack right away. Since Russia fully invaded Ukraine in February 2022, both sides have repeatedly said they do not attack civilians.

Heavy Rain in Mountain Areas of 13 Counties May Continue Until Night, Yilan's Fanfan River Watch for Flooding

The weather office today afternoon sent out a heavy rain warning. Strong storms are happening in the afternoon. 13 counties and cities, including Greater Taipei, should prepare for local heavy rain. This rain is expected to continue until tonight. Also, for Fanfan River (Pengpeng Hot Spring) in Yilan County, a flood warning for mountain areas was given. People should be careful of the river rising quickly and leave the area fast.

The Central Weather Administration today afternoon sent out a heavy rain warning. Storm clouds are growing strong, which means there might be short, strong rain. Today, there is a chance of local heavy rain in Yilan and the mountain areas in the western half of Taiwan. People should watch out for lightning and strong winds. In mountain areas, be careful of landslides and falling rocks.

The areas with heavy rain are the mountain areas of Taipei City, New Taipei City, Taoyuan City, Hsinchu County, Miaoli County, Taichung City, Nantou County, Yunlin County, Chiayi County, Tainan City, Kaohsiung City, Pingtung County, and Yilan County.

In addition, the weather office gave a disaster warning for Fanfan River (Pengpeng Wild Hot Spring) in Yilan County. It reminded people that this area has heavy rain or might have heavy rain. People should watch for the river water rising quickly and leave the area as soon as possible. During this time, stay away from river areas. Do not go upstream, fish, or swim to stay safe.

Indigenous Peoples Cultural Foundation Supports "Taiwan Online News Self-Regulation Alliance" to Show Commitment to Self-Control

The Indigenous Peoples Cultural Foundation and 22 other news media from different areas have announced that they are starting the "Taiwan Online News Self-Regulation Alliance." This alliance will create professional rules, a clear system for self-control, a way for people to complain, and a "news trust badge." These steps will help people trust the media again.

The Indigenous Television (TITV) and Alian Indigenous Radio Station (Alian Radio), which are part of the Indigenous Peoples Cultural Foundation (IPCF), have been actively involved since the planning stage. They are now founding members of the alliance. This shows that indigenous media are committed to self-control, professionalism, and cultural responsibility.

On the day the alliance started, it also announced its self-regulation system. This brings new energy to Taiwan's media world. First, it will create a system for people to complain. This lets the public share their opinions directly. It helps media be more responsible and encourages people to get involved. Second, it will form a self-regulation and advisory committee. This committee will include media members, outside experts, and public groups. It will help deal with disagreements and make rules. Lastly, it will introduce a "news self-regulation trust badge" to help make online news more trustworthy.

Digital Development Minister Lin Yi-ching said in a press release, "A democratic country needs freedom of speech. But a democratic country also cannot let wrong or fake information spread widely, as this can harm the safety of the whole country."

The Indigenous Peoples Cultural Foundation also believes that having indigenous media (TITV and Alian Radio) join is very important. For a long time, indigenous issues were often ignored or reported incorrectly. Only indigenous-led media can speak for these issues well, keeping cultural context and truth together. By joining the self-regulation alliance, they not only show their commitment to good media rules but also act as cultural protectors. They bring different views and deep cultural meanings to Taiwan's media world.

The Indigenous Peoples Cultural Foundation will continue to use culture as its base. Through media power, it will lead the voices of indigenous groups to a wider public stage. It will also work with all its partners to protect truth, seek fairness, and bring more trust and hope to democratic society.
