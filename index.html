TSG Hawks Team Signs New Pitcher From Major League Baseball, Named Namasia

The TSG Hawks baseball team in Taiwan announced today that they have signed a new foreign pitcher. His name is Dinelson Lamet, but he will be known as Namasia. He has played in Major League Baseball (MLB) more than 100 times. His jersey number will be 45. He played 121 games in MLB. Last year, he was on the Dodgers team with Shohei Ohtani.

The TSG Hawks team said today that they signed Namasia to make their pitching stronger. Namasia is 33 years old. He has played for five different MLB teams: the Padres, Rockies, Red Sox, Dodgers, and Royals. Last year, he was a teammate of the famous Japanese player Shohei Ohtani on the Dodgers.

Namasia has played 121 games in his MLB career. He started 60 of those games. He won 17 games and lost 24. He pitched for 367.2 innings and got 474 strikeouts. His earned run average (ERA) is 4.63.

Namasia was a starting pitcher in 2017, 2019, and 2020. In 2020, he started 12 games in MLB and pitched for 69 innings. His ERA was very good at 2.09. He only allowed 39 hits and got 93 strikeouts. Last year, Namasia still played in MLB. This year, he is playing in the Mexican League. He has started 17 games there. He has won 7 games and lost 5. His ERA is 5.01, and he has 88 strikeouts in 88 innings.

The second part of Taiwan's baseball season started on July 4th. The TSG Hawks team currently has 7 wins, 6 losses, and 2 ties in this part of the season. Their win rate is 53.8%, and they are currently in 2nd place.

Ministry of Transportation Connects Companies to Help People Repair Their Homes

Typhoon Danas and strong winds from the southwest affected the southern part of Taiwan. Some people's homes were badly damaged. The Ministry of Transportation said today that there are not enough workers or building materials right now. So, they have asked different groups to help connect people with companies. They hope this will help residents find workers and fix their homes.

The Ministry of Transportation posted on Facebook today that Vice Minister Chen Yen-po had a meeting yesterday. He brought together different departments to check on the repair work in the Tainan disaster area and plan for the future. They have already visited more than 100 damaged homes. They are also still counting how many workers and resources they have. They want to do their best to help people rebuild their homes quickly.

The Ministry of Transportation explained that people cannot find workers to fix their homes because there are not enough workers, materials, or machines. The government has asked different ministries to help. Each ministry has been given an area to care for. The Ministry of Transportation is in charge of four areas in Tainan City: Xinying, Yanshui, Baihe, and Houbi.

The Ministry of Transportation said that if you need to fix your home, you can first tell your local district chief. Then, the Ministry of Transportation will help you find a company. After that, you will talk with the company. Once you agree, you will sign a simple paper. The Ministry of Transportation's job is to connect people with companies, but they do not pay for the repairs.

To make the rebuilding faster after the storm, the Ministry of Transportation also set some rules. Besides finding companies, they will first help fix old single-story houses and homes where the roof is gone, so people cannot live there. This is for quick help. Old materials like asbestos will be put in one place. The local office will then help take them away.

Japanese Prime Minister Ishiba Sends Sympathy for Floods in Central and Southern Taiwan; Lin Chia-lung Thanks Him and Hopes for Future Cooperation

Heavy rains recently caused damage in Taiwan. Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba sent a message to Taiwan to show his sympathy. Taiwan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA) said today that Minister Lin Chia-lung thanked Japan for its kindness. He also said he hopes Taiwan and Japan can work together to solve problems. Both MOFA and Taiwan's office in Japan have already sent their thanks to the Japanese government.

Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba sent a message to Taiwan. He said he was very sorry for those who died and their families. He also felt great sympathy for everyone who was affected by the disaster. He wished for their daily lives to return to normal soon and said, "Go Taiwan!" The Japan-Taiwan Exchange Association shared his message on Facebook in the afternoon.

In response, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs put out a statement in the afternoon. It said that both the Ministry and Taiwan's office in Japan had already thanked the Japanese government right away.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs said that Prime Minister Ishiba also sent a message on July 12. That message was about the damage from Typhoon Danas in Taiwan. This shows the strong and real friendship between Taiwan and Japan. The government of Taiwan and many groups in society are still working very hard to help after the disaster. They want to help people rebuild their homes and get back to their normal lives quickly.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs said that Minister Lin Chia-lung once again thanked the Japanese government. He thanked them for their kind care and real help. He also hopes that Taiwan and Japan will continue to work more closely together in the future. He wants them to build a stronger and friendlier relationship. This way, they can face any challenges together.
