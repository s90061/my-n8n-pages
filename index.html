Wei Hong-liang Gets First Win in Minor League after Pitching 1.2 Innings Without Giving Up Runs

Wei Hong-liang, who was the first pick in this year's baseball draft for the TSG Hawks team, pitched in a minor league game today. He pitched for 1.2 innings and did not let the other team score any runs. His fastest throw was 149 kilometers per hour. The Hawks team won the game 3 to 2 against the CTBC Brothers. This was Wei Hong-liang's first win in the minor league.

Wei Hong-liang, who is 19 years old, started playing in the minor league at the end of August. In his first game on August 28th, he pitched for 1 inning. He did not let anyone hit the ball, and he got two strikeouts without the other team scoring. He played in another minor league game today.

Today, the Hawks' minor league team played against the Brothers' minor league team. After 5 innings, the score was tied at 2-2. In the bottom of the 6th inning, the Hawks team put Wei Hong-liang in to pitch. He walked the first batter, Xu Shao-yu. Then, he got two players out with a fly ball to center field and a ground ball to second base. After that, Hu Meng-zhi got a hit, and a player moved into scoring position. But Wei Hong-liang quickly got better and made Yang Xiang-he hit a fly ball to right field, so no runs were scored.

In the top of the 7th inning, after two players were out, the Hawks team scored more runs with hits from Huang Bing-yang, Lan Yin-lun, and Dong Bing-xuan. Wei Hong-liang continued pitching in the bottom of the 7th inning. He got one player out with a fly ball to center field, and then he struck out Liu Gui-yuan before leaving the game.

Today, Wei Hong-liang threw a total of 30 pitches in 1.2 innings. He allowed 1 hit, got 1 strikeout, and walked 1 batter. He did not give up any runs, and his fastest throw was 149 kilometers per hour. His team won, and Wei Hong-liang got the win.

Wei Hong-liang is on the Hawks team's list of 60 players for this season. The Hawks' pitching coach, Hisanori Yokota, said before that they would let Wei Hong-liang join the first team and travel with them. This showed that they hoped Wei Hong-liang could play in the first team this season.

First Bank: Still Has NT$10 Billion for Home Loans, Focusing on Homes for Personal Use

Recently, public banks' home loans have been getting attention. First Bank announced today that it mainly offers home loans for people who need them to live in. They expect home loan growth to be 5% to 6% this year. The total amount of new loans for the year should be about NT$40 billion. They still have NT$10 billion available for loans by the end of this year.

First Financial Holding Company held a meeting today to talk about its second-quarter results for 2025. They shared their financial numbers and how the company is doing for the first half of the year. Their profit after tax was NT$13.946 billion, which is 1.6% more than last year. This is the highest profit they have ever had for this period. Their earnings per share (EPS) were NT$0.97. First Bank, which is a part of First Financial, had a profit after tax of NT$13.807 billion, which is 7.9% more than last year. This also set a new record for that period. Other companies in the group made a total profit after tax of NT$763 million.

Cai Shu-hui, the Vice President of First Bank, said that the bank mainly gives loans for homes that people truly need to live in. They expect home loans to grow by 5% to 6% this year. The total new loans for the whole year are aimed to be around NT$40 billion. Each month, they give out about NT$7 billion to NT$8 billion in loans, and it takes about two months to process these loans. For group home loans, these mainly come from social housing and loans for renovating old or dangerous buildings. The total amount of money loaned for land and construction went up by NT$15.6 billion, but regular loans for land and construction went down by about NT$8 billion.

Looking at the economy for the whole year, Li Shu-ling, the Vice President of First Financial Holding Company, explained that different organizations believe economic growth will slow down in the second half of the year. It might fall below 2.5%, making the total growth for the year around 4.4%. Next year, because this year's numbers are high, economic growth will also slow down. This is because the market needs time to deal with issues about tariffs (taxes on imported goods). There might be a "gap period" of about half a year. It is clear that exports will be affected in the second half of this year.

Li Shu-ling said that if the tariffs are finally decided, companies should have ways to deal with them. This looks like it will lead to new investments abroad or changes in how assets are managed. This will still create opportunities for the financial industry. She added, "Next year, apart from the housing market which might be slower, the overall economy is not expected to be too bad."

Li Shu-ling said honestly that Taiwanese companies have managed well during recent economic changes. Some weaker companies might still be affected, but generally, she thinks there is no need to worry too much.

First Financial Holding Company said that due to higher US tariffs, changes in currency exchange rates, and Taiwanese companies moving their supply chains, local industries will face business challenges in the second half of the year. Also, the housing market is slowing down, and previous high numbers mean that bank lending growth is expected to slow. The company will actively help customers deal with these business challenges. At the same time, they will remain strong in their operations. They will take advantage of new business chances, such as the opening of the "Asia Asset Management Center" in Kaohsiung. They will also use their many locations overseas to support Taiwanese companies as they change their production plans, which will create international financial services.
