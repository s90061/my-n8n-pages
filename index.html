Belgium Expands Quiet Train Zones, France Creates Family and Business Areas

Paris, October 16 â€“ Taiwan High Speed Rail recently started "quiet carriages," which has caused a lot of discussion. In Belgium, a trial of "quiet zones" was successful, and they started to use them more widely last year. In France, travelers who want quiet can choose first-class or business seats. Parents with children can choose special family areas.

This summer, someone posted on the social media site Reddit about buying a first-class ticket on a French high-speed train (TGV). Next to them was a family with two small children. The person who posted said they knew that young children can be noisy, and it's not their fault. However, the person had not slept well the night before and wanted to rest during the trip. So, they suggested that the French national railway company (SNCF) should create quiet carriages on TGV trains.

There has been a lot of talk about this topic on social media. Some people wished for quiet carriage rules like those in Denmark or Italy. Some even suggested that children should not be allowed in first-class carriages.

The Italian national railway company (Trenitalia) divides business and standard carriages into "Quiet Atmosphere" (Silenzio) and "Happy Atmosphere" (Allegro). When buying tickets, passengers who choose "Quiet Atmosphere" can rest or work quietly. Those who choose "Happy Atmosphere" can talk freely and move around. This is good for friends and families traveling together.

The Belgian national railway company (SNCB) tried "quiet zones" on two busy routes for four months in 2023. In these areas, people could not talk, phones had to be silent, and if people listened to music, watched videos, or used social media, they had to wear headphones. Passengers who wanted to talk or make calls, or those worried their children might bother others, could choose other carriages.

In September last year, the Belgian national railway company shared results from a survey. It showed that 90% of travelers who used the quiet zones were happy. Because of this, they decided to use quiet zones more widely. They will slowly add quiet zones to their main intercity trains, marking them with stickers or pictures. There is no extra cost for these zones.

The Belgian national railway company said that in these areas, passengers should speak softly, keep their headphone volume low, and set their phones to silent.

The French national railway company also tells people to be polite: don't disturb others, keep phones silent, and make calls in the spaces between carriages. Since these are not strict rules, if someone is noisy, train staff or other passengers often ask them to be quieter, but this doesn't always work.

If passengers want to travel without being disturbed, they can choose first-class carriages, where there are fewer people. These tickets cost more, but the space is bigger, and there are even single seats. Passengers can also book seats in "Quiet Spaces" (Espace Calme) on some trains.

Also, some routes have "First Class Business Zones" (Espace Business PremiÃ¨re). Passengers with these tickets can use VIP lounges at stations. Once on the train, they get free drinks and e-newspapers. Most passengers in these areas are business people, so the environment is usually quiet and better for working, reading, or resting.

In regular TGV carriages, there are also "family spaces" (espace famille) that can be booked for families. In both first-class and regular carriages, there are also four-person seats facing each other that can be booked for families or small groups.

Regarding the topic of quiet carriages, some social media users pointed out that while children might cry during a trip, adults using their phones loudly to watch TikTok videos or talking loudly on the phone are also disturbing. The problem is about good manners and respect, not just about children.
