Northern Taiwan to See 38-Degree Heat; Tropical Depression May Become a Typhoon, But Will Not Affect Taiwan

Weather expert Wu Der-rong said that today and tomorrow, it will be very hot. Temperatures in northern Taiwan could reach 38 degrees Celsius. A tropical depression is slowly getting stronger in the South China Sea. Before August 30, it has a 60% chance of becoming a typhoon. However, it will not affect Taiwan. Next week, there will be other weather systems in the far eastern sea of Taiwan.

What is a High Temperature Warning?

The Weather Bureau's "High Temperature Information" defines "high temperature" as when the highest temperature on the ground goes above 36 degrees Celsius. Based on how high the temperature is and how long it lasts, there are three warning levels: yellow, orange, and red.

Wu Der-rong, an associate professor at National Central University's Department of Atmospheric Sciences, wrote in his column "Xie Tian Ji Jiao Shi" for the Meteorological Applications Promotion Foundation. He said that the newest European model shows that today and tomorrow, most areas will be sunny with some clouds. It will be very hot, with northern Taiwan reaching 38 degrees. Around noon, the UV index will be dangerous. People should protect themselves from the sun and avoid heatstroke. The Hengchun Peninsula, Hualien, and Taitung might have some short local rains. In the afternoon, mountain areas might have local showers or thunderstorms.

For temperatures, Wu Der-rong said that today, northern Taiwan will be 24 to 38 degrees, central Taiwan 23 to 37 degrees, southern Taiwan 24 to 35 degrees, and eastern Taiwan 21 to 35 degrees.

Wu Der-rong also said that the newest European model shows that from August 31 to September 2, there will be a bit more moisture from the south. Most areas will be cloudy with some sun. Temperatures will drop slightly, but northern Taiwan will still be warm. Eastern Taiwan and Pingtung might have some short local rains. In the afternoon, mountain areas might have local showers or thunderstorms, and sometimes these might spread to some flat areas. People should be careful of strong local thunderstorms.

Wu Der-rong stated that on September 3 and 4, most areas will be sunny with some clouds. It will be very hot. Around noon, the UV index will be dangerous. People should protect themselves from the sun and avoid heatstroke. The Hengchun Peninsula, Hualien, and Taitung might have some short local rains. In the afternoon, there will be local showers or thunderstorms, mostly in the mountains. People still need to be careful of strong local thunderstorms.

Wu Der-rong pointed out that the newest European ensemble model shows that a tropical depression is slowly developing in the South China Sea. The chance of it becoming a typhoon before August 30 has increased slightly to 60%. Although it is similar to a swordfish (in its shape on the map), its strength is much weaker. It will not affect Taiwan. He also mentioned that the newest model shows that next week, other tropical systems will be active in the far eastern sea of Taiwan. The Northwest Pacific is still a common area for typhoons.

New Taipei to Guard Against Extreme 38-Degree Heat; Areas South of Taoyuan Should Watch for Afternoon Thunderstorms

The Weather Agency said that today, it will be very hot everywhere. Temperatures will generally be between 31 and 36 degrees Celsius. Greater Taipei, Taoyuan, and areas near the mountains in central and southern Taiwan should prepare for temperatures above 36 degrees. Some parts of New Taipei City might even reach an extreme temperature of 38 degrees. On the windward side, Hualien, Taitung, and the Hengchun Peninsula might have occasional showers or thunderstorms. Areas south of Taoyuan should be aware of afternoon thunderstorms.

The Central Weather Agency stated that today, the wind will mostly come from the east. Hualien, Taitung, and the Hengchun Peninsula will have occasional short local showers or thunderstorms. The North Coast of Keelung and the northeastern areas will also have some scattered rain. Other areas will be mostly cloudy to sunny. In the afternoon, areas south of Taoyuan will have short local thunderstorms. When the air rises strongly, there might be heavy local rain.

Regarding temperatures, the highest temperatures in most areas will be 31 to 36 degrees. Greater Taipei, Taoyuan, and areas near the mountains in central and southern Taiwan have a chance of temperatures above 36 degrees. Some parts of New Taipei City might even have extreme temperatures around 38 degrees. Around noon, the UV radiation will be strong, easily reaching high or even dangerous levels.

For the outer islands, Penghu will be sunny with some clouds, 27 to 32 degrees. Kinmen will be sunny with some clouds, 27 to 33 degrees. Matsu will be sunny with some clouds, 27 to 31 degrees.

Also, some areas on the North Coast of Keelung, Taoyuan, and the Hengchun Peninsula might experience average winds of level 6 or higher, or gusts of level 8 or higher. People should be careful.

The Weather Agency said that Tropical Depression TD17 was located 1000 kilometers southwest of Eluanbi at 2 AM. It is moving northwest towards Vietnam and will not directly affect Taiwan.
