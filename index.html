Typhoon Yangliu: 19 Roads Closed as a Precaution, 3 Roads Blocked

The Highway Bureau announced that because of Typhoon Yangliu, 19 provincial roads are closed to traffic as a safety measure. Also, 3 provincial road sections are blocked due to damage. This information is correct as of 10 PM tonight.

The Central Weather Administration says that Typhoon Yangliu is currently near the coast southwest of Penghu. It is moving towards the northwest. The typhoon's strong winds and heavy rain are no longer affecting Hualien, Taitung, Miaoli, Taichung, and Nantou. However, areas south of Changhua, Penghu, and Kinmen are still at risk. Southern Chiayi and Penghu are still experiencing wind and rain. The typhoon is expected to get weaker, and its stormy area will get smaller.

As of 10 PM tonight, the Highway Bureau confirms that 19 provincial roads are still closed as a precaution. They will decide when to open them again based on the weather and road conditions.

These closed roads include:
In Chiayi County:
- Provincial Highway 3, from 295K to 336K (Yunshui to Dapau section) in Zhongpu Township.
- Provincial Highway 18, from 34K to 68.5K (Chukou to Fushan section) in Alishan Township.
- Provincial Highway 18, from 68.5K to 88K (Fushan to Alishan section) in Alishan Township.

In Taichung City:
- Provincial Highway 8, from 85K to 110K (Lishan to Dayuling section) in Heping District.

In Taitung County:
- Provincial Highway 9, from 397K+250 to 398K+600 (Jinlun Bridge) in Taimali Township.
- Provincial Highway 20, from 149K+110 to 199K+000 (Xiangyang to Chulai section) in Haiduan Township.
- Provisional Provincial Highway 105, from 0K to 44K (Meishan Pass to Xiangyang section) in Haiduan Township.
- Provincial Highway 23, from 7K to 16K+840 (Fengnan to Taitung County border section) in Fuli Township.
- Provincial Highway 23, from 16K+840 to 25K+100 (Beiyuan section) in Donghe Township.

In Tainan City:
- Provincial Highway 20, from 47K to 52K (Maopu to Zixia Mountain section) in Nanhua District.

In Kaohsiung City:
- Provincial Highway 20, from 58.3K to 72.5K (Jiaxian to Laonong section) in Jiaxian District.
- Provincial Highway 20, from 79K to 89.5K (Baolai Second Bridge to Taoyuan District Office section) in Taoyuan District.
- Provincial Highway 20, at 91.5K (Dongzhuang Temporary Bridge) in Taoyuan District.
- Provisional Provincial Highway 20, from 0K to 5K (Qinhe to Fuxing section) in Taoyuan District.
- Provincial Highway 27, from 11.5K to 23.1K (Zhongxing to Dajin section) in Liugui District.
- Provincial Highway 29, from 2.7K to 3K (Minsheng Bridge) in Namaxia District.
- Provincial Highway 29, from 4.3K to 4.8K (Sanminghu section) in Namaxia District.
- Provisional Provincial Highway 29, from 0K to 12K (Namaxia to Wulipu section) in Namaxia District.

In Pingtung County:
- Provincial Highway 24, from 27.4K to 37.3K (Dewen intersection to Shenshan section) in Sandimen Township.

There are 3 roads blocked by damage:
- Provincial Highway 8, at 94.5K (Rongxing section) in Renai Township, Nantou County. It is expected to reopen tomorrow, August 14th, at 7 AM.
- Provincial Highway 8, from 167.1K to 184.5K (Tianxiang to Taroko section) in Xiulin Township, Hualien County. It is expected to reopen tomorrow, August 14th, at 10 AM.
- Provincial Highway 21, from 120K to 144K (Tongfu to Tataka section) in Xinyi Township, Nantou County. It is expected to reopen tomorrow, August 14th, at 12 PM.

The Highway Bureau stated they will continue to release information about road closures and problems. They will also deal with any emergencies quickly and put out warnings on road signs, radio, TV, and news.

Taichung Man Arrested for Harassing Underage Girl on Bus

A man named Yan from Taichung was arrested today, August 13th, for harassing a young female passenger on a bus. The police caught him right away after receiving a call. The victim chose to press charges, so the police sent him to the Taichung District Prosecutor's Office. He is being investigated for breaking the "Sexual Harassment Prevention Act."

A Taichung City Councilor, Hsieh Chih-chung from the Democratic Progressive Party, wrote on Facebook today that a man in Fengyuan District is a repeat offender for sexual harassment. It is understood that he has been involved in 3 sexual harassment cases since 2024. This afternoon, the councilor received a call from a parent. They said the man had harassed someone on a bus again. After the councilor contacted the police, they successfully took the man to the police station.

The Fengyuan Police Department in Taichung City said today that their Wengzi Police Station received a report at 3 PM this afternoon. The report said a man was harassing a female passenger on a moving bus.

Police officers arrived and immediately arrested the 47-year-old Mr. Yan. He had behaved improperly towards a young female passenger. The victim decided to report the incident and press charges under the "Sexual Harassment Prevention Act" right there.

The police said they have followed all rules by reporting the sexual harassment case and informing child protection services. After questioning the man, the police sent the entire case to the Taiwan Taichung District Prosecutor's Office for investigation. This is because he violated the "Sexual Harassment Prevention Act."

The police are telling people that if they experience harassment while using public transport, they should be brave and speak up. They should immediately tell the driver, ask other passengers for help, or call the police at 110. The police will come and handle the situation right away.

Chunghwa Telecom to Compensate Customers for July 31st Mobile Outage

Chunghwa Telecom announced today, August 13th, that they will give money back to their mobile phone customers. This is because of a big problem on July 31st early in the morning. Their mobile network system had a software update that went wrong. This caused all mobile phone voice calls across the country to stop working.

Chunghwa Telecom sent out a message today. They explained that on July 31st, very early in the morning, their mobile network system had a software update. Something went wrong during this update. Because of this, people could not make or receive calls on their mobile phones. Even though mobile internet and other services still worked, the company is very sorry for the trouble this caused their customers.

Chunghwa Telecom stated that according to their mobile internet service contract, they will compensate each mobile phone customer. They will give back 8% of the August monthly fee for voice calls. This will be automatically taken off their September bill for calls made within Taiwan.

On July 31st, early in the morning, Chunghwa Telecom had a big problem. Mobile phones for users all over Taiwan stopped working. Chunghwa Telecom said at that time that at 3 AM, a software update for their mobile network system went wrong. This made it impossible to make or receive mobile phone calls everywhere. They started to fix the problem, and services slowly returned to normal after 6 AM that morning.
