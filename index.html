Brothers, Lions' 9/7 Game Postponed Due to Rain; Makeup Game on 10/5 in Intercontinental

The professional baseball game between CTBC Brothers and Uni-President 7-ELEVEn Lions, which was supposed to happen today, was postponed because of the rain. The league announced that the game will now be played on October 5th at Taichung Intercontinental Baseball Stadium.

The Brothers and Lions started a three-game series at the Taichung Intercontinental Baseball Stadium on the 5th. In the first game on the 5th, the Lions lost 1-2 after playing 10 extra innings. Yesterday, the Lions won 7-1, thanks to their new foreign pitcher Cameron Alldred, who pitched 7 innings without letting the other team score.

The game that was planned for today at 5:05 PM could not happen because of the rain. It has been moved to October 5th at 5:05 PM at the same stadium.

Currently, the Brothers are in first place in the overall season with 59 wins and 41 losses. The Lions are in second place with 57 wins and 44 losses, which is 2.5 games behind the Brothers. In the second half of the season, the Brothers are in first place with 25 wins and 15 losses, leading the Rakuten Monkeys by 3.5 games.

President Lai Visits Cimu Temple in Luzhu, Taoyuan: Let Policies Be Closer to Public Opinion

President Lai Ching-te, who is also the leader of the Democratic Progressive Party, visited Cimu Temple in Luzhu, Taoyuan today to burn incense. President Lai said that this visit is to listen to what different groups think and to understand problems. This will help make government policies closer to what people want. He said that if the central government can do something, they will do it without hesitation. However, he did not talk much about plans for elections.

President Lai has started a "Unity Tour" where he will visit all 22 cities and counties in Taiwan to talk to people. Today, he was with others, including the Secretary-General of the Democratic Progressive Party, Hsu Kuo-yung, Deputy Secretary-General Ho Po-wen, Deputy Secretary-General of the Presidential Office Ho Chih-wei, and local elected officials. In the morning, he went to Hsinchu City. In the afternoon, he visited Cimu Temple in Luzhu District, Taoyuan City. After visiting the temple, President Lai talked to reporters.

President Lai explained that this listening tour is to hear the ideas of local party members, government workers, and different parts of society. This will help make future government policies closer to what people want and make them work better. It will also allow party members and local people to talk about problems with development. He said that if the central government can help, they will. The central government and local governments will work together to help cities and the country improve in the future. He did not specifically talk about election plans.

After that, President Lai took a car to the South Garden Resort Hotel to have a meeting with party members and government officials.

Israeli Foreign Minister: War Could End if Hamas Releases Hostages and Disarms

Israel's Foreign Minister, Gideon Saar, said today in Jerusalem that the war in Gaza could end. He was speaking at a press conference with Denmark's Foreign Minister. He said that if Hamas, a Palestinian armed group, lets go of the people they are holding and gives up their weapons, the war could finish.

Reuters news agency reported that yesterday, Hamas repeated its usual position. They said they would release all the hostages if Israel agrees to stop the war and takes its soldiers out of Gaza City.

Yesterday, Israel used air attacks to destroy a tall building in Gaza City. Israel said that Hamas was using this building to "watch" Israeli soldiers. Israel also said they took "steps to reduce harm to ordinary people."

Hamas strongly disagreed with this attack. They said that they do not use homes or other buildings for military reasons.
