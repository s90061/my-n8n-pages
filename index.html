UK's 6th Astute-Class Nuclear Submarine Finishes Important Diving Test

The UK's sixth Astute-class nuclear submarine has recently completed an important diving test. This brings it closer to officially joining the navy.

The UK plans to build seven Astute-class nuclear attack submarines. Five are already in service. The sixth and seventh submarines are still being tested and built. They are named HMS Agamemnon and HMS Achilles, after old Greek stories.

The UK Ministry of Defence announced today that "HMS Agamemnon" successfully finished its first diving test recently. The test lasted two days. It happened at the BAE Systems shipyard in Barrow-in-Furness, in northwest England. BAE Systems is a very big company that makes military equipment.

The test checked many systems and features on the submarine. For example, it made sure the ballast tanks worked well to help the submarine float up. Navy engineers also measured the submarine's balance when it was both floating and diving underwater.

The "HMS Agamemnon" is 97 meters long and weighs 7,400 tons. It has new nuclear technology and systems for finding and sensing things. It can travel around the world without coming to the surface of the water.

Like other Astute-class submarines, "HMS Agamemnon" will carry regular weapons. These include "Tomahawk" long-range missiles for attacking land and "Spearfish" heavy torpedoes. This will help the UK better protect its nuclear weapons at sea, its aircraft carrier groups, and important things underwater.

The naming ceremony for "HMS Agamemnon" happened in April last year. For the ceremony, they used beer made by a local brewery.

Taiwan to Launch Asia Innovation Funding Platform on October 21st to Improve Trading System

Taiwan's Financial Supervisory Commission (FSC) announced today that on October 21st, it will hold the 2025 Taiwan Week. This event will include the "Asia Innovation Funding Platform Launch Ceremony," an "Industry Innovation Day Forum," and the "Asia Innovation Cup Finals." The FSC said it will make changes to its innovation board rules and bond market rules. This will help Taiwan's money market compete better internationally.

The FSC released a statement today. The event will start with the Asia Innovation Funding Platform Launch Ceremony. By making the rules for the innovation board and bond market better and more flexible, Taiwan hopes to make its capital market stronger on a global level. The goal is to bring together important new industries faster. This will create a full system for new ideas and make Taiwan a key place for new businesses and money markets. It will also build a funding market that uses both stocks and bonds.

The FSC explained that on the day of the event, different government groups, the Taiwan Stock Exchange, and the Taipei Exchange will be there. They will all take part in the launch ceremony for the Asia Innovation Funding Platform.

The Industry Innovation Day Forum will talk about important plans and global trends. Mr. Hsiao Chen-Jung, head of industrial development at the National Development Council, and Ms. Joanna Juang, head of semiconductor research for IDC Asia Pacific and general manager for IDC Taiwan, will give special talks.

After this, experts from different areas will have discussions. These experts include Mr. Lin Yu-Sheng, vice chairman of the Taiwan Venture Capital Association; Mr. Lee Kuan-Chih, head of the Small and Medium Enterprise and Startup Administration of the Ministry of Economic Affairs; Mr. Lyu Cheng-Hua, secretary-general of the National Federation of Industries; Mr. Hsieh Jung-Feng, secretary-general of the Taiwan National Innovation & Entrepreneurship Association; and Mr. Chen Chun-Shan, chairman of the Taiwan Digital Governance Association. They will talk about clear plans and resources for new industry development.

The FSC also said that the Asia Innovation Cup Finals will be the last part of the event. This competition will find promising new businesses from Taiwan and other countries. It aims to create an important platform for new ideas and money exchange in Asia. More than 70 teams entered the competition. Nine of these new businesses are from other countries like Singapore, Malaysia, Japan, and Finland. They work in different areas such as artificial intelligence, digital cloud technology, satellite communication, smart healthcare, and semiconductors.

The FSC hopes that this event will help people from new businesses in Taiwan and other countries talk and share experiences. It aims to give new teams more attention and money. It also wants investors and businesses to find chances to work together. This will help Taiwan move towards becoming a center for managing money in Asia.

"The Sound of Magic" Film Symphony Concert Returns, Bringing Back Disney's Golden Age of Animation

The Disney "Sound of Magic" Film Symphony Concert is coming back to Taiwan. The concert will start with old favorite Disney films like "Peter Pan" and "Pinocchio." It will take the audience back to the best time of Disney animation.

According to news from Nuernberg Arts, the Disney "Sound of Magic" Film Symphony Concert uses new video editing and orchestra music. The show will include special video parts made by Disney Animation Studios. These parts combine real song scenes, great movie moments, and art ideas that are usually not shown to the public. This lets the audience see the secret ways Disney creates its films.

Different from usual concerts, "Disney: The Sound of Magic" creates a special experience where you feel like you are part of the show. In the "Villains Medley" part, the audience will hear music from bad characters. These include the Evil Queen from "Snow White" and Scar from "The Lion King." Disney's villains from different times will appear again with the music.

In the "Heroes Medley," the focus is on how characters struggle and grow. It connects many popular characters like Stitch from "Lilo & Stitch," Simba from "The Lion King," Tarzan, and Hercules. It shows how they find strength inside themselves when they face problems, and how they finally become new and strong.

The Disney "Sound of Magic" Film Symphony Concert will happen on December 6th and 7th. It will be at the National Concert Hall in Taipei. Cheng Li-Pin will lead the Evergreen Symphony Orchestra for the performance.
