Wei-Chieh Huang pitched only one inning in his first start in CPBL; Dragons lost to Monkeys 4-7

Wei-Chieh Huang, a pitcher chosen by the Wei Chuan Dragons in the first round of this year's mid-season draft, started his first game in the Chinese Professional Baseball League (CPBL) today against the Rakuten Monkeys. He only pitched for one inning and gave up 5 runs. In the end, the Dragons lost to the Monkeys with a score of 4 to 7.

Wei-Chieh Huang has experience in Major League Baseball (MLB) but has had many injuries in recent years. However, the Dragons still picked him in the first round of the CPBL draft. After playing three games in the minor league to get ready, he was quickly moved to the first team to help as a relief pitcher. Today, he was given the important job of starting against the Monkeys. But Huang did not play well. Also, his teammates made mistakes. He only pitched for one inning and gave up 5 runs, which caused the Dragons to lose.

In the first inning, Huang got three outs without letting anyone on base. But in the second inning, he had problems. Lin Hung-Yu hit a single, then a teammate made a big error. After that, four more hits followed quickly. He also walked Chen Chen-Wei. Huang had to leave the game without getting any outs. The next pitcher, Kuo Yu-Cheng, could not stop the Monkeys from scoring, and they got 5 runs in that one inning.

In total, Huang pitched 1 inning and threw 29 pitches. He faced 9 batters, gave up 4 hits, and walked 1 batter. He lost 5 runs, but 2 of them were not his fault.

In the bottom of the 5th inning, the Dragons' defense had problems again. The right fielder, Tseng Sheng-An, made a mistake when he tried to catch Chen Chen-Wei's ground ball. His throw back to the infield was also bad. This allowed Chen Chen-Wei to run all the way home. The Dragons scored 3 runs in the top of the 7th inning to try and get closer in score. However, they could not score against Chen Kuan-Yu and Chu Cheng-Yang. So, the Dragons lost 4 to 7. Wei-Chieh Huang got his first loss as a starter in his CPBL career.

US Retail Sales Grew More Than Expected in August; No Impact from Trump's Tariffs Yet

The US Commerce Department announced today that retail sales in August went up by 0.6% compared to July. This is the same increase as in July. Compared to August last year, sales grew by 5.0%. Both numbers were better than what experts thought they would be. This shows that the tariffs (extra taxes on imported goods) from former President Trump have not yet widely affected the US economy.

According to Agence France-Presse (AFP), consumer spending is a big reason why the US economy grows. Many businesses in the US have said they feel pressure on prices because President Donald Trump raised tariffs. As these extra costs are passed on to customers, people are watching to see how American shoppers will react.

For example, in August, sales at car and parts dealers in the US went up by 0.5% compared to July. Sales at restaurants and bars grew by 0.7%, and sales at fresh food stores also went up a little by 0.3%.

However, in August, sales at US furniture stores went down by 0.3% compared to July. Department store sales also dropped slightly by 0.1%. The drop in earnings for large department stores was very clear. This means that people might be spending less money on certain types of products.

For some time, importers and retailers in the US have taken on some of the extra tariff costs themselves. But it is not clear how long they can keep doing this. Also, many businesses bought more goods than usual before Trump's new tariffs started. As they use up these goods, they will have to pay higher prices when they buy more.
