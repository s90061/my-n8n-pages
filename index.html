Germany's Border Checks Cost 80 Million Euros, Most Money Spent on Police Overtime

Germany started checking all its borders again in September last year. New information from the Federal Ministry of Interior shows that by the end of June this year, this action has cost about 80.5 million Euros (around 2.8 billion Taiwan Dollars). Most of this money was spent on overtime pay for federal police, which was 37.9 million Euros (about 1.3 billion Taiwan Dollars).

The report also shows that the extra costs for federal police working at the border are about 24 million to 29.1 million Euros every three months. From April to June 2025 alone, the government spent about 8 million Euros on food and housing for police officers. Nearly 3 million Euros were spent on special pay for working difficult hours. Equipment cost 2.6 million Euros, and running the border checkpoints cost almost 2 million Euros.

About 14,000 federal police officers are currently working at the borders. Interior Minister Alexander Dobrindt announced last week that they will continue the land border checks, which were planned for one year. They will also send people back who are not allowed to stay. This has caused questions and disagreements in Germany.

Marcel Emmerich, a spokesperson for internal affairs in the Green Party in parliament, said that tens of thousands of federal police have been sent to the border for these actions, which he called "illegal and useless." He added that this has caused a shortage of police at train stations and airports. It also makes things harder for people who travel to work or for holidays.

The Police Union (GdP) said that checking all borders has made the police's workload "very high." Andreas RoÃŸkopf, the union head, said that as border and immigration rules become stricter, police units across the country are still changing their work schedules. Because there are not enough police, many officers have to give up their planned training and further education.

Last year, there were several knife attacks and car attacks in Germany. The far-right AfD party criticized the ruling party's policies on refugees and immigrants. This helped the AfD gain many votes and become the largest party in many German states.

Chancellor Friedrich Merz had already made border control and stricter immigration policies part of his promises during the parliamentary election in February this year. After taking office in May, he continued to make border checks stronger. The goal is to stop the AfD party from growing more because of immigration and refugee issues, which could make society more far-right and anti-foreigner.

Good Morning World: Resilience Law Budget Increases to 590 Billion, Everyone Gets Cash Without Income Limits

The Executive Yuan meeting on August 14th approved changes to the Resilience Special Regulations. The maximum money set aside will increase to 590 billion New Taiwan Dollars. They also decided when to give out cash to everyone and added 1 billion New Taiwan Dollars for administrative costs. Another 20 billion New Taiwan Dollars will be added to make electricity stronger, and an extra 4 billion New Taiwan Dollars will be given to help disadvantaged groups. The plan will now go to the Legislative Yuan for review. The time for giving out cash has changed: it will start within one month after the special budget is announced and finish within seven months. The Executive Yuan said that because lawmakers from different parties have shared public opinions, and because of new customs policies and natural disasters, they decided not to set an income limit for giving out the money. They will also increase help for disadvantaged groups. If all parties agree, it is expected that the money could be given out in September.

The Executive Yuan on August 14th also approved an extra budget plan. It includes a total of 87.84136 billion New Taiwan Dollars. This money will come from the leftover funds from this year's original budget. The largest part is 63.60554 billion New Taiwan Dollars for general subsidies to special municipalities and county governments. The next largest is 13.27833 billion New Taiwan Dollars for various government projects that are actually needed. Trump and Putin: Strong and Good at Talking, Ukraine's Peace Future Is Not Decided

The leaders of the US and Russia will have a meeting in Alaska to talk about peace in Ukraine. News in the US says that the issue of Ukraine's land is the main topic. Putin feels confident because Russian troops are making progress, so he can suggest ideas to Trump. Trump wants to be a peacemaker. But people are worried if the two will make secret deals when they are alone.

US President Donald Trump and Russian President Vladimir Putin will meet on August 15th in Anchorage, Alaska, which is only 90 kilometers from Russian land. This meeting is very important for history.

In 1867, the US and Russia signed a treaty in Washington. Russia agreed to sell Alaska to the US for 7.2 million US dollars. Putin, as president, will be on American land that was once Russian more than 150 years ago. They will talk about ending the war in Ukraine, but the leader of the country that was attacked is not at the meeting.

The world is waiting to see if the meeting will have any results. If there are results, how will Ukraine and Europe deal with their future? Trump plans to meet with Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy after his talks with Putin.

Russia attacked Ukraine in the spring of 2022. Ukrainian troops fought hard with military help from the US and Europe. The Russian army could not win quickly as they planned, and their economy and finances were badly affected. But as the war continued, help from the US and Europe slowed down. Russia continued to sell energy to India, China, and some European countries, which helped their economy become stable. Russia also recruited more soldiers, and the Ukrainian army is now in a difficult position.

Russian troops have taken almost all of Luhansk in eastern Ukraine. They now control most of Donetsk, Luhansk, Kherson, and Zaporizhzhia, and have connected these areas south to Crimea, which Russia took in 2014. From this, it is clear what Vladimir Putin wants to achieve.

Putin wants to connect Russian land from inland to the Black Sea, taking the port in the Kherson region. This would leave Ukraine with a very short coastline, which would greatly reduce its economic development and strategic importance.

The New York Times said that Putin feels strong because the Russian army is doing better on the battlefield. In the beginning, Russian troops did not have enough equipment and were confused, so the Ukrainian army hit them hard. But Putin used all of Russia's power to change the army and the country's economy. He recruited soldiers, bought weapons, and changed tactics at the front lines, with the only goal of defeating the Ukrainian army.

Now, the war is going well for Russia. The Russian army has more soldiers and equipment than the Ukrainian army and all the help from Western countries combined. Russian soldiers continue to be injured or die, but they are still moving west. The front line is now 1,200 kilometers long, which allows Putin to fight until he gets a peace agreement based on what he wants.

Trump wants to be a "peacemaker" and win a Nobel Peace Prize for his time as president. However, the White House has surprisingly lowered expectations for the meeting. Trump said he believes he can reach an agreement with Putin, but there is still a 25% chance it will fail.

Putin's goal is very clear: he wants other countries to accept and agree that Russia has taken Ukrainian land. Being on the international stage helps him get more attention. People are worried that Trump will be alone with Putin, who is also good at negotiating, and that no one will know if they make secret deals.
