Vice President: Taiwan-US Talks Continue, Hope for Trade Balance and Industry Benefits

Taiwan and the United States started a new round of talks this week. Vice President Hsiao Bi-khim said that the negotiating team is still in Washington. The team is working hard and feels very responsible. They are trying to achieve trade balance and benefits for Taiwan's industries. They also want to find chances for Taiwan and the US to work more closely in technology and other industries.

Ms. Hsiao spoke at a meeting of the Chinese National Association of Industry and Commerce today. She said that Taiwanese businesses have always stood strong when facing problems. This includes past events like the oil crisis, joining the World Trade Organization (WTO), and big changes in world politics.

Ms. Hsiao stated that the world is still not peaceful now. Taiwan is facing trade wars and technology wars from outside. Taiwan is in the middle of the strongest competition in world politics and industry. But Taiwan has a strong industry foundation that has been built over many years. This helps Taiwan be very important in the world during this time of strong competition.

As the world changes quickly, Ms. Hsiao hopes Taiwan can play a stable role. In the past, Taiwan often just reacted to things. Now, Taiwan actively plans its resources and policies. It also looks for ways to work with other countries. In recent years, with changes in global industry, spreading risks, and worldwide trends, Taiwan has also made new partnerships in different areas.

Talking about trade talks with the US, Ms. Hsiao said the negotiation team is still in Washington. The negotiators are very determined and have a strong sense of duty. They are working almost 24 hours a day to get fair trade and benefits for Taiwan's industries. They also want to get chances for Taiwan and the US to work more closely in technology, economy, and other areas in the future.

Besides the US, Ms. Hsiao said Taiwan has been promoting its New Southbound Policy in recent years. It has also signed trade and economic agreements with countries like the UK and Canada. As the world situation changes, more international friends visiting Taiwan want to make their partnerships with Taiwan stronger. Even with global problems, Taiwan is finding new chances.

Ms. Hsiao said that the Chinese National Association of Industry and Commerce has always helped the government and businesses talk to each other. It is also an important place to connect Taiwan's industry strengths. She hopes that bigger companies can help smaller ones in the future. She wants Taiwan's whole industry to grow evenly. This means not just high-tech companies, but also many small and medium-sized businesses and all types of work. She hopes everyone can find new opportunities during these changing times.

Ms. Hsiao also said that the government is working with different departments. They are using new chances from AI trends. They are promoting plans for AI intelligence, digitalization, and becoming carbon neutral. This is to make sure that small and medium-sized businesses are not left behind in world changes. Instead, they can grow with new technology industries. Together, they can make Taiwan's economy stronger.

Two Kaohsiung Youth Orchestras Invited to Japan, Will Perform in Tokyo Concert Hall

Two music groups from Kaohsiung, the KSO Youth Orchestra and the KCO Youth Chinese Orchestra, are invited to perform in Japan. They will play on July 29th at a new concert hall in Setagaya Ward, Tokyo. This visit shows the results of their music exchange.

The Kaohsiung City Bureau of Cultural Affairs announced today that the KSO Youth Orchestra visited Setagaya Youth Orchestra in 2018 and 2019. This year, the KSO Youth Orchestra will go to Japan again with top members from the KCO Youth Chinese Orchestra. Young musicians from Taiwan and Japan will come together to start a new chapter in cultural exchange. The Setagaya Youth Orchestra also plans to visit Kaohsiung in the summer of 2026. Music will connect them and continue this international "music friendship."

Ms. Wang Wen-tsui, the Director of the Cultural Affairs Bureau, said that in 2016, a Setagaya council member came to Taiwan to find a friendly city for cultural exchange. The two sides first signed an agreement to work together. In 2019, after the two cities signed another agreement, the Setagaya Youth Orchestra and the KSO Youth Orchestra worked together. Their exchange started with music and became very close. It has slowly grown to include culture, art, tourism, and sports.

The KSO Youth Orchestra has almost 100 members. It is the biggest youth orchestra in southern Taiwan. Its members come from Kaohsiung and other cities in the south. They practice every week and perform twice a year at the Weiwuying Concert Hall and the Dadong Arts Center. This invitation to perform in Setagaya will help the members see more of the world and gain stage experience. It also helps with cultural exchange between the cities.

For this performance, the KSO Youth Orchestra will be led by the famous young conductor Bao Heng-yi. They will play "Angel from Formosa" by Tyzen Hsiao and "The Moldau" by BedÅ™ich Smetana. The KCO Youth Chinese Orchestra will perform "Under the Bald Cypress Tree" by Yang Chieh-ju and "Spring Morning at Yangmingshan" by Dong Rong-sen. Also, Chen Yi-jou and Ruan Hsin-yu will play Li Bo-chan's "Chu Song" for two bowed string instruments.

For the last part of the performance, the KSO Youth Orchestra and the Setagaya Youth Orchestra will have 100 musicians play together. They will perform "Cinema Paradiso," a famous movie song by the Italian composer Ennio Morricone.

International Physics Olympiad: Taiwanese Students Win 3 Gold, 2 Silver, Rank 5th Globally

Taiwanese students took part in the 55th International Physics Olympiad in France. They won 3 gold medals and 2 silver medals. Taiwan ranked 5th in the world based on the number of gold medals. The best student from Taiwan was Tsai Chung-ta from Taichung First Senior High School. He got the 13th best score overall.

The Ministry of Education announced today that the five Taiwanese physics Olympiad students were chosen from 2374 students. A team of more than 10 professors trained them. They went to France to compete on July 16th.

In the tough competition, Tsai Chung-ta from Taichung First Senior High School won a gold medal (he was 13th overall). Ho Chung-chin from Hsinchu Science Park Experimental High School won a gold medal (he was 15th overall). Su Yun-chieh from Taipei Fuhsing Private School won a gold medal (he was 35th overall). Ho Cheng-yu from Chiayi Senior High School won a silver medal (he was 44th overall). Wang Kuan-yu from Taipei Jian Kuo High School won a silver medal (he was 65th overall).

Tsai Chung-ta's parents are both math teachers. He said that in junior high school, he met a fun physics teacher. This led him to study physics. His strong math skills made physics easy for him. He likes sports like ball games and street dance. He uses his free time to relax.

Ho Chung-chin is very interested in solid-state physics and quantum mechanics. In his second year of high school, he started a physics research club with his classmates and was its leader. In his free time, he reads history. He also studies the design of airplanes and cars. He has been learning classical piano for over ten years.

Taiwanese students have always done well in the International Physics Olympiad. In recent years, Taiwan has always been in the top 5 countries. The Ministry of Education will give gold medal winners NT$200,000 and silver medal winners NT$100,000. They can also enter university physics programs without an exam or be recommended to other university departments.
