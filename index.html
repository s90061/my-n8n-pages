Traditional Chinese Medicine Joins the Gym Business, Aiming for the 30 Billion Sports Market

Not everyone goes to the gym, but many people go to Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) clinics. The TCM field is not only focused on beauty and fertility treatments, but now it is also entering the sports industry, which is worth 30 billion New Taiwan dollars. They are looking for new opportunities with people who do not yet go to gyms but need health support.

"The number of people who need to see a doctor shows how many potential customers there are," said Wu Jun-ching, a doctor who runs a clinic that offers both TCM and emergency services. He told reporters today that less than 5% of gym members are active, but about 70% of people in Taiwan have seen a TCM doctor. Combining TCM and fitness is not just about bringing two fields together; it's a new beginning for the health industry. He calls it a "blue ocean beyond a blue ocean," meaning it's a very new and open market.

Young people often sit for a long time and are very stressed, so "pain" has become a common modern problem. More people now believe in preventing illness. Many people know that exercise can help with pain. Wu Jun-ching said that many office workers who start exercising often feel that it doesn't help, or they even feel more pain. The problem might not be their exercise method, but that their bodies are not ready. At this point, they need TCM to help their bodies from the inside.

Wu Jun-ching believes that as more people get older, it is important to stay active, and muscle strength is key. For older people, gyms might seem strange, but TCM is like an old friend. If TCM is combined with step-by-step training and doctors are always available to give advice, TCM gyms could become a safe way for older people to start exercising.

Li Wei-yi, another TCM clinic director, also said that TCM gyms are not for making strong men. Instead, they give useful exercise advice for people of all ages with different needs. Even if someone only wants to sit without pain or climb stairs without getting tired, they can find a suitable training plan. A good gym, like a good TCM clinic, is not just for athletes; everyone can come in.

Li Wei-yi further explained that the goal of combining TCM with gyms is not just to stop pain, but to prevent it from coming back. Office workers who sit for long periods often have stiff necks and back pain. In the past, they would get temporary relief from massage or acupuncture. But the effect was only for a short time. "We give more than just acupuncture or medicine; we also give exercise plans and 'homework' to help them keep exercising."

Li Wei-yi believes that the main idea of TCM gyms is to change from passive treatment (where someone does something to you) to active recovery (where you do something yourself). This means patients leave the clinic and go to the gym. With a professional coach, they can make their lower back muscles stronger, learn how to stand correctly, and get into the habit of exercising. Only by building muscle strength can people truly say goodbye to pain.

Is Solar Power in Taiwan the Most Expensive? Ministry Says No Easy Comparison

Taipei, August 17 â€“ Some news reports said that solar power is the most expensive in Taiwan. They also said that if Taiwan Power Company (Taipower) built it, the cost would be at least 50% less. The Ministry of Economic Affairs (MOEA) today released a press statement to explain. They said that different countries start their renewable energy efforts in different ways, so it is not fair to compare prices directly. Taiwan's solar power price has already gone down by 60%.

The news reports claimed that solar power is the most expensive in Taiwan and that Taipower could cut costs by 50% if they built it. The MOEA's Energy Administration explained today that Taiwan's system for buying solar power was set up in 2009. This was the first way to support renewable energy.

The MOEA pointed out that in 2010, the price for one unit of solar power was 11.76 New Taiwan dollars on average. But by 2025, it has dropped to 4.30 New Taiwan dollars per unit, which is a 60% decrease. This happened because technology improved and the market became more developed. Also, about 25% of solar power is now sold directly to private businesses in Taiwan. This shows that the market for renewable energy is slowly becoming more varied.

The MOEA's Energy Administration stressed that when countries promote renewable energy, they have different natural resources, how developed their industry technology is, how mature their electricity market is, and how their policies are designed. So, comparing prices directly can give wrong information. For example, India, Japan, and Germany mostly use systems where companies bid for projects or get extra money. These systems are clearly different from Taiwan's system, so it's not fair to compare them directly.

The MOEA's Energy Administration added that the media's claim that "Taipower building it would cut costs by 50%" is not true. Taipower's main job is to make sure there is enough power supply. The cost of solar energy depends on many different things. These include how easy it is to get land, how big the project is, how developed the technology is, and how much prices change in the supply chain. So, Taipower running the project does not automatically lead to a large drop in costs.

The MOEA's Energy Administration stated that the price for buying renewable energy is set every year by a special committee. They follow fair, open, and strict rules. They also consider Taiwan's environmental conditions, goals for promoting renewable energy, and what is happening with technology around the world. This helps them set a fair price. In the future, they will continue to learn from other countries and review the system. This will help Taiwan steadily reach its goal of having 20% green energy and also change policies to meet the needs of industries that require green electricity.
