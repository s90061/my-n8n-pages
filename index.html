Heavy Rain Closes 11 Main Roads and Blocks 5 Across Taiwan

(Central News Agency reporter Huang Qiaowen, Taipei, July 31) Because of the heavy rain on July 28, the Directorate General of Highways said today that as of 10 PM, 11 road sections are closed as a precaution. Also, 5 road sections are blocked due to damage. The Directorate General of Highways reminds drivers that mountain roads can have landslides and falling rocks easily. Road conditions are hard to know, so please do not enter mountain roads if it is not necessary.

The Ministry of Transportation and Communications' Directorate General of Highways sent out a press release to announce the road sections that are now closed as a precaution. As of 10 PM, these include: in Chiayi County's Tapu Township, Provincial Highway 3, from 295K to 336K (Yunshui to Tapu section); in Chiayi County's Alishan Township, Provincial Highway 18, from 34K to 68K+500 (Chukou to Fushan section); in Chiayi County's Alishan Township, Provincial Highway 18, from 68K+500 to 88K (Fushan to Alishan section); in Kaohsiung City's Jiaxian District, Provincial Highway 20, from 58K+300 to 72K+500 (Jiaxian to Laonong section); in Kaohsiung City's Taoyuan District, Provincial Highway 20, at 90K+700 (Sarabua Bridge).

Other closed roads are: in Kaohsiung City's Taoyuan District, temporary Provincial Highway 20 Line 93, from 0K to 5K (Qinhue to Fuxing section); in Kaohsiung City's Taoyuan District and Taitung County's Haiduan Township, temporary Provincial Highway 20 Line 105, from 0K to 44K (Meishankou to Xiangyang section); in Pingtung County's Sandimen Township, Provincial Highway 24, from 27K+400 to 37K+300 (Dewen Road Intersection to Shenshan section); in Kaohsiung City's Liouguei District, Provincial Highway 27, from 12K to 23K (Zhongxing to Dajin section); in Kaohsiung City's Namasia District, Provincial Highway 29, at 2K+880 (Minsheng Bridge); in Kaohsiung City's Namasia District, temporary Provincial Highway 29 Line 11, from 0K to 12K (Namasia to Wulipu section).

There are 5 road sections blocked by disaster: In Nantou County's Jiji Township, Provincial Highway 16, at 11K+600 (Jiji section), there was a mudflow, blocking both directions. In Kaohsiung City's Liouguei District, Provincial Highway 20, at 79K (Lele section), rain caused the road foundation to wash away, blocking the road. In Kaohsiung City's Taoyuan District, Provincial Highway 20, at 81K+100 (Caoshui section), rain caused the road foundation to wash away, blocking the road. In Kaohsiung City's Taoyuan District, Provincial Highway 20, at 91K+500 (Dongzhuang temporary bridge), the river rose over the road, so the bridge is closed. In Kaohsiung City's Taoyuan District, Provincial Highway 20, at 99K (Fuxing section), the river rose over the road, blocking the road. (Editor: Chen Ching-fang) 1140731

Sentec Company: Over Half of Sales Now From Low-Earth Orbit Satellites, New Factory Line in Xizhi Starts in Q4

(Central News Agency reporter Pan Zhiyi, Taipei, July 31) Sentec Technology, a company in the low-Earth orbit satellite and wireless communication business, announced its total sales for the first half of the year today. They reached NT$1.13 billion, which is the highest for this period ever. Sales from low-Earth orbit satellite products made up 52.7% of this total. This shows that these products are the main reason for the company's growth. In June alone, sales from low-Earth orbit satellite products grew to 54% of the total.

Sentec said that to meet the growing demand for low-Earth orbit satellites, they are moving forward with many plans to expand. The second phase of their new factory in Xizhi, Taiwan, is expected to open in the fourth quarter. They have also finished planning to expand their third factory in Qidu and will soon start looking for a company to build it.

For overseas operations, the plan for building the second factory in Vietnam is expected to be finalized by the end of the third quarter. This will help the company to supply products more easily from overseas and increase its total production capacity.

Sentec Technology stated that looking at their orders, the amount of orders from low-Earth orbit satellite customers in June was over NT$180 million. This is about 2.5 times the actual sales from low-Earth orbit satellites for that month. Also, recent calculations show that orders for low-Earth orbit satellite products that have not yet been shipped are over NT$400 million. This means that there is still a high demand for low-Earth orbit satellite products and many orders coming in.

Sentec Technology expects that in the second half of the year, after their sales reach a stable point, shipments of low-Earth orbit satellites will start to increase month by month again. They also hope to see excellent sales results, like "low-Earth orbit satellites breaking through the sky," and continue to be a main driver of growth.

Sentec Technology said that in the second quarter, their key low-Earth orbit satellite customer started testing communications. This caused some product shipments to be delayed. However, this customer is very serious about Sentec's plans to increase production in the future. They asked for short-term, medium-term, and long-term plans for production growth. They also plan to send people to visit the factories in person to make sure that the expansion work will be finished on time. This shows that they trust Sentec and depend on their future cooperation.

Sentec's numbers show that its total sales for the second quarter were NT$514 million. This is a 17% drop from the previous quarter. The main reasons for this were that Positive Through Technology was not included in the sales, and some shipments were delayed because of customer communication tests. Also, the strong increase in the New Taiwan Dollar against the US Dollar in the second quarter had some temporary effects. If we use the same exchange rate as the first quarter, the total profit margin for the second quarter was about 49%. This is only a small difference from the first quarter's profit margin of 50.6%.

Regarding non-operating income and expenses, Sentec said that the amount changed from a positive number in the first quarter to a negative number in the second quarter. This was due to losses from exchange rates. (Editor: Yang Lanxuan) 1140731

Typhoon and Heavy Rain Cause Damage in Central and Southern Taiwan; Financial Companies Donate Over NT$209 Million

(Central News Agency reporter Su Siyun, Taipei, July 31) Typhoon Danas caused a lot of damage in central and southern Taiwan. Many financial companies have given money to help with the disaster relief. Statistics from the Financial Supervisory Commission (FSC) show that financial companies, including financial holding companies, banks, insurance companies, securities firms, investment trusts, and related associations and groups, have donated a total of over NT$209.86 million.

Typhoon Danas hit central and southern Taiwan very hard and caused a lot of damage. Recently, strong rains from the southwest air currents have made life very difficult for many local people, putting them in hard situations.

Lin Chih-chi, the chief secretary of the FSC, said at a regular press conference that he is thankful that financial companies have given money, some under their own names and some as individual parts of larger companies. The FSC's statistics show that financial holding companies have given NT$115.9 million. National banks have given a total of NT$56.1 million. Insurance companies and the Life Insurance Association have given NT$5.75 million. Securities and investment trust associations have given NT$4.115 million. Other groups related to securities have given NT$28 million.

CTBC Financial Holding said it donated NT$20 million to the southern cities and counties. This money will be used for disaster help, emergency medical care, housing for people, and rebuilding, as planned by local governments. CTBC Bank offers a "Family Emergency Loan Service." Customers who have any financial product with the bank (like savings, credit cards, insurance, funds, or loans) can apply for a loan within 3 months after an emergency if they show proof of damage.

KGI Financial Holding announced today that its group will donate NT$20 million. Through its "KGI Social Welfare Charity Foundation" and group volunteers, they will provide help and emergency relief in many ways. KGI Life Insurance quickly started its customer care services. It launched 5 main relief measures: fast claim payments, advance payment for hospital stays, delayed premium payments, special insurance service offers, and flexible travel insurance changes. These measures are to make sure that people affected by the disaster can get quick and reliable insurance support.

Yuanta Financial Holding also announced today that it donated NT$20 million to a special account for disaster relief under the Ministry of Health and Welfare. This money will help with disaster aid, emergency medical care, housing, and later rebuilding. This is a real action to help the disaster areas and shows the strength and unity of businesses. Yuanta Financial Holding said that after the disaster, they immediately started emergency plans and customer care programs.

Nanshan Life Insurance and Nanshan General Insurance announced today that they are donating NT$6 million to the Ministry of Health and Welfare's disaster relief account through the Nanshan Life Insurance Charity Foundation. When Typhoon Danas was close to Taiwan, Nanshan Life Insurance and Nanshan General Insurance quickly started their customer service and care measures. These include no interest on policy loans, no interest on home loans, delayed premium payments, fast claim payments, and advance payments for hospital stays, among others.

To help people affected by the disaster, LINE Bank announced that starting in August, flood-affected households in Taichung, Changhua, Nantou, Yunlin, Chiayi, Tainan, Kaohsiung, and Pingtung can apply for a 3-year low-interest personal loan with no contract needed. Each person can borrow up to NT$200,000, with a single interest rate of 1.88% and no setup fee. The application period ends at the end of August. (Editor: Lin Ke-lun) 1140731
