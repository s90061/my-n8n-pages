Trump: US Sunk 3 Ships Near Venezuela

Today, US President Trump said that the United States has "sunk" a total of three ships near Venezuela. The day before, he confirmed that the US carried out a second deadly attack on people suspected of drug trafficking in the Caribbean Sea.

According to Agence France-Presse, Trump told reporters as he left the White House to visit the UK: "We actually sank three ships, not two. You only saw two."

Trump did not give more details about the third ship or say if more people died.

On September 15, Trump said that US forces hit a second ship in international waters. Three people died in that attack. Trump called them "drug lord terrorists."

Early this month, the US military attacked suspected drug trafficking boats in the Caribbean Sea, and 11 people died. After these attacks, people have often questioned if the Trump government's actions were legal.

The US government showed videos of the two previous known attacks. They said they had strong proof that all the people killed were smugglers trying to bring dangerous drugs into the United States.

However, the US did not give specific details to support these claims.

These actions by the US military have made the situation more tense in the Caribbean area. The US has sent many navy ships to this area. This has made some people think that Washington might want to remove the government in Venezuela.

Robert Redford Dies; Meryl Streep Calls Him a 'Giant Star', Jane Fonda Very Sad

This morning, Robert Redford, a very important person in Hollywood, passed away. Meryl Streep, a famous actress who worked with him in movies like "Out of Africa," honored him. She called him a "giant star." Jane Fonda, an Oscar-winning actress who worked with him in many classic films, also shared her sadness. She remembered him as "a good person in every way."

Agence France-Presse reported that Meryl Streep's publicist shared a short statement with the media. It said: "One of the giant stars has fallen. Rest in peace, my dear friend."

Robert Redford's publicist, Cindi Berger, said in a statement that Robert Redford passed away at his home in Utah this morning. He was 89 years old.

Jane Fonda said: "I was very shocked to hear the news that Bob (Robert Redford) died this morning. I cannot stop crying. He meant a lot to me, and he was a wonderful person in every way. He stood for an America that we must keep fighting for."

During his life, Robert Redford was famous for movies like "Butch Cassidy and the Sundance Kid." When he was young, he was handsome and a well-known "Hollywood golden boy." Later, he also became a good director and won an Oscar for Best Director.

European Stocks Go Down as Market Waits for Fed Meeting

The US Federal Reserve (Fed) started its two-day meeting about money policies today. People expect the Fed might cut interest rates. Because of this, the market was very cautious. The three main European stock indexes all closed lower today.

The FTSE 100 index in London went down by 81.37 points, or 0.88%. It closed at 9195.66 points.

The DAX index in Frankfurt fell a lot, by 419.62 points, or 1.77%. It closed at 23329.24 points.

The CAC 40 index in Paris dropped by 78.71 points, or 1%. It closed at 7818.22 points.
