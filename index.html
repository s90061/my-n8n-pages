After Thailand and Cambodia Agreed to Stop Fighting, Trump Said He Helped and Talked to Both Leaders Again

Today, leaders from Thailand and Cambodia said they would stop fighting. After this, US President Trump said he had spoken to both leaders. He also said that he helped them reach this agreement to stop fighting.

News agency AFP reported that Trump wrote on his social media page, "Truth Social," "I just spoke with the acting Prime Minister of Thailand and the Prime Minister of Cambodia. I am happy to say that after President Trump got involved, both countries agreed to stop fighting and have peace. Congratulations to everyone!"

He also said, "I have told my trade team to start trade talks again."

On July 26, Trump said he had talked to Cambodia's Prime Minister Hun Manet and Thailand's acting Prime Minister Phumtham Wechayachai. He asked them to stop fighting. He also said that the US was discussing trade with both countries. If they continued to fight, the US would not make any trade agreements with them.

The fighting between Thailand and Cambodia at their border lasted for five days. Today, Kaja Kallas, who is a top official for foreign affairs and security policy in the European Union, also praised both countries for agreeing to stop fighting.

Kallas wrote on social media platform X, "After several days of deadly fighting, the agreement for Cambodia and Thailand to stop fighting is a big step forward. It is very important that both countries truly follow the agreement."

Earlier today, Phumtham and Hun Manet held talks in Malaysia to stop the fighting. After the talks, Malaysian Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim announced that Thailand and Cambodia would stop fighting without any conditions. This agreement would start at midnight on July 28.

US-Europe Trade Deal Worries Market, European Stocks Go Down

(London, July 28, Central News Agency) After the European Union (EU) and the United States made a trade agreement, the main stock markets in Europe closed lower today. This shows that the market is worried about parts of the trade agreement that are not fair.

The FTSE 100 index in London went down by 38.87 points, which is 0.43%. It closed at 9081.44 points.

The DAX index in Frankfurt fell by 247.14 points, or 1.02%. It closed at 23970.36 points.

The CAC 40 index in Paris dropped by 33.70 points, or 0.43%. It closed at 7800.88 points.

Russian Airlines Attacked by Pro-Ukraine Hackers, Over 40 Flights Cancelled

(Moscow, July 28, Central News Agency) Russia's national airline, Aeroflot, was attacked by hackers today. A group of hackers who support Ukraine said they did it. Because of this, Aeroflot had to cancel more than 40 flights, mostly domestic ones. The Kremlin said the situation is worrying.

Reuters reported that Aeroflot cancelled these flights after saying their computer systems had problems. The cancelled flights also included flights to Minsk, the capital of Belarus, and Yerevan, the capital of Armenia. Also, at least 10 other flights were delayed.

Aeroflot did not say how long it would take to fix the problem. But at Sheremetyevo Airport in Moscow, Russia's capital, the departure boards showed many red "cancelled" signs. This happened during a time when many Russians are traveling for their holidays.

A hacker group called "Silent Crow" said in a statement that they worked with a Belarusian group called Cyberpartisans BY for this attack. They said the attack was related to Russia's big war in Ukraine.

The statement said, "Glory to Ukraine! Long live Belarus!" Reuters has not been able to check if the statement is true, and Ukraine has not said anything about it yet.

Silent Crow has said before that they were responsible for several hacker attacks this year. Their targets included Russia's property database, the national telecom company, large insurance companies, Moscow's city IT department, and the Russian office of the Korean car company KIA.

Russian prosecutors confirmed that today's problems were caused by a hacker attack. They have started a criminal investigation.

Dmitry Peskov, a spokesman for the Kremlin, said, "The information we are seeing in public is quite worrying. The threat from hackers is still a danger for all large companies that provide services to people."
