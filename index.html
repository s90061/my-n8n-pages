Zheng Hao-jun Pitches Well, Gao Yu-jie's 2 Hits and 3 RBIs Help Brothers Win

On August 9, CTBC Brothers won against Fubon Guardians with a score of 8-0. The win was greatly helped by pitcher Zheng Hao-jun, who pitched 5 innings without letting any runs in. Catcher Gao Yu-jie also played very well, getting 2 hits and 3 RBIs. Gao Yu-jie was chosen as the MVP (Most Valuable Player) for the game.

The game was played at the Brothers' home stadium in Taichung. Zheng Hao-jun, also known as "Mei Mei," started the game as pitcher. For the first 3 innings, Zheng Hao-jun did not allow any hits. He only allowed some players from the Guardians team to get on base by walking them or hitting them with the ball. In the 4th inning, with two outs, Shen Hao-wei from the Guardians hit a double, but Zheng Hao-jun still did not let them score.

Zheng Hao-jun threw 71 pitches in total. He pitched for 5 innings and only allowed 1 hit. He gave 4 walks or hit batters and got 3 strikeouts. He did not let any runs score, and this was his 4th win this season. The fastest pitch he threw was 154 kilometers per hour.

In the first inning, the Brothers scored first. Yue Zheng-hua hit a double, Xu Ji-hong walked, and Wang Zheng-shun hit a single. In the second inning, Jiang Kun-yu walked, and then Gao Yu-jie hit a single to add another run.

In the third inning, with one out, Wang Zheng-shun walked, and Zeng Song-en was hit by a pitch. They moved closer to scoring because of a wild pitch. Jiang Kun-yu hit a single to get a run. Then, Yue Dong-hua walked, and Gao Yu-jie hit a single that brought in 2 more runs. This helped the Brothers get a bigger lead.

In the fifth inning, the Brothers started to score again with one out. Yue Dong-hua walked, and Gao Yu-jie was hit by a pitch. Zhang Ren-wei hit a single to score a run. Yue Zheng-hua hit another single to keep the game going. Then, with a ground ball and a sacrifice fly, they scored 3 more runs. This made their lead even safer.

Gao Yu-jie played as the catcher and did well both in hitting and defending. He had 3 chances to hit and got 2 hits, bringing in 3 runs. He was chosen as the MVP of the game. When he was asked how he builds trust and good teamwork with the pitchers, Gao Yu-jie said they talk a lot. During games, he shares his ideas to see if they both agree. He said they keep making changes and discussing things to have good teamwork.

Indian Air Force: 6 Pakistani Military Planes Shot Down During May Fight

New Delhi, August 9 â€“ India's Air Force chief, Mr. Singh, said today that during a fight with Pakistan in May, India shot down five of Pakistan's fighter jets and one military plane. This was the most serious military fight between these two countries in many years. This is the first time India has said this, months after the conflict happened.

Mr. Singh said in a meeting in Bengaluru, a big city in southern India, that most of Pakistan's planes were shot down by Russia-made S-400 missiles. He showed electronic information to prove these hits. He said, "We have confirmed that at least five were shot down, plus one large plane."

Mr. Singh explained that the large plane he mentioned might have been a spy plane. It was shot down from 300 kilometers away. He said, "This is actually the farthest known shot down by a ground-to-air missile ever." The people listening, including air force officers, retired military members, government workers, and business people, clapped loudly.

Pakistan's military did not quickly respond to questions about this. Mr. Singh did not say which types of Pakistani fighter jets were shot down. But he did say that during the air attacks, India also hit a spy plane and "a few F-16 fighter jets" that were in hangars at two air force bases in southern Pakistan.

These two nuclear-armed countries in South Asia had a military fight from May 7 to May 10. Pakistan's air force uses fighter jets from China and American F-16s. Before this, Pakistan had said that none of its planes were shot down in the fight. Pakistan also said that it had shot down six Indian planes, including a French-made Rafale fighter jet. India has said that it had some losses but denied losing six planes.
