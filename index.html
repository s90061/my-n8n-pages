Typhoon Yangliu Affects Roads: 19 Closed for Safety, 3 Blocked

The road authority said that Typhoon Yangliu has caused problems on roads. As of 10 PM today, 19 main roads are closed to prevent danger. Also, 3 main road sections are blocked because of damage.

The Central Weather Administration said that Typhoon Yangliu is now near the southwest coast of Penghu. It will move to the west-northwest. The typhoon's center has already moved away from Hualien, Taitung, Miaoli, Taichung, and Nantou. But it is still a threat to areas south of Changhua, Penghu, and Kinmen. Strong wind and rain continue in areas south of Chiayi and Penghu. The typhoon is expected to become weaker, and its strong wind area will get smaller.

The road authority said that 19 main roads are still closed for safety as of 10 PM today. They will decide when to open them again based on the weather and road conditions.

These roads include: Chiayi County, Jhongpu Township, Provincial Highway 3, from 295K to 336K (Yunshui to Dapau). Man Arrested for Harassing Young Passenger on Taichung Bus

In Taichung, a man named Yan was on a bus this afternoon. He allegedly sexually harassed a young female passenger. The police were called and caught him right there. The victim decided to press charges. After talking to the police, the man was sent to the Taichung District Prosecutor's Office for investigation under the "Sexual Harassment Prevention Act."

Hsieh Chih-chung, a Taichung City Council member from the Democratic Progressive Party, wrote on Facebook today. He said that a man known for sexual harassment has appeared in Fengyuan District. He has been involved in 3 sexual harassment cases since 2024. This afternoon, he received a report from a parent. The man had again harassed someone on a bus. After contacting the police, they successfully took the man to the police station.

The Fengyuan Police Department of Taichung City Police Bureau said today that the Wengzi Police Station received a report at 3 PM this afternoon. A man was harassing a female passenger on a moving bus.

When the police arrived, they immediately arrested 47-year-old Mr. Yan. He was accused of improper behavior towards a young female passenger. The victim immediately filed a complaint and pressed charges based on the "Sexual Harassment Prevention Act."

The police said they have reported the sexual harassment case and the child protection case as required. After questioning, the entire case was sent to the Taiwan Taichung District Prosecutor's Office for investigation under the "Sexual Harassment Prevention Act."

The police urge people who face similar harassment on public transport to be brave. Tell the driver immediately, ask other passengers for help, or call 110 to report it. The police will come and help right away.

Chunghwa Telecom Offers 8% Compensation for Phone Outage on July 31

Chunghwa Telecom had a problem with its mobile network on July 31st early in the morning. A software update went wrong, and people could not make or receive calls on their mobile phones across the country. Today, Chunghwa Telecom announced that it will give all mobile phone users a compensation. They will get a discount equal to 8% of their August monthly fee for voice calls. This will be automatically taken off their September bill.

Chunghwa Telecom released a message today. They said that on July 31st, early in the morning, their mobile network system had a problem with a software update. This affected mobile voice services. Even though mobile internet and other services worked fine, it still caused trouble for users, and they are very sorry.

Chunghwa Telecom announced that according to their mobile broadband service contract, they will give each mobile phone user a compensation. It will be 8% of their August monthly fee for domestic voice calls. This amount will be automatically deducted from their September bill for domestic voice call charges.

On July 31st, early in the morning, Chunghwa Telecom experienced a large-scale outage. Many users across Taiwan could not use their mobile phones. Chunghwa Telecom said at the time that the problem started at 3 AM. It was because a software update for the mobile network system went wrong. This made it impossible to make or receive mobile phone calls everywhere. They said the service started to return to normal after 6 AM that morning.
