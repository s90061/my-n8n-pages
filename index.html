TSG Hawks Sign New MLB-Level Foreign Pitcher, Namasia

The TSG Hawks baseball team in Taiwan announced today that they have signed a new foreign pitcher, Dinelson Lamet, who has played more than 100 games in Major League Baseball (MLB) in the United States. He will wear number 45.

The TSG Hawks team said today that they signed the MLB-level new foreign pitcher, Namasia, to make their pitching stronger. Namasia is 33 years old. He has played for several MLB teams, including the Padres, Rockies, Red Sox, Dodgers, and Royals. Last year, he was a teammate of Japanese baseball star Shohei Ohtani on the Dodgers team.

Namasia has played 121 games in the MLB. He started 60 of those games. He won 17 games and lost 24. He pitched 367.2 innings and struck out 474 batters. His earned run average (ERA) was 4.63.

Namasia was a starting pitcher in 2017, 2019, and 2020. In 2020, he started 12 games in the MLB and pitched 69 innings. His ERA was only 2.09. He allowed only 39 hits and struck out 93 batters. Namasia also played in the MLB last year. This year, he played in the Mexican league. He started all 17 games there, with 7 wins and 5 losses. His ERA was 5.01, and he struck out 88 batters in 88 innings.

The second half of the Chinese Professional Baseball League started on July 4. The TSG Hawks currently have 7 wins, 6 losses, and 2 ties. Their winning percentage is 0.538, and they are in second place.

Ministry of Transportation Helps People Repair Homes

Typhoon Danas and strong winds from the southwest caused heavy damage in southern Taiwan. Many people's homes were badly damaged. The Ministry of Transportation and Communications (MOTC) said today that there is a shortage of workers and materials. So, they have gathered different departments to help connect people with companies. They hope this will help residents find workers to repair their homes.

The MOTC stated on Facebook today that Vice Minister Chen Yan-po called a meeting yesterday with different departments. They checked the progress of repairs in the Tainan disaster area and planned what to do next. They have visited over 100 damaged homes. They are also looking for more workers and supplies to help people rebuild their homes quickly.

The MOTC said that because there are not enough workers, materials, or machines, people cannot find workers for repairs. The government has asked all ministries to help. Each ministry is responsible for certain areas. The MOTC is responsible for Xinying District, Yanshui District, Baihe District, and Houbi District in Tainan City.

People who want to repair their homes can first register with their district chief. The MOTC will then help connect them with companies. Residents will talk with the companies and sign a simple contract if they agree. The MOTC only helps connect them; it does not pay for the repairs.

To make rebuilding faster, the MOTC also set rules for action. Besides connecting companies, they will first repair old single-story houses and homes with damaged roofs that cannot be lived in. They will also collect waste materials like asbestos tiles in one place. The district office will help remove them.

Shigeru Ishiba Shows Sympathy for Southern Taiwan Floods, Joseph Wu Thanks Him and Hopes for Cooperation

Recently, heavy rain caused disasters in Taiwan. Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba wrote a message to show his sympathy to Taiwan. Taiwan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs said today that Foreign Minister Lin Chia-lung thanked Japan again for its care. He hopes Taiwan and Japan can work together to face challenges. Both the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Taiwan's office in Japan have thanked the Japanese government.

Japanese Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba wrote a message to Taiwan. He said he was sad for those who died and their families. He also showed sympathy to those affected by the disaster. He prayed for them to return to normal life soon and said, "Go Taiwan!" The Japan-Taiwan Exchange Association shared his message on Facebook this afternoon.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs released a press statement this afternoon. It said that both the Ministry and Taiwan's office in Japan thanked the Japanese government right away.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs said that Ishiba also wrote a message on July 12 to show sympathy for the damage caused by Typhoon Danas in Taiwan. This shows the deep and true friendship between Taiwan and Japan. The Taiwanese government and people are still working hard to help with rescue and recovery. They want to help those affected rebuild their homes and get back to normal life quickly.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs said that Foreign Minister Lin Chia-lung again sincerely thanked the Japanese government for its warm care and real support. He hopes that Taiwan and Japan will continue to work more closely in related areas. They want to build a stronger, friendlier, and more resilient partnership to face all challenges together.
