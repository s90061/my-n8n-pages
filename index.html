Tech Giants' Financial Reports Watched, US Stocks Mostly Up, S&P and Nasdaq Reach New Highs

US stock markets mostly went up today. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq reached new high points. This happened because big technology companies like Alphabet and Tesla will share their financial reports this week. Also, investors hope for trade agreements that could make the US tariffs less harmful to the economy.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went down a little, by 19.12 points (0.04%), ending at 44323.07 points.

The S&P 500 index went up a little, by 8.81 points (0.14%), ending at 6305.60 points.

The Nasdaq index went up by 78.52 points (0.38%), ending at 20974.18 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index went up a little, by 6.80 points (0.12%), ending at 5739.42 points.

White House: Trump May Send More Tariff Letters Before August 1

White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt said today that US President Trump might send more letters about tariffs before August 1.

Bloomberg News reported that Leavitt also told reporters outside the White House that more trade agreements might be made before this deadline.

Leavitt explained that August 1 is the day the US will start collecting "equal tariffs" (tariffs that match what other countries charge).

She also said that Trump, US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent, Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick, and Trade Representative Jamieson Greer are talking closely with trade partners.

Markets Watch Trade Talks and Russian Energy Exports, International Oil Prices Slightly Down

International oil prices went down a little today. This is because there has not been enough progress in trade talks in the US. Also, new rules from the European Union have not stopped Russia from selling its energy. This makes people still worry about how much oil is needed.

In New York, the price for West Texas Intermediate crude oil for August delivery went down by 14 cents, to $67.20 per barrel.

In London, the price for Brent crude oil for September delivery went down by 7 cents, to $69.21 per barrel.
