The city hopes more volunteers will join.

Mayor Huang Wei-che and Guo Nai-wen, the head of the Social Affairs Bureau, went to the site in the morning. They showed care for the people affected by the disaster and thanked the volunteers who were helping clean.

Mayor Huang added that this typhoon made everyone feel that "we are all in this together." People from places like Kaohsiung, Pingtung, Changhua, New Taipei, and Taoyuan saw how badly Tainan was hit. Some people gave money, and others helped with rescue work. This was very touching.

Mayor Huang also said that the typhoon affected a very large area, and many people still need help. If people in Tainan have free time, they can contact the city government to join the group helping people clean up their homes. Also, it has been raining a lot recently, so people must be careful to prevent dengue fever or other diseases. He hopes everyone can work together to get through this difficult time.

The Tainan City Social Affairs Bureau shared information. After the typhoon, they started the "Tainan City Post-Disaster Volunteer Recruitment Platform." They invited many kind people to join. So far, 645 volunteers have gone to the disaster areas. They move through neighborhoods, bringing hope to people with their hands and smiles. This has become a touching sight after the typhoon.
