European School Publication Supports Unification, Causes Questions; Chen Chang-wen Says It's Free Speech

Chen Chang-wen, the head of Taipei European School, spoke today. His school's magazine has caused questions because it talks about unification. He said he writes about many topics, like climate and energy. He also writes about Russia and Ukraine. He added that the Constitution of the Republic of China gives people freedom of speech.

News reports say that Chen Chang-wen used the school's magazine to say that both sides of the Taiwan Strait should not stop trying to reach a goal of diverse unification. He also said that President Lai Ching-te calling China a "hostile force outside our borders" goes against his duty to the Constitution.

Taipei City Councilor Chao Yi-hsiang, from the Democratic Progressive Party, told Central News Agency that political ideas in a school magazine are not proper. It goes against the rule that schools should stay neutral in politics. Especially because students at Taipei European School come from families of diplomats and foreign business people. Using the school magazine to spread political and cross-strait views seems to have a strong political purpose and is very inappropriate.

The Taipei City Government's Department of Education said that this is a school's internal magazine. They have asked the school to decide for themselves and explain it to the public when ready.

Wanderer Festival, FireBall Festival to Take Place in Autumn; Japanese and Korean Musicians to Join

Taiwanese music festivals will happen one after another this autumn. The Wanderer Festival will start first in Tainan in October. In November, there will be the FireBall Festival, planned by the Golden Melody Award-winning band Fire EX. The band Flesh Juicer, which also won a Golden Melody Award for best band, will perform. Musicians from Japan and Korea will also join.

The Wanderer Festival will begin on October 17 in Tainan. Besides musicians like Mr. Miss and Mango Jump, today new performers were announced. These include the Tokyo rock duo BANSHIMOKU, ASOBOiSM, Da Dao San Ming Zhi, NINI Metal, TRAEGO, Flesh Juicer, Korean singer-songwriter KIM PUREUM, Japanese band TENDOUJI, and NUTS.

ASOBOiSM also said through the organizers today that when they performed in Taipei last year, they felt the audience's excitement and the city's energy. They have always wanted to perform in Taiwan again. Even though this is their first time performing in Tainan, they are excited to share their music and make memories with their fans. "I am truly very excited!" they said.

The FireBall Festival will happen on November 22 and 23 at Rakuten Taoyuan Baseball Stadium. The lineup includes the popular Korean band SE SO NEON and the Japanese band 04 Limited Sazabys from Nagoya, who will visit Taiwan again after seven years. Taiwanese performers include Tizzy Bac, MATZKA, The Crane, and Chou Tzu-tsung. Flesh Juicer, which has played at every FireBall Festival, will also be there. Advance tickets are still on sale.
