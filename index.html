Man Who Lied About Being a Master's Graduate from NTHU Sentenced to 1 Year and 2 Months for Investment Fraud

Hsinchu County, August 4 (CNA) â€“ A man named Lu applied for a programming technician job at a company. He lied, saying he had a master's degree from National Tsing Hua University (NTHU). He also claimed he had been asked by TSMC to create software to make their machines work better. Because of these lies, he was hired and tricked people into giving him over NT$2.35 million. The Hsinchu District Court has sentenced him to 1 year and 2 months in prison for fraud. He can appeal this decision.

Court documents from the Hsinchu District Court show that in 2019, Mr. Lu applied for a programming technician job at a company in Hsinchu City and was hired. However, Mr. Lu had only partly finished his studies at a private university, Chung Hua University. He falsely claimed he had graduated with a master's degree from the Department of Information Management at National Tsing Hua University.

The court documents also state that Mr. Lu even told the company's boss, Mr. Peng, that he used to run a company in the semiconductor industry. He said he had good technology and connections in that field. He also claimed to know high-ranking people at TSMC well. He lied, saying he had been asked to develop software for semiconductors to help TSMC's machines work better.

The court documents point out that Mr. Peng believed Mr. Lu had this experience. So, Mr. Peng signed a contract with Mr. Lu, hiring him as a special technical consultant. Mr. Peng paid him a signing bonus of NT$600,000. Mr. Lu also tricked Mr. Peng into investing more than NT$2.35 million for development work. Later, Mr. Lu kept delaying the work. This is when Mr. Peng found out he had been tricked.

The judge decided that Mr. Lu did not try to earn money honestly. He used the victim's trust and made up stories about developing semiconductor software. He then took money unfairly. This shows he did not follow the law and did not respect other people's property. The judge said his actions should be strongly criticized.

The judge said that during the trial, Mr. Lu kept making excuses and did not show he was sorry. So, the judge sentenced Mr. Lu to 1 year and 2 months in prison for fraud. The money he got illegally, NT$2,958,495, will be taken by the government. He can appeal the decision.

They have also planned a joint event with the international brand Smurfs and invited Nantou County Magistrate Hsu Shu-hua to be the ambassador.

At the press conference, Mayor Lu Shiow-yen said that 19 dance groups from 7 different countries will perform. This is the largest number of groups ever. Since the event is celebrating its 10th year, they have planned a big promotion with the Belgian comic characters, the Smurfs. From now on, you can see Smurf figures in different parts of Taichung.

She added that they invited Hsu Shu-hua, who collects over 3,000 Smurfs, to be the event's ambassador. They also borrowed hundreds of her Smurf items for a special exhibition. To protect these items from damage, the Taichung City Government even bought insurance for them. People who like the Smurfs can visit the exhibition.

In addition, Mayor Lu said that people can sign up online or in person for the "All-People Stepping Dance Fun Competition." Participants can legally perform freely on the streets. There is also a total prize money of NT$100,000. Over 30 groups have already signed up for the competition.

Hsu Shu-hua said it is not easy for an event to run for 10 years in a row. She came to Taichung to learn from their experience and hopes to learn a lot. This year, Taichung, Changhua County, Nantou County, and Miaoli County also promoted their areas in Hong Kong and Japan. Also, this year's National Day Party will be in Taichung, and the National Day Fireworks will be in Nantou. Taichung and Nantou will promote these events together.

She said that because she loves the Smurfs, when Mayor Lu invited the Belgian Ambassador to Taiwan to the event, she also received three Smurfs in the colors of the Belgian flag from the Ambassador. She was very touched by this gift.
