Official Document Shared Online; FDA Head Apologizes, Staff Member Punished

The head of the Food and Drug Administration (FDA), Jiang Zhi-gang, said sorry today. This was because an employee took a picture of an official paper from KMT lawmaker Wang Hung-wei and posted it online. Mr. Jiang said this is completely wrong. The employee who did it has been punished and moved to a different job. The FDA will teach its staff better ways to handle official papers.

Wang Hung-wei wrote on Facebook that on August 1st, she sent letters to different government groups asking for information about their projects. But on the morning of August 6th, someone from the Food and Drug Administration took a photo of her official letter. They then shared it on the social media site Threads and made fun of it. This action goes against being fair in the government and also against rules for handling documents from the Executive Yuan.

This morning, Jiang Zhi-gang went to a meeting at the Legislative Yuan. Before the meeting, reporters asked him about Wang Hung-wei's paper being photographed and shared. He said he was very sorry and that he should officially apologize to the public.

Jiang Zhi-gang said, "This kind of behavior is absolutely not allowed." He explained that they immediately started to look into the matter and began an investigation. After they found out what happened, they quickly punished the employee who was involved. They moved this employee to a different job. The FDA will not let any employee break rules when handling official papers. The agency will teach and explain more about this to its staff.

Typhoon Yang-liu Affects 5 Provincial Road Sections, Causing Blocks

Typhoon Yang-liu caused damage to roads. Today, the Highway Bureau said that as of 8 AM, 5 roads are still blocked. One of them is the temporary road on Provincial Highway 20, close to Kilometer 93, between Qinhe and Fuxing in Taoyuan District, Kaohsiung City. This section is expected to open again by 5 PM.

Typhoon Yang-liu has moved away. This morning, the Central Weather Administration removed the typhoon warning. The Highway Bureau said in a press release that by 10 PM last night, 19 road sections were closed to prevent danger. After checking them at 8 AM today, 5 roads are still blocked. All other roads are now open.

The 5 blocked roads include Provincial Highway 8 in Xiulin Township, Hualien County, from Kilometer 167.1 to Kilometer 184.5 (from Tianxiang to Taroko). At Kilometer 175.4 (west exit of Jinheng Tunnel), water and falling rocks have blocked the road. This section is expected to open again this morning.

The Highway Bureau also said that the temporary road on Provincial Highway 20, near Kilometer 93, from Kilometer 0 to Kilometer 5 (between Qinhe and Fuxing) in Taoyuan District, Kaohsiung City, is flooded. This is because the Laonong River rose quickly, bringing mud and rocks that covered the road from Kilometer 3 to Kilometer 5. This section is expected to open again by 5 PM.

The Highway Bureau pointed out that on Provincial Highway 20, near Kilometer 105, from Kilometer 0 to Kilometer 44 (from Meishan Pass to Xiangyang) in Haiduan Township, Taitung County, there was a landslide at Kilometer 12.5. Other parts of this road are still being checked. The opening time will be decided based on the actual situation.

The Highway Bureau said that on Provincial Highway 20 in Haiduan Township, Taitung County, from Kilometer 149.11 to Kilometer 199 (from Xiangyang to Chulai), there are many landslides. These have blocked both sides of the road at different points, such as Kilometer 155.1, 161.1, 173.9, 177.7, 179.6, 180.9, 181.5, and 189.5 (from Liyuan to Xinwu). These sections are expected to open again by 3 PM today.

Also, in Xinyi Township, Nantou County, trees have fallen on Provincial Highway 21, from Kilometer 120 to Kilometer 144 (from Tongfu to Tataka). This section is expected to open again by noon today.

Typhoon Yang-liu Causes Road Collapse, Hualien Chi Mei Elementary School Closed on August 14th

Because of Typhoon Yang-liu, Chi Mei Elementary School in Ruisui Township, Hualien County, will be closed today. This means no work and no classes. The road leading to the school, County Highway 64, is blocked due to a landslide. The county government made this announcement.
