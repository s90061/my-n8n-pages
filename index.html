Madagascar's President Says He Was Almost Killed, Is Now in a Safe Place

The president of Madagascar, Rajoelina, who is facing difficulties, said today that he is hiding in a "safe place" after someone tried to kill him. People are putting more pressure on him to leave his job. He asked everyone to follow the country's rules.

News agency AFP reported that this speech was delayed two times. It was the first time he spoke publicly since a rebel group supported protests against the government. There were also rumors that he had run away from Madagascar.

Rajoelina, who is 51 years old, said in a live speech: "Since September 25, there have been many tries to kill me and even to start a coup (when the military takes control of the government). A group of military people and politicians planned to kill me."

He said: "I had to find a safe place to protect my life." But he did not say where he was hiding.

Young people mostly started these protests. At first, they were about the poor country's long-term problems with electricity and water. Later, they became a bigger movement against the government, asking Rajoelina to step down.

Rajoelina used to be the mayor of the capital city, Antananarivo. He became president after a coup. Today, he asked everyone to respect the constitution (the country's main law) to solve the growing political problem. He said: "There is only one way to solve these problems, and that is to follow the constitution that we have now."

Trump Changes His Mind About China, Making Trade Problems Less Strict; US Stocks Go Up

US President Donald Trump recently said he would add a 100% tax on goods from China. But later, he changed his mind and was less strict. This made the stock market in New York get better after a big drop last weekend. All four main stock indexes went up today.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went up by 587.98 points, which is 1.29%. It closed at 46067.58 points.

The S&P 500 index went up by 102.21 points, which is 1.56%. It closed at 6654.72 points.

The Nasdaq index went up a lot, by 490.18 points, which is 2.21%. It closed at 22694.61 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index went up a lot, by 315.86 points, which is 4.93%. It closed at 6723.46 points.

US and China Trade Problems Seem to Get Better, Oil Prices Go Up a Little

The United States showed that it is willing to make a trade deal with China. This made the new trade problems between the two big oil-buying countries less tense. After this, international oil prices went up a little today.

On the New York Mercantile Exchange, the price for West Texas Intermediate crude oil for November went up by 59 cents. It reached 59.49 US dollars per barrel.

In London, the price for North Sea Brent crude oil for December went up by 59 cents. It reached 63.32 US dollars per barrel.
