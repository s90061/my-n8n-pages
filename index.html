Moscow Airport Collision: Rossiya Airlines Plane's Tail Damaged

Russian news agencies reported that a Rossiya Airlines plane hit another plane today. This happened at Moscow's Sheremetyevo Airport on a runway. The back part (tail) of the Rossiya Airlines plane was damaged.

Reuters reported that the plane was a Sukhoi Superjet. The event happened this evening when the plane was getting ready to fly to St. Petersburg, Russia's second-largest city.

The people on the plane were taken off. They were then put on a different plane to continue their journey.

Unofficial Russian Telegram channels said that the other plane involved in the collision was an Airbus A330 from China Hainan Airlines. This plane was supposed to fly to Beijing.

6.2 Magnitude Shallow Earthquake Hits Venezuela; Colombia and Other Neighboring Countries Feel It

The U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) reported that a 6.2 magnitude earthquake happened today in western and northern Venezuela. This includes the capital city, Caracas. The Venezuelan government stated that there are no reports of serious damage so far.

Agence France-Presse (AFP) reported that, according to the USGS, the earthquake's center was about 24 kilometers from Mene Grande. This is a small oil town with few people in Zulia state. The earthquake happened at a shallow depth of 7.8 kilometers.

The Colombian Geological Survey measured the earthquake at 6.1 magnitude and called it a "shallow" earthquake. Neighboring Colombia and Caribbean islands like Aruba, CuraÃ§ao, and Bonaire also felt the quake.

In Venezuela, strong earthquakes are not common. Because of this, many people in cities like Caracas and Maracaibo were scared when buildings shook. They are not used to such strong shaking.

Many people ran into the streets to be safe. However, Interior Minister Diosdado Cabello said on state television that the earthquake "did not cause major damage to buildings."

Venezuela's national earthquake research agency, Funvisis, measured the earthquake at 5.4 magnitude.

About 80% of Venezuelans live in areas where earthquakes can happen. However, Venezuela has not had a big earthquake since 1997. That year, an earthquake in Cariaco, Sucre state, killed 73 people.

In 1976, an earthquake in Caracas killed almost 300 people and injured 2000 others.
