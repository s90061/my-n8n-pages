US Federal Reserve Lowers Interest Rate as Expected, US Stocks Show Mixed Results

The US central bank, called the Federal Reserve (Fed), had a meeting about interest rates. They lowered the interest rate by a small amount, which many people expected. After the meeting, the head of the Fed, Jerome Powell, spoke about how the job market is not very strong. Because of this, the main US stock markets had different results today.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average went up by 260.42 points, which is 0.57%. It closed at 46018.32 points.

The S&P 500 Index went down by 6.41 points, which is 0.10%. It closed at 6600.35 points.

The Nasdaq Index went down by 72.63 points, which is 0.33%. It closed at 22261.33 points.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index went down by 19.01 points, which is 0.31%. It closed at 6060.22 points.

Oil Prices Around the World Drop as Markets React to Federal Reserve's Decision

Today, people who trade oil were thinking about new information on how much oil the US has. They also considered the US central bank's (Federal Reserve) choice to lower interest rates. Because of this, oil prices around the world went down today, stopping a three-day rise.

The price for US oil, called West Texas Intermediate crude, for October ended lower by 0.7%. It closed at $64.05 for one barrel.

The price for Brent crude oil, which is traded in London for November, went down by 0.8%. It closed at $67.95 for one barrel.

Israeli Army Works Faster to Clear Gaza City; More Than 65,000 Palestinians Dead in Two-Year Conflict

The Israeli army announced today that they will open another new road for 48 hours. This road is for Palestinians to leave Gaza City. Israel is also trying harder to move people out of the city and fight thousands of Hamas soldiers.

News reports say that many hundreds of thousands of Palestinians are currently hiding in Gaza City. A lot of these people do not want to go south, even though Israel has told them to. This is because the trip is dangerous, life in the south is difficult, there is not enough food, and they are afraid they will never be able to return home.

Ahmed, who teaches at a school there, said in a phone call, "Even if we wanted to leave Gaza City, how can we be sure we can come back? Will the war stop? This is why I would rather die in Sabra, my own neighborhood."

Health authorities in Gaza reported that Israeli air attacks and shootings today killed at least 50 people in different parts of the Gaza Strip. Out of these, 39 people died in Gaza City.

These officials said that with this new count, the total number of Palestinians who have died in the two-year war between Israel and Hamas is now over 65,000.

Palestinian officials and rescue workers also said that the real number of dead might be even higher. Many bodies are still under the broken pieces of buildings that have fallen down.
