China's Inner Mongolia and Yunnan have earthquakes. Many people felt them.

On July 26, early in the morning, two earthquakes happened in China. One was in Alashan League, Inner Mongolia, and the other was in Puer City, Yunnan. Many people in different parts of China said they clearly felt the ground shake.

China's earthquake network reported that the earthquake in Alashan Left Banner, Inner Mongolia, happened at 1:02 AM on July 26. It was a 4.7 magnitude earthquake. The earthquake started 10 kilometers deep underground. Its center was at 38.24 degrees North and 104.02 degrees East.

The center of the earthquake was 91 kilometers from Minqin County in Gansu. It was also 118 kilometers from Jingtai County, Gansu; 126 kilometers from Liangzhou District, Gansu; 129 kilometers from Shapotou District, Ningxia; and 131 kilometers from Gulang County, Gansu. It was 126 kilometers from Wuwei City, Gansu, and 197 kilometers from Yinchuan City.

According to Chinese news, many internet users from Gansu and Ningxia said they felt the earthquake strongly. Some said, "Gansu felt it clearly," and "We were shaken awake." People in Yinchuan also said the shaking was very strong.

The Alashan League Emergency Management Bureau said that they started a Level 4 emergency response for earthquake relief. They are doing work based on their emergency plans. This area is in the middle of a desert. So far, no one has been reported injured or dead.

China's earthquake network also reported that another earthquake happened at 5:31 AM on July 26. It was a 3.1 magnitude earthquake in Ning'er County, Puer City, Yunnan. It started 10 kilometers deep underground. Its center was at 23.16 degrees North and 101.12 degrees East. Many internet users from Yunnan also said they were woken up by the earthquake.

Train Delay: No Relief for Extra Train Conductor, Taiwan Railways Delayed by 16 Minutes

Today, July 26, was the first time people voted to remove politicians from office. Taiwan Railways (TRA) added an extra train, the 5371 Tze-Chiang Limited Express, from Qidu to Taitung. When the train arrived at Chaozhou Station, the train conductor's replacement was not there. After quickly sending someone else, the train was delayed by about 16 minutes. TRA said they will find out who is responsible.

Today, people voted to remove 24 Kuomintang lawmakers and Hsinchu Mayor Kao Hung-an, who was suspended. To handle more travelers, TRA added extra trains for the weekend, including the 5371 Tze-Chiang Limited Express from Qidu to Taitung today. But news came out that when the train reached Chaozhou Station, the train conductor's replacement could not be found.

TRA explained that a deputy manager in charge of assigning train staff in Kaohsiung made a mistake. They wrote the wrong date for the extra train, so a conductor was not assigned. After the train staff department was told, they immediately sent a conductor who was waiting to work.

Because of this mistake, where a conductor was not assigned, the train left about 16 minutes late. TRA said that their Southern Operations Office will review what happened and make improvements. They will also find the people responsible for the mistake to stop it from happening again.
