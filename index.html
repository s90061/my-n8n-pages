US and Brazil Hold High-Level Talks; Trump Pushes Brazil to Drop BRICS Currency Idea

Brazil's Foreign Minister Mauro Vieira will meet with US Secretary of State Marco Rubio in Washington on October 16. They will have important talks about economic and political topics between the two countries. Brazil's Foreign Ministry sees this meeting as a key moment to restart discussions about tariffs (taxes on imported goods) and sanctions (official orders that limit trade or contacts with a country).

During this meeting, Trump announced that the US would offer Argentina up to $20 billion in currency swaps to help make its economy stable. US Judge Orders to Stop White House from Firing Many Federal Workers During Government Shutdown

The US government shutdown has now entered its third week. Today, a federal judge made a temporary order. This order stops the Trump administration from firing thousands of federal government employees during the shutdown. The judge said that the Trump government's actions to cut jobs were against the law.

US news channels, such as CNN and CNBC, reported that a US judge issued this temporary order today. This happened five days after the Trump government told more than 4,000 federal employees that they would lose their jobs.

During a court meeting, Federal District Judge Susan Illston said, "The actions that are happening right now are against the law." She then issued the temporary order.

Two labor unions, which represent tens of thousands of federal employees, asked the judge to prevent the Trump government from firing these workers. The Trump government had previously warned that it would cut jobs during the shutdown. US President Donald Trump also said many times that these job cuts would be aimed at "Democratic agencies" or programs.

NBC News reported that Judge Illston stated that the Trump government "used the break in government spending and operations to think that all rules no longer applied. They thought laws no longer controlled them, and that they could change the government structure they did not like as they pleased."

Illston also added that she believes the plaintiffs (the unions) can show that the Trump government's actions were illegal, went beyond its authority, and were "random and not based on good reasons."

Skye Perryman, the CEO of "Democracy Forward," a group that supports the unions, said: "The President seems to believe that the government shutdown distracts people from his harmful and unlawful actions. However, the American people are holding him accountable, including through the courts."

The US government shutdown has now reached its 15th day. Today, a bill that could have ended the shutdown by providing temporary money failed again in the federal Senate.

The Republican Party wants to first pass a "clean" temporary funding bill. This bill would simply keep government spending at its current levels for seven weeks to prevent the government from stopping its work. However, the Democratic Party wants to include rules in the bill, such as extending tax credits for health insurance. This is to make sure that some important social programs do not stop when their funding runs out. But the two parties cannot agree on the spending bill. The federal government officially shut down on October 1. Leaders from both parties are blaming each other for causing this government shutdown.

During the US government shutdown, Trump and his administration have recently fired many federal workers. According to US media reports, more than 4,000 people have lost their jobs. The government departments affected include Health and Human Services, Homeland Security, Treasury, Education, Commerce, Energy, and Housing and Urban Development.

Russ Vought, the Director of the White House Office of Management and Budget (OMB), said earlier today that the number of job cuts could "go above ten thousand people."
