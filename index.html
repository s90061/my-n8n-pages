Ni Peipei Receives Special Award at Golden Bell Awards, Thanks Her Late Brother Ni Minran

Taipei, October 11 (CNA) â€“ The Golden Bell Awards for radio shows were held tonight. Ni Peipei, a well-known DJ who has worked in radio for over 50 years, received a special award. She thanked her brother, Ni Minran, who has passed away. She said, "He was like a star in the sky who taught me how to be professional at work."

This year, the Minister of Culture, Li Yuan, personally gave the Special Contribution Award to Liao Jianghua, a recording engineer, and to DJ Ni Peipei, who has worked in radio for half a century.

In the 1980s, Ni Peipei became a very popular DJ with her show "Tonight's Stars." She helped and inspired many people. She then moved from being a radio person to managing a radio station. She has been working for 50 years without stopping.

Ni Peipei spoke with feeling and said her first teacher was her late brother, Ni Minran. "He was like a star in the sky. He was very talented and full of new ideas. He influenced me a lot. He taught me how to speak well and how to be professional at work."

Also, in the past, recording engineers were very important for radio shows. They had to create all sound effects by hand. This made radio shows feel alive. Liao Jianghua, a recording engineer, worked behind the scenes his whole life. This was his first time to be on stage. Liao Jianghua thanked the listeners. He said that because of them, recording engineers exist today. He hopes that this art and culture can continue.

Tang Qiyang won an award for her podcast "Lifestyle Program" last year, which was her first time at the Golden Bell Awards. This year, she gave out an award. She said, "I wish all radio people well. Keep sharing your voices."

Du Mansheng, an experienced actor and a well-known voice actor, also gave out an award with actress Wang Juan. Du Mansheng, who is over 80 years old, joked that he watched Wang Juan's shows when he was younger, and he can still see her shows now.

After questioning, the prosecutor believed that Mr. Liu committed serious crimes. These include driving under the influence of drugs, causing death, and using and having Class 2 drugs. He could face at least 3 years in prison. He will also have to pay a lot of money for damages. The prosecutor thought he might try to run away.
