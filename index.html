National Holiday Weekend Starts with Traffic in Northern Taiwan; Highway Bureau Says 11th Will Also Be Busy

Taipei, October 10 (CNA) â€“ Today is the first day of the National Day holiday weekend. The Highway Bureau said that there was a lot of traffic in the northern part of Taiwan. They expect that tomorrow, many tourists will visit popular places, causing more traffic. They suggest that people use public transport or check traffic with an app.

The Ministry of Transportation and Communications' Highway Bureau released a statement today. It said that by 3 PM today, many roads in northern Taiwan had heavy traffic. These included parts of Provincial Highway 2 (Longmen to Fulong, Tamsui Zhuwei to Hongshulin, and Dawulun to Waimushan), Provincial Highway 15 (Longmi Road to Guandu Bridge, and Fengshan Creek Bridge to Zhugang Bridge), Provincial Highway 62 (Qidu to Anle), and Provincial Highway 65 (Tucheng to National Freeway 3, and Xinzhuang to National Freeway 1).

Tomorrow is the second day of the National Day holiday weekend. The Highway Bureau thinks that tourist spots will become busy with cars. Some provincial roads that might have heavy traffic are Provincial Highway 2 (Guandu to Tamsui, Wanli to Dawulun, and Fulong), Provincial Highway 9A (Xindian to Wulai), Provincial Highway 3 (Daxi to Cihu, and Gukeng to Meishan), and Provincial Highway 21 (Sun Moon Lake area).

Also, main roads might have some traffic for a short time. For example, Provincial Highway 61 (Zhongzhang Bridge and Fengbi to Xiangshan), and Provincial Highway 88 (Wujia System to Fengshan).

Roads connecting to highways might also be busy. These include Provincial Highway 64 to National Freeway 3 Zhonghe Interchange, Provincial Highway 65 to National Freeway 3 Tucheng Interchange, Provincial Highway 74 to National Freeway 3 Wufeng Interchange, Provincial Highway 18 to National Freeway 3 Zhongpu Interchange, and Provincial Highway 86 to National Freeway 1 Rende System Interchange.

The Highway Bureau has talked with bus companies to make sure there are enough buses. They will also add more buses if many people need them. As of 12 PM today, buses on the western national freeways made 2244 trips and carried 41,915 people. Buses on the eastern national freeways made 644 trips and carried 13,059 people.

Based on current ticket sales, there are still 67.38% seats available for western national freeway buses tomorrow, and 83.82% for eastern national freeway buses. People who need tickets can check the websites of different bus companies.

The Highway Bureau has planned other routes for provincial roads. They suggest drivers use the Happy Highway App to get live traffic information. They also recommend avoiding busy times and roads, and following traffic information from the police radio and "Variable Message Signs (CMS)" on the road to avoid traffic jams.

Japan-Taiwan Exchange Group Has Lunch with President Lai, Stresses "No Trouble in Taiwan Strait"

Taipei, October 10 (CNA) â€“ A group of Japanese lawmakers visited Taiwan for the Double Ten National Day. Mr. Sasaki Norihisa, who works for the Japan-Taiwan Exchange Group (Nikko Kon), said today that after the celebrations, they had lunch with President Lai Ching-te. During the lunch, they gave a letter from Ms. Sanae Takaichi, the newly elected leader of Japan's LDP party. They strongly said that "nothing bad must happen in the Taiwan Strait." They also said that their goal to keep peace and stability in the Taiwan Strait is the same as Taiwan's.

The Japan-Taiwan Exchange Group is a group of Japanese lawmakers from different parties who support Taiwan. Mr. Furuya Keiji, a member of parliament and the head of this group, led a "Japanese Parliamentary Delegation to Celebrate the Republic of China National Day." This group came to Taiwan for the Double Ten National Day events. There were 28 members of parliament, and they held a press conference in the afternoon.

Mr. Kaneko Yasushi, who is a vice-head of the group, said that Mr. Furuya Keiji had to go back to Japan after President Lai's speech and the performances. This was because the LDP party had an urgent meeting for its leaders.

When Mr. Sasaki Norihisa spoke, he said he was happy to lead the group of lawmakers to Taiwan. He also said that after the National Day ceremony, the Japan-Taiwan Exchange Group had lunch with President Lai. They also gave President Lai a personal letter from Ms. Sanae Takaichi to Mr. Lai Ching-te, who is the head of the DPP party.

Mr. Sasaki Norihisa said that at the lunch, he spoke for the Japan-Taiwan Exchange Group. He told President Lai and Vice President Hsiao Bi-khim that they felt very sorry about the damage caused by Typhoon Kabsa in Hualien. He also gave them a list of people who gave money for disaster relief. Japan's Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, Transport and Tourism will send floating devices to Taiwan by October 17. These devices will help watch water levels in reservoirs, showing Japan's support for working together on disaster prevention.

Mr. Sasaki Norihisa also mentioned that this year is the 100th anniversary of the National Palace Museum. After the press conference, he will go to the museum to give them a valuable pottery artifact from Japan for an exhibition.

Talking about the situation in the region, Mr. Sasaki Norihisa said that China is putting more and more pressure on Taiwan. He said, "Nothing bad must happen in the Taiwan Strait." He also said that Taiwan, Japan, the United States, and Australia should work together more closely. The Japan-Taiwan Exchange Group said they are committed to keeping peace and stability in the Taiwan Strait, and this is the same as Taiwan's commitment.

When asked about the contents of Ms. Sanae Takaichi's letter, Mr. Sasaki Norihisa said he "did not know." He thought it was a reply to President Lai's congratulatory message. However, he also said that sending a personal letter to the head of the DPP party is not new. Mr. Fumio Kishida, a former leader of the LDP party, also sent similar letters before.

The media asked about Mr. Minoru Kihara, who is the secretary-general of the Japan-Taiwan Exchange Group and a member of parliament, not joining the group. He will soon become the Chief Cabinet Secretary. Mr. Sasaki Norihisa answered that Mr. Kihara Minoru could not visit Taiwan because a family member was ill, and there was "no political reason."

Ms. Yasuko Komiyama, a vice-head of the Japan-Taiwan Exchange Group and a member of parliament from the Constitutional Democratic Party, added that the Japan-Taiwan Exchange Group has always included members from different parties. She said that Mr. Furuya Keiji has also been a minister twice. No matter which political party they are from, they all care a lot about the partnership with Taiwan and play important roles in the government.

Rock in Taichung Starts October 11, Over 50 Local and International Bands to Play

Taichung, October 10 (CNA) â€“ The "Rock in Taichung 2025" music festival will take place for two days starting tomorrow. It will be held at the Fulfillment Amphitheater and Wen-Hsin Forest Park. Over 50 bands from Taiwan, Japan, Korea, Hong Kong, Thailand, and Singapore will play their music.

The Taichung City Information Bureau announced today in a press release that "Rock in Taichung 2025" will have exciting performances on October 11 and 12. This year, famous singers and bands will perform. These include Golden Melody Award winner HUSH, popular band Meixiu Group, very popular band Old Wang Band, double Golden Melody winner Hong Pei-yu, and very popular Japanese band Cody.Lee (Lee).

Mr. Luan Zhi-yi, the director of the Taichung City Government Information Bureau, explained that "Rock in Taichung" is now in its 18th year. It is one of the most famous free music festivals in Taiwan. This year, it will be the most international festival ever. More than 50 bands from Taiwan, Japan, Korea, Hong Kong, Thailand, and Singapore will perform. Also, the festival is working with the "ROOKIE A GO-GO" stage from Japan's Fuji Rock Festival. This shows how Taichung is connected to music around the world.

Meixiu Group said that all the songs they prepared this year are the ones their fans want to hear most. They invite everyone to come and sing along loudly. This is the third time Meixiu Group will play at Rock in Taichung. They are excited to make the place exciting again. HUSH also invited fans to come and enjoy the music. He said he has "a spicy setlist that will make you sweat and cry. Audience members can dance, cry, or laugh and just be themselves."

Mango Jump band believes that the spirit of rock music is "doing something with all your heart." This is their fourth time at Rock in Taichung. They remember that "last year was the first time we played at Fulfillment Amphitheater. The crowd was amazing, and since then, we have really wanted to play there again." They also really love Taichung's BBQ and hot pot and look forward to eating them.
