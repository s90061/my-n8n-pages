Tesla Approves Large Stock Reward to Keep Musk Leading

New York, August 4th â€“ Electric car company Tesla announced today that its CEO, Elon Musk, will receive 96 million restricted shares. This is a temporary reward, worth about 29 billion US dollars (around 864.5 billion New Taiwan dollars). This comes while his old pay agreement is still being discussed in court.

Reuters reported that Musk's 2018 pay agreement was for a record 55.8 billion US dollars (about 1.66 trillion New Taiwan dollars). But a court in Delaware canceled it last December. The court said the way Tesla's board approved it was not right and was unfair to shareholders.

Musk appealed in March this year. He said the judge made several legal mistakes when canceling his pay agreement.

Later, Tesla said its board had created a special group to talk about some pay issues related to Musk. They did not give more details.

Tesla shared information with US regulators today. The special group said that if the Delaware court fully approves Musk's 2018 performance award for being CEO, then these new temporary stock awards will be canceled or reduced. This is to make sure he doesn't get paid twice.

For Musk to get these shares, he must stay in a key leadership role at Tesla until the end of 2027. Also, he cannot sell the shares for five years, unless it's to pay for taxes or the cost of buying the shares.

Tesla's special group said this reward is to slowly increase Musk's voting power. Musk and Tesla's shareholders have always said this is important to keep him focused on Tesla's business growth.

The group said, "We know that Musk's other business projects, interests, and needs will take up a lot of his time and attention. But... we also believe this reward will encourage him to stay at Tesla."
