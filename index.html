BLACKPINK Concert in Kaohsiung: City Announces Traffic Control and Transport Information

The K-pop girl group BLACKPINK will hold their world tour concerts on October 18th and 19th at the Kaohsiung National Stadium. The Transport Bureau announced today that parts of the city will have traffic control measures. There will also be more MRT and Taiwan Railways trains, and free shuttle bus services.

BLACKPINK's "DEADLINE" world tour will be in Kaohsiung on October 18th and 19th at the Kaohsiung National Stadium. The Kaohsiung City Transport Bureau released information about transport today.

The Kaohsiung City Transport Bureau said that Dayun Avenue will be closed from 8 AM to 12 AM on October 18th and 19th. Only emergency vehicles, official vehicles, and shuttle buses can enter. Other cars and motorcycles are not allowed to pass or park. Parking rules will be strictly enforced on nearby roads.

The Transport Bureau also expects heavy traffic on Zuonan Road, Cuihua Road, Junxiao Road, and National Highway No. 10. They suggest taking Minzu Road, Bo'ai Road, or High-Speed Rail Road for north-south travel. For east-west travel, they suggest taking Jiachang Road, Xinzhuang 1st Road, or Shuiguan Road.

People who need to pick up or drop off passengers can use the temporary parking areas at MRT stations further away. To get to the concert, take the MRT to R17 World Games Station on the Red Line. From there, it's a 5 to 8-minute walk to the venue. For leaving the concert, Kaohsiung Metro has planned to increase MRT services. Trains will come every 3 to 5 minutes to help people leave smoothly.

Also, there will be free shuttle buses at the concert venue. From 2:30 PM to 6:30 PM, buses will take people from High-Speed Rail Zuoying Station and Taiwan Railways Xinzuoying Station to the concert. From 9:30 PM to 11:30 PM, buses will take people from the concert back to High-Speed Rail Zuoying Station and Taiwan Railways Xinzuoying Station.

Motorcycle parking areas will be on the northwest side of Kaohsiung National Stadium, the west side of Junxiao Road (from Dayun Avenue to Haigong Road), and the west side of Junxiao Road military dependents' village (from Weiyi Road to Weisi Road). These areas will be open from 8 AM to 11 PM on October 18th and 19th. Fans who ride motorcycles should use these areas and leave on time.

The Transport Bureau stressed that, as usual, there will be no car parking at the venue. They ask people not to drive to avoid not finding a parking spot and being late for the concert. Also, Taiwan Railways and Taiwan High-Speed Rail will add more northbound trains for fans from other cities. You can check the train schedule and ticket information on their official websites.

Taoyuan Airport Holds Food Festival, Passengers Vote for Favorite Airport Food

Taoyuan Airport Company announced today that from January to September, the airport had 35.22 million passengers. This is a 5.24% increase compared to the same time last year. To make food and service better, the airport is holding a food festival. Passengers can vote for their favorite airport food.

Today, the airport company held the "2025 Taoyuan International Airport Food Festival Press Conference" in Terminal 2. Five dancers performed with popular airport foods like fried chicken, ramen, egg cakes, pineapple cakes, and bubble tea. They created a lively atmosphere. A popular cheerleader also joined as the event's ambassador. Yang Wei-fu, the chairman of Taoyuan International Airport Company, wore a chef's apron and announced the start of the "Golden Feast Awards" voting event.

Yang Wei-fu told reporters that as more people travel by air internationally, the total number of passengers at Taoyuan Airport is going up. From January to September 2025, the airport had 35.22 million passengers, which is 5.24% more than last year. The airport company will work hard to make food and service better in the future. They want Taoyuan Airport to be a welcoming start and end to every trip. The airport was recently named the number one most friendly airport in the world by APH, a British travel service company.

Yang Wei-fu pointed out that Taoyuan Airport has always worked to promote Taiwanese food. Currently, there are nearly 80 shops selling Taiwanese food and souvenirs at the airport. Through this event, they hope passengers will use Google Maps to vote for their favorite food. Taiwanese food has shown great quality in past reviews. This event also aims to make delicious foods from all over Taiwan known around the world.

The airport company said that from now until December 15th, they invite passengers to leave reviews and ratings on Google Maps. They can choose their favorite airport food from nearly 80 restaurants and souvenir shops. During the event, airport restaurants will offer special deals. If you spend over NT$300 at airport souvenir shops, you will receive a limited-edition gift until supplies run out.

Former Airline Manager Accused of Spying for China; MAC Says Staff Are Highly Alert

The High Prosecutors Office announced today that a former manager named Qiao from an airline tried to get secret information about research projects from officials at the Mainland Affairs Council (MAC) for Chinese intelligence. He also tried to bribe them with NT$100,000, but they refused. The MAC said today that its staff are very aware and careful.

The Taiwan High Prosecutors Office issued a press release. It said that from 2012 to 2014, when Qiao was an airline representative in mainland China, he tried to get and collect secret information about outsourced research projects from MAC officials for Chinese intelligence agencies.

The High Prosecutors Office found that Qiao also tried to give NT$100,000 in cash as a bribe for the Chinese intelligence agencies. However, the official refused the money, so he did not succeed.

The MAC responded in writing today. They said that many of the MAC's research projects are secret, and many people try to get this information. Luckily, the MAC staff are very careful and alert.

Yesterday, prosecutors charged Qiao with breaking the National Security Act and corruption laws.
