China Says No More Talks with Czech President After He Met Dalai Lama, Czech Office Says "No Change"

China is unhappy because the Czech President, Petr Pavel, met the Dalai Lama in India. Because of this, China said it will not talk to President Pavel anymore. The Czech President's office said that this does not change anything. They said that the Czech Republic and China do not have direct talks between their presidents anyway.

On July 27, Czech President Petr Pavel met the Dalai Lama, who is a spiritual leader from Tibet, in India. He also wished him a happy 90th birthday. China was very unhappy and complained about this meeting.

Lin Jian, a spokesperson for China's Foreign Ministry, said that President Pavel ignored China's repeated warnings and strong protests. He went to India to meet the Dalai Lama anyway. China said this seriously broke the Czech government's promise to China. They also said it harmed China's control over its land. Because of President Pavel's "bad and challenging actions," China decided not to have any talks with him.

The Czech President's office replied on X (formerly Twitter). They said, "The President's meeting with the Dalai Lama was a private meeting. Right now, there are no direct talks between the presidents of the Czech Republic and China. So, this action does not change the current situation."

Oil Prices Fall as People Think About Talks Between US and Russian Presidents

Investors are looking at two things. First, US President Trump decided to make the trade break with China longer. Second, they are closely watching what might happen when Trump talks with Russian President Vladimir Putin later this week. Because of these thoughts, world oil prices ended lower today.

The price of West Texas Intermediate crude oil for September on the New York Mercantile Exchange went down by 79 cents or 1.24%. It closed at 63.17 US dollars per barrel.

The price of Brent crude oil from the North Sea in London for October went down by 51 cents or 0.77%. It closed at 66.12 US dollars per barrel.
